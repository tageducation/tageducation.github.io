1. How much is the passing score of 50 items test? What is the formula to get the passing score?​


No matter how many items there are on a test, 75% are required to pass. Therefore, 37.5 out of 50 is required to pass the test. It will be 38 if we round it off to the nearest whole number.

50 x 0.75 = 37.5 or 38

How to calculate the percentage of your score on a test? And how do you know if you passed or you failed the examination?

Start by counting how many of the test's questions were successfully answered before calculating your grade. Subtract that result from the total number of test questions. The test grade is then calculated as a percentage by multiplying the result by 100.

Example 1:

You have a 20-item test and you got 15 correct answers.

15 ÷ 20 = 0.75 x 100% = 75% = PASSED

Always remember that the passing grade is always 75% and above.

Example 2:

You have a 40-item test and you got 38 correct answers.

38 ÷ 40 = 0.95 x 100 = 95% = PASSED

Always remember to multiply the quotient of your score and the total number of items to 100, so that you can get the percentage.

Example 3:

You have a 50-item test and you got 35 correct answers.

35 ÷ 50 = 0.7 x 100 = 70% = FAILED

Example 4:

You have a 10-item quiz and you got 5 correct answers.

5 ÷ 10 = 0.5 x 100 = 50% = FAILED

Example 5:

You have a 35-item long test and you got 28 correct answers.

28 ÷ 35 = 0.8 x 100 = 80% = PASSED

50% of the 13 students is 6.5 ≈ 7. And from the CF column, we can see that this number lies in the class interval [21-26]. Both values are included in the table, We will take the lower value i.e. 21 or 23. So the passing score would be 23.

28. sarah passed her exam.The exam is 50 questions. Sarah got 48% right in the exam. What is she's score?​


Answer:

If Sarah got 48% of the questions right in a 50-question exam, it means she got:

48/100 * 50 = 24 correct answers

Score = (Number of Correct Answers / Total Number of Questions) x 100%

Score = (24 / 50) x 100%

Score = 48%

Therefore, Sarah's score is 48%
