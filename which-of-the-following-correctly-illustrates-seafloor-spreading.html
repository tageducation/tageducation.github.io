4. which of the following is the main concept proposed by the seafloor spreading theory ?​


Answer:

The main points of seafloor spreading theory include the idea that molten material moves in at divergent plate boundaries. This movement creates a new crust and pushes the plates apart.

Explanation:

Seafloor spreading is explained and proven by evidence. Under the oceans lie long mountain chains or ridges. The ocean floor crust on either side of a mid-ocean ridge shows the youngest rock closest to the ridge and the oldest rock farther from it. Also, the pattern of magnetic reversals on either side of the ridge is precisely the same as each other.

About Earthquake

️ An earthquake is a shaking or shaking of the earth's surface. Earthquakes can cause damage to fire.

In science, the study of earthquakes is called seismology. Seismology explains that an earthquake is an event of propagation or propagation of earthquake waves that reach the earth's surface due to the sudden and surprising release of potential energy possessed by layers beneath the earth's surface.

Earthquakes occur because of changes or movements of the earth's crust called tectonic plates and result in a significant change in the location of the ground surface from its original position. The epicenter is the location of the earthquake in the earth's crust where earthquake energy is released. The center of the earthquake itself is divided into two, namely the epicenter (earthquake point on the earth's surface) and hypocenter (earthquake point below the earth's surface).

Earthquake Impact

This sudden movement can certainly be bad for the surrounding population. From landslides to fires, the following are the effects of earthquakes :

1. Ground shaking or shaking

2. Liquefaction (soil turns into liquid)

3. Landslide

4. Tsunami

5. Secondary Hazards (such as short circuit or gas leak which can cause fire)

12. Illustrate the process of seafloor spreading​


Answer:

Seafloor spreading occurs at divergentplate boundaries. As tectonic plates slowly move away from each other, heat from the mantle's convection currents makes the crust more plastic and less dense. The less-dense material rises, often forming a mountain or elevated area of the seafloor. Eventually, the crust cracks.
