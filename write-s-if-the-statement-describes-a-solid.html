The reaction equation is a condition that is written using a chemical formula with a function as a description of the quality and identity of a substance in a chemical reaction. This equation is also called the way it is used to show a relationship between the chemical substances that came before or after it.

The use of chemical formulas is a way of writing these reaction equations, as well as the results of existing equations. Symbols and formulas are used as a way of viewing the materials and products of a reaction equation. The left side is the material, while the right side is the result or product of the reaction equation.

The reaction equation also shows information about the physical condition of the compound, such as arrows which are interpreted as signs of chemical reactions in the form of reactants and to form products and react to become. Aqueous, solid, gas and liquid are the four physical states of compounds that are important to know.

Solid form is a chemical substance that is solid, in this case every solid substance is known by the letter symbol (s).

The liquid form is a chemical substance that is liquid in nature, the form of a liquid substance is always liquid and is symbolized by the letter (l).

Gas is the only form of chemical substance that can move freely and is able to occupy an entire volume, symbolized by the letter (g).
