The following are properties of a parallelogram:

The opposite sides of a parallelogram are congruent.

AB≅CD, AD≅BC

The consecutive angles of a parallelogram are supplementary.

∠DAB and ∠ABC are supplementary.

∠ABC and ∠BCD are supplementary.

∠BCD and ∠CDA are supplementary.

∠CDA and ∠DAB are supplementary.

The opposite angles of a parallelogram are congruent.

∠DAB ≅ ∠DCB

∠CBA ≅ ∠CDA

The diagonals of a parallelogram bisect each other.

O is the midpoint of BD.

O is the midpoint of AC.

Each diagonal of a parallelogram divide it into 2 congruent triangles

ΔABD≅ ΔCBD

ΔABC≅Δ ADC

6. A trapezoidal lot ABCD has sides BC parallel to AD. AB = 395m, AD = 530m and BC = CD. Angle CDA = 86 deg. Determine area of the lot.


A trapezoidal lot ABCD has sides BC parallel to AD. AB = 395 m, AD = 530 m and BC = CD. Angle CDA = 86 deg.

The area of the lot ABCD is 180,860.625 m²

To solve this problem, you can use the formula for the area of ​​a plane figure.

The Area Formula

1. ​​A square

The formula for the area of ​​a square is to multiply the lengths of its sides.

Area = s x s

2. The formula for the area of ​​a triangle

The area =  [tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex] x (base x height)

3. A area of ​​the trapezoid, the formula is:

The area = ½ × sum of parallel sides × height.

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

A trapezoidal lot ABCD has sides BC parallel to AD. AB = 395 m, AD = 530 m and BC = CD. Angle CDA = 86 deg.

Question:

Determine area of the lot.

Solution:

Step 1

Find the height of the trapezoid.

Sin 86⁰ = height ÷ hypotenuse

height = Sin 86⁰ x hypotenuse

height = 0.99 x 395 m

height = 391.05 m

Step 2

Find the area of ​​the trapezoid.

The area = ½ × sum of parallel sides × height

The area = ½ × (395 m + 530 m) × 391.05 m

The area = ½ × 925 m × 391.05 m

The area =  ½ × 361,721.25 m²

The area = 180,860.625 m²

So, the area of the lot ABCD is 180,860.625 m²

Answer:

400 cm²

Step-by-step explanation:

Rotate trapezoid ABCD 180 degrees to get quadrilateral maybe AB.FG

From the given information, AB = AD + BC.

Thus,

[tex]\sf AB = AD + BC = AF = BG[/tex]

This implies that quadrilateral AB.FG is a square (since the trapezoid is also right-angled)

Since E is the midpoint of CD, E is also the midpoint of B.F.

Notice that [tex]\sf B.F = 2BE[/tex]

Thus,

[tex]\sf B.F = 2(20)[/tex]

[tex]\implies \sf B.F = 40[/tex]

Observe that B.F is also the diagonal of square AB.FG.

Hence,

[tex]\sf Area_{\square AB.FG} = (\frac{40}{\sqrt{2} })^2[/tex]

[tex]\implies \sf Area_{\square AB.F.G} = 800[/tex]

Going back, we know that square AB.FG is made up of 2 trapezoid ABCD.

Hence, the area of trapezoid ABCD is 800/2 = 400.
