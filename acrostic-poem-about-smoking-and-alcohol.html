25. what is the best acrostic poem about smoking


Smoking kills. Many studies revealed that smoking cigarettes is associated with contracting various diseases like lung cancer, throat cancer and other lung-related diseases. Hence, it is important that we do not attempt to even smoke. Addiction to smoking, once started, is usually inevitable. Quitting is difficult as one had already built a tolerance for nicotine and the other dangerous substances in the cigarette.

Below is an acrostic poem on smoking.

Smoke coming out of your mouth

Makes its way to mine, I inhale, I smell

Olfactory senses are overwhelmed

Killing me softly, indirectly as second hand smoking

Is what you I am experiencing.  

No, it's not cool. It is disgusting.

Gangrenous feet, black, tarred lungs is what I will have.

Smoking kills, smoking ills

Mind what you inhale, mind what you exhale

Ohh, I would love that you quit

Knowing that smoking could mean losing you

Instead of smoking, why not exercise

Nudge those stubby feet a little

Go the extra mile, not the extra smoke.

In the poem above, I focused on the point of view of the person who loves someone who smokes and wants him or her to stop the addiction because of health reasons. There are many dangers to smoking including the risks of getting addicted to other drugs as well as a cigarette is considered as a gateway drugs which increases the risks of a person abusing illegal drugs as well. In the poem, the persona admonishes the smoker and attempts to persuade him to stop.

 

Some of the dangers of smoking includes: causes certain cancers lowers the immune system makes one prone to emphysema and other lung diseases increases risks of heart diseases detrimental to reproductive health has negative effects on dental health

Quitting Smoking

Many programs are available to help someone quit smoking. The only one preventing people to stop is themselves. Hence, seek help as soon as possible.
