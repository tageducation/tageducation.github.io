1. Compare and contrast the social dance from the dancesport


Answer:

Social dance and dancesport are both forms of dancing, but they have some distinct differences.

Social dance refers to a wide range of dance styles that are typically performed in a social setting, such as a party, club, or ballroom. Social dances include styles like the waltz, tango, salsa, and swing. These dances are often performed with a partner and are designed to be enjoyed by dancers of all skill levels. The focus is often on having fun and enjoying the music, rather than on technical proficiency or competition.

Dancesport, on the other hand, is a competitive form of dance that is often performed in a formal setting, such as a ballroom or dance studio. Dancesport includes styles like the cha-cha, rumba, jive, and samba. These dances are highly choreographed and require a high level of technical skill and training. Dancers are judged on their technique, footwork, and presentation, with the goal of winning competitions.

In summary, social dance is more casual and focused on enjoyment and having fun, while dancesport is more formal, competitive, and focused on technical skill and winning competitions.

What are social dances?

What Is Social Dance? Social dance forms weren't made for stages, but were instead created in streets and clubs, often by members of oppressed communities, as a means of expression and communication. Examples include salsa, swing dance, hustle, and samba, though there are many more.

What are ballroom dances?

Ballroom dancing is a partnership dance where couples, using step-patterns, move rhythmically, expressing the characteristics of music. Ballroom dancing consists of two styles: the Smooth, or Standard, and the Rhythm, or Latin. The Smooth, Standard style focuses on the elegance, grace and fluidity of movement.

What are dance mixers?

A mixer dance, dance mixer or simply mixer is a kind of participation dance in a social dance setting that involves changing partners as an integral part. Mixing can be built into the dance choreography or can be structured to occur more randomly.

What are dance sports?

DanceSport is the activity that combines sport and dance, and that allows the participants to improve physical fitness and mental well-being, to form social relationships and to obtain results in competition at all levels.
