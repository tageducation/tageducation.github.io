24. Why padre faura expected that they would talk about the stars​


Padre Faura expected they would talk about the stars because Padre Faura was on the roof of Ateneo Municipal, more particularly in the Manila Observatory. According to the poem ‘Padre Faura witnesses the execution of Rizal’ in the first verse “I stand on the roof of the Ateneo Municipal” and in second verse “Months ago, Pepe came to me in the observatory”. The Manila Observatory is a scientific research institution with research work in the fields of atmospheric and earth science. With that being said, Padre Faura was expecting they would have a conversation about the stars because Pepe came to him when he was in the observatory.

Further explanation:

Padre Faura is a well-known figure in the Philippines. He is best known for his work as an educator and his dedication to helping others. Padre Faura passed away in 1930, but his legacy continues on through the many institutions that he helped to create.

In his life, Padre Faura was the director of the Manila Observatory for many years and played a large role in its development. He also made significant contributions to the field of meteorology, particularly in the areas of weather forecasting and climate research. In recognition of his work, the Father Faura Memorial Lecture Series was established at the observatory.

The Manila Observatory is located within the campus of the Ateneo de Manila University in Quezon City, Philippines. It was founded by the Society of Jesus, also known as the Jesuits, in 1865 as the Observatorio Meteorol gico del Ateneo Municipal de Manila. It was later given the name Observatorio Meteorol gico de Manila. It's been capable of conducting weather tracking and earthquake forecasting and today studies seismic and geomagnetic phenomena as well as radio and solar physics.

The Manila Observatory is a scientific research institution with research work in the fields of atmospheric and earth science. It advocates a science-based approach to sustainable development and poverty reduction through its principal focus on the areas of climate change and disaster science.
