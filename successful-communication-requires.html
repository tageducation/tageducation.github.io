7. Which of the following statements is NOT TRUE? Non-verbal communication does not complement verbal communication. Communication requires a speaker and a listener who ideally should be individuals who share a commonness of experience. Communication comes in varied forms and patterns depending on the given situation. Success in communication depends largely on the perfect chemistry between the sender and the listener.


Answer:

Let us take a look at all of the statements before we decide which one is not true. This is going to help us understand communication on a deeper level.

1. Non-verbal communication does not complement verbal communication.

Verbal communication or speech is truly the most obvious form of transmitting information. However, it is not always enough. Non-verbal communication, which comes in the form of body language, gestures, movements and more, is also a valid way to convey feelings and information. The two of them should be used together whenever possible to most effectively deliver what we want to say.

2. Communication requires a speaker and a listener who ideally should be individuals who share a commonness of experience.

A speaker and a listener are both required in order to have communication. It is not a one-way street because it involves relaying information or feelings from one person to another. You cannot call it communication if there is only one party involved in the exchange.

3. Communication comes in varied forms and patterns depending on the given situation.

Ultimately, there is more than just one way to communicate. It is wrong to think that there is a single template that will lead to successful communication. Different situations call for different actions and modes. The context is necessary before we decide on the best way to convey what we think or feel. Every interaction is going to be different for this reason.

4. Success in communication depends largely on the perfect chemistry between the sender and the listener.

The sender and the listener must both be active participants in a conversation or exchange to ensure successful communication. While chemistry is not the only thing that determines the success of an exchange, it is a big factor. If there is no chemistry between two participants, there is a good chance that it will result in communication breakdown.

Based on my assessment, the correct answer is the first one. It is NOT TRUE that "Non-verbal communication does not complement verbal communication." These two modes go hand in hand when it comes to conveying our thoughts and feelings. Non-verbal communication is helpful and can enhance our verbal communication. The rest of the statements are correct, so this is the only possible answer in my opinion.

Communicators are parties who act as senders of messages to the communicant in a communication process. In other words, a communicator is a person or group of people who take the initiative to be a source in a relationship.

2. Communication is an on-going process. TRUE

Communication is dynamic. This means that the communication process is not permanent, but occurs continuously and changes from time to time

3. Communication is unintentional. TRUE

Communication can occur intentionally or unintentionally. Intentional communication occurs when a message that has a specific purpose is sent to the intended recipient. But messages that are accidentallysent or are not meant for a specific person to receive.”

4. Communication may not be possible in some situations. TRUE

In communicating there are obstacles that can interfere with the delivery of messages within the organization, these barriers are divided into three namely technical barriers, semantic barriers, and behavioral barriers.

5. Context is a crucial component of communication. TRUE

Communication context is the environment in which the communication process occurs.

6. Efficient reading is required in decoding a message effectively.TRUE

Effective communication occurs when the message conveyed by the communicator can be received well or equally by the communicant, so that there is no misunderstanding. Communicating effectively means that communicators and communicants both have the same understanding of a message

Can be Verbal communication done in oral or written form, while non-verbal communication generally uses body language such as hand movements, facial expressions, head shaking, signs, actions and so on.

A communicator must convey a clear message to the recipient so that the recipient understands and understands the message conveyed so that communication runs effectively.

9. Noise affects the communication process. TRUE

Noise makes communication ineffective.

10. One advantage of a written communication is that it provides records and references. TRUE

Written communication can be used as evidence for documentation. Written communication overcomes barriers to oral communication. Written communication is more structured, directed, and planned.

11. Oral communication is an essential element for the success of an organization or a team. TRUE

Verbal communication gets a direct response and can provide clear and detailed explanations.

12. People communicate to meet their needs. TRUE

Communication seeks to bridge between a person's thoughts, feelings, and needs with the outside world.

The sender wants to communicate by sending a message to the recipient
