13. what would you do to empower yourself?​


Self-empowerment can be defined as a situation in which a person with full awareness makes a decision to become independent, have self-confidence, and continue to develop themselves in a better direction. This includes making positive decisions, taking action to move forward, and believing in one's ability to carry out decisions.

In other words, humans in modern life need to have a strong identity and identity. So that we can play a role and take part in the process of globalization, not only as passive spectators and swayed in the global value order which is sometimes not in accordance with the values ​​and morals of the Indonesian nation. In order to be able to play an active role in global change, a balanced self-management ability is needed in terms of cognitive, attitude or values ​​to face challenges, obstacles and take advantage of opportunities.

This is related to management. management means management. The term management is growing rapidly in business organizations, and these days it is often

used in the field of self-development psychology with the term self-management. In simple terms, self-management can be interpreted as a person's ability to manage, control himself all his potential, especially cognitive aspects, attitudes, values ​​to achieve success and happiness in life.

To achieve success in life is not easy, but there are many challenges, obstacles that must be faced proportionally. Therefore, individuals must be able to evaluate themselves honestly, then set life goals, determine strategies or tips for achieving life goals, and evaluate performance to make improvements in the future. In achieving the goal, it takes hard work, sincerity and optimization of the potential that exists in us. The author feels the need to offer a strategy to achieve success through effort, (1) self-empowerment, (2) developing self-discipline, (3) improving performance, and (4) managing stress

The empowerment process is usually carried out in two ways, namely conceptually and empirically. Empowerment conceptually means increasing the ability to create ideas or more specifically the process of self-awareness. Furthermore, empirical empowerment is carried out by individuals to improve life in a practical way. Self-empowerment steps include (1) examining self-limitations, (2) expanding boundaries, (3) establishing a positive inner dialogue, (4) seeking support.

headline caught your attention and you found yourself wondering, what does make a truly great counselor, you are not alone. In fact, many of today’s leading counselors say it pays to never stop asking — and trying to answer — that very question.

No matter where they are in their professional journeys, counselors can still benefit by learning from their colleagues’ experiences and reflections, says Jeffrey Kottler, a professor of counseling at California State University, Fullerton, president of Empower Nepali Girls and a prolific author who has written and presented on master counselors.

“I’ve always mistrusted anyone who claims they understand what counseling is all about and how it ‘really’ works,” Kottler says. “The process is far too mysterious and complex to ever truly get a handle on all the nuances. Far too often, we don’t trust ourselves when we totally buy into what supposed other ‘experts’ tell us is an approximation of so-called truth. Counseling is such a private enterprise that takes place behind closed doors. We rely on self-reports by counselors, and sometimes their clients, about what happened in the room, but I’m not sure those assessments are all that accurate and robust. So I think we are

all works in progress, striving to do better. I think it’s reassuring to most of us to find out that we are all dealing with similar issues and that we aren’t alone in this struggle.”

The best counselors in the field aren’t necessarily those who are most well known but rather those who are always reaching toward greatness and flat out working harder than everyone else, Kottler says. “[These counselors] are constantly questioning what they do and why, being brutally honest with themselves about their work and its outcomes,” he says. “They are always soliciting feedback from their clients and colleagues, begging for the most frank assessments about what is working and what is not. Most of all, they are often so humble that they don’t seek attention or the limelight but just quietly go about their extraordinary commitment to helping others.”

Is the topic of “great counseling” worth exploring? Absolutely, Kottler says. “Heck, if we can’t figure out what makes a counselor great, then how can we possibly ever work toward the goal of excellence?”
