Answer:

The pole is 8 meters tall.

EQUATION:

N:10 = 12:15

Multiply the means

[tex]10 \times 12 = 120[/tex]

Multiply the extremes

[tex]n \times 15 = 15n[/tex]

then equate

[tex] \frac{15n}{5} = \frac{120}{15} [/tex]

cancel 15 and divide 120 to 15

[tex]120 \div 15 = 8 \\ n = 8[/tex]

to check:

[tex] \frac{8}{10} = \frac{12}{15} \\ 8 \times 15 =120 \\ 12 \times 10 = 120[/tex]

Therefore, the pole is 8 meters tall.#Carryonlearning

11. A tree cast a shadow of 30m when a 9m post nearby cast a shadow odf 90m.Find the height of the tree


Answer:

The height of the tree is 3 meters.

Step-by-step explanation:

Ratio and Proportion Problem:

Height of tree: x

Shadow : 30 m

Height of nearby post: 9 m

Shadow: 90 m:

Equation:

x : 30 = 9 : 90

Find the height of the tree:

90x = (9)(30)

90x/90 = 270/90

x = 3

The height of the tree is 3 meters.

25. A tree casts a shadow of 15m when a pole 5m high casts a shadow of 4m.how high is the tree?


18.75 meters
15/4=a
a*5
18.75 meters

Let mhigh be the height in meters and mshadow be the length of shadow in meters. Let x be height of the tree in meters. Let's find x.

Ratio of the tree to pole by shadow and height in meters...

[tex]\frac{15mshadow}{x}=\frac{4mshadow}{5mhigh}[/tex]

[tex]\frac{15mshadow}{x}=0.8mshadow/mhigh[/tex]

[tex]15mshadow=(0.8mshadow/mhigh)(x)[/tex]

[tex]x=18.75mhigh[/tex]

The tree is 18.75 meters high.

[tex]\large{\mathbb{PROBLEM:}}[/tex]

The shadow of an object varies directly as its heights. A man 1.4meter tall cast a shadow 0.7m at the same time. A tree casts a shadow of 1.3m. How high is the tree?

[tex]\: \:[/tex]

[tex]\large{\mathbb{SOLUTION:}}[/tex]

According to the situation, the height of a man is directly proportional to the shadow of a tree and the shadow of a man is directly proportional to the height of a tree. Let us use the proportion in solving the problem and use x to determined the height of a tree. [tex]\small{\tt{1.4:1.3=0.7:x}}[/tex]

[tex]\small{\mathbb{GIVEN:}}[/tex]

1.4m (height of a man)0.7 m (shadow of a man)1.3m (shadow of a tree)x = ? (height of a tree)

[tex]\small{\mathbb{SOLVING:}}[/tex]

To calculate the height of a tree, we must multiply the height of a man(1.4m) by the shadow of a tree(1.3m) divided by the shadow of a man(0.7m).

[tex]\small \tt{x}[/tex] = [tex]\large \tt \frac{1.4×1.3}{0.7}[/tex]

[tex]\small \tt{x}[/tex] = [tex]\large \tt \frac{1.82}{0.7}[/tex]

[tex]\small \tt\green{x=2.6}[/tex]

[tex]\: \:[/tex]

[tex]\large{\mathbb{ANSWER:}}[/tex]

Therefore, the height of the tree is 2.6m

[tex]\: \: \:[/tex]

[tex]\large{\mathbb{CHECK:}}[/tex]

Check if both proportions will have the same product. [tex]\small{\tt{1.4:1.3=0.7:2.6}}[/tex]

[tex]\small{\tt{1.4×1.3=1.82}}[/tex] [tex]\small{\tt{0.7×2.6=1.82}}[/tex] [tex]\small{\tt{1.82=1.82}}[/tex]
