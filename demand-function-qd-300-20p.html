2. Demand function: Qd=300-20P


The relationship between the quantity demanded for a commodity (the dependent variable) and the price of the commodity is represented by the demand function.

In the case of a linear demand function, the demand curve's slope is constant over its entire length. Mathematically, a linear demand equation is written as:

Qx = a – bPx

A stands for the total demand at the zero price in this equation.

B stands for slope or the angle between Qx and Px.

Change in Qx for Change in Px can also be used to indicate B.

The equation above can be used to determine the demand for a commodity at any given price if the values of a and b are known.

Given a = 300, b = 20, and P = 1:

The demand function is:

Qd = 300 – 20 (P)

Therefore, Qd = 300 – 20 (1)

or Q = 280 units

Given a = 300, b = 20, and Qd = 200:

The demand function is:

Qd = 300 – 20 (P)

Therefore, 200 = 300 – 20 (P)

or P = 5

Given a = 300, b = 20, and P = 6:

The demand function is:

Qd = 300 – 20 (P)

Therefore, Qd = 300 – 20 (6)

or Q = 180 units

Given a = 300, b = 20, and Qd = 100:

The demand function is:

Qd = 300 – 20 (P)

Therefore, 100 = 300 – 20 (P)

or P = 10

Given a = 300, b = 20, and P = 15:

The demand function is:

Qd = 300 – 20 (P)

Therefore, Qd = 300 – 20 (15)

or Q = 0 units

A relationship between one variable and its determinants is described by a demand function.

   It details the volume of items purchased for the subject and related goods at various prices, as well as various income levels and different estimates for other factors that influence demand. A person's demand function describes how much of a commodity they would demand at different prices given their income, the costs of comparable goods, and their preferences. A single demand function is the basic building block of the demand theory, and its formula is D = f(P) (ii). Calculating demand elasticity requires dividing the percentage change in quantity demanded, q, by the percentage change in price, p. This concept is sometimes referred to as price elasticity of demand. Despite the fact that a demand curve is a certain kind of curve, the demand function produces a range of demand curves, and the original demand curve may change.

   A demand function is described by the equation p=f(x), p = f (x), where p represents the unit price and x represents the quantity in question. A demand function is typically characterized as a decreasing function of x, meaning that it gets smaller as x grows.

10. "Brainliest ko po makatama rito"Kung ang Qd ay 100 sa demand function na Qd = 300 – 20P. Ilan ang value ng presyo?


PRESYO

Katanungan:

Kung ang Qd ay 100 sa demand function na Qd = 300 – 20P. Ilan ang value ng presyo?

Kasagutan:[tex] \color{hotpink}{ \bold{10}}[/tex]

— — — — — — — — — —

Base sa katanungan nakalahad sa itaas, ang demand function ay:

[tex]\underline{ \boxed{ \sf Qd=300-20P}}[/tex]

- Gamit ang ibinigay na demand function, ipalit o ilagay lamang natin ang value ng Quantity demanded sa variable na Qd 'saka ito lutasin upang makuha natin ang value ng presyo

Kompyutasyon:[tex] \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \sf \: Qd = 300 – 20P \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \sf \: 100 = 300 - 20P \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \sf \: 20P = 300 - 100 \\ \sf \frac{ \cancel{20} P}{ \cancel{20} } = \frac{200}{10} \\ \: \: \sf \: \: \: P = \underline{ \boxed{ \blue{ \sf 10}}}[/tex]

Checking:

Kung ang presyo ay 10, ang dapat na Qd o Quantity demanded nito ay 100.

Gamit ang ibinigay na demand function, i-kompyut naman natin ngayon ang Qd (Quantity demanded) upang ma kumpirma na tama ang ginawa nating kompyutasyon sa itaas.

[tex] \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \sf Qd = 300 \ – \: 20P \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \sf \: Qd = 300 \: – \: 20(10) \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \sf \: Qd = 300 \: – \: 200 \\ \sf \: Qd = \underline{ \boxed{ \color{darkblue}{ \sf 100 }}}[/tex]

Samakatuwid, kung ang Qd ay 100 sa ibinigay na demand function, ang magiging value ng presyo nito ay 10.
