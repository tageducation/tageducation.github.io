17. The processor or the CPU is the main component of the computer. Identify different Types of processors, and explain the factors that influence individual choices when Selecting a processor.


Answer:

When selecting a processor, you must consider its overall processing speed. To determine its processing speed, we have the following specifications:

Clock FrequencyCoresMax Turbo FrequencyCacheOverclockable? (optional)etc.

Clock Frequency is the amount of cycles a core or CPU goes through every second. Meaning, the higher the Clock Frequency, the faster your processor is. Clock Frequency is measured in hertz, usually in GHz or gigahertz.

Cores are the CPU, the ones that does execution instructions. The more cores you have, the more processing you can do simultaneously.

Max Turbo Frequency the nominal frequency that all cores can reach at the same time with power and thermal limits in consideration.

Whenever the processor has to go out to main memory to retrieve data, it ends up sitting idle for most of the time. It’s for this reason that processors make use of a cache on the physical chip. Cache takes advantage of spatial and temporal locality to reduce the amount of time the CPU(s) spends waiting on other devices.

However, these are not the only factors that should be took into consideration when selecting a processor, you should also consider the other parts of the computer, most importantly the GPU or Graphics Card, cause if you have a less powerful processor in contrast to a powerful Graphics Card, you will experience a bottleneck, and you wont be able to use your powerful Graphics Card to its full capacity.
