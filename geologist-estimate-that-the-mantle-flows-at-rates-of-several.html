What does the Plate Tectonic Theory explain about the lithosphere of

the Earth?

Answer:

A. It is fragmented into several segments

The plate tectonic explains that the plates are divided into several segments which are moving in a certain direction as being influenced by the convection current of the mantle.

Three Types of Plate Boundaries Created by Plate movement:

Transform Fault Boundary -  Two plates are sliding past each other. Divergent Plate Boundary -  Two plates are moving away from each other. Convergent Plate Boundary -   Two plates are moving towards each other.

Expected Geological Event to Occur  in Transform fault boundary:

Earthquake

Expected Geological Events to Occur  in Divergent plate boundary:

Earthquake Rift Valley Mid-ocean Ridge Seafloor spreading Volcanic activity in the form of fissure eruptions

Expected Geological Events to Occur  in Convergent plate boundary:

Earthquake Trench Volcanic arc ( volcanoes ) Subduction process Tsunami Mountain ranges

Earth’s Mantle

It is a common misconception that the mantle is liquid, but the truth is, the mantle is actually a rigid, solid layer made of silicate rocks known as perovskite, so the answer is letter A.

Explanation:

The mantle is not a metallic layer because it is made of silicate rocks. The inner core and the outer core are considered as metallic layers because they are made of iron and nickel alloy. The only liquid layer of the earth is the outer core. The hottest layer of the earth is the inner core.
