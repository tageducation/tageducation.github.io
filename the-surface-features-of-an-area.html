2. What surface feature result when the mantle in the area melts?


Answer:

hot spots

Explanation:

Mantle plumes are the likely cause of hot spot volcanic regions not created by plate tectonics. As a mantle plume reaches the upper mantle, it melts into a diapir. This molten material heats the asthenosphere and lithosphere, triggering volcanic eruptions.
