9. Give an example of in-group and out-group.


in-group and out-group.

An in-group is a social group to which a person mentally identifies as belonging in sociology and social psychology. An out-group, on the other hand, is a social group with which a person does not identify. It's possible for people to identify with their own peer group (or family), community (or sport), political party (or gender), race (or religion), or nation (or both). The psychological membership of social groups and categories has been linked to a wide range of occurrences.

Henri Tajfel and colleagues popularized the word during their work on social identity theory in the 1970s. The minimal group paradigm was used to determine the importance of in-group and out-group classification.
