Answer:

Once upon a time, in a small village nestled in the heart of a dense forest, lived a young girl named Lily. Lily was known for her kind heart, gentle nature, and her love for nature. She spent most of her days wandering through the forest, admiring the beauty of the trees and flowers.

One day, while exploring a remote corner of the forest, Lily stumbled upon a mysterious man. The man was tall, dark, and ruggedly handsome, with piercing green eyes that seemed to bore into her soul. His name was Jack, and he was a traveler passing through the village.

Lily was instantly drawn to Jack's rugged charm and his mysterious aura. As they began to talk, she realized that Jack was a man of few words, and he seemed to carry a heavy burden with him. Despite his rough exterior, Lily could sense that Jack was a good man at heart.

As the days passed, Lily and Jack grew closer, and a strong bond began to develop between them. However, their relationship was not without its challenges. Jack had a dark secret that he was reluctant to share with Lily, and she began to suspect that he was involved in something illegal.

Despite her fears, Lily remained committed to Jack, and she refused to believe that he was anything other than a good man. However, as Jack's secrets began to unravel, Lily found herself caught in the middle of a dangerous conflict, with her life hanging in the balance.

In the end, Lily's love for Jack was put to the ultimate test. She had to decide whether to trust her heart and stand by Jack's side, or to listen to her fears and walk away. In a dramatic finale, Lily made her choice, and the fate of her relationship with Jack was sealed forever.

Plot - How the author arranges events to develop the basic idea; it is the sequence of events in a story or play. The plot is a planned, logical series of events having a beginning, middle, and end. The short story usually has one plot  so it can be read in one sitting. There are five essential parts of plot: 1) Exposition (introduction) - Beginning of the story; characters, background, and setting revealed. 2) Rising Action - Events in the story become complicated; the conflict is revealed. These are events between

        the introduction and climax.

• Conflict - Essential to plot, opposition ties incidents together and moves the plot. Not merely

limited to arguments, conflict can be any fOfI1)of struggle the main character faces. Within a short

story, there may be only one central struggle, or there may be many minor obstacles within a

dominant struggle. There are two ~ of conflict:

o Internal- Struggle within one's self.

• Character vs. Self - Struggles with own soul, physical limitations, choices, etc.

o External - Struggle with a force outside one's self.

• Character vs. Character - Struggles against other people.

• Character vs. Nature - Struggles against animals, weather, environment, etc.

• Character vs. Society - Struggles against ideas, practices, or customs of others

3) Climax - Turning point of the story. Readers wonders what will happen next; will the conflict be resolved

or not? Consider the climax as a three-fold phenomenon:

• Main character receives new information.

• Main character accepts this information (realizes it but does not necessarily agree with it).

• Main character acts on this information (makes a choice that will determine whether or not objective is met).

4) Falling action - Resolution begins; events and complications start to fall into place. These are the events

        between climax and denouement.

5) Resolution (Conclusion) - Final outcome of events in the story.

II. Setting - Time and location that a story takes place. For some stories, the setting is very important; while for

others, it is not. When examining how setting contributes to a story, there are multiple aspects to consider:

1) Place - Geographical location; where is the action of the story taking place? 2) Time - Historical period, time of day, year, etc; when is the story taking place? 3) Weather conditions - Is it rainy, sunny, stormy, etc? 4) Social conditions - What is the daily life of the character's like? Does the story contain local colour (writing

that focuses on the speech, dress, mannerisms, customs, etc. of a particular place)?

5) Mood or atmosphere - What feeling is created at the beginning of the story? Cheerful or eerie?

III. Character - There are two meanings for "character": 1) a person in a fictional story; or 2) qualities of a person.

1) People in a work of fiction can be a(n):

• Protagonist - Clear center of story; all major events are important to this character.

• Antagonist - Opposition or "enemy" of main character.

2) Characteristics of a character can be revealed through:

• his/her physical appearance

• what he/she says, thinks, feels, dreams and what he/she does or does not do

• what others say about him/her and how others react to him/her3) Characters can be ...

o Round - Fully developed personalities that are affected by the story's events; they can learn, grow,

or deteriorate by the end of the story. Characters are most convincing when they resemble real

people by being consistent, motivated, and life-like.

• Flat - One-dimensional character

• Dynamic - Character who does go through change and "grows" during a story

• Static - Character does not go through a change.

26. V. Reflection A short story is The elements of a story are: The setting is the: The characters are Plot is made up of :​


Short story-A short story is a fully developed story which is shorter than a novel and longer than a fable. It typically takes just a single sitting for reading. Short Story focuses on the incidents bigger or smaller and evokes strong feelings from its readers. A short story often has a few characters in the plot.

Elements of a story are:

Setting

Character

Point of View

Conflict

Plot

Theme

Setting-A setting (or backdrop) is the time and geographic location within a narrative, either nonfiction or fiction. It is a literary element. The setting initiates the main backdrop and mood for a story.

Character-A character is a person, animal, being, creature, or thing in a story. Writers use characters to perform the actions and speak dialogue, moving the story along a plot line. A story can have only one character (protagonist) and still be a complete story.

Plot-In a literary work, film, story or other narrative, the plot is the sequence of events where each affects the next one through the principle of cause-and-effect. The causal events of a plot can be thought of as a series of events linked by the connector "and so".
