Answer:

•A rational function is defined as the quotient of polynomials in which the denominator has a degree of at least 1 . In other words, there must be a variable in the denominator. The general form of a rational function is p(x)q(x) , where p(x) and q(x) are polynomials and q(x)≠0 .

•Rational equation is a type of equation where it involves at least one rational expression, a fancy name for a fraction

For example, 2x+14=x3 2 x + 1 4 = x 3 is a rational equatio

•Rational inequality is an inequality that contains a rational expression. Inequalities such as32x>1,2xx−3<4,2x−3x−6≥x, and 14−2x2≤3x are rational inequalities as they each contain a rational expression.
