22. what kind of motion caused by earth's rotation about it's axis​


This spinning movement is called "Rotation".

What is Rotation of Earth?

Rotation of the earth is the circulation of the earth around its axis. The Earth moves from west to east, or appears counterclockwise. The earth circulates, but the organisms in it do not rotate. This is due to the Earth's gravity.

Earth's rotation time

One rotation of the Earth takes 23 hours 56 minutes, or 24 hours minus 4 minutes. When the earth rotates, the atmosphere that surrounds the earth also rotates. by the earth's rotation

Effect of Earth's Rotation

1.Occurrence of day and night

The alternation of day and night is a result of the rotation of the earth. The Earth is divided into two parts: one facing the sun and one facing away from it. Areas of the earth experience sunlight when they are on the part facing the sun. On the other hand, the part opposite the sun will be night.

2. Regional time differences

Also, due to the earth's rotation, there is a time difference depending on the region. The area facing the sun is constantly changing. The shift in area causes time differences between one region and another.

3. Simulation of the sun's daily motion

This diurnal motion of the Earth also causes the apparent daily motion of the Sun. Every day we seem to see the sun rotating from east to west, or marking the rising and setting of the sun.

5 Seasonal changes in the constellations due to Earth's rotation

4. River bend formed by seawater

The appearance of waves and coastal currents is caused by the wind. These currents rotate clockwise in the Southern Hemisphere. In the northern hemisphere, on the other hand, ocean currents rotate counterclockwise. This deflection is caused by the rotation of the earth as it rotates under a moving object such as water or air.

5. Differences in Earth's Gravitational Acceleration

Due to the rotation of the earth, it moves away from the center. As a result, the shape of the Earth becomes imperfect, with the poles being smaller in diameter than the equator. This condition causes gravity to be faster in the polar regions.

Earth's rotation process

As previously explained, the earth's rotational motion is from west to east, or counterclockwise. The rotational speed of the Earth is 1,770 km/h. Earth's rotation will continue to slow down due to the moon's gravitational pull. Each day, gravitational motion is slowing by 1.7 milliseconds per day compared to a century ago.

The solar system is not simply a collection of stationary planets, moons, and other bodies around the sun. Each planet rotates, or spins, on its axis.

The rotation of the Earth on its axis causes day and night. As the Earth rotates, only one-half of the Earth faces the sun at any given time. The half facing the sun is light (day) and the half facing away from the sun is dark (night). The animation below shows the Earth's rotation.

When viewed above the North Pole, the Earth rotates counterclockwise, from west to east. This is also called a prograde rotation. Because of this direction of rotation, we see the sun rising every day in the east and setting in the west. If a planet spins in a clockwise direction, it is said to have a retrograde rotation. Venus is an example of a planet with a retrograde rotation.
