15. what is memorized speech


WHAT IS MEMORIZED SPEECH

• This type of speech requires the speaker to commit themselves to memorize their notes before delivering it. There is a possibility for the risk of sounding mechanical during a memorized delivery. Through this, the speaker needs to keep their memorized speech short, with effort in their facial expressions and the tone of their voice

• This type of speech is can be very tough and mechanical but  

• Memorized speech is useful during special or rare occasions.

• This type of speech is best suited for the introduction of guests, acceptance of awards and recognition, toast, eulogy, tribute and the like.

DISADVANTAGES OF MEMORIZED SPEECH

1. There is a tendency that you might forget what you are supposed to say. Long pauses might create a very awkward moment between the speaker and the audience.  

2. The speaker might memorize the speech mechanically which might result in a very unnatural delivery.  

3.  They might focus on content. They will give more attention on the essence of what you are telling than what you deliver the speech  

4. The speaker might be too tied to remembering your script. This will not give you chance to pay attention and respond to audience feedback.

ADVANTAGES OF MEMORIZED SPEECH

1. The speaker does not need notes anymore. Since the speech is memorized, the speaker does not have to worry about when to read and when to glance at your audience.  

2. The speaker can plan gestures, facial expressions, and movement. It will easier for the speaker to do non verbal communication when they know the speech by heart.

3. The speaker can concentrate on visual aids and props. A memorized speech will helps the speaker focus more on your props if you have any.  

4. The speaker will feel more confident. If you know that you have committed the speech to memory, the speaker will not be anxious about running out of words or not knowing what to say.
