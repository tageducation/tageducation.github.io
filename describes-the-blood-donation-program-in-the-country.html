6. Deinfe Blood Donation?


Blood donation refers to a voluntary act of a person drawing blood from him/her for current or for future medical use such as blood transfusion, fractionation (breaking down of a whole blood component into fractions) blood samples for testing and many other related purposes.  Donation may be of whole blood or of specific four (4) blood components such as red blood cell, white blood cell, platelets and plasma.

 

Donation may be done paid or unpaid. In third world countries, limited supply of blood forces other family relatives to donate blood for their sick family members in exchange to the blood available in the blood bank.  Many blood donors donate as an act of charity, and in some cases there are incentives for donating such as paid time off from work.

NATIONAL BLOOD SERVICES ACT

Republic Act No. 7719, otherwise called the National Blood Services Act of 1994, elevates intentional blood gift to give adequate stockpile of safe blood and to control blood donation centers. This act expects to teach public mindfulness that blood gift is a compassionate demonstration.

The National Voluntary Blood Services Program (NVBSP) of the Department of Health is focusing on the adolescent as volunteers in its blood gift program this year. As per RA No. 7719, it intends to make public awareness on the significance of blood gift in saving the existences of millions of Filipinos.

Mission:

Blood Safety

Blood Adequacy

Sane Blood Use

Proficiency of Blood Services

Objectives:

The National Voluntary Blood Services Program (NVBSP) expects to accomplish the accompanying:

1. Improvement of a completely intentional blood gift framework;

2. Fortifying of a broadly planned organization of BSF to expand productivity by concentrated testing and handling of blood;

3. Execution of a quality administration framework including of Good Manufacturing Practice GMP and Management Information System (MIS);

4. Fulfillment of greatest use of blood through judicious utilization of blood items and part treatment; and

5. Improvement of a sound, feasible maintainable administration and subsidizing for the broadly planned blood organization.

23. Why is the National Voluntary Blood Services Program or NVSB of the Department of Health targeting the youth as volunteers in it's Blood Donation Program?​


Answer:

The National Voluntary Blood Services Program of the Department of Health is targeting the youth as volunteers in its Blood Donation Program for several reasons:

The youth are generally healthy and fit, making them suitable donors for blood.

The youth are often more open-minded and willing to take part in voluntary programs, such as blood donation.

Engaging the youth in blood donation can help raise awareness about the importance of regular blood donation and encourage them to continue donating blood in the future.

The youth are the future of the country, and engaging them in voluntary programs can help develop a culture of volunteerism and social responsibility among the younger generation.

The NVSB program aims to increase the number of blood donors in the country, and targeting the youth can help achieve this goal by providing a new pool of potential donors.
