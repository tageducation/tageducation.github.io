DEFINITION:

Linear text refers to traditional text that needs to be read from beginning to the end while nonlinear text refers to text that does not need to be read from beginning to the end. As their names imply, linear texts are linear and sequential while non-linear and non-sequential.

CONTENT:

In a linear text, a reader can make sense of the text by reading sequentially, from beginning to the end. However, in a nonlinear text, the reading path is nonlinear and non-sequential; thus, the reader can choose his own reading path.

EXAMPLES:

Examples of a non - linear text:

tables

charts

maps

photo graphs

12. Differences of non linear text and linear text


Linear text refers to traditional text that needs to be read from beginning to the end. Generally, texts printed on paper are considered as linear texts. Novels, poems, short stories, letters, educational texts, all those texts we read from the beginning to the end, are linear texts.

Nonlinear text is the opposite of linear text. This type of text has many reading paths since it’s the readers who decide the sequence of reading, not the author of the text. Most people consider texts with visuals or graphs along with it as examples for nonlinear texts. Some examples include flowcharts, charts, and graphs (ex: pie chart, bar graphs), graphical organizers such as knowledge maps and story maps.

Linear Text:

Linear text refers to written or spoken communication that follows a sequential and logical order. In linear texts, the content is arranged in a particular order, and the reader or listener is expected to consume it in the same order. Examples of linear texts include novels, textbooks, news articles, and business reports.

Non-Linear Text:

Non-linear text refers to written or spoken communication that does not follow a sequential and logical order. In non-linear texts, the content is presented in a way that allows the reader or listener to navigate through it in various directions or order. Examples of non-linear texts include websites, social media posts, interactive multimedia presentations, and hypertext documents.

In essence, the primary difference between linear and non-linear texts is the way the content is structured and presented. Linear texts present information in a predetermined order, while non-linear texts allow for a more interactive and flexible approach to consuming information.

20. opinion of linear text and non linear text​


Answer:

Linear text refers to a type of text that is organized in a linear or sequential manner, meaning that it is presented in a specific order and is meant to be read from start to finish. This type of text is common in fiction books, news articles, and instructional materials.

On the other hand, non-linear text refers to a type of text that is organized in a non-linear or non-sequential manner, meaning that it does not have a specific order and can be accessed and read in any order. This type of text is common in websites, online documents, and multimedia presentations.

Both linear and non-linear text have their own set of advantages and disadvantages. Linear text is often easier to follow and understand, as it follows a clear and logical progression. However, it may be less interactive and engaging for some readers. Non-linear text, on the other hand, allows for more flexibility and interactivity, but it may be more confusing for some readers who are not used to this type of format. Ultimately, the choice between linear and non-linear text depends on the purpose and audience of the material being presented.
