1. Describe how a balloon rocket works


Answer:

As the air escapes from the balloon, it exerts a force on the ground and the air outside of the balloon. According to Newton's Third Law of Motion, as the gas is released from the balloon and pushes against the outside air, the outside air pushes back. As a result, the rocket is propelled forward by the opposing force.

The air that escapes from the balloon exerts a force on the earth and the air around it. As the gas is released from the balloon and pushes against the outside air, Newton's Third Law of Motion states that the outside air pushes back. As a result, the opposing force propels the rocket forward.

14. briefly described how the three laws of motion occur in rocket balloon ?​


Answer:

Rocket balloons are governed by Newton's Laws of Motion. The First Law describes how an object acts when no force is acting upon it. So, rockets stay still until a force is applied to move them. Likewise, once they're in motion, they won't stop until a force is applied. Newton's Second Law tells us that the more mass an object has, the more force is needed to move it.

A larger rocket will need stronger forces (eg. more fuel) to make it accelerate. The space shuttles required seven pounds of fuel for every pound of payload they carry. Newton's Third Law states that "every action has an equal and opposite reaction". In a rocket, burning fuel creates a push on the front of the rocket pushing it forward. This creates an equal and opposite push on the exhaust gas backward.
