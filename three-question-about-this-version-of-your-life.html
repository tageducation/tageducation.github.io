1. if one thing is was suddenly gone give three questions about this version of your life​


In this alternate, version of my life the pandemic never happened, the questions I would ask are: Are you graduating college this year? How’s our grandfather doing? Are our friends happy?

For the first question, it is because I am still having a hard time because of the pandemic. The most personal non-person casualty it caused is the pandemic. Schools have to be closed down and continued online. I was never the most financially-stable person, my family couldn’t afford internet connection and my university just . . . forgot about me. They stopped sending modules after that one time. All my life, I’ve been taught that graduating from higher education is the key to escaping the bitterness of not being able to afford an easy life and I lost it.

I hope that the version of me that never had to have the experience of the pandemic is graduating alongside my peers. I hope that they never had to know the sadness of being left behind.

The pandemic took my grandfather away from me. He was the man that practically raised me more than my parents. He was my constant supporter and emotional crutch. In another universe, I’d like to know how he’s doing and remind the alternate version of myself to treasure their time with him more.

If there is a world with this alternate version of me, then there must also exist an alternate version of my friends. I’d want to know if they are doing better in life. I wish they had a gentler time coming of age in that version of the world.

Just like I wished that there exist a gentler world for myself I also wished for a gentler world for my friends.

Regardless of what could have beens and what ifs I wish all versions of myself, as well as this one, a kinder future.

Disclaimer: The answer given is only an example and not a reflection of my true experience.

26. three versions of second low of thermodynamics​


Answer:

The first law of thermodynamics states that, when energy passes into or out of a system (as work, heat, or matter), the system's internal energy changes in accord with the law of conservation of energy.

The second law of thermodynamics states that in a natural thermodynamic process, the sum of the entropies of the interacting thermodynamic systems never decreases. Another form of the statement is that heat does not spontaneously pass from a colder body to a warmer body.

The third law of thermodynamics states that a system's entropy approaches a constant value as the temperature approaches absolute zero. With the exception of non-crystalline solids (glasses) the entropy of a system at absolute zero is typically close to zero.
