9. Cardo does jogging, running, and dancing because he wants to improve his  cardiovascular endurance. What principle of physical fitness does he manifest? Answer: D. Principle of Specificity

Explanation: The principle of specificity states that in order to produce the desired effect, sports training should be relevant and appropriate to the sport for which the individual is training. Furthermore, training should progress from general conditioning to specific training for the specific skills needed in that sport or activity.

10. Which of the following given statements is true about Principles of

Reversibility? Answer: A. The benefits of training are lost with prolonged periods without training.

Explanation: The reversibility principle states that when you stop working out, the effects of training are lost. It is also known as the "use it or lose it" principle.

11.  Which part of the exercise program stimulates beneficial adaptation when  performed regularly? Answer: Exercise Load

Explanation: It is the total amount of exercise you do over a week, and it can be measured by two factors: duration and intensity.

12. Which of the following is true about warm-up? Answer: C. It increases the blood flow to the working muscles without an abrupt  increase in lactic acid accumulation.

Explanation: Warming up helps your body prepare for aerobic activity. Warming up may also aid in the reduction of muscle soreness and the reduction of your risk of injury.

13. Which part of the exercise program is done after exercise load? Answer: Cool-down

Explanation: Cooling down is a simple exercise that can be performed after a more strenuous activity to allow the body to gradually transition to a resting or near-resting state.

14. How much MET will you spend while playing basketball game? Answer: D. 8 METs

Explanation: The metabolic equivalent of task (MET) is an abbreviation for the metabolic equivalent of task. One MET represents the amount of energy expended while sitting quietly. METs can be used to rate the intensity of physical activities.

Explanation: Moderate-intensity activity is typically comprised of exercises that raise your heart rate to 50 to 60 percent of its resting rate.
