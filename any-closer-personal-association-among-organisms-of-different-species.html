Compounds

ELEMENTS AND COMPOUNDS

Elements:

- O2

- Cl2

- Br2

Compounds:

- NaCl

- H2O

Explanation:

Elements are pure substances that are made up of a single type of atom. These elements are found in the periodic table of elements and currently, there are 118 elements in the periodic table of elements. They are classified as either metals, metalloids, and nonmetals. Since they are made up of a single type of atom, elements cannot be broken down into simpler substances. Examples are copper, carbon, boron, gold, or magnesium. In nature, there are elements that exist as diatomic molecules. Examples are hydrogen (H2), nitrogen (N2), fluorine (F2), oxygen (O2), iodine (I2), chlorine (Cl2), bromine (Br2). They may be made of two atoms but are of the same type of atoms thus making them still elements.

Compounds on the other hand are pure substances that are made up of two or more elements in definite proportions. There are different compounds and they are classified as ionic, molecular, or metallic compounds. They are represented by a chemical formula. Since compounds are made up of two or more elements that are chemically combined, they can be broken down also using chemical reactions. Examples are water, sodium chloride, sugar, ammonia, or methane.
