FURTHER EXPLANATION

Algebra

A branch of mathematics known as algebra deals with symbols and the mathematical operations performed on them. Variables are the name given to these symbols because they lack set values. We frequently observe the constant changes in specific values in our day-to-day situations. But the need to depict these shifting values is ongoing. These values are frequently represented in algebra by symbols such as x, y, z, p, or q, and these symbols are known as variables. In order to determine the values, these symbols are also subjected to various addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division arithmetic operations.

Algebraic equation

2x + 4 = 8

Where

2 = coefficient of xx = variable 4 and 8 = constant + = arithmetic operation of addition

A statement that implies two identical identities separated by the symbol "=" is called an equation. In contrast, an expression is a collection of phrases separated by a plus or minus sign.

Terms that have identical variables and exponents are said to be like terms.

Algebraic Operations

There are some fundamental algebraic operations:

1. Addition and Subtraction

The algebraic action is an addition or subtraction when a plus sign (+) or (-) separates two or more terms in an algebraic equation. Similar terms and unlike terms are always added or subtracted individually since they are considered to be two separate quantities. Two different quantities cannot be added or subtracted together mathematically.

Example:

Adding like terms: 3x + 7x = 10x.Adding two unlike terms: 12x + 5y.Subtract like terms: 7x - 4x = 3x.Subtract two unlike terms: 12x - 5y.

2. Multiplication

The algebraic operation carried out is multiplication when two or more terms in an algebraic equation are separated by the multiplication sign "×". We apply the Laws of Exponents when multiplying like terms or unlike terms.

Example:

multiplying like terms: 4y × 3y = 12y²multiplying unlike terms: a × b² = ab².

3. Division

Any algebraic action that is done is division when two or more terms in an algebraic equation are separated by the division sign "/". Similar terms that are divided into like terms can be more easily simplified than terms that are divided into, unlike terms.

Example:

Dividing like terms: 8x/4x = 2.Division of unlike terms: x²/3y.
