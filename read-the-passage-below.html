Explanation:

Changes in matter come in two different kinds. The Physical change and the chemical change. Physical change refers to the changes in matter that do not change the chemical buildup of a substance. Chemical changes are changes in matter that cannot be returned to their original state.

Similarities

Physical changes and chemical changes both affect the state of matter

Differences

For Physical Change:

Do not change the make-up or identity of the matter.

Does not change the matter's identity.

Physical changes are temporary

For Chemical Change:

Turn the matter into a new kind of matter with different properties.

Chemical changes are permanent

They are irreversible

Here are some of the examples of Physical Change:

Molded clay

Crashing a cola can.

Melting an ice brick

Boiling water to 100 degrees centigrade

A mixture of sand and water

Dissolving solutes such as coffee, milk, or sugar.

Shredding documents

Cutting timbers.

Here are some of the examples of Chemical Change:

Rusting of iron

decomposition of wastes

fermentation

cooking egg

Photosynthesis

Mixing acids and reactants

Digestion of food

Matter is around us. Changes also occur every second whether we are aware of them or not. Atom is the building block or the most basic component of all the matter around the universe. The three states of matter are solid, liquid and gas.

Solid

They are definite when it comes to shape and volume. The atoms and molecules that make up this state are closely or tightly attached to each other. Examples of solids are brick, stone, chalk, plate, computer, phone, clay, etc.

Liquid

Unlike solids, liquids do not have a definite shape but they have volume. Liquids follow the shape of the container where they are put. The molecules and atoms are loosely bounded. Examples of liquids are oil, water, juice, liquid milk, and dishwashing liquid.

Gas

This type of state of matter does not have a definite volume or shape. It has mass but the atoms and molecules are so far to each other. Examples of gasses are smoke, air, Helium, Nitrogen, and many more.
