Answer:

The brain is the center of the nervous system. It interprets stimuli and tells the body how to react. The brain has three major parts. The part that controls balance, coordination and muscle movement is called the cerebellum. It makes sure that the muscles work well together. For example, a gymnast is able to balance on a beam because of the cerebellum. The medulla is a long stem that connects the brain to the spinal cord. It tells one’s body to do things without thinking about them. Digesting food or breathing even while asleep are examples of these involuntary actions. On the other hand, there are actions that one decides to do. It is the largest part of the brain—the cerebrum—that is responsible for these voluntary movements.
