22. How does heat travel through the pan ? A.By radiation B.By convection C.By dispersion D.By conduction


The answer to this question is letter D. By Conduction

 

Below is an explanation why conduction is the reason why heat can travel through a pan:

 

When we speak of heat transference, conduction is a process by which two objects with different temperature are in contact. The object with the warmer temperature affects the cooler temperature object by means of heat. Heat travels from the warmer temperature object (with fast moving molecules) to the cooler object (with slow moving molecules). As the faster moving molecules collide with the slow moving molecules, it gains thermal energy then eventually is turned into faster moving molecules. The process continues until both objects have the same temperature. The best heat conductors are metals and other solid objects.
