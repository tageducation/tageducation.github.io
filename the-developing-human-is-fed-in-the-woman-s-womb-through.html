8. what are proofs developing in the mother's womb is a living human being?


Answer:

The proof that a developing fetus in the mother's womb is a living human being can be demonstrated through various scientific and biological factors. Here are some of the proofs:

Heartbeat: As early as six weeks into gestation, the fetal heart begins to beat, indicating the presence of a living organism.
Brain function: By the end of the second trimester, the fetus is capable of exhibiting various brain functions such as dreaming, responding to stimuli, and even learning and remembering.
Fetal movement: Fetal movement can be detected as early as 8-10 weeks and becomes increasingly pronounced as the fetus grows and develops.
DNA: The developing fetus has its own unique DNA, distinct from that of the mother, indicating that it is a separate and independent entity.
Medical technology: Advances in medical technology have allowed for detailed images of the developing fetus to be captured through ultrasound, further confirming its status as a living human being.

Overall, these various proofs demonstrate that a developing fetus is indeed a living human being, with its own unique genetic makeup, brain function, and physical capabilities.

Answer:

Babies do not exactly “breathe” in the womb; at least not by inhaling air they way they do after delivery. Instead, oxygen travels through the mother's lungs, heart, vasculature, uterus, and placenta, finally making its way through the umbilical cord and into the fetus.
