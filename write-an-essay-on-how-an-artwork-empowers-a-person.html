Write an essay on how an artwork empowers a person

People view art as a tool for empowerment and healing. People believe in the liberation of art from the limits of the studio, the museum and the hands of working artists. Rather, humans promote the artistic growth and contribution of every person as an artist. I believe ART It is a return to the arts of ancient origins as shamanism and art as a rite – a holistic practice and the self-integration of inner and outer unity by art and its operation. It's creative playing, curious, alchemical, mystical, healing, feminizing, and playful. It's the mind of the beginners. Art resides inside everyone's inner child. It thrives in a state of flow. It blossoms in the drive to learn oneself and to value the mystery of life. It nurtures the desire of man to bring about peace and harmony, aligns with the natural cycles of life and love. It's all inclusive and non-judgmental by nature. Art emancipates people to stand safely in the no-fear zone and discover new possibilities. This transformational art falls in the service of health and dignity. Make, create is a mantra in the universe. Being an artist is not just about putting a pencil on paper or a brush on canvas. Art does and does mean a lot more than that to so many people. Art is a way of seeing the universe, a way of speech, always a compulsion: we absolutely have to make! Art can be a way to explore the world or your imagination. Art can encourage an early painter to take steps to learn a new medium, or an experienced artist to experiment with a new technique. Art is teaching, and art is understanding. Art could be exciting, inspiring, and an act of passion. Art can be a challenge: both challenging and satisfying at the same time, leading to a feeling of achievement—or a desire to do more. Art can be a message: of politics, of harmony, of violence, of elegance. Art may be an outlet, an idealization, or simply a way of behaving. Art can be whatever, anything you let it be.

20. Write an essay about “Empowering Political Relationships Through ICT’.​

Explanation:

Answer:

ICT is often promoted as a solution to a range of social ills. This is particularly true for the use of ICT in developing countries. ICT deployment can address numerous issues but its overall aim is the empowerment and emancipation of individuals with the aim of improving society. This chapter takes a critical approach to such discourses and asks which claims to emancipation are raised and how they are underpinned. Using a Habermasian framework, the chapter undertakes a critical discourse analysis of the Egyptian ICT policy. This important document, which has inspired much African ICT policy, is analyzed to identify the validity claims it raises. These claims, many of which are explicitly aimed at emancipation and empowerment, are then contrasted with social realities as well as the overall structure of the policy document. This comparison shows that empowerment is not only not achieved, but arguably not a primary aim of the policy in the first place. Instead, claims to empowerment are used to legitimize particular aims which conflict with empowerment. The chapter concludes with a critical reflection of the chosen approach and findings.
