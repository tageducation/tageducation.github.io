24. Nell’s mom makes chocolate milk with 30 mL of chocolate syrup for every 2 ounces of milk. Nell’s dad adds 65 mL of chocolate syrup for every 5 ounces of milk. Whose chocolate milk is more chocolatey?


Answer:

Neil's mom chocolate milk is more chocolatey.

Step-by-step explanation:

Comparing Ratios

Let us analyze the given problem.

What is asked?

The chocolate milk that is more chocolatey.

What are the given?

Neil's mom chocolate milk - 30 mL of chocolate syrup for every 2 ounces of milk

Neil's dad chocolate milk - 65 mL of chocolate syrup for every 5 ounces of milk

There are two steps to know which chocolate milk is more chocolatey.

1. Express the given in ratio as fraction form.

We need to express them in ratio to see the relationship of the amount of chocolate syrup and milk.

2. Divide the numerator by the denominator.

What is the number sentence?

1. Express the given in ratio as fraction form.

ratio = chocolate syrup / milk

2. Multiply the numerator by the denominator.

numerator ÷ denominator = N

Solution:

Step #1

ratio = chocolate syrup / milk

Neil's mom chocolate milk = ‌30⁄2‌

Neil's dad chocolate milk = ‌65⁄5‌

Step #2

numerator ÷ denominator = N

Neil's mom chocolate milk

= ‌30⁄2‌ = 30 ÷ 2 = 15

Neil's dad chocolate milk

= ‌65⁄5‌ = 65 ÷ 5 = 13

15 is more than 13.

Final Answer:

Neil's mom chocolate milk is more chocolatey.

Ratio

A ratio shows the relative sizes of two or more values. It indicates how many times one number contains another.

Different Ways to Express Ratio

Word Form (to)

Fraction Form (/)

Colon Form (:)

Examples:

"30 mL of chocolate syrup for every 2 ounces of milk"

Word Form = 30 to 2

Fraction Form = ‌30⁄2‌

Colon Form = 30:2

Fraction

Fraction represents a part of a whole.

Parts of Fraction

Numerator

- the top number

- tells the number of parts taken from the whole

- the shaded parts

Denominator

- the bottom number

- tells the total number of equal parts

Fraction bar (/)

- separates the numerator and the denominator

Types of Fraction

Proper Fractions - The numerator is less than the denominator.

Examples: 1/3, 3/4, 2/7

Improper Fractions - The numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator.

Examples: 4/3, 11/4, 7/7

Mixed Fractions - A whole number and proper fraction together.

Examples: 1 1/3, 2 1/4, 16 2/5
