6. The time male bungee jumper is freely falling 1.5s. What is the velocity of the jumper at the end of 1.5s


#BrainlyFast

Problem:

The time male bungee jumper is freely falling 1.5 s. What is the velocity of the jumper at the end of 1.5 s?

Given Data:

Vi (initial velocity) = 0 m/s (start of free fall at maximum height zero velocity)

a (acceleration g) = 9.8 m/s²

t (time) = 1.5 s

Vf (final velocity) = ? unknown

Solution:

To solve the problem, we can use the formula Vf = at since Vi = 0 m/s.

Vf = at

   = 9.8 m/s² × 1.5 s

   =  14.7 m/s

Answer:

The velocity of the jumper at the end of 1.5 s is = 14.7 m/s

During free fall, the velocity of the bungee jumper from the maximum height will increased by 9.8 m/s every second as it falls back to the ground. In 1.5 s, the velocity of the bungee jumper going down is 14.7 m/s.

Answer:

In a previous part of Lesson 1, it was said that work is done upon an object whenever a force acts upon it to cause it to be displaced. Work involves a force acting upon an object to cause a displacement. In all instances in which work is done, there is an object that supplies the force in order to do the work. If a World Civilization book is lifted to the top shelf of a student locker, then the student supplies the force to do the work on the book. If a plow is displaced across a field, then some form of farm equipment (usually a tractor or a horse) supplies the force to do the work on the plow. If a pitcher winds up and accelerates a baseball towards home plate, then the pitcher supplies the force to do the work on the baseball. If a roller coaster car is displaced from ground level to the top of the first drop of a roller coaster ride, then a chain driven by a motor supplies the force to do the work on the car. If a barbell is displaced from ground level to a height above a weightlifter's head, then the weightlifter is supplying a force to do work on the barbell. In all instances, an object that possesses some form of energy supplies the force to do the work. In the instances described here, the objects doing the work (a student, a tractor, a pitcher, a motor/chain) possess chemical potential energy stored in food or fuel that is transformed into work. In the process of doing work, the object that is doing the work exchanges energy with the object upon which the work is done. When the work is done upon the object, that object gains energy. The energy acquired by the objects upon which work is done is known as mechanical energy.

18. the time a male bungee jumper is freely falling is 1.5 seconds what is the velocity of the end of 1.5 seconds


Answer:

The final velocity of the male bungee jumper freely falling at the end of 1.5 seconds is -14.7 m/s.

Explanation:

For a rectilinear motion, one of the formula we use is Vf = Vi + at

where:

Vf is the final velocity

Vi is the initial velocity

a   is the acceleration along the horizontal

t    is the time

For a free falling body, we change the symbol of acceleration, a to g where g means acceleration due to gravity.

Now, the formula free falling body becomes Vf = Vi + gt.

The acceleration due to gravity has a constant value of 9.8 [tex]\frac{m}{s^{2} }[/tex]. Since the direction of the falling body is downward, the sign for the acceleration due to gravity is said to be negative.

The given data for the problem are the following:

t = 1.5 seconds

g = -9.8 m/s

Vi = 0    

The initial velocity is zero because when the bungee jumper has reached the maximum height, his final velocity becomes zero which is his initial velocity in falling down.

Solve for the velocity of the bungee jumper:

Let us use the formula for free falling body:

Vf = Vi + gt

Substitute the given data:

Vf = 0 +(-9.8 [tex]\frac{m}{s^{2} }[/tex])(1.5[tex]s[/tex])

Vf = -14.7 m/s

Therefore, the final velocity is -14.7 m/s.

25. A 59 kg man has a total mechanical energy of 150,023. J. If he is swinging downward and is currently 2.6 m above the ground, what is his speed ?


Explanation:

To determine the speed of the man, we need to use the formula for mechanical energy, which states that the total mechanical energy of an object is equal to its kinetic energy plus its potential energy. In this case, we know the total mechanical energy of the man (150,023 J) and his height above the ground (2.6 m), so we can solve for his kinetic energy and then use the formula for kinetic energy (1/2mv^2) to find his speed.

First, let's calculate the man's potential energy by using the formula for gravitational potential energy: PE = mgh, where m is the mass of the man, g is the acceleration due to gravity, and h is the height above the ground. Plugging in the values from the question, we get:

PE = 59 kg * 9.8 m/s^2 * 2.6 m = 1518.52 J

Now that we know the man's potential energy, we can subtract it from his total mechanical energy to find his kinetic energy:

KE = 150,023 J - 1518.52 J = 148,504.48 J

Finally, we can use the formula for kinetic energy to find the man's speed:

KE = 1/2 * m * v^2

v = sqrt(2*KE/m)

v = sqrt(2 * 148,504.48 J / 59 kg)

v = sqrt(5039.69 J/kg)

v = approximately 71.2 m/s

Therefore, the man's speed is approximately 71.2 m/s when he is 2.6 m above the ground.
