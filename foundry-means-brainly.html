What does foundry mean?

foundry. ( ˈfaʊndrɪ) n, pl -ries. 1. (Metallurgy) a place in which metal castings are produced. 2. (Metallurgy) the science or practice of casting metal. 3. (Metallurgy) cast-metal articles collectively

What is the origin of the word 'foundry'?

Origin: From fonderie. Origin: [See Foundery.] A foundry is a factory that produces metal castings. Metals are cast into shapes by melting them into a liquid, pouring the metal in a mold, and removing the mold material or casting after the metal has solidified as it cools. The most common metals processed are aluminium and cast iron.
