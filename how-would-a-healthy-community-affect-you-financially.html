24. how would you relate the role of financial managers​


Answer:

A financial manager distributes the financial resources of a company, is responsible for the budget planning, and sports the executive management team by offering insights and financial advice that will allow them to make the business decisions for the company.

The Role of Financial Managers

Financial managers ensure the financial health of an organization through investment activities and long-term financing strategies.

Overview

Financial managers perform data analysis and advise senior managers on profit-maximizing ideas. Financial managers are responsible for the financial health of an organization. They produce financial reports, direct investment activities, and develop strategies and plans for the long-term financial goals of their organization. Financial managers typically:

Prepare financial statements, business activity reports, and forecasts,Monitor financial details to ensure that legal requirements are met,Supervise employees who do financial reporting and budgeting,Review company financial reports and seek ways to reduce costs, Analyze market trends to find opportunities for expansion or for acquiring other companies, Help management make financial decisions.

The role of the financial manager, particularly in business, is changing in response to technological advances that have significantly reduced the amount of time it takes to produce financial reports. Financial managers’ main responsibility used to be monitoring a company’s finances, but they now do more data analysis and advise senior managers on ideas to maximize profits. They often work on teams, acting as business advisors to top executives.

Financial managers also do tasks that are specific to their organization or industry. For example, government financial managers must be experts on government appropriations and budgeting processes, and healthcare financial managers must know about issues in healthcare finance. Moreover, financial managers must be aware of special tax laws and regulations that affect their industry.

30. How would you imagine a world without financial intermidiaries?​


Answer:

A world without financial intermediaries would be a world without banks, credit unions, investment firms, and other financial institutions that act as intermediaries between savers and borrowers. Instead of depositing their money with a bank and borrowing from the bank, individuals and businesses would have to find ways to directly connect with one another to borrow and lend money.

One possible way this could work is through peer-to-peer lending platforms, where individuals and businesses can directly lend and borrow money from each other. However, these platforms still act as intermediaries, as they facilitate the lending and borrowing process and often charge fees for their services.

Without financial intermediaries, it could also be more difficult for individuals and businesses to access financial products and services such as loans, mortgages, and investment opportunities. Financial intermediaries play a vital role in connecting savers and borrowers and providing access to financial products and services, so their absence could significantly impact the economy.
