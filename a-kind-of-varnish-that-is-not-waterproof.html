1. a kind of varnish that is not waterproof?​


There are several things that affect non-waterproof varnishes, namely the lack of solvent used, contaminated finishing materials, application in too cold air temperatures, application of a coating layer before the layer underneath is completely dry, application of a heavier or unsuitable type of coating, or the use of thinner which dries too quickly. The solution is very easy, namely to check first whether the old paint layer has completely dried before applying the new coating.

The character of the finishing material used will also greatly affect this excessive application. The presence of excessive problems in finishing will cause damage after the initial finishing. Due to the dry period that should be obtained after one night, because the paint layer is too thick, it is very easy to get scratches and even fingerprints. Understanding the character of the finishing materials used, such as when applying water-based paint, must be very thin, which is very different from finishing materials from solvents which are usually applied very thickly in one swipe.

Varnish materials for wood such as paint, varnish and also wood stain will give the wood an appearance. It also provides protection to the wood surface and also provides a clean appearance. Many people only know that the appearance of the surface is of utmost importance when choosing a finishing material for wood. Although many problems can arise from the finishing results, the main problem is not only because of the finishing materials used. As has been explained, problems can arise due to weather factors and the type of wood used.

11. A kind of finishing material that is designed to be chip-resistant, waterproof, and breathable?​


Answer:

Finishing Materials

(in construction), materials and items used to improve the service and decorative qualities of buildings and structures, as well as to protect structural members from atmospheric and other effects. The main finishing materials in modern construction include finishing mortars and concretes; natural and artificial masonry materials; decorative ceramics; materials and items made from wood, paper, glass, plastic, and metals; and paints and varnishes.

Finishing materials are usually designed for interior or exterior finishing; some materials are used for both (for example, natural decorative stone, ceramic materials, and architectural glass). A special group consists of materials and items for covering floors, which must meet a number of specific requirements (negligible wear, high impact strength, and so on). Finishing materials also include acoustic materials, which are used simultaneously as sound-absorbing coatings and as a decorative finish for the interiors of theaters, concert halls, and motion-picture theaters.

An arbitrary distinction is made between finishing materials proper, which are used mainly to form decorative and protective coatings (varnishes and paints, wallpaper, polymeric films, linoleum, and so on), and structural finishing materials, which also perform the functions of enclosing members and are components of such members (decorative concrete, facing brick, glass blocks, and molded glass).
