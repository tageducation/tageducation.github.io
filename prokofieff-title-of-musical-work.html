28. which one of the musical composition of Sergei prokofieff is intended only for children?​


musical composition of Sergei prokofieff is intended only for children

Sergei Prokofiev (1891-1953) wrote not just "serious" pieces like Classical Symphony (1917), but also "light" works like Peter and the Wolf (1936), which has been accepted into the classical canon despite being a children's work.

Sergei Sergeyevich Prokofiev (27 April [O.S. 15 April] 1891 – 5 March 1953)[n 2] was a Russian composer, pianist, and conductor. He is regarded as one of the major composers of the twentieth century, having created recognized masterpieces in a variety of musical genres. The March from The Love for Three Oranges, the suite Lieutenant Kijé, the ballet Romeo and Juliet—from which "Dance of the Knights" is taken—and Peter and the Wolf are among his most well-known compositions. He finished seven operas, seven symphonies, eight ballets, five piano concertos, two violin concertos, a cello concerto, a symphony-concerto for cello and orchestra, and nine completed piano concertos among the established forms and genres in which he worked.

Prokofiev, a graduate of the Saint Petersburg Conservatory, rose to prominence as an outspoken composer-pianist with a series of viciously discordant and virtuosic compositions for his instrument, including his first two piano concertos. With his symphonic Scythian Suite, constructed from music initially composed for a ballet commissioned by Sergei Diaghilev of the Ballets Russes, Sergei Prokofiev established a decisive break from the usual composer-pianist group in 1915. Sergei Diaghilev commissioned three more ballets from Prokofiev: Chout, Le pas d'acier, and The Prodigal Son, all of which created a stir among reviewers and colleagues when they were first performed. However, opera was Prokofiev's true passion, and he composed several operas, notably The Gambler and The Fiery Angel. During Prokofiev's lifetime, he only had one operatic success.
