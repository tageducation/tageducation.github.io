9. choose one artist that you know who deserve to be awarded/recognized as National Artist of the Philippines,and why​


One artist that deserve to be awarded as National Artist of the Philippines is Fernando Cueto Amorsolo.

Filipino artists who have made significant contributions to the cultural heritage of the country. Filipino artistic accomplishment at its highest level and to promote creative expression as significant to the development of a national cultural identity.

Fernando Cueto Amorsolo (born Fernando Amorsolo y Cueto; 30 May 1892 – 24 April 1972) was one of the most important artists in the history of painting in the Philippines. [2] Amorsolo is a Filipino portrait and rural landscape painter. He is renowned for his skill and mastery in the use of light.

After graduating from the University of the Philippines, Amorsolo worked as a draftsman for the Public Works Bureau, as the chief artist at the Pacific Commercial Company, and as a part-time instructor at the University of the Philippines (where he would work for 38 years).

After three years as an instructor and commercial artist, Amorsolo was given a grant to study at the Academia de San Fernando in Madrid, Spain by Filipino entrepreneur Enrique Zóbel de Ayala. During his seven months in Spain, Amorsolo sketched in museums and along the streets of Madrid, experimenting with the use of light and color.

Through a Zóbel grant, Amorsolo was also able to move to New York City, [6] where he discovered postwar impressionism and cubism, which would be a major influence on his work.

Amorsolo set up his own studio upon his return to Manila and painted superbly during the 1920s and 1930s. The Rice Planting (1922), which appeared on posters and travel brochures, became one of the most popular images of the Commonwealth of the Philippines.

Starting in the 1930s, Amorsolo's work was exhibited widely both in the Philippines and abroad. His bright, optimistic pastoral images set the tone for pre-World War II Philippine painting. Except for the darker World War II era paintings, Amorsolo has painted serene and peaceful landscapes throughout his career.

30. recognize the different action of each level of organization in an organism​


Multicellular organisms are made of many parts that are needed for survival. These parts are divided into levels of organization. There are five levels: cells, tissue, organs, organ systems, and organisms. All living things are made up of cells.

The diagram shows five levels of organization in a multicellular organism. The most basic unit is the cell; groups of similar cells form tissues; groups of different tissues make up organs; groups of organs form organ systems; cells, tissues, organs, and organ systems combine to form a multicellular organism.
