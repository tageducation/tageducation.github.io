13. 1. Your younger sister cuts her finger with a knife? 2. Your friend falls down the stairs from the 2nd floor and hardly stand? 3. Your classmate has drunk poison? 4. Your cousin who does not know how to swim falls into a deep river? 5. What should we remember when giving first aid? 6. Why is it important to understand and follow the basics of first aid? 7. What is the ultimate goal in giving first aid?​


1.) Check that the knife isn't a danger to anyone else.

Take a quick look at the wound. You've said it's a cut, so I imagine the finger is intact.Tell her to press her thumb against the damaged finger and hold it above her head while I get a sterile dressing.Apply dressing. Assess whether medical help is needed. See if she's showing signs of shock (a very deep cut may cause this) - if so, get her to lie down with her feet up and the arm supported across her chest.If she's in shock, medical help will be needed. If the finger is seriously damaged, medical help will be needed. If not, make her a cup of tea and finish the dinner yourself.

2.) Ask them how they feel and if anything hurts. Don't make them move or stand up any more than they can manage, rather let them sit in one place. Call the doctor; there may be a spine injury.

3.) Get the person into fresh air as soon as possible. If the person vomits, turn his or her head to the side to prevent choking. Begin CPR if the person shows no signs of life, such as moving, breathing or coughing.

4.) Place the drowning person on their back on a flat surface, and be careful when handling them as they may be unconscious after bumping their head against something.

Try to call the drowning person and shake their shoulders to make sure they are responding.If the person does not respond, check their breathing.Ask someone to call the Red Crescent (997).If the person is breathing: Place them in the recovery position and warm them up with clothes or blankets. Change their wet clothes while waiting for the ambulance.

5.) It’s important that you commit these 10 golden rules to memory. Even if you’re not injured, you might encounter someone who is, and who needs treatment.

Remember the “Three P’s.”Check the scene for danger before you provide help.To treat cuts and scrapes, apply gentle pressure, disinfectant, and bandages.To treat sprains, apply ice and compression at intervals and keep the limb elevated.To treat heat exhaustion, use cool fluids, cool cloths, and shade.To treat hypothermia; use warm fluids and warm covering.To treat burns, determine the burn type and severity. Cover the wound with loose cloth to prevent infection.Use an EpiPen to treat allergic reactions.To treat fractures, keep the fractured area stable and immobilized, and apply a cold pack.Perform CPR if an injured person stops breathing.

6.) It gives you tools to prevent the situation from becoming worse. In some situations if a patient doesn't receive basic first aid care immediately their situation will deteriorate – often rapidly. By being able to provide basic care you can stabilize a patient until emergency medical services arrives

7.) The purpose of first aid is to minimize injury and future disability. In serious cases, first aid may be necessary to keep the victim alive.
