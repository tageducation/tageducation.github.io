13. The following situations demonstrate potential energy except


The following scenarios, with the exception of the car parked at the top of a hill, illustrate potential energy. Potential energy, one of two types of energy, is the latent energy present in an item at rest. Any object's potential energy can be used as a gauge to determine how much it might be able to exert in the way of force, heat, or work. The total of an object's mass, distance from the outside world, electrical charge, distance from other objects, and internal elastic forces is used to calculate that object's potential energy as a result (that is, any internal mechanical force).

Potential energy is a type of stored energy that depends on how various system elements interact with one another. A spring's potential energy rises when it is compressed or stretched. If a steel ball is raised above the ground as opposed to falling to the ground, it has more potential energy.

Formula:

Potential Energy = mgh

The mass is m (kg).

The acceleration caused by gravity, or g, is 9.8 m/s2 at the Earth's surface.

The height is h (m).
