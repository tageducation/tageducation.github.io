THE SUM OF THE FIRST 25 MULTIPLIES OF 8

Sequence is actually the range of a function having for its domain the set of positive integers or a subset of it. If all of the positive integers comprise the domain, then the sequence is infinite; otherwise, it is finite. Also, sequence is a particular order in which related events, movements, or things follow each other.

Kinds of a Sequence: Arithmetic Sequence Geometric Sequence Harmonic Sequence Fibonacci Numbers

Arithmetic Sequence – is a sequence of numbers such that the difference of any two successive members of the sequence is a constant.

Sum of the Arithmetic Sequence – is an indicated sum of the first n terms of an arithmetic sequence. The sum of n terms is denoted by S_n.

Naming the sum of the first n terms of the sequence by Sn, 0 + 1 + 2 + 3 + … + n – 1. The indicated sum is an arithmetic series. Sn is the sum of the terms. The sum of n terms can be more compactly written as

    Sn= t1+t2+t3+t4+t5+t6+t7+t8.

Formula of the Sum of the Arithmetic Sequence:

The formula of the sum of the arithmetic sequence has two types, such as:

Sn=n/2 [〖2a〗1+(n-1)d]

Sn – sum of the terms or unknown

n – number of terms

a1 – first term of the sequence

d – common difference

Sn=n/2 (a1+an )

Sn – sum of the terms or unknown

n – number of terms

a1 – first term of the sequence

an – last term of the sequence

Find the sum of the first 25 multiples of 8. Solution and Process:

Determine the given in the problem.

Sn – unknown

n – 25

a1 – 8

d – 8

Substitute the given in the first formula, S_n=n/2 [〖2a〗_1+(n-1)d].

S_n=n/2 [〖2a〗_1+(n-1)d]

S_25=25/2 [2(8)+(25-1)8]

Solve the given problem.

S25=25/2 [2(8)+(25-1)8]   - Solve first the operation inside the parenthesis.

  Multiply 2 and 8 after that subtract 25 and 1.

S25=5,200/2     - Divide 5,200 and 2.

S25=2,600     - Answer in the given problem.

27. find the sum of the first 25 multiples of 8.


n Sum of the First Term of a Sequence

To determine the number of the first n terms of a sequence, it can be done manually if the number that is calculated is still small, but if the number is large, then we can use a formula that we can make ourselves based on low numbers.

For the Arithmetic number sequence, the sum of the first terms is obtained using a predetermined formula, as well as for the Geometric number sequence.

On Arithmetic Sequences:

[tex]S_{n} =\frac{n}{2} (2a+(n-1)b)[/tex]

Where a is the first term and b is the difference between the second term minus the first [tex](a_{2} -a_{1} )[/tex].

On the Geometry sequence:

[tex]S_{n} =\frac{a(r^{n} -1)}{r-1}[/tex] if r>1 or

[tex]S_{n} =\frac{a(1-r^{n} )}{1-r}[/tex]  if r<1

Where a is the first term and r is the ratio obtained by dividing the second term by the first term.

Examples such as questions asked:

Multiples of 8: 8,16,24,32,40,48,56,64,…..

First term=a=8

n=25 (1,2,3…)

Asked:

The sum of the first 25 terms of a multiple of 8 is S25=?

Answer:

A series of multiples of 8 is included in the example of an arithmetic sequence, because the difference between the terms is constant, so find the sum of the first n terms using the following formula:

[tex]S_{n} =\frac{n}{2} (2a+(n-1)b)[/tex]

[tex]S_{25} =\frac{25}{2} (2.8+(25-1)8)[/tex]

[tex]S_{25} =\frac{25}{2} (16+(24)8)[/tex]

[tex]S_{25} =\frac{25}{2} (16+192)[/tex]

[tex]S_{25} =\frac{25}{2} (208)[/tex]

[tex]S_{25} =25.104[/tex]

[tex]S_{25} =2600[/tex]

How about we search for it manually? Because the number of 25 multiples of 8 is not too much, so it's still easy for us to write it down. And this method is quite successful for those of you who sometimes forget the formula, especially if you are testing with a tense mental condition, this manual method is quite effective not only in finding the first n number of terms in an Arithmetic number sequence, but also applies to a Geometric number sequence.
