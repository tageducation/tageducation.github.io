11. How does the poem make you feel about the importance of recognizing and performing a role in life effectively?


poem-
        All the world's a stage,
And all the men and women merely players;
They have their exits and their entrances,
And one man in his time plays many parts,
His acts being seven ages. At first, the infant,
Mewling and puking in the nurse's arms.
Then the whining schoolboy, with his satchel
And shining morning face, creeping like snail
Unwillingly to school. And then the lover,
Sighing like furnace, with a woeful ballad
Made to his mistress' eyebrow. Then a soldier,
Full of strange oaths and bearded like the pard,
Jealous in honor, sudden and quick in quarrel,
Seeking the bubble reputation
Even in the cannon's mouth. And then the justice,
In fair round belly with good capon lined,
With eyes severe and beard of formal cut,
Full of wise saws and modern instances;
And so he plays his part. The sixth age shifts
Into the lean and slippered pantaloon,
With spectacles on nose and pouch on side;
His youthful hose, well saved, a world too wide
For his shrunk shank, and his big manly voice,
Turning again toward childish treble, pipes
And whistles in his sound. Last scene of all,
That ends this strange eventful history,
Is second childishness and mere oblivion,
Sans teeth, sans eyes, sans taste, sans everything.

20. How does this poem provide the reader a glimpse of what martial law was like ?how did the poem make you feel?


The poem that I have chosen to analyze is Memories II by Aida F. Santos.

Aida F. Santos is a creative writer, a feminist, and a professor. Some of her works have been included in the National Encyclopedia of Artists and Writers. Her works tackle and discuss social issues that affect women.

How does this poem provide the reader with a glimpse of what martial law was like?

The poem Memories II was about a wife who was looking for her husband who was illegally detained during Martial Law in the Marcos era. Her husband may have suddenly vanished like hundreds of others during the time. People who were vocal about the injustices of the Marcos regime were persecuted, detained, tortured, killed, and their human rights violated. Their own families and friends, even those who have kept silent, were not safe from these kinds of injustices.

At the very start of the poem, readers would already feel a dark and chilling sense of what is to come as the poet describes what is about to happen to her. She is about to be violated sexually by an unknown man, most likely a henchman or military police. During Martial Law, there was an unusual imbalance of power that was tipped in favor of Marcos and his supporters.

Marcos made sure that the people who carried out his will were to remain untouched and rewarded. This included the military and the police who were directly under his order. It is also unusual for the Marcoses to have henchmen in the form of bodyguards to pick up people who expressed displeasure in the regime and torture them.

The wife who just wanted to find her husband met a cruel fate wherein instead of finding answers and solace, she was raped instead. True to the events during the time, women were sexually and physically abused and had no one to turn to for justice. They were abused just because the people who held power can. There was no rhyme nor reason for the action of those people. The wife, who thought of her husband instead of crying out, just held it together in the hopes that she would be shown mercy after and eventually find her husband.

How did the poem make you feel?

The poem enraged me and at the same time made me want to cry for the wife and her husband. While this may just be a poem, this was true for many women during the Marcos regime.

People vanished, men and women were violated, even teenagers experienced the harsh reality of bitter adulthood under Martial Law. What makes me angrier is that people who are brainwashed by propaganda paid for by the Marcoses during the last few years are now saying that these were all lies and that people who were tortured, killed, and raped deserved it.

Ferdinand Marcos and his family, his supporters and his cronies are all corrupt, cruel, and merciless.

The Filipino people must never forget, they must remember, and they must cry for justice! If we fail to do this as a nation, we will always have politicians and powerful people who will always get away with crime and will always be voted into power despite their criminal activities.
