Music in Japan Korea and China

Japanese, Korean, Chinese music have something in common because of their similarities cultural heritage so that the music between them is related to one another. One of the main reasons for the overlapping vocal music of East Asian countries, China, Japan and Korea, is because they have similar traditional clothing, cutlery and finally language. Traditional music from China, Korea, and Japan shares similarities in a few key areas. Due to the fact that the music of both cultures is based on a pentatonic scale, for example, five notes in either the traditional music of Japan or China compose an octave. A profound history may be found in both the traditional music of China and Japan. Notably, Chinese Tang music is a component in the early Gagaku form of Japanese music. Linearity, transparency, and word orientation are the three traits that they share the most.

One can comprehend many of the fundamental concepts upon which ancient Japanese music was founded by studying a combination of source Buddhist music theory tomes, part books, and contemporary performance practice. Given what has already been said about the origins of Japanese court and religious music, it is not surprising to learn that both genres' entire tonal systems consist of the 12 tones of the Chinese system. The pronunciation of the character for each pitch's name is the only distinction between the Chinese and Japanese tones.

The Noh (n-kan) flute's upper bore spans over sevens (as A-G) rather than octaves, unlike the Lilinu ryuteki flute, which has an appearance similar to that of the Noh (n-kan) flute. The flute may give a precise pitch to set the tone of the vocal entry, but in modern performances, its primary purpose is to signal form transitions and play one of the roughly twelve common dance pieces used in different Noh dramas. Dance pieces are divided into sections, and each portion is either memorized by heart or written out using a flute mnemonic enclosed in an eight-square grid that roughly corresponds to Korean notation.
