1. strength and weaknesses of conservatism​


The strongest attribute of conservatism, as well as its most essential distinguishing value, may also be its weakness. True conservatism's very basis, from which it is founded and anchored, and from which it derives its intrinsic power and goodness, may also be its greatest insecurity and weakness. This weakness and insecurity will severely limit conservatism's ability to effectively fight the political and cultural wars that are currently being waged in post-modern countries against progressivism, while still posing a threat to conservatism's and liberty's very existence. True conservatism's unwavering trust in the individual's authority and inalienable rights, or merely the idea of individualism as a whole, is the flaw.

Strengths of Conservatism  

True conservatism is, at its heart, an appreciation for and defense of the individual, as well as individual freedom, liberty, and property. Along with this sense of individualism comes a firm belief in personal responsibility, transparency, merit, and self-determination, all of which are essential for a free and civil society to work properly.  

Conservatism is also the belief of the free man, free in his inalienable rights, intrinsic liberty, power, and from unnecessary or punitive government interference, as well as equal justice under the law. A free society, made up of free people, can not always be equitable or equitable in its consequences, but it allows a man to choose his own path. The very pillar of liberty is the free man following his own self-interests, according to his own merits, and responsible to himself, the rule of law, civil society, and democratic values. As a result, every free society's smallest and most fundamental entity is the free citizen.  

With no single right or wrong response on the subject, conservatism may entail a vast and varied variety of camps or schools of thought, as well as unlimited individual pressures and variants on each. Conservatives should agree to disagree in a civil manner. True conservatism is thus about educating, leading, and justifying to people why liberty and individualism are the best option and direction. Intimidation, threats, bullying, and the silencing of dissent and criticism, as seen on the left, are fundamentally opposed to conservatism.

Weaknesses of Conservatism  

Since progressivism is more than just a theoretical or intellectual philosophy, it is a collectivist phenomenon with a totalitarian goal, it necessitates the collective dynamic. Better still, progressivism is the mob's collective dynamic. As a result, it derives support from the mob, mass figures, and the party. Despite the fact that the movement is made up of numerous sub-movements, with different leaders and social justice advocates fighting for different causes, progressivism has an innate capacity to get diverse people together under the banner of progressivism.  

This hive mentality is particularly evident and noticeable at every large-scale demonstration, rally, or march, where, regardless of the reason—anti-police, anti-war, anti-conservative, etc.—a horde of political protestors, social justice warriors, and opportunists are ready at a moment's notice to flood the streets and cause mayhem, often without clear instructions. However, it is frequently even more nuanced than that. While the so-called "masters" of the progressive movement frequently give orders directly to the group-think hive, the ordinary liberal, motivated by indoctrination, impulse, and the innate desire to protect the hive, will spontaneously swarm to the defense or advancement of progressivism on their own initiative.  

A herd of with like liberals stands ready to spontaneously swarm to the cause, whether on an online message board or forum, or on a college campus. Individual leftists, tiny groups of liberals, and massive opposition mobs are all motivated by the same hive mind mindset, spouting the same indoctrinated platitudes and misinformation.
