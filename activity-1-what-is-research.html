Explanation:

1. MICE-play an essential role in both vaccine and drug development for Covid-19. Ordinary mice are not susceptible to the disease, so scientists breed genetically altered mice to make them susceptible to Covid-19.

2.MONKEYS-have a similar immune system to humans and are currently being used in many coronavirus studies across the world. The most common type of monkey used is the rhesus macaque, but scientists are also conducting studies using African greens and marmosets.

3.FERRETS have been used to study Covid-19 because they show similar symptoms to the disease as humans, their lung physiology is also similar to ours, and they can spread the virus to other ferrets through the air. They are often used in studies of influenza for similar reasons.

4.PIGS-are used in Covid-19 vaccine testing to help scientists better understand how safe and effective a new vaccine is and therefore whether it is suitable for human trials. These animals are also being used to test the effectiveness of new types of ventilators, before they are tested on humans.

5.HAMSTERS-are being used to study the effects of the coronavirus as these animals are a good model to study the effect of the virus on the lungs. Hamsters are being used to study the effects of Covid-19 as they can display similar symptoms to humans when infected. This similarity makes also them a good model for studying transmission, the effects of the virus (particularly on the lungs), and suitable treatments.

25. 1. What is Research? 2. What is inquiry and its nature? 3. What is the difference between inquiry and research? 4. How important is research in your daily life activities? 5. Why is there a need to conduct research? in​


ANSWER:Research is a systematic and scientific investigation of a particular topic or issue, with the goal of advancing knowledge and understanding in that field. It involves gathering, analyzing, and interpreting data, and drawing conclusions based on the findings.Inquiry is the process of asking questions and seeking information, often to solve a problem or address a particular issue. Its nature is exploratory, and it involves examining existing knowledge and identifying gaps that need to be filled. Inquiry can take many forms, from formal research studies to more informal, everyday forms of questioning and investigation.The main difference between inquiry and research is that inquiry is a broader concept that encompasses a variety of ways of seeking information and understanding the world, while research is a more specific and formal process that follows established methodologies to generate new knowledge and test hypotheses.Research is important in daily life activities because it can help us make more informed decisions and solve problems more effectively. For example, conducting research on the best options for a particular purchase, such as a new phone or car, can help us make a more informed decision and get better value for our money. In addition, research can help us understand and address issues in our personal and professional lives, such as health concerns, workplace challenges, or social and environmental problems.The need to conduct research arises from the desire to advance knowledge and understanding in a particular field, address unanswered questions or gaps in current knowledge, and solve practical problems. Research can help us identify new opportunities and develop more effective solutions to complex problems, and it plays an important role in driving innovation, progress, and development in a wide range of fields, from science and technology to social policy and business.
