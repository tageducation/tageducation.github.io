1. F. Reaction Time: Stick Drop Test1 Trial2nd Trial3d TrialMiddle Score​


Answer:

Stick drop test is usually done as a science class project. Stick drop test aims to determine how long will it take for a person to respond when an object dropped by measuring how far the object may fall before being caught.

Explanation:

How to do the Stick Drop Test Find a partner to sit in the chair with good upright posture and eyes looking across the room.   Have your partner place his or her forearm that it extends over the edge of the table. Ask your partner to place his or her thumb and index finger on either side of the bottom of the vertically placed ruler. The number “1” should be on the bottom, the “30” near the top.  Let your partner practice holding the ruler with those two fingers. Ask your partner to remove his or her fingers from the ruler  Tell your partner that you will release the ruler without any warning.  Drop the ruler when he or she is not attentive. When your partner catches it, record the number on the ruler displayed just over her thumb. The lower the number, the faster her reaction time. Record and repeat the procedure two more times.

The rationale behind the test

Reaction time is the time it takes from when your eye first notices something to when your body will move or react to catch it. This activity will let you do a measurable experiment to assess reaction time and determine how it can be improved with practice.
