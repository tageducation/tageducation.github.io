19. vaporation Evaporation is a process of separating mixtures which involves heating the solution until the solvent evaporates leaving behind the solid residue. Heat being the main component in this process separates the mixtures of solid from a liquid. As liquid goes in the air in a form of gas when heated, changing liquid to gas as an example of physical change. Some examples of evaporation are boiling water, drying clothes, and drying of wet roads after heavy rain. Evaporation is used to obtain a solid substance that has dissolved in water or any other liquid. The dissolved substance is left as a solid residue when all the water has evaporated.
