11. 5 examples of claims of value


Claims of value are claims that assert some things are more or less desirable than others. It usually states that one thing, or idea is better or worse than another. This also means that either "good" must be kept or "bad" must be discarded. They are not that straightforward since claims of values involve comparison and evaluation--weighing of options in other words. Below are 5 examples of claims of value with explanation.

5 examples of claims of value

Eating vegetables is better than eating meat.  

In this example - the claim to value is being better than something. Of course arguments could be presented either way.

It is improper to double-charge a client.

This one has no direct comparison in the sentence, only an implied one -- that there is one thing that is proper.  

Stem cell research is objectionable because it tinkers with embryonic cells.

As with above, there is no direct comparison but the claim to value is in the phrase "is objectionable"

The death penalty should be reinstated to prevent rape and murder.

This one proposes something hence the claim to value is found in the "should be" phrase.

A democratic government is superior to that of an autocracy.

Here there is a comparison between an autocratic government and a democratic one with the claim to value in the phrase "is superior to".

When a sentence calls for a judgment, chances are there is a claim to value.

24. claims of value examples


Answer:

Some examples of a claim of value would be saying that something is immoral, claiming something is wrong, or stating that something is worse or better than something else. A claim of value is a statement in which something is lauded as good or decried as bad.

A claim of value is not based on factual information the way a claim of fact is. Rather, it is based on opinion. Morality or the "goodness" or "evil" of a thing cannot be quantified or measured, meaning that assertions to that effect cannot be based on data.

Typically, claims of value survive by consensus, with the majority of a population agreeing that certain acts, behaviors or items are either "good" and should be kept or "bad" and should be discarded.

30. Claim of value examples


A claim of value is a statement that expresses your opinion or judgment about the worth, merit, or morality of something. Here are some examples of claims of value:

1. Renewable energy sources are better for the environment than fossil fuels.

2. Art education is essential for the development of creative and critical thinking skills.

3. Universal healthcare is a fundamental human right and should be provided by the government.

4. Animal testing is unethical and should be banned.

5. Capital punishment is immoral and should be abolished.

6. Social media is harmful to mental health and well-being.

7. Education is the key to reducing poverty and promoting economic growth.

8. Beauty pageants are exploitative and should be banned.

9. Gun control laws are necessary to prevent gun violence and protect public safety.

10. Marriage is a sacred institution that should be reserved for opposite-sex couples.

Note that these are just examples, and you can make a claim of value about almost any topic. It's important to back up your claims with evidence and reasoning to make a convincing argument.
