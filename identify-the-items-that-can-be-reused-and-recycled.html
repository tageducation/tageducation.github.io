Things that can be reused and recycled

Reused: Glass jar, box, old tires, news paper, water bottle

Recycle: Newspaper, water bottle, used wrapper, food scraps

Recycling

Recycling entails collecting used and discarded materials, processing them, and transforming them into new products. It reduces the amount of waste thrown into community trash cans, making the environment cleaner and the air more breathable.

Reusing

The action or practice of reusing an item, whether for its original purpose or to fulfill a different function, is referred to as reuse. It should be distinguished from recycling, which is the process of converting used items into raw materials for the production of new products.

How to reuse and recycle?

The majority of household garbage can be recycled and reused. Organic kitchen waste, such as leftover foods, vegetable peels, and spoilt or dried fruits and vegetables, can be recycled by placing them in garden compost pits. Old newspapers, magazines, and bottles can be sold to kabadiwalas, or people who buy these items from people's homes.

You can help to reduce waste in your own home by recycling and reusing certain items. You can use old calendars to cover your books, as well as old greeting cards. Paper can also be made at home using a simple process, and it is possible to paint on it.
