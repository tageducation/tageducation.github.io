8. what is netiquette?​


In cyberspace, internet etiquette is called "Netiquette."

Netiquette is a combination of two words, namely internet and etiquette, which means ethics on the internet. That is, ethics, which are manners, are also applied when using the internet. Netiquette serves as a rule for internet users.

The Purpose of Applying Netiquette to Internet Activities Basically, the goal of Netiquette is to regulate good manners on the internet. The internet is like a community that applies different rules. Therefore, with netiquette, each individual can communicate well within the community.

Furthermore, the goal of internet ethics is to provide security in oneself.In an era where there is a lot of fraud and false information, internet users must be good at sorting out which ones are safe and correct.

Three Rules of Applied Netiquette

After knowing what Netiquette is, readers need to understand the general rules of ethics in cyberspace. Although these rules sound basic, there are still many internet users who have not implemented them. Here are three general rules of netiquette:

1. Make Security Plans Before Using the Internet

When connecting a device to the internet, the reader unconsciously accepts the risks of the internet world. These risks can be in the form of viruses or hackers that can endanger the privacy of readers. Therefore, readers must prepare for security measures such as installing an antivirus and activating a firewall.

2. Don't Trust The Internet Too Much 

The internet is a free place; therefore, genuine or false information is spread freely on the internet. In addition, fraud in cyberspace is rife and takes up a lot of the identity and privacy of the victim. Therefore, readers are required to be careful when browsing cyberspace.

3. Be considerate of other Internet users

The most important thing on the internet is ethics. Readers must understand and respect the privacy of other internet users when interacting on social media or forums.

 Rules in the application of netiquette 

1. Keep in Mind If Readers Interact With Humans

2. Use Common Sense in Cyberspace

3. Be respectful of others on the internet.

4. Recognize Safe Websites When Surfing the Internet.

5. Take note of the letters and language used.

6. Respect others' privacy when interacting

7. Keep Internet debates under control
