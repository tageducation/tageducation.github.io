22. a volleyball is tossed vertically upward, with an initial velocity of 5 m/s and caught back at the same level when it was thrown. what is the velocity of the ball at that point?


Answer:

The velocity of the ball is - 5 m/s.

Explanation:

Tossing an object upward or a free fall of an object is a special type of projectile motion. In tossing a volleyball vertically upward gives an initial velocity and as it moves upward, the velocity decreases until the vertical velocity is zero. A maximum height was reached by an object when the vertical velocity became zero.

The vertical velocity decreases because the pull of gravity is downward. Thus, when the object goes back to its level, the velocity becomes negative because of its downward direction.  

The time an object tossed upward is the same time it goes back from where it started.

[tex]g=9.8m/s^2[/tex]     this becomes negative because the pull of the gravity is

where

[tex]V_{1}[/tex]     is the final velocity, unit is in m/s

[tex]V_{0}[/tex]     is the initial velocity, unit is in m/s

[tex]g[/tex]      acceleration due to gravity

[tex]t[/tex]       is the time, unit is in seconds

Solving the problem

1. Find the time from using the initial velocity up to the final velocity where a maximum height was reached by the volleyball

[tex]V_{1} =V_{0} +gt[/tex]

0 = 5 m/s + (- 9.8 m/[tex]s^2[/tex])t

28. A juggler tosses three balls alternately vertically upward each ball has an initial velocity of 5m/s how high does each ball rise


Answer:

Good Day!

Problem:

A juggler tosses three balls alternately vertically upward each ball has an initial velocity of 5m/s how high does each ball rise?

Given:

a (acceleration due to gravity) = -9.8 m/s^2

Vi (initial velocity) = 5 m/s

Vf (final velocity ) = 0 m/s

d (distance) = ?

t (time) = ?

Solution:

olution:Solving for distance:

Using the formula: Vf^2 = Vi^2 + 2ad

Vf^2 = Vi^2 + 2ad

(0 m/s)^2 = (5 m/s)^2 + 2 ( -9.8 m/s^2) d

0 m^2/s^2 = 25 m^2/s^2 + 2 ( -9.8 m/s^2) d

0 m^2/s^2 = 25 m^2/s^2 + (- 19.6 m/s^2) d

0 m^2/s^2 - 25 m^2/s^2 = ( -19.6 m/s^2) d

- 25 m^2/s^2 / -19.6 m/s^2 = d

1.275 m = d ( distance )

Solving for time:

Using the formula: a = (Vf-Vi)/t

or t = (Vf-Vi)/a

t = (Vf-Vi)/a

= (0 m/s - 5 m/s) / -9.8 m/s^2

= - 5 m/s / -9.8 m/s^2

t = 0.51 s

For Checking :

d = (Vf-Vi)/2 × t

1.275 = (0 m/s + 5 m/s) /2 × 0.51 s

1.275 = 5 m/s / 2 × 0.51 s

1.275 = 2.5 m/s × 0.51 s

1.275 = 1.275

Answer:

The ball take 0.51 seconds in the air and at a height or distance of 1.275 m
