16. Peter tchaikovsky music composition


Answer:

Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky, Tchaikovsky also spelled Chaikovsky, Chaikovskii, or Tschaikowsky, name in full Anglicized as Peter Ilich Tchaikovsky, (born April 25 [May 7, New Style], 1840, Votkinsk, Russia—died October 25 [November 6], 1893, St. Petersburg), the most popular Russian composer of all time. His music has always had great appeal for the general public in virtue of its tuneful, open-hearted melodies, impressive harmonies, and colourful, picturesque orchestration, all of which evoke a profound emotional response. His oeuvre includes 7 symphonies, 11 operas, 3 ballets, 5 suites, 3 piano concertos, a violin concerto, 11 overtures (strictly speaking, 3 overtures and 8 single movement programmatic orchestral works), 4 cantatas, 20 choral works, 3 string quartets, a string sextet, and more than 100 songs and piano pieces.

18. Write 5 trivias and 5 compositions of Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky. ​


Answer:

5 Trivias about Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky:

1.Tchaikovsky's most famous work, the ballet "Swan Lake," was not well-received when it premiered in 1877. It was only after Tchaikovsky's death that the ballet gained widespread popularity.

2.Despite being a celebrated composer, Tchaikovsky was often criticized for his unorthodox approach to composition. Many of his contemporaries considered his music too emotional and lacking in structure.

3.Tchaikovsky had a lifelong fear of contracting cholera. He avoided drinking water, and would often rinse his mouth with champagne instead.

4.In 1877, Tchaikovsky received a letter from a woman claiming to be his secret admirer. He eventually discovered that the letter was a hoax, and was devastated by the experience.

5.Tchaikovsky died at the age of 53, just nine days after conducting the premiere of his Symphony No. 6. The cause of his death remains unknown, but some historians believe that he may have committed suicide.

Answer:

The Russian ballet patriarch Fyodor Lopukhov has called Swan Lake a “national ballet” because of its swans, which he argues originate from Russian lyrically romantic sources, while many of the movements of the corps de ballet originated from Slavonic ring-dances.

Answer: The Russian ballet patriarch Fyodor Lopukhov has called Swan Lake a “national ballet” because of its swans, which he argues originate from Russian lyrically romantic sources, while many of the movements of the corps de ballet originated from Slavonic ring-dances. According to Lopukhov, “both the plot of Swan Lake, the image of the Swan and the very idea of a faithful love are essentially Russian”.
