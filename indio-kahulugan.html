13. what is a indio to society


First of all, the indigenous people of the Philippines are referred to as Indios (for colonists, who are of pure Austronesian descent and negritos). It was only applied to Christianized natives who lived in proximity to the spanish colonists.

Before, when Spaniards used to colonize the Philippines, people saw themselves in literal terms of color. 

This was also applied to the U.S. before. The U.S, which was very segregationist/racist, came in and imposed black/white categories that were very hard to implement. We had an U.S sponsored dictator called Trujillo who tried to push the term Indio (Indian/Native American literally) as an identifier for skin color that is between light brown and dark brown. With time, this only became a term associated with skin color but NOT with Native Americans. In other words, there is no linkage between the term Indio and being Native American, or us thinking we are natives. The real term used for someone who is Native American or of partial Native-American descent in the D.R. is “Raza India” (Indian race), or “Gente India” (Indian People) which is different from indian color. In short, Dominicans do not use the term Indio to deny blackness but to more accurately describe a color which is not as light as white and not as dark as Moreno, although this largely depends on the person and there are huge overlaps between Indio and Moreno.

25. ano ang katungkulan ng indio


Sila ang pangunahing pinagkunan ng mga tributo at iba pang uri ng buwis; naging trabahador na nagtayo ng mga simbahan, gusali at sasakyang-pandagat at tagaputol ng troso; at nagsilbing dagdag na puwersa ng mga Espanyol sa kanilang hukbong sandatahan sa panahon ng mga digmaan at ekspedisyon.

Ano nga ba ang Indio?

Ito ay salitang Kastila na tumutukoy sa mga Pilipinong hindi nakapag-aral. Isa ito sa limang uri ng antas sa lipunan. Ang paggamit ng katawagang ito noong panahon ng Espanyol ay karaniwang may halong pag-alipusta sa mga katutubong Filipino. May pahiwatig ito ng pagiging isang nilalang na mababa ang katayuan sa lipunan, tamad, at mangmang.

Ano ang Antas sa Lipunan?

Ito ay isang lista para sa Antas na ginawa sa pamamagitan ng pyramid chart. Ang Antas sa Lipunan ay ginawa ng mga Espanyol.

Iba pang Antas sa Lipunan

Insulares- Ito ay tumutukoy sa mga ipinanganak sa Pilipinas na may dugong purong Kastila ang mga magulang. Ito ay itinuturing na pangalawang pinakamataas na antas.

Peninsulares- Ito ay tumutukoy sa mga Espanyol na nakatira sa Pilipinas ngunit ipinanganak sa Espanya. Ito ay itinuturing na pinakamataas na antas.

Mestiso- Ito ay tumutukoy sa mga Pilipino na hindi purong Pilipino. Sila ay mga anak ng Pilipino at Kastila o Pilipino at Tsino.

Principalia- Ito ay tumutukoy sa mga  Pilipinong nabibilang sa mataas na uri ng lipunan noong panahon. Sila ay mas makapangyarihan at mas maraming pribelehiyo o karapatan. Tinatawag din silang Ilustrado.

28. from indio to filipino history​


Answer:

One of the greatest homage ever made was when Ninoy Aquino proclaimed to the world: “The Filipino is worth dying for.” His martyrdom proved that he was sincere. But we can also sincerely ask ourselves, who is this Filipino worth dying for? Surely it cannot be those congressmen whose recent antics and machinations have been an international embarrassment. Neither can it be the ruling economic and political elite that have ruled this country for generations. Nor can it be the rich who have made money in this country; but, have spent it abroad rather than investing in their own country. Nor is it the countless politicians who parrot the same promises election after election.

In searching for this Filipino, it is important to look back at history and remember that before the 1880s, the term “Filipino” actually referred to the people of Spanish parentage born in the Philippines. The Malayan, native born inhabitants of the Philippine islands were called “indio” or “indigenta.” This class or group occupied the lowest level in a highly stratified class society.  

At the highest level were the “peninsular” who were of Spanish parentage and born in Spain. Then came the Filipino; next the mestizo, mixed Spanish and native parentage; then the mestizo chino or mestizo de sangley; then the chino or sangley. Considered as outsiders were the moro or Muslim;  the isolated indigenous tribes in the mountains; and roving bands of aetas.
