2. Explain the importance of understanding the risk factors associated with disasters and how they can be mitigated.

3. Discuss the various risk factors associated with disasters, such as:

- Natural hazards (e.g., earthquakes, hurricanes, floods, etc.)

- Human-induced hazards (e.g., pollution, deforestation, etc.)

- Socio-economic factors (e.g., poverty, poor infrastructure, etc.)

4. Explain the different strategies that can be used to mitigate the effects of disasters, such as:

- Early warning systems

- Building resilient infrastructure

- Developing emergency response plans

- Promoting risk awareness

5. Highlight the importance of young people in disaster risk reduction and provide examples of how they can get involved.

6. Conclude by emphasizing the need for young people to be aware of the risks associated with disasters and how they can help to reduce these risks.
