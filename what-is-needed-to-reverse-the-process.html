Which processes can be reverse and which cannot be reversed? Reversible processes are those that can be returned to its original state. On the other hand, irreversible processes are those that once done cannot be reverted. There are many examples of this. For example, if you cut a tree, that process is not reversible. 3. what is needed to reverse the process?


Pressure greater than the osmosis pressure of the solution can be carried out to reverse the process, this process is called Reverse Osmosis

Further explanation

Osmosis pressure is the minimum pressure given to the solution so that there is no osmotic displacement from a more dilute solution to a more concentrated solution.

General formula:

[tex]\large {\boxed {\bold {\pi \: = \: M \: x \: R \: x \: T}}}[/tex]

π = osmosis pressure (atm)

M = solution concentration (mol / l)

R = constant = 0.08205 L atm mol-1 K-1

T = Temperature (Kelvin)

Osmosis itself is the process of moving particles through the semipermeable wall. Usually from aqueous solution to a thicker solution. There will be a difference in hydrostatic pressure due to the height difference that has not changed anymore. When this is said to achieve equilibrium where the same velocity occurs from both directions of the flow. This hydrostatic pressure (due to the difference in height between the 2 solutions) can be called osmotic pressure.

The pressure needed to prevent the osmosis process is called osmosis pressure

The principle of reverse osmosis is to pass a thicker solution by applying osmotic pressure through a semipermeable membrane to a thinner solution.

There is an isotonic condition if two different solutions have the same osmotic pressure, hypertonic if one solution has a higher osmotic pressure, and hypotonic if one solution has a lower osmotic pressure

So osmosis is the process of moving parts with low solvent concentration (hypotonic) to high solvent concentration (hypertonic).

25. what process is the reverse of respiration​


Answer:

Cellular Respiration and Photosynthesis are almost opposite processes because photosynthesis removes carbon dioxide from the atmosphere while cellular respiration puts back carbon dioxide. Cellular respiration uses oxygen and has it's waste product of carbon dioxide (CO2).

Photosynthesis

In respiration oxygen is involved, carbon dioxide is released and occurs all the time of day whereas in photosynthesis oxygen is released, carbon dioxide is involved and occurs in the presence of sunlight. The reactants in respiration are the products of photosynthesis. Therefore, respiration is said to be the reverse of photosynthesis.
