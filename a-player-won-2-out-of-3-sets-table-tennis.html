A game is won when a player scores four (or more) points: 15, 30, 40 and the game-winning point. Should both players make it 40, then the score is called "deuce." Following deuce, a player must win two consecutive points: the first point, known as "advantage" and the game-winning point.

Table Tennis Rules

THE TABLE

1. The table shall have a rectangular surface of 274 cm. (9 ft.) length, 152.5 cm. (5 ft.) the width. It shall be  assisted such that its upper surface, referred to as the playing surface, is laid down in a horizontal plane of  

76 cm. (2 ft. 6 in.) above the ground.  

2. It shall be made of any material and shall produce a uniform bounce of approximately 23 cm. (8 3/4 in.)  when the regular ball fall from a height of 30 cm. (12 in.) above the surfacearea.

3.  The playing surface shall be dark and matt, with a white line of 2 cm. (3/4 inch) long on each side.  

4.  The lines are 152.5 cm wide. (5 ft.) the edges or ends shall be referred to as the end lines and shall be  considered as continuing forever in both directions.  

5. The lines are 274 cm wide. (9 ft.) edges or sides shall be referred to as side lines.

6.  For doubles, the playing surface shall be divided in half by a white line 3 mm. (1/8 in.) long, parallel to the side lines, referred to as the center line. The permanent marking of the center line shall not invalidate the  single play table. The middle line shall be treated as part of a right-hand court.  

7.  The playing surface shall be considered to include the top edges of the table, but not the sides of the top of the table below the bottom.
