11. 2. The quotient is 25 and the divisor is 14. The dividend is 353. I amthe remainder what number am I?​


The quotient is 25 and the divisor is 14. The dividend is 353. I am  the remainder what number am I?​

Answer: The remainder is 3.

Step-by-step explanation:

353 ÷ 14 = 25 r. 3Since the first digit of the dividend is not divisible by the divisor, we consider the first two digits (35). Now, 35 is not divisible by 14 but 14 × 2 = 28 so, write 2 as the quotient. Write 28 below 35 and subtract 35 - 28 = 7. Since 7 < 14, we will bring down 3 from the dividend to make it 73. 73 is not divisible by 14 but 14 × 5 = 70, so, write 5 as the quotient. Write 70 below 73 and subtract 73 - 70 = 3.  Now 4 < 14. Thus, remainder = 3 and quotient = 25.

More information:

Long Division is a method for dividing huge numbers that divides the problem into multiple steps that must be completed in a specific order. The dividend is divided by the divisor in the same way that ordinary division problems are divided, giving the quotient and, in some cases, a remainder.

Parts of DivisionThe dividend is the number that has to be divided. In the equation above, the number 353 is the dividend. The divisor is the number that will divide the dividend. In the equation above, the number 14 is the divisor. The quotient is the result of division. In the equation above, the quotient is 25. The remainder is the leftover part or the number left after certain steps and cannot be divided further. In the equation above, the remainder is 3.
