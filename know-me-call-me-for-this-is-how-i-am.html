Correct Answer: lake

15. know me: call me,for this is how I am


The answer to this riddle would be "name". The name of someone or something is what we use to call them or give them identification.

Explanation:

We use a name to call something or someone. A name is what represents a person, animal, thing, event, or idea. In the riddle, it says call me for this is who I am. It literally translates into a name because you cannot call someone by their hair color unless that is his nickname.

There are different kinds of names we have in the present and we will explain each of them below. Each of them can be used in different ways and situations.

AKA, Alias, Nicknames, Moniker, Sobriquet
AKA stands for also known as. These names are used to call someone other than their real names.
Formal name, Proper name, Scientific name
These types of names are used internationally and are the ones accepted by the law written in documents.
Endearment
An alternative name given to a person really close to you such as a lover, sibling, or even a close friend.
Nom De Plume or Pen Name
These are names used by a writer who wants to stay anonymous with his works.
Title
A name that is given to a person with a position or an ordinary person with a title. Examples of this would be the name Mr. president, madame secretary, or Boss.
