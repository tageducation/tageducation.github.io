26. What can you say about “ It is the pride of every Filipino family to have children who have attained higher education” ? ​


Answer:

In a country where higher education is a scarcity for those in the lower classes, having a child who even just finished an undergraduate degree is something a Filipino family takes pride into. An average cost of 58,000 Php to 73,000 Php for private universities will cover one's tuition for a year. For other basic necessities, expect to spend 204,000 Php to 291,000 Php per year which can also cover transportation, food, and other miscellaneous expenses. With costs ranging from 1,048,000 Php to 1,456,000 Php for a whole 4 year undergraduate course along with other fees like food and transportation (and not to forget theses that can cost less than 20,000 Php), getting your child to graduate an undergraduate course is a hell of a chore.

Not only the feeling of getting your child to graduate college from your own blood sweat and tears satisfying, but the hopes of having a better future is now a reachable dream, because most decent paying jobs in the Philippines require at least an undergraduate degree.
