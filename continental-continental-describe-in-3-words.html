15. describe oceanic continental plate boundary in 3 words​


Answer :

Two plate convergence

Explanation:

When oceanic crust converges with continental crust, the denser oceanic plate plunges beneath the continental plate. This process, called subduction, occurs at the oceanic trenches. The entire region is known as a subduction zone. Subduction zones have a lot of intense earthquakes and volcanic eruptions. The subducting plate causes melting in the mantle above the plate. The magma rises and erupts, creating volcanoes. These coastal volcanic mountains are found in a line above the subducting plate. The volcanoes are known as a continental arc.

Describe each type of plate boundaries in 3 words.

Answer:

1. Convergent - two plates collide

a.) continental-continental - two continental converge

b.) oceanic-oceanic - one plate subducted

c.) oceanic-continental - oceanic plates subduct

2. Divergent - plates move away

3. Transform - slide opposite directions

Continental-continental

When two continental plates collide, they collide and form mountains. The magnificent Himalaya Mountains are the result of a convergent plate boundary of this type. The Appalachian Mountains were formed by the ancient convergence of Pangaea.

Oceanic-oceanic

One of the plates (oceanic crust and lithospheric mantle) is pushed or subducted under the other at an ocean-ocean convergent boundary.

Oceanic-continental

The subduction of an oceanic plate beneath a continental plate results in the formation of a line of volcanoes known as a continental arc and the occurrence of earthquakes.
