Step 1: Enumerate the given values.

Given:

Mass of solution = 500 g

Percent by mass = 5.3%

Step 2: Identify what is being asked.

Required:

Amount of glucose and water

Step 3: Solve for the amount of glucose and water

Solution:

The formula for percent by mass is given by:

percent \: by \: mass \: = \frac{mass \: of \: solute}{mass \: of \: solution} \times 100percentbymass=massofsolutionmassofsolute×100

Amount of glucose:

To solve for the amount of glucose, use the formula and subtitute the given values. The mass of solute is the amount of glucose.

5.3 = \frac{mass \: of \: solute}{500 \: g} \times 1005.3=500gmassofsolute×100

mass \: of \: solute \: = \frac{(5.3)(500g)}{100}massofsolute=100(5.3)(500g)

mass \: of \: solute = 26.5gmassofsolute=26.5g

Amount of glucose is 26.5 g

Amount of water

= 500 g - 26.5 g

= 473.5 g

Amount of water is 473.5 g

16. How many grams of glucose is needed to prepare 800mL of 5% glucose solution?


Answer: You would need 20g of glucose.

Since we want a 5% glucose solution, the mass (grams of glucose) divided by the volume (mL of the solution) should be 5% (0.05).

From the given 400mL, the equation would then be:

x g/ 400 = 0.05 g/mL

x g = (400mL)(0.05 g/mL)

x g = (400mL)(5g / 100mL)

x = 20 g

20. how many grams of carbon are in 39 grams of glucose?​


Answer:

mass C = 15.6 g

Explanation:

Calculating the molar mass of glucose

molar mass = (12 g × 6) + (1 g × 12) + (16 g × 6)

molar mass = 180 g

Calculating the percentage of carbon

%C = [(6 × 12 g) / 180 g] × 100

%C = 40 %

Calculating the mass of carbon in glucose

mass C = (%C / 100) × mass glucose

mass C = (40 / 100) × 39 g

mass C = 15.6 g

25. How many grams of carbon are in 39.0 grams of glucose (C6H12O6)?​


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of C₆H₁₂O₆.

molar mass = (12.01 g/mol × 6) + (1.008 g/mol × 12) + (16.00 g/mol × 6)

molar mass = 180.16 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the mass percent of C in C₆H₁₂O₆.

[tex]\text{\%C} = \frac{\text{12.01 g/mol × 6}}{\text{180.16 g/mol}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\text{\%C = 40.00\%}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the mass of C in C₆H₁₂O₆.

[tex]\text{mass of C} = \frac{\text{\%C}}{100} \: × \:\text{mass of C₆H₁₂O₆}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass of C} = \frac{40.00}{100} \: × \:\text{39.0 g}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of C = 15.6 g}}[/tex]
