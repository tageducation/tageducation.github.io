MEANING

Subduction is a geological process that takes place at convergent boundaries of tectonic plates where one plate moves under another and is forced to sink due to high gravitational potential energy into the mantle. Regions where this process occurs are known as subduction zones. Rates of subduction are typically measured in centimeters per year, with the average rate of convergence being approximately two to eight centimeters per year along most plate boundaries.

Plates include both oceanic crust and continental crust. Stable subduction zones involve the oceanic lithosphere of one plate sliding beneath the continental or oceanic lithosphere of another plate due to the higher density of the oceanic lithosphere. This means that the subducted lithosphere is always oceanic while the overriding lithosphere may or may not be oceanic. Subduction zones are sites that usually have a high rate of volcanism and earthquakes. Furthermore, subduction zones develop belts of deformation and metamorphism in the subducting crust, whose exhumation is part of orogeny and also leads to mountain building in addition to collisional thickening.

30. Subduction is illustrated in which of the following?A. convergent plate boundary B. transform fault boundaryC. divergent plate boundary D. subducting plates​


Subduction is illustrated in A. convergent plate boundary. Subduction is a geological process that occurs at the boundaries between tectonic plates where one plate slides under another and is forced down into the mantle. The regions where this process occurs are known as subduction zones.

Subduction is the process that occurs when one tectonic plate moves under another and sinks into the mantle. The Earth's surface is made up of several large plates that move around on the Earth's surface. When two plates collide, they can either slide past each other, or one can be forced underneath the other. Subduction occurs at convergent plate boundaries, where two plates are moving towards each other.

The downward movement of the plates creates a deep ocean trench. As the plate continues to sink, it starts to melt and forms magma. Magma rises up and pushes against the overlying plate, causing it to buckle and create mountains. Earthquakes often happen along convergent plate boundaries as the plates grind against each other.
