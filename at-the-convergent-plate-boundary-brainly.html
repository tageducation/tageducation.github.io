3. What are the processes that occur in converging, diverging, and transform plate boundaries?converging plate boundaries—diverging plate boundaries—transform plate boundaries—​


Answer:

Divergent boundaries -- where new crust is generated as the plates pull away from each other.

Convergent boundaries -- where crust is destroyed as one plate dives under another.

Transform boundaries -- where crust is neither produced nor destroyed as the plates slide horizontally past each other.

17. what are the function of Divergent Plate boundaries and Convergent plate boundaries and Transform Plate boundaries?​


Answer:

A divergent boundary occurs when two tectonic plates move away from each other. Along these boundaries, earthquakes are common and magma (molten rock) rises from the Earth's mantle to the surface, solidifying to create new oceanic crust. When two plates come together, it is known as a convergent boundary.

24. 2. Give examples of geologic features/events happening along: Converging continental-continental plate boundary Divergent boundary Converging oceanic-continental plate boundary Transform plate boundary Converging oceanic-oceanic plate boundary ​


Answer:

Converging continental-continental plate boundary

Examples of continent-continent convergent boundaries are the collision of the India Plate with the Eurasian Plate, creating the Himalaya Mountains, and the collision of the African Plate with the Eurasian Plate, creating the series of ranges extending from the Alps in Europe to the Zagros Mountains in Iran.

Divergent boundary

Mid-Atlantic Ridge.

Red Sea Rift.

Baikal Rift Zone.

East African Rift.

East Pacific Rise.

Gakkel Ridge.

Galapagos Rise.

Explorer Ridge.

Converging oceanic-continental plate boundary

Examples of ocean-continent convergent boundaries are subduction of the Nazca Plate under South America (which has created the Andes Mountains and the Peru Trench) and subduction of the Juan de Fuca Plate under North America (creating the Cascade Range).

Transform plate boundary

The most famous example of this is the San Andreas Fault Zone of western North America. The San Andreas connects a divergent boundary in the Gulf of California with the Cascadia subduction zone. Another example of a transform boundary on land is the Alpine Fault of New Zealand.

Converging oceanic-oceanic plate boundary ​

Examples of ocean-ocean convergent zones are subduction of the Pacific Plate south of Alaska (creating the Aleutian Islands) and under the Philippine Plate, where it creates the Marianas Trench, the deepest part of the ocean.

Difference between divergent and convergent boundaryA divergent (constructive) plate boundary occurs when two plates move AWAY from each other in opposite directions. As these plates move apart, they cause cracks in the earth's surface, allowing magma from the earth's mantle to fill the void. This allows for the formation of new features such as volcanoes and mountain ridges. A convergent (destructive) plate boundary is one in which the two plates are moving IN THE SAME DIRECTION. The subduction zone occurs when the oceanic crust subducts beneath the continental crust, resulting in earthquakes and fold mountains.Divergence indicates that two forces are moving apart, whereas convergence indicates that two forces are moving together.Divergence indicates how two trends move further apart, whereas convergence shows how they move closer together.
