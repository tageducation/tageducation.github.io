4. irections: Compare and contrast concave lensens and convex lenses Draw the lens Do light rays converge Or diverge? Is the image upright Or upside down? ast Is the image smaller Or larger than the object Concave Lenses Concave​


Explanation:

Concave lenses and convex lenses are both types of lenses that are used to manipulate light.

A concave lens is a lens that is thinner at the center and thicker at the edges. Light rays that pass through a concave lens diverge, meaning they spread apart. The image formed by a concave lens is always smaller than the object and is also inverted or upside down.

A convex lens, on the other hand, is thicker at the center and thinner at the edges. Light rays that pass through a convex lens converge, meaning they come together. The image formed by a convex lens is always larger than the object and is also upright or right side up.

A concave lens is typically used to correct hypermetropia or farsightedness, while a convex lens is typically used to correct myopia or nearsightedness.

In terms of appearance, a concave lens is curved inward and a convex lens is curved outward.

A concave lens:

Diverges light rays

Forms an inverted, smaller image

A convex lens:

Converges light rays

Forms an upright, larger image

28. describe and compare the images formed in a convex lens and in a concave lens. explain hoe the images are formed in these type of lenses​


Answer:

When light passes through a convex lens, it is bent, or refracted, towards the center of the lens. This causes the light rays to converge at a single point, known as the focal point. The image formed by a convex lens is therefore always located behind the lens, at the focal point. This type of lens is used to create a magnified image of an object.

On the other hand, when light passes through a concave lens, it is bent away from the center of the lens. This causes the light rays to diverge, or spread out, after passing through the lens. The image formed by a concave lens is therefore always located in front of the lens, and it is usually smaller and inverted compared to the object. This type of lens is used to create a reduced, or shrunken, image of an object.

Explanation:

In summary, the main difference between a convex lens and a concave lens is the way in which they refract light. Convex lenses cause light to converge and form a magnified image, while concave lenses cause light to diverge and form a reduced image

Eyeglasses. Eyeglasses are the most common form of eyewear used to correct or improve many types of vision problems. They consist of a frame that holds 2 pieces of glass or plastic, which have been ground into lenses to correct refractive errors. Refractive errors can include trouble seeing far away (nearsightedness or myopia) and trouble seeing close up (farsightedness or hyperopia). They can also include blurring due to an irregularly shaped cornea (astigmatism). Eyeglasses work by adding or subtracting focusing power to the eye's cornea and lens.

Contact lenses. Contact lenses are worn directly on the cornea. Like eyeglasses, contact lenses correct refractive errors. They do this by adding or subtracting focusing power to the cornea and lens.
