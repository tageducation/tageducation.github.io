16. State the relationship of current, voltage, and resistance.


Answer:

The relationship between voltage, current, and resistance is described by Ohm's law. This equation, i = v/r, tells us that the current, i, flowing through a circuit is directly proportional to the voltage, v, and inversely proportional to the resistance, r. In other words, if we increase the voltage, then the current will increase. But, if we increase the resistance, then the current will decrease. We saw these concepts in action with the garden hose. Increasing the pressure caused the flow to increase, but getting a kink in the hose increased the resistance, which caused the flow to decrease.

18. 1. State the relationship of current, voltage, and resistance.​


George Simon Ohm (1789–1854) formulated the relationship between electric current (I), resistance (R) and potential difference (V), which became known as Ohm's law.

The value of electrical resistance is a comparison or quotient between voltage and electric current. The relationship is regulated by the following equation:

R = V/I

Note :

R = the value of electrical resistance in ohms.

V is the value of the mains voltage in volts.

I = the value of electric current in amperes

In the picture above, the voltage generated by the battery is symbolized by V, the electric current is symbolized by I, and the resistance is represented by the resistor R.

 

Ohm's law is one of the basic sciences of electronics. This one science is very useful to us in calculating the current, voltage, or resistance of an electrical circuit.

.It is known that the greater the potential difference that appears, the stronger the current flowing will be even greater. The magnitude of the ratio between the potential difference and the electric current is always the same, or constant. So the potential difference is equivalent to the current (V  I). If viewed mathematically, it can be written that V = m x l, where m is a constant ratio between potential difference and current strength.

 

Types of Resistance

In everyday life, there are several types of resistance or resistors that are often used according to their needs. The types of barriers are as follows:

a. Permanent Resistor

In fixed resistors, which are generally made of carbon or thin nichrome wire, the resistance value is symbolized by various colors that are circular on the outer shell. The color symbol has a meaning according to its location.

b. Resistor Variable

There are two kinds of variable resistors on the market, namely rotating and shifting variable resistors. Basically, the workings of the two resistors are the same, namely by sliding or rotating the sliding contact to increase or decrease the resistance value as needed. We can find these variable resistors in volume systems in radios, tape recorders, and other electronic equipment.
