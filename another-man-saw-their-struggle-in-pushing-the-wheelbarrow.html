18. A worker pushes a sled with a force of 40 n over a level distance of 6.0 m. if a frictional force of 16 n acts on the wheelbarrow in a direction opposite to that of the worker, what total work is done on the wheelbarrow?


GIVEN:
F = 40 N
f = 16 N (frictional force)
d = 6.0 m

UNKNOWN QUANTITY:
W (work)

EQUATION:
W = ΣFd, where

   W is work
   ΣF is net force
   d is displacement or distance

SOLUTION:

W = ΣFd, but we have to compute ΣF first...

   ΣF (net force) is the total force acting on the object so,
   ΣF = F + f, where

   F is the force given by the push of the worker
   f is the frictional force acting on the opposite direction

   ΣF = 40N + -16N    (f is negative bec of the opposite direction)
   ΣF = 24N

Going back to the main formula,
W = ΣFd
W = (24N)(6.0m)
W = 144 Nm or 144 J

The total work done on the wheelbarrow is 144 joules. :)
