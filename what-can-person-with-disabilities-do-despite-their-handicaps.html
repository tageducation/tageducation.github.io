1. What can persons with disabilities do despites their handicaps?


Answer:

Persons with disabilities are capable of doing many things despite their handicaps. Their disabilities may present challenges in some areas, but there are often ways to work around these challenges and find ways to live fulfilling lives.

Here are some examples of activities that persons with disabilities can participate in:

Education: Persons with disabilities can pursue education at all levels, from elementary school through college and graduate school. Educational institutions have an obligation to provide reasonable accommodations to ensure that persons with disabilities have access to education.Employment: Many persons with disabilities are able to work in a variety of fields. Employers are required by law to provide reasonable accommodations to enable persons with disabilities to perform their jobs.Sports and physical activity: There are many adaptive sports and physical activities that persons with disabilities can participate in, such as wheelchair basketball, wheelchair racing, adaptive skiing, and many others.Creative pursuits: Persons with disabilities can also participate in creative pursuits, such as painting, writing, music, and other forms of artistic expression.Advocacy: Persons with disabilities can advocate for their rights and the rights of others with disabilities. They can also work to raise awareness about disability issues and promote inclusion in society.

These are just a few examples of the many things that persons with disabilities can do. It's important to remember that every person with a disability is unique and has their own strengths and abilities. With the right support and accommodations, persons with disabilities can live full and rewarding lives.

14. how do handicap and disability differ​


Answer:

Handicap And Disability are often used interchangeably; however, they have distinctly different meanings, particularly when used by the medical community. Disability describes the mental or physical limitation a person has, and handicap refers to the disadvantage she experiences because of it. Disability is a functional limitation with regard to a particular activity; and handicap refers to a disadvantage in filling a role in life relative to a peer group.
