3. It is an equal keyframe animation from point A to point B. O a. Smooth Keyframe O b. Constant Keyframe c. Manual Keyframe d. Automatic Keyframe W Ö e. Linear Keyframe​


Linear Keyframe is the most basic type of keyframe in animation. This is the default type of keyframes in some of the most popular animating software nowadays.

The answer to your question is:

e. Linear Keyframe

There are other techniques for keyframes such as mentioned in the options from the question. We will discuss each of the following:

Smooth KeyframeConstant KeyframeManual KeyframeAutomatic Keyframe

Smooth Keyframe

Smooth Keyframes are techniques made by animators to have a more fluid transition from one element to another. It can be often found in 3D animations where they do not use still images, unlike the traditional 2D animation does. It makes all the harsh transitions smoother by fixing the issues.

Constant Keyframe

Constant keyframes are also called continuous keyframes. It works between two or more finished keyframes where they use a constant amount of speed transition depending on desired results.

Manual Keyframes

More advanced animators do not rely on linear keyframes or the default typesetting of keyframes. They manually adjust the keyframes for a more variety of results.

Automatic Keyframes
When animators build animations, automatic keyframes automatically record the keyframes. It automatically functions itself without the animators' effort.

23. equal keyframe animation from point A to point B.


Smooth Keyframe.

Keyframe animations allow for more than one goal value to be attained at one time throughout the animation. In other words, the intermediate value specified by each keyframe might vary, and the animation value specified by the last keyframe is the one that is attained. You may create more intricate animations by supplying numerous values to animate.

A keyframe, also written as “key frame”, is something that defines the starting and/or ending point of any smooth transition. In the case of animation or film or video, that item may be a sketch or a specific frame of a shoot. Every shot, whether animated or live-action, is divided up into frames. Keyframes may be thought of as the most significant frames of a shot that establish the parameters for the remaining frames and identify the changes that will take place as transitions throughout.

In image processing, a frame or keyframe is a timeline location that represents the beginning stages or the end of a process. It contains insider information that determines when the process can begin and end. Over time, middle frames are encoded between these meanings to maintain the effect of the movement. For graphics and movie shooting, the keyframe is a sketch that decides the start and end points of any seamless transition. These are called frames because their position over time is calculated as images in a storyboard.

What is a smooth keyframe?

This implies that any animation begins instantly and ends as soon as it reaches the last keyframes. This is OK for some applications, but you should typically smooth out your keyframes. When a keyframe is smooth, it begins slowly, accelerates, and then gently stops.
