Answer:

A generator consists of three main parts: A fuel system, an engine, and an alternator. The fuel system supplies stored chemical energy to the engine, where its combustion takes place. As a result, mechanical energy is generated, which is supplied to the alternator.

What are the three basic parts of an electric generator?

The three most basic parts of an electric generator are; dc power supply, armature coil and battery.

THE PARTS

» DC (direct-current) power supply - The one that converts power -- a machine that delivers electrical output in only one functioning direction.

» Battery - Provides power to the engine. The most common used battery for generators is lead acid battery.

» Armature - Enables the flow of electrical energy as it becomes a magnetic field, and will then be delivered to the communicator.
