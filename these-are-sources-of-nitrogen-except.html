21. . A nucleotide is composed of the following EXCEPT?A. deoxyribose B. nitrogenous bases C. phosphate D. sperm cell​


NUCLEOTIDE

A nucleotide is an organic monomeric compound consisting of a pentose sugar, a nitrogenous base, and a phosphate group.

Based on a question that has several options. The meanings of some of these options are as follows:

1.Deoxyribose/DNA:

Deoxyribose/DNA is a polymer consisting of a series of nucleotide monomers that form long chains and are twisted with a double helix structure. DNA is the genetic material that contains genes that carry traits and is involved in all hereditary and biosynthetic processes in organisms. DNA molecules can be found in the nucleus of cells and to a lesser extent in the mitochondria. The nitrogenous bases of DNA are adenine, guanine, cytosine, and thymine.

2. Nitrogen base

Nitrogenous bases are part of the nucleotide constituents of DNA and RNA. Heterocyclic nitrogenous bases that form nucleotides are called purines and pyrimidines. The nitrogenous bases that make up DNA are adenine (A), thymine (T), guanine (G), and cytosine (C). The difference between the nitrogenous bases that make up RNA and DNA is Thymine (T), which in RNA is replaced by Uracil (U).

Purine = Adenine and Guanine

Pyrimidine = Cytosine, Thymine, and Uracil.

3. Phosphate

Phosphate is one of the components that make up nucleotides. The phosphate is bonded to the 5' carbon by esterification so the bond is called a phosphodiester. To form nucleotide molecules and nucleic acids, the phosphodiester bond, namely the 5'-phosphate group on the nucleotide unit, will bind to the 3'-hydroxyl group on the other nucleotide unit. All phosphodiester bonds form long linear chains with specific polarities at the 5' and 3' ends. The 5' end of the nucleotide is called the 5' end, and the 3' end is called the 3' end. In contrast to the phosphodiaster bond, the bond linking a pentose sugar to a phosphate group is called a covalent bond.

4. Sperm cells

Sperm cells are not a component of nucleotides.Sperm cells are the result of the male reproductive organs that are released along with semen when a man ejaculates.Sperm cells can be male or female.Consists of a sperm hood, head, and tail.

From the statements above, we can see that the compounds that make up nucleotides are pentose sugars, nitrogenous bases, and phosphates.
