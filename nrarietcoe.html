RECREATION

Recreation is a recreational pursuit, with leisure being unstructured time. The "need to do something for recreation" is a fundamental component of human biology and psychology. Recreational practices are often performed for entertainment, amusement, or leisure and are considered as "fun."

Play, recreation and work

Recreation is difficult to differentiate from the broader concept of play, which is often used to refer to children's recreational activities. Children can mimic behaviors that represent the realities of adult life in a playful manner. It has been suggested that play or recreational activities are reservoirs or expressions of surplus energy, channeling it into socially appropriate activities that satisfy individual and community needs without coercion, while still giving enjoyment and enjoyment to the user. A traditional viewpoint holds that work is aided by recreation, with recreation serving to "recharge the batteries" so that work efficiency improves. Work, an occupation usually conducted out of economic need and beneficial for society and structured within an economic system, may often be pleasurable and self-imposed, blurring the line between work and leisure. Many entertainment events are both work and leisure for one person. A leisure practice can become a source of income over time, and vice versa. As a result, for a guitarist, playing an instrument will be both a career and a recreation.

NEW NORMAL

A new normal is the environment in which an economy, population, or other entity rests after a crisis, where it varies from the situation that existed prior to the start of the crisis. One thing is certain: we all know that adapting to change can be difficult. Change, whether scheduled or unplanned, incremental or abrupt, is unavoidable and an essential part of being human. COVID-19 has caused an extraordinary change in our way of life over the last few months. Who would have imagined, before the pandemic, that toilet paper would become as valuable as gold? And now, even going to the grocery store on a weekly basis feels like an experience in and of itself! We've (reluctantly) let go of our old normal and are now transitioning for what seems to be our current normal And as society starts up again, there will be constraints in place, and we will face fundamental changes in our way of life.

QUARANTINE

A quarantine is a restriction on the movement of people, animals, and products imposed to discourage the transmission of disease or pests. It is also used in the context of disease and sickness, restricting the travel of people who have been subjected to a communicable disease but do not have a verified medical diagnosis. It differs from medical exclusion, which separates people that have been found to be afflicted with a communicable disease from the stable population. Border protection also includes quarantine considerations.
