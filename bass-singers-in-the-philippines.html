What is an Alto?

Alto is the lowest female voice type. It ranges from F3 to F5, but there are still a lot of singers who can sing above or below the average. It has a little similarity with mezzo-sopranos, but their voices are usually richer and fuller and registers lower than mezzos. Its the same as tenor in male voice type.

There are three female voice types:

AltoMezzo-SopranoSoprano

Mezzo Soprano is the middle range female voice type for females, its typical vocal range lies between A3 and A5.

Soprano is the highest female singing voice. And its vocal range lies between C4 and C6.

20. Filipino singer with a bass voice


Filipino singer with a bass voice

Bass- The bass voice is the lowest of male singers 'voices. The bass is heavy and deep, and low. The notice scale is usually from the low E to the middle C above. Bass singers have a tessitura that hovers below middle C, throughout the octaves. True bass singers are rare.  

A bass is typically the lowest and darkest of male voices, and is suitable for several position forms. The word bass originates from the Italian word basso, meaning small. Some singers in this group are called bass-baritones because they have voices which range from bass to baritone voice.  

1. Jonathan de la Paz Zaens, a former member of the Philippine Madrigal Singers, joined the group in its 1989 world tour, covering the USA, Europe, Mexico and Southeast Asia. He was also the delegate of the Philippines at the Norway (1993) and South America (1994) World Youth Choir.

2. Andrew Fernando- Is a Philippine Baritone and Foreign Singer based in the US.  

3. Jose Mari Chan - Is a Philippine singer, songwriter, and sugar businessman. He is currently president and chief executive officer of Binalbagan Isabela Sugar Company, Inc. (BISCOM)and A. Chan Sugar Company. He is also the founder and chairman of Signature Music, Inc.  

Awarded as one of The Philippines 'Ten Outstanding Young Men in 1974. He has received a Lifetime Achievement Award from the Music Industry Philippine Association and The Metro Pop Foundation. Winner of the first ever ABS-CBN "ELITE Platinum Prize" in 2005 and winner of the Dr. Jose P Rizal Prize for Excellence 2006.  

4. Noel Azcona -Baritone / Opera Singer Conservatory of Music Manila, Santo Tomas University. Baccalaureate in voice.
