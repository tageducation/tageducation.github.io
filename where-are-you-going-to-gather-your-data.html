19. where are you going to gather your data scope and delimitation


Answer:

The scope details how in-depth your study is to explore the research question and the parameters in which it will operate in relation to the population and timeframe. The delimitations of a study are the factors and variables not to be included in the investigation

Explanation:

Scope and delimitations are two elements of a research paper or thesis. The scope of a study explains the extent to which the research area will be explored in the work and specifies the parameters within which the study will be operating. For example, let's say a researcher wants to study the impact of mobile phones on behavior patterns of elementary school children. However, it is not possible for the researcher to cover every aspect of the topic.

So the scope will have to be narrowed down to a certain section of the target population. In this case, the scope might be narrowed down to a group of 50 children in grades 3-5 of one specific school. Their behavior patterns in school may have been observed for a duration of 6 months. These would form the delimitations of the study.

Thus, delimitations are the characteristics that limit the scope and describe the boundaries of the study, such as the sample size, geographical location or setting in which the study takes place, population traits, etc. Additionally, the researcher might also choose to use some research tools and methodologies to collect data but not others. These delimitations might be imposed for practical reasons, such as lack of time or financial resources to carry out a more thorough investigation. The delimitation section of the study should explain why specific choices were made while others were excluded and how this might affect the outcome of the research.

29. 4. Where are you going to gather your data?​


Gather data is carried out to obtain the information needed in order to achieve the research objectives. The gather data process is determined by the variables in the hypothesis. Data obtained through several methods.

1. Interview: This method of gathering data is done directly by the researcher through questions and responses or interviews with resource people who serve as informants to gather the information required for the study. Interviews are divided into two categories, namely structured and unstructured interviews.

Structured interview - Researchers have made a list of questions systematically. Researchers can also use various research instruments such as recorders, cameras for photos, and other instruments.Unstructured interview - The researcher did not use an interview guide that contained specific questions, but only contained important points from the problem that the respondents wanted to explore.

2. Observation: Direct observation was used to acquire data using observational methods. The five senses are used by researchers to make on-site observations of the research object, which are then recorded in notes or other recording mediums. Participatory observation, direct or covert observation, and unstructured observation are the three types of observation.

3. Questionnaire: Questionnaires are a more efficient data collection method if the researcher knows for sure the variables to be measured and knows what is expected of the respondents. In addition, the questionnaire is also suitable for use when the number of respondents is quite large and spread over a wide area. Questionnaires can be categorized into two types, namely open questionnaires and closed questionnaires. An open questionnaire is a questionnaire that gives freedom to the research object to answer. Meanwhile, a closed questionnaire is a questionnaire that has provided answer choices to be chosen by the object of research.

4. Document: This method of gathering data makes use of documents as one of the data sources to finish the study. Written sources, visual media, and photographs are all examples of documents that can be utilised. The researcher may select a technique for this study based on the needs of the study as well as the methodology of choice.
