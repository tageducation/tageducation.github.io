Answer:

The COVID-19 pandemic forced universities around the world to shut down their campuses indefinitely and move their educational activities onto online platforms. The universities were not prepared for such a transition and their online teaching-learning process evolved gradually. We conducted a survey in which we asked undergraduate students in an Indian university about their opinion on different aspects of online education during the ongoing pandemic. We received responses from 358 students. The students felt that they learn better in physical classrooms (65.9%) and by attending MOOCs (39.9%) than through online education. The students, however, felt that the professors have improved their online teaching skills since the beginning of the pandemic (68.1%) and online education is useful right now (77.9%). The students appreciated the software and online study materials being used to support online education. However, the students felt that online education is stressful and affecting their health and social life. Examples Using Opinion Marking Signals​Adidas sneakers, in my opinion, are more comfy than Nike shoes.I firmly believe that Samsung phones are less sturdy than iPhones.Personally, I prefer the croissant here to the one from the bakery across the street.In my opinion, a Lenovo laptop will last longer than an HP one.Personally, I believe you are able to do these naturally.I can make the same claim regarding a great number of linguistic concepts.Hiking is one of my favorite outdoor pursuits.Swimming in the ocean is undoubtedly more enjoyable than swimming in a pool, in my opinion.I believe dogs are friendlier than cats.I firmly believe that going for a run every day elevates your mood.I believe using the train is simpler than taking a car there.

What are opinion marking signals?

Words or phrases that convey views regarding something are known as opinion-marking signals. They are valuable cues in the shape of words and phrases that help us recognize opinion assertions.

Words that serve as opinion marking signals are those that start an opinionated sentence. When you want to share your personal opinion, you use them. Or if you want to make it clear that this is just your opinion and not a fact or a statement made by an authority figure.

Anything you say before expressing your opinion can serve as an opinion marking signal. Even if it isn't formally recognized as such, it is a marking signal as long as it conveys your need to communicate your opinion.

It is rather simple to recognize opinion marking signals. One is that they do it immediately after offering opinions, but that is too evident. They also start phrases, which is another bonus.

Understanding the idea of opinions will help you recognize these signs. There is no way to establish that an opinion is a fact. It's simply one person's way of thinking. Opinion marking signals are actually useful for indicating that the next line will only be a mere opinion and not a reality.
