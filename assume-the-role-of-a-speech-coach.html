6. Learning Task 5: Assume the role of a speech coach. Let us say, you have astudent who will be speaking in front of a large audience for the first time. Theidea of facing a great number of people alone causes panic and anxiety to the saidstudent. As a coach, what important pieces of advice or public speakingtechniques will you share with the said student? List at least five (5) statements oadvice in your notebook. pahelp please ​


Giving a speech is also the ability to express what is in your mind. It is a way of showing how you feel, experienced or heard.  

Giving a speech may terrify some individuals but with the right approach and mindset, anyone can wow the audience by keeping these things in mind:

Preparation
There is nothing more important than preparation. Whether the speech is prepared or not, preparation will be a great factor to ace your speech. Even experts in speech making feel nervous every time they give out their rehearsed speech. This is why it is important to prepare your piece before delivering it to a huge audience.

Organize your thoughts
Organizing thoughts is a systematic way of arranging your ideas in one. It helps your audience understand and follow the flow of your speech. Speeches sound more professional if they are well-organized. It would also be easier to transition from one idea to another as long as they convey connected concepts.
Considering the audience
Whether you are giving a speech for your school or in a huge audience of entrepreneurs, it is really important to know to who you are giving your speech. Say for example you are delivering your speech to elementary students. We need to match the words, pacing, and speed of the speech to their age. If we are delivering a speech to teachers, we should be more professionals and choose the right words for them to understand more what we are saying.
Be Confident
No matter how good the content of your speech is, if you do not sound confident, you will lose your reputation as a good speaker. No matter how good the facts and evidence you are giving, the audience will have hesitations to believe in you because you will look unsure of the things you are sharing. There is no perfect speech but we can make it believable by being confident with the available resources we have.
Know the purpose
The main question would be 'Why am I delivering this speech?'. Think of the benefits your audience can get from your speech. It is also important to analyze why you are giving a speech. Know the purpose at the end of the end and focus on long-term knowledge they can get.

28. the football team coach giving his last minute instructions to his players what type of speech style​


Answer:

Formal Speech Style

Explanation:

What distinguishes the formal speech style from the consultative style is the lack of listener participation. Unlike in interactions that use the consultative style, the listener does not participate, in most cases, in an interaction that requires the formal style. A formal style is often used in formal situations, such as in a graduation ceremony, an official meeting, a television newscast, and a conference, where there is only one-way communication. Since there is little or no feedback from the listeners or audience, the speaker needs to plan ahead of his or her utterances, or what he or she is going to say. Other characteristics of the formal style are:

 

a. Interruptions are not allowed. Only the speaker is the source of information.

b. Pronunciation is very clear.

c. Words are carefully chosen, and grammar rules are followed.

d. Sentence structures are more complex and varied.

e. Extensive and technical vocabulary is required.

f. Ideas are cohesive and organized.

g. All background or necessary information is clearly given.

This speech style is also used in sermons, speeches, and announcements. This is also the style that is used for academic and business writing.
