18. Map about magmatism​


Answer:

Magmatism is the emplacement of magma within and at the surface of the outer layers of a terrestrial planet, which solidifies as igneous rocks. It does so through magmatic activity or igneous activity, the production, intrusion and extrusion of magma or lava. Volcanism is the surface expression of magmatism.

Geological map showing the Gangdese batholith, which is a product of magmatic activity about 100 million years ago.

Not to be confused with Magnetism.

Magmatism is one of the main processes responsible for mountain formation. The nature of magmatism depends on the tectonic setting.[1] For example, andesitic magmatism associated with the formation of island arcs at convergent plate boundaries or basaltic magmatism at mid-ocean ridges during sea-floor spreading at divergent plate boundaries.

On Earth, magma forms by partial melting of silicate rocks either in the mantle, continental or oceanic crust. Evidence for magmatic activity is usually found in the form of igneous rocks – rocks that have formed from magma.

2. Plutonism is the process by which magma rises through the crust and crystallizes as an intrusive igneous rock beneath the Earth's surface.

EXPLANATION:

Magmatism is the entire activity of magma, from melting, the process when magma rises to the planet's surface, until it solidifies and forms rock. This is a direct reflection of the internal thermal state of the planet and its chemical and physical constitution.

On Earth, magmatism is closely related to tectonism, which is itself directly related to large-scale thermal convection processes in the mantle and core. Magma itself is a product of thermal instability and density in the Earth's mantle and crust. On planets like the Moon, Io, and Europa, processes different from those on Earth produce magmatism. Considerable meteoroid impacts have produced efficiently, especially early on, thick sheets of magma on all terrestrial planets and sometimes even on younger Earths.

Mechanisms:

Volcanism is the primary process by which chemical differentiation through partial melting of parental upper mantle peridotite occurs to produce, firstly, basaltic oceanic crust, and in the second and more complex fusion stage, "andesitic" continental crust. The evolutionary trend of magmatism is towards a layered and thermally stable earth. The geological record shows that modern patterns of volcanism and plate tectonics have existed for more than a billion years, as a mechanism for Earth's differentiation.

Magma is a composite fluid material consisting of solid minerals (mostly silicates), molten rock with or crystals and gas bubbles suspended in a molten silicate metric and is the product of partial melting within the earth, either in the upper mantle or in the earth's crust. As the stones move up (or add water) they start to melt a little. These small molten clumps migrate upwards and coalesce into larger volumes that continue to move upwards. The rheological contraction between the viscous magma in the magmatic chamber and the surrounding rock, which has elastic properties, has allowed for the occurrence of pressure field disturbances, which at magmatic pressures that are not equal to lithostatic pressures, can give rise to various modes of rock failure, causing eruptions.

During an eruption, inelastic processes including magmatic fungal erosion and thermal effects, can form continuous channels that support magma flow, driven by pressure differences between the magma body and surface vents. The magma then cools and crystallizes.
