Answer:

10 Various Entreprenual Traits:

1. Opportunity seeker. Able to recognize and seize opportunities to build up the business.

2. Patient. Must be able to put up with a lot of demanding tasks in his or her line of work.

3. Prudent. Should be rational rather than emotional in handling obstacles and making

decisions. He/She should also be firm enough when making decisions without hurting others.

4. Sociable. Personally factors such as emotional stability, personal relations, sociability,

consideration, and tactfulness are important contributors to the entrepreneur’s success.

5. Demand for efficiency and quality. One should strive for excellence to give the best quality

product or service.

6. Systematic. Should be orderly and organized.

7. Flexible. As a caregiver, you usually have a flexible schedule for seeing clients. You can take

appointments at various times of the day, whenever is convenient for you.

8. Technical knowledge. A responsible caregiver is always updated with technical knowledge

and its application.

9. Sense of independence and self-confidence. Develop trust in yourself that you can render

the best service to clients especially to elders.

10. Healthy. You should be in good health and optimistic at all times.

a. Broom – used in sweeping dirt; it is made up of twigs and bristles tied together and attached

to the handle.

b. Dustpan – a pan on which to sweep the dust and dirt.

c. Duster – a cloth used in removing dirt and dust.

d. Mops – made of rags, yarns or other absorbent materials secured around a stick, and used to

wet or swab floors and tiles.

e. Vacuum cleaner – an electric appliance used for cleaning floors, carpets and furniture by

suctioning the dirt.

f. Disinfectants – supplies such as soap, detergents and sprays used for cleaning and washing  the cleaning tools, also used for scrubbing the hands.

Washing Tools and Ironing:

a. Washing Machine – an electric appliance used for washing linens and ironing clothes.

b. Flat iron – an electric appliance used along with the ironing board to iron or press linens and

clothes or diapers.

c. Dryer – an electrical appliance designed for drying washed linens and clothes.

Tools and Equipment for the Preparation of Meals:

a. Airpot – an electric appliance used for boiling and keeping water hot.

b. Food Processor – an electric appliance used for slicing, dicing, shredding, and whipping food

ingredients.

c. Blender – an electric appliance which is used to blend, mix, puree or whip food.

d. Coffee maker – an electric appliance that brews hot coffee.

e. Chopping board – a flat wooden or plastic board where meat, fish, and vegetables can be cut

or sliced.

f. Ladle – a long handled spoon used for serving soup.

g. Oven – a chamber used for baking, roasting and heating food.

h. Microwave oven – a kitchen appliance that heats or cooks food by electromagnetic radiation

quickly, efficiently and evenly.

i. Stove – designed to supply heat for the purpose of cooking food.

j. Dishwasher – a mechanical device used for cleaning and washing dishes, utensils and pots.

k. Bread knife – has a serrated metal blade used for cutting soft bread.

l. Bottle sterilizer – used to sterilize feeding bottles.

Equipment Used in Taking Vital Signs.  

a. Sphygmomanometer – an instrument used in measuring blood pressure. There are two kinds

of sphygmomanometers. There is the mercury-based type and another is the digital type. The

digital sphygmomanometer is mercury-free and consists of a cuff that is applied in one hand for

testing. A stethoscope is attached to a valve that inflates automatically, the data is displayed on

the gauge.

b. Stethoscope – an instrument used for listening to the action of the heart, lungs, etc. Has a

circular piece placed against the chest and tubes leading to the ears.

c. Thermometer – used in taking body temperature. Body temperature is a measurement of the  amount of heat in the body.
