9. What principle do you wish to live by in your life and in your pursuit of the most important things you want to happen before you die?


Answer:

Your time is limited, don’t waste it living someone else’s life. Don’t be trapped by dogma, which is living the result of other people’s thinking. Don’t let the noise of other’s opinion drown your own inner voice. And most important, have the courage to follow your heart and intuition, they somehow already know what you truly want to become. Everything else is secondary.

Here are ways to live your life to the fullest:

Live every day on a fresh new start. Don’t be held back by what happened yesterday, the day before, the week before, the year before, and so on.

Be true to who you are. Stop trying to please other people or be someone else. It’s better to be an original version of yourself than an exact duplicate of someone else.

Quit complaining. Don’t be like the howling dog, always howling and never doing anything. Stop complaining about your problems and work on them instead.

Be proactive. Stop waiting for others around you to do something and take action yourself instead.

Rather than think “what if”, think “next time”. Don’t think about things you can’t change (namely what has happened and thoughts of other people) or unhappy things because these are disempowering. Instead focus on the things you can action upon. That’s the most constructive thing you can do in any situation.

Focus on WHAT vs. How. Focus on WHAT you want first, before you think about HOW to do it. Anything is possible, as long as you set your mind, heart and soul to it.

Create your own opportunities. You can wait for opportunities to drop in life. Or, you can go out there and create your own opportunities. The latter is definite and much more empowering.

Live more consciously each day. Stop sleepwalking through life. Your life is something to be experienced, not coasted through.

Be committed to your growth. In the Map of Consciousness, there are 17 levels of consciousness – from Shame to Enlightenment. The higher level of consciousness you are in, the richer your life experience. Achieving higher consciousness comes from your commitment to growth.

Know your inner self. This means knowing who you are and what you represent. Be clear of your personal identity.

Answering the question of "How can we survive without clothing,food and shelter"?

Food

In general, it is likely that a person could survive between 1 and 2 months without food. As many different factors influence the length of time that the body can last without food, this period will vary among individuals.

Clothing and shelter

My gut feeling with no shelter or clothing you won’t last very long.

You will probably won’t make through a single rainy day at 70 with no clothing, you’ll eventually pass out from hypothermia and likely never wake up unless it got warmer.

Our Basic Needs

A traditional list of immediate "basic needs" is food (including water), shelter and clothing. Many modern lists emphasize the minimum level of consumption of "basic needs" of not just food, water, clothing and shelter, but also sanitation, education, and healthcare. Different agencies use different lists.

Real world has always been a subject of dilemma for me. Not just me but everyone fails when it comes to 'comprehensive understanding' of real universe. Now comes virtual world, Where we know all about it, we know its virtual, we can be whomever we want just by its knowledge, even supremely habile ones.

Well, the time we would completely live in a virtual world quitting phantasmagoria of bitter and sweet which compiles the 'so-called' life is not that far. Talks are that, metaverse 3.0 is just a mile away.

My views could be a contravention of general morality, but still makes some sense than this whole play of life.

In life we experience challenges, trials, problems and sometimes we don't always get we want and that's why we say real world sucks. But even in darkness there's beauty. Those challenges are sometimes what we need to become someone better than we were and we are. A work well earned is a work well deserved, in the real world, we are taught to work hard for what we want and when we end up accomplishing it, we feel some sort of satisfaction. Rather than having something automatically, it's a great boost in our pride having thise accomplishments.

Life isn't all about material things, when you get to look beyond what we could possess, we will found something much valuable and much more priceless. Real world may entail hardships but out of those difficulties you will grow and discover real treasures such as love, the gift of friendship, sense of fulfillment, and you learn to appreciate even the tiniest joyful things.
