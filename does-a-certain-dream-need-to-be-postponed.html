1. does a certain dream need to be postponed?​


Answer:

Every time one of my dreams collapsed it was because I let somebody else interfere, take over, decide for me. I just did not have the strength to keep these persons far from my life, I thought I needed them.

What I needed was “I”, being myself; and can you imagine that for a very long time I thought that the other person was more important?

Life has slowly taught me that simple thing, which is that I am born, I live, I die - nobody else will do this for me. And when I understood this I started to live my own life, making mistakes which of course were rewarded with “Didn’t I tell you? Why did you not let me do it?” But these mistakes made me stronger, more resilient and sure of myself, of my own strength, my own value.

Now my dream is becoming a fabulous reality: I have a big place on a warm island, some houses to renovate or build (one of my passions), a flourishing Airbnb, as many animals as I like, friends coming and going… Nobody sneers at me because I am a vegetarian or like to walk barefoot even when doing my local shopping.

I am thankful for the problematic person whose negativity forced me to grow straight and strong, and who taught me to say NO to dream-killers.

Never more will anybody do this to me.

What is procrastination?

Procrastination is the instance of subconsciously postponing or setting aside actions and decisions until the last minute, or past their original deadline. Some people procrastinate because they are worried or they don't feel good about a task, so they avoid thinking about it instead, which leads them to keep being anxious for longer than was needed.

Example:

The common example of procrastination in students is putting off assignments and projects until the deadline. This is known as academic procrastination.
