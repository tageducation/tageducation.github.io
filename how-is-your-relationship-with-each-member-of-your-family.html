26. 5. How can your relationship influence each member of thefamily​


Your relationship with a spouse/partner influences all members of your family, but most obviously so for children and grandchildren. Children (and grandchildren) will likely have an easier time adjusting to their new environment if there was some sort of history behind the change. For example, if you were married before moving to another state, it might be hard for you as an adult to move away again; however, it may be much less difficult if the reason you moved was due to marriage rather than divorce or death. Your current relationships can help ease adjustment as well. If your son has recently had trouble at school, he may feel more comfortable staying home this summer if his parents are nearby. In contrast, if your daughter-in-law just got her first job, she could possibly benefit from spending several weeks helping around the house.
