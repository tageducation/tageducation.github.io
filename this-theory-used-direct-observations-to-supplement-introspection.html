1. This theory used direct observations to supplement introspection


Introspection is a research method that is primarily patterned on Socrates' advice to "know thyself" which has remained a motto of psychology. In connection to this, a theory developed by William James to supplement introspection by using direct observations. This theory is known as Functionalism, that also focuses on behavior in addition to mind and consciousness. Furthermore, it is influenced by Darwin's theory of evolution.

2. A psychologist who initiated the idea of functionalism and focused on direct observations to supplement introspection


A psychologist who initiated the idea of functionalism and focused on direct observations to supplement introspection is William James.

Explanation :

William James was a philosopher from the United States who became one of the pioneers of pragmatism. Apart from being a philosopher, James is a psychologist and philosopher who became one of the pioneers of educational psychology.

One of the great contributions of William James is to spark an idea or ideas of functionalism which focuses on direct observation in the completion of introspection. In its description this theory emphasizes how a mental activity can help individuals to adapt in their environment.

In its application the functionalist idea seeks to explain mental processes in a more systematic and accurate way. Rather than having to focus on the elements of consciousness, functionalists focus on the goals of consciousness and behavior. Functionalism also emphasizes individual differences that have a significant impact on education.
