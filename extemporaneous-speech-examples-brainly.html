There are different types of speech and are classified depending on the surrounding circumstances. The types of speech include impromptu speech, manuscript speech as well as extemporaneous speech Kim (2017). An impromptu speech is delivered by a person without prior preparations for example when one is asked to deliver a speech at a friend's birthday party. Manuscript speech is the type of speech whereby the speech is read to an audience from a paper script as it is on the paper without changing anything. 30. What is an extemporaneous speech?


Extemporaneous speech are actually prepared and rehearsed speech that appears to be off the cuff and therefore spontaneous.  It cannot be equated with impromptu speech as speaking off the cuff with little or no preparation.  Seasonal speakers usually deliver their speeches extemporaneously with a natural style of conversation with the audience.  Although extemporaneous speaking may not require memorization and manuscript speaking, organizing and preparing the content and notes ahead of time contributes to better, spontaneous output.
