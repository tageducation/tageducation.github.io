16. 1. A girl flips a coin into a 50m deep wishing well. If she flips the coin upwards with an initial velocity of 5 m/s: a. How high does the coin rise? b. How long does it take to get to this point? c. How long does it take for the coin to reach the bottom of the well? d. What is the velocity when the coin hits the bottom of the well?


Answer:

a. The height created by flipping the coin is 1.28 m

b. The time for the coin to get a max. height is 0.51 seconds

c. The time for the coin to reach the bottom of the well is 4.77 seconds

d. The velocity when the coin hits the bottom of the well is

- 41.69 m/s

Explanation:

Here, we have a projectile motion problem. Let us assume that the hand of the girl in flipping the coin was at the mouth of the well.

For the given data:

V₀ = 5 m/s

y₀ = 50 m

Solving the problem

a. To solve for the height a coin can reach from flipping, let us use the formula

V² = V₀² - 2gy

0 = 5² - 2(9.8)y

Take note that the final velocity, V is zero at maximum height.

Transposing 2(9.8)y to the left side of the equation

2(9.8)y = 5²

Divide both sides of the equation by 2(9.8)

y = 5² / 2(9.8) = 1.28 m

b. Now, let us solve for the time for the coin to reach a maximum height using the formula

V = V₀ - gt

0 = 5 - (9.8) t

Transposing (9.8)t to the left side of the equation

(9.8) t = 5

Divide both sides of the equation by 9.8

t = 5/9.8 = 0.51 s

c. Now, let us solve for the time for the coin to reach the bottom of the well using the formula

y = y₀ + V₀t - ¹/₂gt²

Take note that this formula refers to getting the height at any given time

Let us substitute the given data

0 = 50 + 5t - ¹/₂(9.8)t²

0 = 50 + 5t - 4.9t² or

4.9t² - 5t - 50 = 0

Let us use quadratic formula to solve for the time.

[tex]t=\frac{5+\sqrt{5^2+4(4.9)(50)} }{2(4.9)}[/tex]

t = 3.75 seconds

This refer to the time where the coin falls from the mouth of the well to the bottom.

Now, let us include the time in flipping the coin to solve for the total time. for the coin to reach the bottom of the well.

Total time = 2(0.51 s) + 3.75 s = 4.77 seconds

Here, we multiply 0.51 s to 2 because the time for the coin to go up is the same time in going down.

d. Finally, let us solve for the final velocity for the coin to reach the bottom of the well. Here, we will use the total time and initial velocity as inputs to the formula

V = V₀ - gt

V = 5 - (9.8)(4.77 s)

V = - 41.69 m/s
