14. What is the result of positive motivation?


Positive motivation is a rewards-based encouragement that is intended to boost the performance of other people. The results of positive motivation include the following:

It can increase the self-confidence or self-esteem of other people. It can provide encouragement to other people to achieve their goals and objectives. It can push other people to perform better. It can make people rely too much on rewards and bonuses. What is Positive Motivation? Positive motivation refers to the motivation that can bring pleasure to other people, and can attend to their needs. Hence, positive motivation is often defined as a rewards-based encouragement. What are the Results of Positive Motivation?

Because of positive motivation, the following results can come out:

Positive motivation can increase the self-confidence or self-esteem of other people. Motivating a person can make him or her feel that she can do it. Positive motivation can provide encouragement to other people to achieve their goals and objectives. Giving people rewards and bonuses encourages people to achieve their targets so that they can reap the rewards. Positive motivation can push other people to perform better. With rewards at stake, people increase their energy, do their best, and perform better than before. Positive motivation can make people rely too much on rewards and bonuses. Because of positive motivation, some people are discouraged to do something that has no rewards or bonuses at the end. Types of Positive Motivation

Positive motivation can be classified into two:

Internal Positive Motivation - This comes from one's self: talents, abilities, skills, passion, self-validation, etc. This can result to a sense of fulfillment, more energy, and satisfaction. External Positive Motivation - This comes from outside, such as monetary rewards, recognition, trophies, etc. This can result to stress, anxiety, etc.
