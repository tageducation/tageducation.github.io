Teachers and students have identifiable styles of talking to each other that linguists call a register. A register is a pattern of vocabulary, grammar, and expressions or comments that people associate with a social role. A familiar example is the “baby-talk” register often used to speak to an infant. Its features—simple repeated words and nonsense syllables, and exaggerated changes in pitch—mark the speaker as an adult and mark the listener as an infant.

CONSULTATIVE SPEECH STYLE

The third level of language. It is basically unplanned speech since the speaker uses the participation and feedback of the listener. The speaker will supply background information, while again, the listener participates continuously.It is formal and societal expectations accompany the users of this speech. It is a professional discourse.Examples: communication between a superior and a subordinate, doctor and patient, lawyer and client, lawyer and judge, teacher and student, counsellor and client.

FORMAL SPEECH STYLE

This type uses formal words and expressions and is mostly seen in writing rather than speaking. It also disallows the use of ellipsis, contractions, and qualifying modal adverbials.Used only for imparting the information. Straightforward and direct. This style does not allow interruptions.This language is used in formal settings and is one-way in nature.This use of language usually follows a commonly accepted format. It is usually impersonal and formal. Examples: sermons, rhetorical statements and questions, speeches, pronouncements made by judges, announcements.

30. Communicative styles frozen formal consultative casual intimate


A person's communication strategy in a specific circumstance is referred to as their communicative style. In light of the fact that people frequently employ several styles depending on the circumstance, this is not necessarily a sign of personality or character. Some communication styles consist of:

1. Frozen

Depending on the formality levels desired for an audience and an objective, speaking styles change. The most formal is referred to as the frozen style, which makes use of a somber demeanor and intricate words. Not necessarily a sign of personality or character, as people frequently adopt different coping mechanisms depending on the circumstance.

2. Formal

An official exchange of information is often referred to as formal communication. Whatever your position within an organization, learning formal communication techniques and recognizing formal communication will help you communicate more effectively with coworkers and management.

3. Consultative

Open-ended inquiries are used in consultative communication to elicit all viewpoints, uncover underlying problems, and expose personal motivations. The group's leader invites input from the followers and demonstrates their willingness to do so before making decisions.

4. Casual

When speaking informally, the speaker is relaxed and at ease. The speaker's message is what the listener is focused on, not how they communicate it. The informal, direct, and timely nature of casual speech is another way to describe it. It frequently ignores established communication norms.

5. Intimate

It implies that you discuss topics that are truly important. It indicates that your spouse has the same level of trust in you and that you are not scared to express your true thoughts and feelings. Here are some suggestions for improving your interaction and connection.
