About Headlines

Headlines should be clear and specific, telling the reader what the story is about, and be interesting enough to draw them into reading the article. Avoid repetition - Headlines summarize; they don't repeat the lede. They Win Pennant!

How to Write a News Article: Headlines

•5-10 words at the most

•should be accurate and specific

•Use present tense and active verbs, but don't start with a verb

•Use infinitive form of verb for future actions

•Do not use articles - a, an, the

•Do not use conjunctions like and - you can substitute a comma

24. tell whether a journalistic article is a news article sport news article feature article and an opinion article.​


Answer:

A “news article” and a “sports article” are similar, in that they tend to report activities-events that worthy (in some way) of interest and writing about.

A “feature article” is about a topic that editors think warrant more in-depth coverage. The tend to require a long period of research and tend to be longer.

An “opinion article” reports “facts,” but the writing is slanted toward what the facts mean and what the implications are, as well as, the position of the writer or the editorial board. This type of writing is often intended to be controversial and disputable.
