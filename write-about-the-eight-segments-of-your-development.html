Eight Segments of Human Development:

contextual selfemotional selfintellectual selfinteractional selfnutritional selfphysical selfsensual selfspiritual self or life force

Contextual self refers to the segment of human development that explains man's relationship with his environment including his response to colors, light, seasons, sound, space, temperature, weather, and his impact on this environment.

Emotional self refers to the segment of human development that explains man's feelings including those that he wishes to avoid, feelings he enjoys, his past and present feelings, and interrelated feelings.

Intellectual self refers to the segment of human development that explains man's assessment of how well his reasoning are and solving problem skills, his capacity to create and learn, his overall knowledge, wisdom he had acquired, and his insights.

Interactional self refers to the segment of human development that explains man's description of his strengths and weaknesses with regards to intimate relationships and relationship towards classmates, family, friends, and strangers in the community.

Nutritional self refers to the segment of human development that explains man's way of nourishing himself, foods he likes and dislikes, and the reasons for disliking them.

Physical self refers to the segment of human development that explains man's description of himself including his facial appearance, height, quality of skin and hair, weight, and description of body parts such as chest, legs, neck, and waist.

Sensual self refers to the segment of human development that explains man's feelings as a sensual person and the manner by which he allows information to go in and out of his body.

Spiritual self or life force refers to the segment of human development that explains man's concept of religion and faith including his spiritual connection with others.
