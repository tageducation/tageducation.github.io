Area Of the Sector of the Circle

The area of a sector of a circle is the total space enclosed within the boundary of the sector. A sector always begins from the center of the circle. The sector of a circle is defined as the part of the circle that is enclosed between its two radii and the arc adjoining them. The semi-circle is the most common sector of a circle, which represents half a circle. Consider the examples below.

1. Asked: The area of the shaded region.

  Given: r = 9 in

  Operation: multiplication

  Number Sentence:  Area of a Sector of Circle = (θ/360º) × πr2  

  Solution: Area of a Sector of Circle = (θ/360º)(πr²)

                  Area of a Sector of Circle = [tex]\frac{120}{360}[/tex] (3.14)(9 in)²  

                  Area of a Sector of Circle =  [tex]\frac{120}{360}[/tex] (3.14)(81 in²)

2. Asked: The area of the shaded region.

  Given: r = 2 in

              m∠GHJ = 90°

              π = 3.14

  Operation: multiplication

  Number Sentence:  Area of a Sector of Circle = (θ/360º) × πr2

                                   Area of the Right Triangle = [tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex] length x width

  Solution: Area of a Sector of Circle = (θ/360º)(πr²)

                  Area of a Sector of Circle = [tex]\frac{90}{360}[/tex] (3.14)(2 in)²  

                  Area of a Sector of Circle =  [tex]\frac{90}{360}[/tex] (3.14)(4 in²)

                  Area of a Sector of a Circle = (.25)(3.14)(4 in²  

                 Area of a Sector of a Circle = 3.14 in²

 Area of the Right Triangle = [tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex] length x width

Area of the Right Triangle = [tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex] (2 in) x (2 in)

Area of the Right Triangle = [tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex] (4 in²)

Area of the Right Triangle = [tex]\frac{4}{2}[/tex] in²

Area of the Right Triangle = 2 in²

Area of the Shaded Region = Area of a Sector of a Circle - Area of the Right Triangle

Area of the Shaded Region = 3.14 in² - 2 in²
