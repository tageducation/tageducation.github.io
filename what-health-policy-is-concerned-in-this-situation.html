1. What health policy/policies is/are concerned in each situation?


In the continuously evolving climate of laws in the Philippines, particularly those that affect the health sector, it is imperative that students know where or how to stand to certain issues and policies. There are four (4) situations and we will answer what health policy/policies is/are concerned in each situation.

A factory worker voluntarily underwent HIV testing. The result shows that the worker is positive with the infection. He / She reported about it to the administration. He / She is now hearing discriminatory remarks from the co-workers. Apparently, the administrator spread the news of his condition to the workers.

On the first situation, the health policies concerned are the Republic Act 8504 or the Philippine AIDS Prevention and Control Act of 1998, which promulgates policies and measures to prevent and contain HIV/AIDS in the country.  To know more about AIDS and HIV, you may click on this link: https://brainly.ph/question/260276.

A couple is getting married in a few months. They are required to attend seminars on family planning.

On the second, the primary health policy is the newly-passed Republic Act 10354 or the Responsible Parenthood and Reproductive Health Act. To know more about this policy, click here: https://brainly.ph/question/198126.

Upon scouting the area, neighbor A identifies a certain plant as a source of an addictive substance. The area is owned by a rich businessman in the city.

For the third situation, Republic Act 9165 or the Comprehensive Dangerous Drugs Act of 2002, which protects the public from harmful effects of dangerous drugs to their physical and mental well-being. For more insights regarding this law, click here: https://brainly.ph/question/1000295.

An old person consults a doctor and is advised to undergo alternative health care. The old person is apprehensive and doubtful of the health care option.

For the last situation, the health policy concerned is the Traditional and Alternative Medicines Act or the Republic Act 8423 that provides for the improvement of quality and delivery of healthcare services in the Philippines through the development of traditional and alternative healthcare.

Apart from the aforementioned policies, here is an initial list of health policies that are also essential in the evolving health climate in the country:

Consumer Act Tobacco Regulations Act Cybercrime Prevention Act Anti-Child Pornography Act National Blood Services Act Anti-Hazing Law National Environmental Awareness and Education Act Seat Belts Use Act Anti-Drunk and Drugged Driving Law

These laws are the ones that comprise what national health policy the Philippines has. And from the situations given above, you might have the idea of what is the importance of health policy. Apart from ensuring health and welfare of the whole citizenry, these policies also aim to prevent injury, death, and inconvenience to others.

Based on the situation given, the factory administrator violated the right to confidentiality of their worker by spreading his HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) – test result to the entire workplace. The Philippine HIV and AIDS Policy Act. The employer did not fulfill his duties to protect the welfare of their employee.

Explanation:

Duty of the Employer according to Law

Section 48, Article VI of the Philippine HIV and AIDS Policy Act declares that ”it shall be the duty of private employers to prevent of deter acts of discrimination against persons living with HIV and to provide procedures for the resolution, settlement, or prosecution of acts of discrimination.”

The worker was discriminated because of his perceived HIV status

Penalties under the Law

The worker may file a case against his employer based on the following provisions of the law:

Any person who shall violate provisions on discriminatory acts and practices shall, upon conviction, duffer the following penalty: Imprisonment of 6 months to 5 years, and/or A fine of not less than P50,000.00, but not more than P500,000.00, and Imposition of administrative sanctions

Any person who has obtained knowledge of confidential HIV and AIDS information and uses such information to malign or cause damage, injury, or loss to another person shall face liabilities under  The Civil Code of the Philippines and the  Data Privacy Act
