✏️RIGHT ANGLE==============================

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{Question:}} [/tex] What is tan β if cos β = 3/4?

√7/3 √7/4 (4√7)/7 (3√7)/7

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{Answer:}} \: \: \LARGE \tt \green{\frac{\sqrt{7}}{3}} [/tex]

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{Reason:}} [/tex] Let's make a right triangle out of the given. If the cosine of beta is 3/4. Thus, the the length of its adjacent is 3 units and the measure of the hypotenuse is 4 units since the equal ratio of cosine is adjacent side over hypotenuse.

[tex] \sf cos \: \beta = \frac{3}{4} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf cos \: \beta = \frac{adjacent}{hypotenuse} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases} \sf 3 = Adjacent (leg) \\ \sf 4 = Hypotenuse \end{cases} [/tex]

» Before we will find the tangent of beta, let's look for the opposite side of beta since the ratio of tangent requires the opposite side. We will be using the Pythagorean Theorem to identify the side.

[tex] \sf a^2 + 3^2 = 4^2 [/tex]

[tex] \sf a^2 + 9 = 16 [/tex]

[tex] \sf a^2 = 16 - 9 [/tex]

[tex] \sf a^2 = 7 [/tex]

[tex] \sf \sqrt{a^2} = \sqrt{7} [/tex]

[tex] \sf a = \sqrt{7} [/tex]

» The opposite side of beta measures [tex] \sf \sqrt{7} [/tex] units. Now we could find the value of tan β using the tangent ratio.

[tex] \sf tan \: \beta = \frac{opposite}{adjacent} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf tan \: \beta = \frac{\sqrt{7}}{3} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \large \therefore \underline{\boxed{\tt \purple{tan \: \beta = \frac{\sqrt{7}}{3}}}} [/tex]
