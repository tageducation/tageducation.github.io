9. ___ is a long narrative poem, telling heroic deeds or events.


A long narrative poem about the deeds of a hero is called an epic. The word epic is derived from the Latin word epicus meaning word, story, or poem.  

An epic is a long narrative poem usually telling how an extraordinary man or woman journeyed to deal with supernatural, like gods and goddesses or other superhuman forces.  

Even before writers like Dante, Milton, and Virgil wrote their epics, such form of poetry has already started even before the forms of writing were created. The rhythmic patterns and measures made it easier for the people then to memorize the story and pass it on to the next generation.  

The most famous epic poem and considered as the oldest is the Epic of Gilgamesh, which was recorded in the ancient Sumer during the rise of the Neo-Sumerian Empire. This is a heroic poem example as it details the journey of Gilgamesh, king of Uruk. He is a legendary and mythical figure in this epic.  

Meanwhile, the longest epic written is the Mahabharata, which is of ancient Indian origin.  

The following would be the epic poem characteristics:

1. There is no exposition; the story begins at the middle of the plot (medias res)  

2. The setting could cover many places, nations, or even worlds or universes.

3. Opens with the invocation of an inspirational goddess (muse)  

4. Opens by stating the theme.

5. Uses epithets (a term in place of a name)

6. Includes an epic catalogue or a long list of items, places, people, etc.  

7. Uses long and formal speeches.

8. Includes divine intervention on human affairs.

9. The heroes would be a reflection of the values of the civilization.

10. Often times, there would be the tragic descent of the hero into the underworld or hell.
