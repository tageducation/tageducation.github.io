17. 1.all information from the internet are reliable.2.giving credit to the owner of the copied work is optional.3.all the information can be found on the internet.whats your Facts, Opinion, and Reason?​


Answer:

1. Opinion

2. Opinion

3. Opinion

Explanation:

FACTS OR OPINION?

1. Statement: “All information from the internet is reliable.”

Opinion

Reason: There is no way to guarantee the quality of material obtained on the Internet because anyone can publish anything. Beyond a basic knowledge of Web page development and access to a hosting computer, there are no real restrictions or editing processes for posting information on the Web. Anyone can publish an opinion, satire, a hoax, or simply fake data. Not every website is the same. They differ in terms of quality, intent, and bias.

2. Statement: “Giving credit to the owner of the copied work is optional.”

Opinion

Reason: It is not optional; credit must always be given to the proper owner. Crediting the sources you utilize not only establishes the validity of your study and increases your credibility but also helps you avoid plagiarism. It's critical to understand that plagiarism is academic dishonesty regardless of whether you planned to plagiarize or the plagiarism occurred unintentionally. Remember that plagiarism is the same as copyright in the Philippines, and it might be prosecuted under Republic Act 10175's cybercrime law, even if plagiarism is not a crime, according to the Department of Justice. Even still, copyright infringement is the same.

3. Statement: “All information can be found on the internet.”

Opinion

Reason: Maybe MOST information is found on the Internet by utilizing search engines, but not ALL. There is still a fraction of information that people are yet to discover and put on the internet.

Notes:Facts provide information about the world. They rely on observation or investigation, and in most cases, empirical data and information are used. Opinions reflect someone's views, beliefs, personal perspectives, or values and are founded on assumptions that cannot be proven. It reveals what people believe about the world.
