The process of finding fair market value:

Under the asset approach method, fair market value (FMV) is calculated by calculating the adjusted assets and liabilities held by the company. It takes into account intangible assets, off-balance sheet assets, and unrecorded liabilities.

Explanation:

Fair market value of Securities is the value that can be obtained from Securities transactions conducted between free Parties, not due to coercion or liquidation. Determination of the fair market value of securities actively traded on the Stock Exchange using information on the last trading price of securities on the Stock Exchange. In the event that the last trading price on the Stock Exchange does not reflect the fair market value of the securities at that time, the investment manager is required to determine the fair market value of the securities based on the method specified, in the collective investment contract and prospectus by considering:

Government Securities using price information that issued by a Government Securities trading operator outside the Stock Exchange. Corporate bonds use the price information available in the system.

Determination of the fair market value of securities traded on foreign Stock Exchanges using price information from reliable sources that can be accessed through mass media or available internet facilities.Determination of the fair market value of securities from companies that are declared bankrupt or likely to be bankrupt or fail to pay the principal or interest of the securities, must be calculated based on good faith and full responsibility of the investment manager using conservative principles and applied consistently.

4. The difference between the fair value of the shares to be subscribed and the price required to be paid for those shares is the market value


False, the difference between the fair value of the shares to be subscribed and the price required to be paid for those shares is the intrinstic value.

Explanation:

Market value is the price an asset fetches in the market and is commonly used to refer to market capitalization. Market values are dynamic in nature because they depend on an assortment of factors, from physical operating conditions to economic climate to the dynamics of demand and supply. Market value is calculated by multiplying a company's outstanding shares by its current market price.

While the intrinsic value is a measure of what an asset is worth. This measure is arrived at by means of an objective calculation or complex financial model. Intrinsic value is different from the current market price of an asset. Essentially, the model seeks to find the intrinsic value of the stock by adding its current per-share book value with its discounted residual income.
