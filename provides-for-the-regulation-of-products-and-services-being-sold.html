20. Provides for the regulation of narcotic and other related substances being sold


Answer: Philippine Drug Enforcement Agency (PDEA)

Explanation:

In the Philippines, the regulation of narcotics and other related substances being sold is the responsibility of the Philippine Drug Enforcement Agency (PDEA), which is the primary government agency responsible for enforcing the country's drug laws and policies.

The PDEA is tasked with regulating the production, distribution, and sale of controlled substances, including narcotics and other related substances. It works to enforce the country's Comprehensive Dangerous Drugs Act of 2002, which establishes the rules and regulations for the handling of controlled substances.

The PDEA carries out its regulatory role in several ways. For example, it sets limits on the number of controlled substances that can be produced and sold, and it monitors the production and distribution of these substances to ensure compliance with the law. The PDEA also conducts investigations and enforcement actions against individuals and organizations involved in the illegal sale of controlled substances.

Overall, the Philippine Drug Enforcement Agency is responsible for the regulation of narcotics and other related substances being sold in the Philippines. This regulation helps to protect public health and safety and prevent the illegal sale of these substances.

24. This act provides for the regulation of products and services being sold and offered in the country


Regulation of The Products and Services Sold

Provides for the regulation of products and services being sold and offered in the country is consumer act. The Philippines' Consumer Act establishes the rights of consumers and the duties of sellers, producers, retailers, distributors, and manufacturers in order to safeguard consumers from unfair trade practices. Nevertheless, these are not the only sources of consumer rights. In order to protect them for the goods and services they purchase, consumers need regulations on goods and services offered and sold domestically. The nation requires various businesses to outline product features and risks and to give consumers comprehensive information about products that consumers may purchase.

Regulatory measures of products and services sold and offered:

1. The government makes sure that a product is registered and has a license from the food and drugs administration before it is allowed to be sold in a particular nation. The Food and Drug Administration is in charge of product quality and safety licensing and registration.

2. The manufacturer of the goods and services sold should obtain the necessary manufacturing licenses and follow them in order to assure the quality and safety of the goods and services. To ensure that the organization is legitimate, the criteria must be met by the business providing goods and services to the public. To guarantee that the general public is handled with safe food handling procedures, the business and its surroundings must be inspected.
