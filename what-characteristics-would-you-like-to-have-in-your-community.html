In general, the characteristics of a good civil society are independent living, have a high sense of tolerance, actively participate in all public policy formation, cooperate voluntarily, uphold the values of justice and honesty, recognize and respect differences, have national integrity. strong, upholding human rights and the rule of law as well as being open and transparent.

In addition, it requires the support of socio-political organizations as a forum for interest groups with high independence, it also requires a public sphere or adequate space for the people to have access to state administrative institutions, judicial institutions and representatives or NGOs.

Some characteristics of good civil community:

People's participation.

People in a civil society are not fully dependent on the state, but they strive to improve the quality of life and themselves independently.

Autonomous.

Civil society or civil society is defined as a society that seeks to fulfill its own needs, always develops the power of creativity to obtain happiness and fulfill the demands of living freely and independently, while still referring to the applicable laws and regulations.

Not value free.

Civil society highly upholds human values ​​so that the things they do are always on the path of virtue and produce a positive impact on themselves (society) in general.

Accepting and respecting each other.

Upholding mutual respect, respect, and acceptance of all forms of differences so that in the social peace that is built, the beauty of diversity can be seen that enriches culture and becomes a positive added value.

Non-discriminatory

Civil society must put problems above differences so that there are no clashes between groups that smell like racist and discrimination. It is manifested in a neat and modern organizational body in an effort to create stable relationships between elements of society.
