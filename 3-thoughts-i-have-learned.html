Culture signifies the different beliefs, traditions, and practices, and Society represents the people whos are practicing and following these cultures. These myriads for the aspects play a huge role in who and what we are right now. Below are the three things that I learned from these subjects.

First, I learned that we are all separated and classified by our own beliefs and practices. However, these differences are not a hindrance to unite and embrace of discrepancies that we have. These should be not a measurement of how we should treat and approach other people differently from what we are used to.

Second, I learned that these differences and the contrasts we have in our cultures and Society are also helpful and efficient. Why? Because of this, we all can know and understand each other's differences by trying to concede them, and we are to interpret these myriads by being open and welcoming. It helps in a way that because of these, we have a world that is large, deep, and comprehensive to explore. We can learn many things and adapt them too.

Third, I realized that there is no differentiation in these cultures and Societies; literally, yes, it has, physical features, language, traditions, myths, ideas, and environmental aspects. Still, I am focusing on explaining that these differences are just a mere visible boundary. Once we understand and respect others, there should not be a difference anymore, and it is not a basis of humankind. The important here is that we should always accept, respect, and learn from this.

Remember, physical aspects and outer characters should not be the basis of humanity. Always appreciate these differences and variations. Loving and valuing diversity is a great way to accept this, and there is no room for biases and inequalities here.
