2. How does topography affect climate?


Topography as defined by most books is the term used to describe a detailed study of the Earth’s surface. Topography is ever changing as it includes the mountains and valleys as well as the rivers and roads. But with the ever changing topography of this planet we live in, how does topography affect climate itself?

It actually plays a huge factor on a certain country’s climate. Technically, it affects the climate depending on the area’s location. If you need to visualize it, imagine where the equator line lies, any country that is far below or far above the equator line experiences cold climate while any country which is near the equator has tropical or warm climate.  

A good example is when humid air comes from the sea, it flows up going to the mountains. When collected, it comes down or condenses as water vapor. The fall in the high altitude is how we experience precipitation or rain. This may be familiarized as the water cycle with the touch of topography to be certain. (https://brainly.ph/question/86521)

16. why does topography affect climate?​


Answer:

The effects of topography on the climate of any given region are powerful. Mountain ranges create barriers that alter wind and precipitation patterns. Topographical features such as narrow canyons channel and amplify winds. Mountains and plateaus are exposed to the cooler temperatures of higher altitudes. The orientation of mountains to the sun creates distinct microclimates in areas such as the Alps, where entire villages remain in the shade for most of the winter season.
