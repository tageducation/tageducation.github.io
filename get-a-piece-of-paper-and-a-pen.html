9. 1. Get a piece of paper and a pen. 2. Doodle for a minute without thinking. Just feel free to do it in whatever direction. 3. After a minute, try to draw a square, rectangle, circle, and triangle at the back of the paper. Processing Questions 1. How do you compare the two activities? 2. What did you feel when you were doodling and drawing figures? 3. Between the two activities, what needs certain control or discipline? Why do you think so?​


The following displays the questions after the subject is asked to do two activities:

1. Make a doodle free and without any direction

2. Draw square, rectangle, circle, and triangle shapes

Explanation:

1. There are two very different activities, where in the first task we are given a freedom of expression and imagination in the form of a doodle painting, we are not asked to think or follow a concept. While in the second task we have to follow an order to describe several shapes such as, square, rectangle, circle, triangle which of course this shape has special rules in its depiction.

2. When I was doodling, I feel like have no pressure but also confused at the same time. Having the freedom to express images to someone who has a high level of imagination and creativity will be very easy, but for people who have limitations in terms of imagination this will be very confusing and actually produce something that is not clear and meaningless.

When I draw a predetermined figures, there is a feeling of wanting to produce a perfect and neatly arranged result according to the original shape. This is because the drawn figures (shapes) such as circles, squares, etc have rules that have been ordered.

3. Between the two activities, the second activity (drawing figures) is the one that requires control and discipline. In this activity, we have to follow the instructions that have been directed, no matter how much someone have an imagination has on the art things, one must remain disciplined and follow the requests given, namely drawing squares, rectangles, circles, and triangles according to their shape or additional improvisation. While the first activity requires a very high creative and imaginative nature.
