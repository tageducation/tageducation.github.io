9. What are the similarities and differences of every individual?


The similarities of individuals far outweigh the differences as long as we do not dwell on every minute detail of our diversities. If we look at the overall broad similarities of individuals, are we all not seeking "Life, Liberty and the pursuit of Happiness"? It is only when we categorize these three staples of existence that we begin to believe we are different from one another.
We all want to have a life. One individual may want to have a simple life with few burdens due to social achievement or possession of products while another individual may want to achieve great social status and accumulate as many possessions as they can. Depending on how we view these two individuals, they are either very similar or very different. If we perceive these two individuals as being similar, there is nothing we would do to try to change the lives of either. If we view these individuals as being different, each individual would want to make laws preventing the other from being able to live their life the way they want to. This falls under the ideology of fascism; one person feeling they have some sort of right to dictate how another lives their life.
We all want the liberty to be able to do what we believe we should have the right to do. This one gets more complicated because there are many things that an individual may want to do that may adversely affect what other individuals want to do. Obviously, in a civilized society, no one should have the liberty to enact a direct crime on another. For an example we can use a current hot topic. One individual believes everyone should have the liberty to own firearms as long as they are used responsibly and never used to enact a crime. Another individual believes that they should have the liberty to make firearm ownership illegal based on the assumption that a few will use the firearms in an illegal manner. If we view these individuals as being the same in their desire for liberty, there is nothing we would do to change either of their lives. If we view these individuals as being different because of the diversity in their beliefs, then we must create adversarial laws attempting to appease each of the differences based on the belief of the one individual who believes they should have the liberty to take away the liberty of another. Again, we are now wading into fascist waters,
We all want to be able to pursue happiness for us as individuals. Happiness is a very subjective issue.
