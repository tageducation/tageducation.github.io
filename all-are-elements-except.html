25. all of the following are elements of narrative writing except?​


Narrative Writing

There are five simple yet crucial components of narrative writing. Character, setting, plot, conflict, and resolution are the five main parts of a story. These crucial components ensure that the narrative flows smoothly and that the reader can follow the action as it develops.

1. Characters

The people that the tale is about are the characters. The reader should be able to imagine each character by the time they are introduced by the author. This is accomplished by giving in-depth explanations of a character's physical characteristics and character traits. There should be a primary character in every story. The plot's progression is decided by the main character, who also typically finds a solution to the story's central issue. The other characters, however, are also crucial because they give other information, justifications, or actions. Throughout the novel, all characters should remain consistent to the author's descriptions so that the reader can comprehend what is happening, believe what is happening, and possibly even predict which character will do what next.

2. Setting

The action takes place in a setting. The setting or surrounds of the story should be described in enough detail for the reader to feel as though they can visualize the scene. Everyday settings can help a reader better visualize the story and feel connected to the plot, compared to unusual settings (such as a fantasy world).

3. Plot

The real story on which the entire book is built is called the plot. A storyline should have a very distinct beginning, middle, and end along with all the required exposition descriptions and suspense so that the reader can understand the action and follow it from beginning to end.

4. Conflict

There is a conflict to be resolved in every tale. This conflict and the protagonists' efforts to settle it serve as the plot's focal points. The climax of a story occurs just before its resolution and is when the action is at its most thrilling.

5. Resolution

The way the activity is resolved is the problem's answer. For instance, Katie frequently settles disputes by working out a solution for two opposing characters or by correcting any errors she made while pretending to be someone else. The resolution must resolve all conflict-related issues and match the tone and originality of the rest of the story.
