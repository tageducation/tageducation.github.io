13. 1. In this lesson, I learned that skimming is ___________________. 2. I learned that scanning is ____________________________________. 3. I learned that extensive reading is _________________________________.


Answer:

1.) Skimming is defined as advantageous to preview a more comprehensive and detailed lesson or when reviewing a selection heavy in content. Doing skimming can save you hours of arduous reading wherein all you have to do is prepare yourself to hurry throughout the sheets; in this case, you will not read every word of the context. However, the downside of skimming is that you may miss and skip many essential details and points and overlook the more nuanced shadings of meaning. Prompt reading or perhaps even study lessons may be necessary. That's why it is also not an appropriate way to read.

2.) Scanning is described as the technique of using keywords and organizational cues only. If the goal of skimming is a bird's-eye view of the context, the purpose of Scanning is to determine and swoop down on selective data within long text sections with approximately little to do with your topic or claim.

3.) Extensive reading is essential in our daily lives. In this way, we can avoid misunderstanding, confusion, and fake news all over the media and even with the papers we are reading. Practicing extensive reading can help us understand the idea and get reliable data or facts that we can use, especially if we are reading with the terms academic and business.

I have learned that Political Science is 4. I have learned that Demography is 5. I have learned that Geography is 6. I have learned that History is 12​


Answer:

Sociology and anthropology involve the systematic study of social life and culture in order to understand the causes and consequences of human action. Sociologists and anthropologists study the structure and processes of traditional cultures and modern, industrial societies in both Western and non-Western cultures. They examine how culture, social structures (groups, organizations and communities) and social institutions (family, education, religion, etc.) affect human attitudes, actions and life-chances.

Sociology and anthropology combine scientific and humanistic perspectives in the study of society. Drawing upon various theoretical perspectives, sociologists and anthropologists study areas such as culture, socialization, deviance, inequality, health and illness, family patterns, social change and race and ethnic relations. Combining theoretical perspectives with empirical research allows students an opportunity to develop new insights and a different perspective on their own lives. This combination also helps students to understand everyday social life as a blend of both stable patterns of interaction and ubiquitous sources of social change.

30. .1. In this lesson, I learned that skimming is2. I learned that scanning is3. I learned that extensive reading is​


Answer:

1. Skimming often refers to the way in which one reads at a faster rate to gain the general idea about the text without paying heed to the intentional and detailed meaning of the text. For Example - When one reads the text only in order to understand the thesis statement, in one or two lines.

2. Scanning is commonly used in everyday life, for example when looking up a word in a dictionary or finding your friend's name in the contacts directory of your phone. Scanning and another quick reading skill, skimming, are often confused, though they are quite different.

3. Possible examples of extensive reading material are magazines, graded readers, novels and, yes, even comic books!
