5. A sample of hydrogen at 47Â°C exerts a pressure of .329 atm. The gas is heated to 77Â°C at constant volume. What will its new pressure be?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

To convert the temperature from degree Celsius to kelvin, add 273 to the temperature expressed in degree Celsius.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_1 & = \text{0.329 atm} \\ T_1 & = 47^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{320 K} \\ T_2 & = 77^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{350 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final pressure by using Gay-Lussac's law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \frac{P_1}{T_1} & = \frac{P_2}{T_2} \\ P_2T_1 & = P_1T_2 \\ \frac{P_2T_1}{T_1} & = \frac{P_1T_2}{T_1} \\ P_2 & = \frac{P_1T_2}{T_1} \\ & = \frac{(\text{0.329 atm})(\text{350 K})}{\text{320 K}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{0.360 atm}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the new pressure will be 0.360 atm.

8. Under constant-pressure conditions a sample of hydrogen gas initially at 88°C and 9.6 L is cooled until its final volume is 3.4 L. What is its final temperature?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

To convert the temperature from degree Celsius to kelvin, add 273 to the temperature expressed in degree Celsius.

[tex]\begin{aligned} V_1 & = \text{9.6 L} \\ T_1 & = 88^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{361 K} \\ V_2 & = \text{3.4 L} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final temperature (in degree Celsius) by using Charles' law.

To convert the temperature from kelvin to degree Celsius, subtract 273 from the temperature expressed in kelvin

[tex]\begin{aligned} \frac{V_1}{T_1} & = \frac{V_2}{T_2} \\ T_2V_1 & = T_1V_2 \\ \frac{T_2V_1}{V_1} & = \frac{T_1V_2}{V_1} \\ T_2 & = \frac{T_1V_2}{V_1} \\ & = \frac{(\text{361 K})(\text{3.4 L})}{\text{9.6 L}} \\ & = \text{128 K} \\ & = \boxed{-145^{\circ}\text{C}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the final temperature of the gas is -145°C.

13. A sample of hydrogen gas is placed in a 3.00 L closed container under a pressure of 2.45 atm. The same gas sample had a pressure of 4.21 atm when the temperature was increased to 35.7˚C. What was the initial temperature of the gas in ˚C? ​


GAY LUSSAC'S LAW

===========================================

[tex]\large\underline{\sf{Problem:}}[/tex]

A sample of hydrogen gas is placed in a 3.00 L closed container under a pressure of 2.45 atm. The same gas sample had a pressure of 4.21 atm when the temperature was increased to 35.7°C. What was the initial temperature of the gas in ℃?

===========================================

[tex]\large\underline{\sf{Answer:}}[/tex]

[tex] \qquad \qquad \large \boxed{ \bold{ \purple{\: \: -93.415439^{\circ}C \: \: }}} \\ [/tex]

===========================================

[tex]\large\underline{\sf{Solution:}}[/tex]

When a gas is trapped inside a container whose volume cannot change, the pressure is directly proportional to the Kelvin temperature.

Gay Lussac's Law

[tex]\qquad \qquad \sf \implies\boxed{ \bold{ \: \: \dfrac{\green{P_1}}{\purple{T_1}}=\dfrac{\red{P_2}}{\blue{T_2}} \: \: }} \: \: \bigstar \\[/tex]

where :

[tex]\qquad \sf \implies \: \green{P_1} = \green{Initial \: Pressure}[/tex]

[tex]\qquad \sf \implies \: \purple{T_1} = \purple{Initial \: Temperature}[/tex]

[tex]\qquad \sf \implies \: \red{P_2} = \red{Final \: Pressure}[/tex]

[tex]\qquad \sf \implies \: \blue{T_2} = \blue{Final \: Temperature} \\ [/tex]

Temperature must be in kelvins (K).

To convert Celsius to kelvins, add 273.15.

Given values :

[tex]\qquad \qquad \sf \implies \green{P_1} = \green{2.45 \: atm}[/tex]

[tex]\qquad \qquad \sf \implies \red{P_2} = \red{4.21 \: atm}[/tex]

[tex]\qquad \qquad \sf \implies \blue{T_2} = \blue{35.7^{\circ} C} = \blue{35.7} + \orange{273.15} = \blue{308.85 \: K} \\ [/tex]

Substitute the given values into the equation and solve for T₁ :

[tex]\qquad\implies \sf \dfrac{ \green{P_1}}{ \purple{T_1}}=\dfrac{ \red{P_2}}{ \blue{T_2}} \\[/tex]

[tex]\qquad\implies \sf \dfrac{ \green{2.45\:atm}}{ \purple{T_1}}=\dfrac{ \red{4.21\:atm}}{ \blue{308.85\:K}} \\[/tex]

[tex]\qquad\sf \implies \sf \purple{T_1}=\dfrac{ \green{2.45\:atm} \cdot \blue{308.85\:K}}{ \red{4.21\:atm}} \\[/tex]

[tex]\qquad\sf \implies \sf \purple{T_1}=\dfrac{ \green{756.6825\:K}}{ \red{4.21\:atm}} \\ [/tex]

[tex]\qquad\sf \implies \purple{T_1}= \pink{ \pmb{179.73...\:K }}\\ [/tex]

To convert kelvins to Celsius, subtract 273.15 :

[tex]\sf \implies \purple{ T_1}= \pink{179.73...\:K} - \orange{273.15\:K}= \bold\purple{-93.415439^{\circ}C} \\[/tex]

[tex]\qquad\sf \implies \bold\purple{T_1}= \underline{\bold {\purple{-93.415439^{\circ}C} }}\\[/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex] The previous initial temperature of the gas was -93.415439℃.

===========================================

15. Under constant pressure condition, a sample of hydrogen gas initially at 850C and 7.2 L is cooled until its final volume is 4.1L What is its final temperature?​


Charles LawV1/T1 = V2/T2

where in, V1 = first volume

T1 = first temperature

V2 = second volume

T2 = second temperature

Given: V1 = 7.2L

T1 = 358.15K

V2 = 4.1L

Note: Dealing with temperature from each of the Gas Law must always be in Kelvin.

°C → K

85°C + 273.15K = 358.15K

Unknown: T2 = ?

Formula: T2 = V2T1 / V1

Note: To get the formula, apply cross-multiplication, division and cancellation of like terms using the original formula of Charles Law.

V1/T1 = V2/T2 ← cross-multiply

V1T2 = V2T1 ← divide both sides by V1

V1T2 / V1 = V2T1 / V1 ← cancel like terms

T2 = V2T1 / V1 ← final formula for T2

Solution: T2 = V2T1 / V1

= (4.1L)(358.15K) / 7.2L

= 1468.415L•K / 7.2L

= 203.95K

Note: "L" is cancelled out, so the unit for temperature will remain which is "K".

Reminder: Cancellation of units is applicable when the other same unit is on the denominator.

Final Answer: T2 = 203.95K

       P = 128.353575 / 25.0

P ≈ 5.134 atm

Pressure is 5.134 atm

(Note: cancel the unit mol, K, and L what remain is the unit of pressure atm) Remember to used kelvin temperature every time your calculating temperature in gas law.

Hello!

A sample of 5.25 moles of hydrogen at 25.0°C occupies a volume of 25.0L. Under what pressure is this sample?

We have the following data:

v (volume) = 25 L

n (number of moles) = 5.25 moles

T (temperature) = 25 ºC (in Kelvin)

First let's convert the temperature on the Kelvin scale, let's see:

TK = TºC + 273.15

TK = 25 + 273.15

TK = 298.15

P (pressure) = ? (in atm)

R (gas constant) = 0.082 atm.L / mol.K

We apply the data above to the Clapeyron equation (gas equation), let's see:

[tex]P*V = n*R*T[/tex]

[tex]P*25 = 5.25*0.082*298.15[/tex]

[tex]25\:P = 128.353575[/tex]

[tex]P = \dfrac{128.353575}{25}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\boxed{P \approx 5.134\:atm}}\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]
