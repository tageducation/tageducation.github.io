1. who proposed the liberal theory of global citizenship​


Explanation:

The concept of global citizenship, or the idea that individuals have responsibilities and rights as members of the global community, has been developed and discussed by a number of scholars and thinkers over the years. However, one of the most well-known proponents of the liberal theory of global citizenship is Martha Nussbaum, an American philosopher and legal scholar who has written extensively on the topic.

In her work, Nussbaum argues that global citizenship should be grounded in a set of universal human capabilities, or the basic abilities and functions that all human beings should be able to fulfill in order to live a minimally decent life. These capabilities include, but are not limited to, the ability to think, to use reason, to communicate, to form and maintain relationships, and to participate in the political process.

Nussbaum contends that the realization of these capabilities is essential for the development of a just and inclusive global society, and that individuals have a responsibility to promote and defend the rights and capabilities of others. She also argues that states have a responsibility to protect and promote the capabilities of their citizens, and to respect the human rights of individuals within and beyond their borders.

Overall, Nussbaum's work has had a significant influence on the way that global citizenship is understood and theorized, and has helped to shape the way that individuals and societies think about their responsibilities to each other and to the global community.

Rights and Responsibilities

A global citizen is someone who sees themselves as part of an emerging sustainable world community, and whose actions support the values and practices of that community. Many people today identify with being global citizens as more and more aspects of their lives become globalized.

Being a global citizen does not mean that you have to give up the other citizenship identities you already have, e.g. your country citizenship, your allegiance to your local community, religious, or ethnic group. Being a global citizen just means that you have another layer of identity (with the planet as a whole) added on to who you are. And if you take that identity seriously, there are a new set of rights and responsibilities that come with it.
