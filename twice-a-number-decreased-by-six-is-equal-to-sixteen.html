1. twice a number decreased by six is equal to sixteen


The Mathematical phrase "twice a number decreased by six is equal to sixteen" translated to an algebraic equation is 2x - 6 = 16. The "x" represents the number and the "decreased by" means that you have to subtract six from twice the number.

Step-by-step explanation:

Finding The Unknown Number In An Algebraic Equation

The algebraic equation refers to the statement of equality of two algebraic expressions. They are connected by an equal sign. The algebraic expression is a combination of variables, constants and algebraic operations. The variables are the letters that represent the unknown values. Then, the constants are the fixed and known values.

Going back to the algebraic equation above, 2x - 6 = 16, let us now find the unknown number.

Solution:

2x - 6 = 16

2x = 16 + 6

2x = 22

2x/2 = 22/2

x = 11

The number is 11.

Checking:

Plug in the number in the algebraic equation to check if the answer is correct.

2x - 6 = 16

2 (11) - 6 = 16

22 - 6 = 16

16 = 16 ✔
