DEFINITION OF INTERACTIONAL SELF:

Interactional self is the descriptions of someone's strengths or advantages and weaknesses in intimate relationships. Besides, it's also about relationships to family, friends, society, and strangers, in social settings.

EXPLANATION:

THREE TYPES OF INTERACTIONAL SELF:

In every active interaction implies the existence of interpersonal communication, and vice versa, every interpersonal communication contains interaction. It's hard to separate the two. On that basis, Shaw (1976:10) distinguishes interaction into three types, namely verbal interaction, physical interaction, and emotional interaction.

Verbal interaction occurs when two or more people make contact with each other by means of articulation. The process occurs in the form of exchanging conversations with each other.Physical interaction that occurs when two or more people make contact by using body languages. For example facial expressions, body position, body movements, and eye contact.Emotional interaction occurs when individuals make contact with each other by expressing feelings. For example, shedding tears as a sign of sadness, emotion, or even too happy.
