22. You were provided with data showing the arrival time of the P and S-waves recorded from three seismic stations. Which of these can you possibly determine? a. the damage at the focus b. the distance to the earthquake c. the intensity of the earthquake d. the location of the epicenter


Answer:

After you were given the data from three seismic stations, what would be able to determine after looking at the arrival time of the P and S waves?  

The recorded data from the three stations will allow you to determine the location of the earthquake’s epicenter, so my answer would be letter d. This is done by using a compass and a single map that covers the three areas around where the seismographs were located. To get the data before you can use the compass, one has to take a look at what the seismograph provided.

Explanation:

The steps one will take after receiving the data from the three locations:

First, get your pencil and determine and mark the first vibration or the P-wave. This will appear as a quick spike recorded by the seismograph from one of the three locations provided. Then follow the vertically written seismograph line until you reach the second spike or major vibration and mark that as your S-wave. With this, since the seismic data has been divided into seconds, you can now write down how many seconds took place between the P and S-waves. These steps will be repeated in all three locations.

Now it is time to fill-in the Data table which will contain the following columns:

P-wave arrival time S-wave arrival time Lag time (S-wave - Pwave=Lag) The distance from the city or town (this is done using a graph, this will also let you know how wide will open your compass based on the unit chart at the bottom of the map)

After completing the steps above, you can now use the compass to make three circles around the map with each circle’s center being the location where the seismograph recorded the data. Wherever they meet will be the epicenter of the earthquake.
