1. These are all types of vacuoles, except Select one: a. Food vacuoles b. Gas vacuoles c. Protein vacuoles d. Contractile vacuoles


Answer:

C. Protein Vacoules

Explanation:

The types are:

1. Sap Vacuoles

2. Contractile Vacuoles

3. Food Vacuoles

4. Air Vacuoles.

Vacuoles are membrane-bound sacs within the cytoplasm of a cell thatfunction in several different ways. In mature plant cells, vacuoles tend to be very large and are extremely important in providing structural support, as well as serving functions such as storage, waste disposal, protection, and growth. Vacuoles have multiple functions. The vacuole is enclosed by a membrane, called a tonoplast. The number and size of the vacuoles in different plant cells vary greatly. Young cells contain a larger number of smaller vacuoles but, taken as a whole, occupy only a minor part of the cell volume.

Type 1. Sap Vacuoles:

They are fluid filled vacuoles or vesicles which are separated from the cytoplasm by a selectively permeable membrane called tonoplast. It has a number of transport systems for the passage of different substances.

Type 2. Contractile Vacuoles:

They occur in some protistan and algal cells found mostly in fresh water. A contractile vacuole has a highly extensible and collapsible membrane. It is also connected to a few feeding canals

Type # 3. Food Vacuoles:

They occur in the cells of protozoan protists, several lower animals and phagocytes of higher animals. A food vacuole is formed by fusion of phagosome and a lysosome.

Type # 4. Air Vacuoles (Pseudo-vacuoles, Gas vacuoles):

They have been reported only in prokaryotes. An air vacuole is not a single entity, neither it is surrounded by a common membrane.

A vacuole is a membrane-bound organelle. Vacuoles are closed sacs, made of membranes with inorganic or organic molecules inside, such as enzymes. They have no set shape or size, and the cell can change them as it wants. They are not in most eukaryotic cells.

Plant cells have a large vacuole that helps maintain the structure of the plant cell by storing water. Vacuoles are distributed throughout the cell's cytoplasm. Most are spaced equidistantly between the cell membrane, the nucleus, and the cell's other large organelles.
