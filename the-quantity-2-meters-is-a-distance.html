Distance refers to the total length of the path covered by an object or person, regardless of the direction. It is measured in units such as meters, kilometers, miles, etc.

Displacement, on the other hand, takes into account both the distance and direction of the movement. It refers to the change in position of an object or person from its initial point to its final point, measured in a straight line (i.e., shortest distance) between the two points.

1. the boy ran 50m to the east and another 50m to the west. What is the displacement and the distance travelled by the boy?

Solution:Distance travelled by the boy = Distance to the east + Distance to the westDistance travelled = 50m + 50m = 100mDisplacement = Final position - Initial positionHere, the final position is the same as the initial position as the boy started and ended at the same point.Hence, displacement = 0m

Therefore, the boy travelled a distance of 100m and his displacement is 0m.

Therefore, Joy travelled a distance of 17m and her displacement is 3m north.

3. An athlete ran 2 rounds in 75m oval track. Find his distance and displacement.

Solution:To find the distance and displacement, we need to draw a diagram of the oval track.The athlete ran 2 rounds, so he covered a total distance of 2 x perimeter of the oval trackPerimeter of oval track = 2 x (length + width)Given, the length of the oval track is 75m and the width is 45m.Hence, perimeter of oval track = 2 x (75m + 45m) = 240mDistance travelled = 2 x 240m = 480mTo find the displacement, we draw a straight line from the starting point to the final point.As the athlete started and ended at the same point, the displacement is 0m.

Therefore, the athlete travelled a distance of 480m and his displacement is 0m.
