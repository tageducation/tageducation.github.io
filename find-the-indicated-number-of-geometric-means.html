aₙ = nth term

a₁ = first term

r = common ratio

n = term position

solution for the second term:

aₙ = -32 ([tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex]) ²⁻¹

aₙ = -16

solution for the third term:

aₙ = -32 ([tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex]) ³⁻¹

aₙ = -8

The third term is -8.

Consequenly, the geometric sequence of the given problem is -32, -16, -8, 4.

What is geometric sequence?

A geometric progression, also known as a geometric sequence in mathematics, is a set of non-zero numbers where each term comes after the first by multiplying the previous term by a fixed, non-zero number called the common ratio. A geometric series is constructed by multiplying each term by the same number as the one preceding it. When a term is referred to as a "geometric series," it means that it is the geometric mean of its two neighbors, just as the term "arithmetic series" indicates that each phrase is the arithmetic mean of its two neighbors.
