Step-by-step explanation:

y=2x3 ; x=3

You are correct that exponents come first before multiplications. One way to remember the order of operations is "PEMDAS", which stands for Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication, Division, Addition, Subtraction.

Actually, the order is parentheses, exponents or roots, multiplication or division, addition or subtraction.

After you get enough experience with solving these problems, you won't have to think about PEMDAS.

Anyway, the important thing here is the the exponent "3" only applies to the "x". Also, "x" is the variable here and it has a value of "3" (you know that because it says x=3 after the semicolon).

So you can plug the value "3" into the first equation:

y = 2x3 ; x = 3

y = 2·33

y = 2·27

y = 54

Note: If there were parentheses around the "2x", then the answer would be different.

y = (2x)3 ; x=3

y = [2·3]3

y = 63

y = 216

Also note: What you did (when you tried 2x2x2) would have been correct if the problem said

y = 23·x ; x=3

y = 2·2·2·x

y = 2·2·2·3

y = 8·3

y = 24
