What is a Quadratic Equation?

A quadratic equation is a polynomial equation (multiple terms) of variable 1 which has the highest power of two. Remember, yes, the highest rank is two! So, if you look for the power of three in a quadratic equation, you won't find it, right?

Now, the general form of a quadratic equation can be written as follows:

ax² + bx + c = 0

With a, b, c € R and a≠ 0

Information:

x = variable

a = coefficient of x²

b = coefficient of x

c = constant

How to Solve Quadratic Equations

There are three ways to solve quadratic equations, namely by using factorization, perfect squares, and quadratic formulas, also known as ABC formulas.

1. Factorization

Factorization or factoring is a way of finding a solution to a quadratic equation, by finding a value which, when multiplied, will produce another value.

2. Perfect Square

Perfect squares is a way to solve a quadratic equation by completing the square to make it perfect. The form of a perfect quadratic equation is a form of equation that produces rational numbers.

Solve quadratic equations with perfect squares using the following formula:

(x + p)² = x²+ 2px + p²

3. Quadratic Formula

In addition to using factorization and completing a perfect square, quadratic equations can also be solved using a quadratic formula or commonly known as the ABC formula.
