a= lw

w= 5 cm

if l= 3w-6

l= 9 cm

Therefore, the length of the rectangular lot is 9 cm and its width is 5cm.

6. The length of a rectangular lot is 6 less than thrice its width. If the area is 45 square cm, whatis the length and the width of the rectangle?​​


Answer:

If W is the width, then the length, L, would be 3W - 6, and the area would be W*(3W - 6) or 3W^2 - 6W. So the equation to solve would be 3W^2 - 6W = 45. Subtracting 45 from both sides gives 3W^2 - 6W - 45 = 0, which simplifies to W^2 - 2W - 15 = 0. This can be written as (W - 5) * (W + 3) = 0, so W = 5 or W = -3. Width isn’t something that we think of as a negative number (how wide is a plot that is -3 feet wide?), so taking W as 5 gives a length of 9 and an area of 45. Taking W as -3 gives a length of (3 * -3) - 6 = -15, which times -3 also gives an area of 45.
