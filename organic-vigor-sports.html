13. Al-Directions: Perform at least 10 activity/sport and categorize weather it is low, moderate, and vigorous activity to strengthening our body physically fit. Example: Dancing Moderate Intensity Activity/Sports Categorize 1-10​


Answer:

Exercise experts measure activity in metabolic equivalents, or METs. One MET is defined as the energy it takes to sit quietly. For the average adult, this is about one calorie per every 2.2 pounds of body weight per hour; someone who weighs 160 pounds would burn approximately 70 calories an hour while sitting or sleeping.

Moderate-intensity activities are those that get you moving fast enough or strenuously enough to burn off three to six times as much energy per minute as you do when you are sitting quietly, or exercises that clock in at 3 to 6 METs. Vigorous-intensity activities burn more than 6 METs.

Organic vigor refers to the physical fitness of the heart and lungs. The word 'vigor' refers to the emotional or physical vitality, strength, or energy that can be gained by diet and exercise.  

Physical Fitness and Gymnastics

This course involves a discussion on physical fitness and its elements, how to measure or compare fitness depending on the definition standard, and how to preserve and attain fitness. Physical exercises focus on the production of the following components: endurance, distance, agility, flexibility, control, determination, cardio-respiratory and organic vigor. Physical education, as part of the education system, is a trend that aims to improve the ability of a person that enables them to achieve complete fitness and to act as a reference in the creation of good life. Fitness is the main focus of physical education. Physical health is the capacity to execute one's everyday tasks effectively without excessive exhaustion and with sufficiently reserved resources. In order to achieve a well-fitting body, the following components must be observed.

7 components of Physical Fitness

Organic Vigor – Refers to the soundness of the heart and lungs that contributes to disease resistance. Endurance – Ability to maintain long-lasting contractions when a variety of muscle groups are used Strength – Quality to be high Power – Refers to the capacity of muscles to unleash full strength during the shortest amount of time. Flexibility – Capacity of muscle groups and body joints to pass through the usual range of motion; ability to execute a wide range of motion; Agility – quickness and lightness of movement Speed – The capacity to travel quickly in the shortest amount of time Physical health assessments can be performed twice a year, pre-tests at the beginning of the academic year and post-tests at the end of the school year in order to be able to test the individual's potential for conducting everyday activities.
