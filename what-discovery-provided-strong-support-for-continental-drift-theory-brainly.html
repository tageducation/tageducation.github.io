24. What are the pieces of evidence he provided to support the continental drift theory? explain each.


Answer:

The evidence for continental drift included the fit of the continents; the distribution of ancient fossils, rocks, and mountain ranges; and the locations of ancient climatic zones.

●Identical rocks, of the same type and age, are found on both sides of the Atlantic Ocean. Wegener said the rocks had formed side-by-side and that the land had since moved apart.

●Mountain ranges with the same rock types, structures, and ages are now on opposite sides of the Atlantic Ocean.

●Ancient fossils of the same species of extinct plants and animals are found in rocks of the same age but are on continents that are now widely separate. —Fossils of the seed fern Glossopteris were too heavy to be carried so far by wind.

●Wegener used fossil evidence to support his continental drift hypothesis. The fossils of these organisms are found on lands that are now far apart.

●Grooves and rock deposits left by ancient glaciers are found today on different continents very close to the equator.

Continental Drift Theory
* proposed by Alfred Wegener
* is the movement of the Earth's continents relative to each other, thus appearing drift across the ocean bed
* Alfred Wegener first thought of this idea by noticing that the land masses of the Earth almost fit together like a jigsaw puzzle. Where in the continents were once join together in a single landmass and had since drift apart. He supposed that the mechanism causing the drift might be the centrifugal force of the Earth's rotation or the astronomical precession.
