Contemporary Filipino Artists

Here are some of the contemporary Filipino artists:

Ernest Concepcion (1977-present)

Intense emotion, deconstructing images in paintings, sculptures, and installations are the focus of Concepcion's works. He creates art like recording a music album, where each painting is from a series of nine.

Ronald Ventura (1973-present)

Ventura’s work is known to consist of multiple layers, using imagery that focuses on the human form.

Oscar Villamiel (1953-present)

Villamiel is a multimedia artist known for his large-scale installations consisting of objects found in local communities.

Dex Fernandez (1984-present)

Dexter practices a variety of mediums ranging from painting to street art and animation. His work is influenced by pop culture, graffiti, children’s drawings, and tattoos creating pieces that challenge people’s views on fine art. He is known locally for his on-going series of Garapata street art.

Neil Pasilan (1971-present)

Pasilan has moulded clay figures for most of his life and continues to use this in his work. He has become known for his paintings that hold multiple layers, using different mediums to expose new forms.

Kawayan de Guia (1979-present)

He illogically arranges texts and icons to compose a painting, depicting the human form in new ways. He also creates sculptures and massive art installations.

Leeroy New (1986-present)

Leeroy’s work blends theatre, fashion, film, production design, and public art.
