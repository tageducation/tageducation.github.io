1. "Africa my Africa" is a lyrical poem by the promising French-born Senegalese poet, novelist, and writer David Diop. The poem first appeared in 1961 in Coups De Pilon, his collection. It addresses the problems that white colonialism brought to Africa. In addition, it sends a message to Africans to bring about freedom and change. The poem's popularity stems from its straightforward presentation of African disorders and their cures.

2. The poem expresses the speaker's affection for and concern for his home country. It begins with him addressing his beloved homeland and informing the audience that Africa was once home to valiant warriors for whom their grandmothers sang on the riverbanks. Although he has never visited, his attachment to his home country is evident in these words.

3. In reference to his blood, he states that his veins are filled with black African blood. Consequently, he can feel the suffering of his people. As the poem progresses, he emphasizes how whites tortured and made Africans the focus of their cruelty.

4. After discussing the harsh reality, he compares Africa to a tree and expresses hope that this land will once again bear fruit and blossom.
