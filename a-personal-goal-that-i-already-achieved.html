8. a personal goal that i have already achieved example


A personal goal that I have already achieved is to have a master's degree in MBA.

In setting personal goals, the principles that we must understand are S.M.A.R.T which contains the following meanings:

Specific

Make sure that you know exactly and in detail what you want to achieve. The clearer and more specific your goal is, the more likely it is to achieve it.

Measurable

Your goals must be clearly measurable, if you are not able to measure progress on the goals to be planned, it is likely that you will lose interest in achieving your goals.

Achievable

The goals set must be possible to achieve, and to achieve these goals, of course, systematic steps are needed so that they get you closer to achieving your goals.

Realistic

Your goals must be realistic, not dreamy.

Timely

The goals you set must have a clear time limit; Milestones and deadlines for their achievement need to be set so that you have a clear picture of how effective your efforts are in achieving your goals.

The steps in setting Personal Goals are as follows:

Determine what your goal isSet beliefs and principles in yourself to achieve these goals, because if you are not sure and do not have strong principles, then many things can affect you so that you stop trying to achieve your goals.Build self-motivation; The goals you have set should motivate you and not weaken you. This self-motivation will make the desire to achieve your goals stronger. Motivation is the key to achieving goals.Set goals using SMART principles (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Realistic, Time Base.Write down your goals and place them where you can easily see them, to generate motivation within yourself and energize you to achieve them.Set milestones and priorities to achieve your goals.Make a plan to achieve your goal
