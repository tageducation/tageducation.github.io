4. if a sample of fluorine gas occupies 2.50 L at 100.0 C and 3.00 atm what volume will it occupy at STP? ​


Answer:

V₂ = 5.49 L

Explanation:

Note: At STP, the pressure and temperature of a gas are 1.00 atm and 273.15 K, respectively

Converting °C to K

T₁ = 100.0°C + 273.15 = 373.15 K

Calculating the final volume of fluorine gas

V₂ = V₁ × (P₁ / P₂) × (T₂ / T₁)

V₂ = 2.50 L × (3.00 atm / 1.00 atm) × (273.15 K / 373.15 K)

V₂ = 5.49 L

Boyle's law : A sample of fluorine gas occupies a volume of 500 mL at 760 torr. Given that the temperature remainscthe same , calculate the pressure required to reduce its volume by ⅓.

***Note: In SI the pressure can be mmHg or atm

We have the following information:

P1 (initial pressure) = 760 torr → P1 (initial pressure) = 1 atm

V1 (initial volume) = 500 mL

P2 (final pressure) = ? (in atm)

V2 (final volume) = 500*1/3 ≈ 166.66 mL

We have an isothermal transformation, that is, its temperature remains constant, if the volume of gas in the container decreases, then its pressure increases since it is known that pressure and volume of a gas are inversely proportional. Applying the data to the formula of Boyle's Law, we have:

[tex]P_1*V_1 = P_2*V_2[/tex]

[tex]1*500 = P_2*166.66[/tex]

[tex]500 = 166.66\:P_2[/tex]

[tex]166.66\:P_2 = 500[/tex]

[tex]P_2 = \dfrac{500}{166.66}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\boxed{P_2 \approx 3\:atm}}\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

Answer:  

The pressure required is approximately 3 atm

13. a sample fluorine gas occupies a volume of 400 ml at 760 torr. given that the temperature remains the same calculate the pressure required to reduce it's volume by [tex]1 \2[/tex]​


GASES: BOYLE’S LAW

A sample fluorine gas occupies a volume of 400 ml at 760 torr. Given that the temperature remains the same calculate the pressure required to reduce its volume by [tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex].

GIVEN:[tex] \rm P_1 = 760\: torr[/tex][tex]\rm V_1 = 400 ml[/tex][tex]\rm V_2= \frac{1}{2}V_1[/tex]

SOLUTION:

Find the value of the final volume.

[tex]\rm V_2= \frac{1}{2}V_1 = \frac{1}{2} (400 \: ml) = 200 \: ml [/tex]

Rearrange the equation [tex]\rm P_1V_1 = P_2V_2[/tex] to get the equation that will solve for [tex]\rm P_2[/tex]

[tex]\rm P_2=\frac{P_1V_1}{V_2}[/tex]

Substitute all the given values to solve for the final temperature. The unit mL is cancelled out to give the final unit which is torr.

[tex]\rm P_2=\frac{P_1V_1}{V_2}[/tex]

[tex]\rm P_2=\frac{(760\:torr)(400\: mL)}{200\: mL}[/tex]

[tex]\rm P_2=\frac{304 \: 000 \: torr}{200}[/tex]

[tex] \boxed{\rm P_2=1 \: 520 \: torr}[/tex]

Convert the pressure in atmosphere, atm, using the equation [tex]\rm atm=\frac{torr}{760}[/tex]

[tex]\rm atm=\frac{torr}{760}[/tex]

[tex]\rm atm=\frac{1 \: 520}{760} = 2 [/tex]

Using the same equation, we can say that the initial pressure is at one atmosphere (1 atm).

ANSWER:

Therefore, the pressure required to reduce its volume by [tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex] is 2 atm.


14. At 300K, the given amount of fluorine gas has a volume of 30.0 L. What will be the temperature if the gas occupies a volume of 25 L at constant pressure?


Strategy:

The temperature and volume of fluorine change but the amount and pressure of gas remain the same. What equation would you use to solve for the final temperature?

Solution:

We start with

[tex]\frac{P_{1}V_{1}}{n_{1}T_{1}} = \frac{P_{2}V_{2}}{n_{2}T_{2}}[/tex]

Because [tex]n_{1} = n_{2}[/tex] and [tex]P_{1} = P_{2}[/tex]

[tex]\frac{V_{1}}{T_{1}} = \frac{V_{2}}{T_{2}}[/tex]

which is Charles's law. Next, we write

Initial Conditions

[tex]V_{1} = \text{30.0 L}[/tex]

[tex]T_{1} = \text{300 K}[/tex]

Final Conditions

[tex]V_{2} = \text{25.0 L}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = \: ?[/tex]

The final temperature is given by

[tex]T_{2} = T_{1} \times \frac{V_{2}}{V_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = \text{300 K} \times \frac{\text{25.0 L}}{\text{30.0 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\blue{T_{2} = \text{250 K}}[/tex]
