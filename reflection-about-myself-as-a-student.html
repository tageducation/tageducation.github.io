5. reflection about myself as a social worker​


Answer:

Social workers support individuals and their families through difficult times and ensure that vulnerable people, including children and adults, are safeguarded from harm. Their role is to help improve outcomes in people's lives. They maintain professional relationships and act as guides and advocates.

Being a social worker involves heavy amounts of emotional labor and going the extra mile with often limited resources to help patients. Keep an eye on your energy level and monitor your own physical and mental health carefully, and when and where you need to, step away for a time to recharge.

The truth, however, is that we all have the same 24 hours. If someone asks you to do something, he’s taking that out of his 24 hours and putting it in yours. Does it make sense? Are you the best person to do it? Have you built the kind of relationship to justify it?

28. blog about myself as a humss student​


Answer:

As a Humanities and Social Sciences (HUMSS) student, I have had the opportunity to explore a wide range of subjects that have broadened my understanding of the world around me. From studying literature, history, and philosophy, to delving into the intricacies of politics and social issues, I have gained a deep appreciation for the complexity and diversity of human experience.

One of the things I love most about being a HUMSS student is the interdisciplinary nature of the program. By studying subjects from different perspectives, I have been able to make connections between seemingly disparate topics and gain a more holistic understanding of the world. For example, studying literature has given me a deeper understanding of the human condition, while taking a course on politics has helped me understand the ways in which power and ideology shape our societies.

Another aspect of the HUMSS program that I have found particularly enriching is the focus on critical thinking and analysis. By learning to question assumptions and challenge received wisdom, I have developed skills that are essential for navigating the complexities of the modern world. I am proud to say that I can now approach any topic with an open mind and a questioning attitude, which is something that will serve me well in my future pursuits.

Of course, being a HUMSS student is not without its challenges. The workload can be heavy at times and the course material can be dense and complex. But, the rewards are well worth the effort. In the end, I am grateful for the opportunity to study such a wide range of subjects and I know that the skills and knowledge I have gained as a HUMSS student will serve me well throughout my life.
