2. 5 examples of claims of policy


Claims of Policy

Claims of policy is one of the three types of claims: value, policy, and factual claims. Claims of policy usually attempts to persuade you or to change your mind in order for you to take some action or  change behavior to resolve an issue or a problem. It usually provides solution and it is often procedural.

Five (5) examples of claims of policyAn individual must be at least 18 years old in order for him or her to apply for a driver's license.Reckless drivers should have a mandatory jail term of not shorter than two months.The church should not interfere with the decision of the state or the government.Abusive partners should be charged five to eight years of imprisonment.The custody of a child should be depending on whom did the child grew up with.

28. 5 examples of claim of fact, value and policy about climate change​


A fact claim is a statement about how things were in the past, how they are in the present, or how they will be in

the future.

Value claims are arguable statements concerning the relative merits of something which is measured subjectively

(e.g., "Hawaii is a better place to go for summer vacation than Colorado.").

A policy claim is a statement regarding the merits of a course of action. What makes it arguable is that, even

though people may not be totally certain about the proper course of action to take, they still must act. To argue in

defense of a policy claim is to state that, given what we know now, it’s best to act in the manner proposed.
