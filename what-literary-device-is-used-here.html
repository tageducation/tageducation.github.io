9. Without it I was like a bear in a cave Drowsing through a shadowy winter It rings and spring has come Guide questions: a. To what does the speaker compare man's situation before the advent of the telephone? B.what value do people give to the telephone when he has the access to it. C.what literary device is used here​


Answer:

a. The speaker compares man's situation before the advent of the telephone to that of a bear in a cave drowsing through a shadowy winter. This suggests a sense of isolation and lethargy, as if life before the telephone was dull and uneventful.

b. When people have access to the telephone, they generally give it a high value because it allows them to communicate quickly and easily with others. The telephone enables people to stay connected and exchange information with friends, family, and colleagues, which is especially important in modern society where people are often spread out and have busy schedules.

c. The literary device used in this excerpt is metaphor. The speaker compares man's situation before the telephone to that of a bear in a cave drowsing through a shadowy winter. This is not meant to be taken literally, but rather as a figurative comparison to emphasize the sense of isolation and inactivity that existed before the telephone

30. it took you forever to be here . identify what is literary devices or technique was used?​


Answer:

Blank verse

Explanation:

Blank verse is a literary device defined as un-rhyming verse written in iambic pentameter. In poetry and prose, it has a consistent meter with 10 syllables in each line (pentameter); where, unstressed syllables are followed by stressed ones, five of which are stressed but do not rhyme.
