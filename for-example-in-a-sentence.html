11. A. Type 5 examples of simple sentences and 5 examples of compound sentences.


Answer:

Simple Sentences

A simple sentence has the most basic elements that make it a sentence: a subject, a verb, and a completed thought.

Examples of simple sentences include the following:

1. Joe waited for the train.

"Joe" = subject, "waited" = verb

2.The train was late.

"The train" = subject, "was" = verb

3.Mary and Samantha took the bus.

"Mary and Samantha" = compound subject, "took" = verb

4.I looked for Mary and Samantha at the bus station.

"I" = subject, "looked" = verb

5.Mary and Samantha arrived at the bus station early

Compound Sentences

A compound sentence refers to a sentence made up of two independent clauses (or complete sentences) connected to one another with a coordinating conjunction. Coordinating conjunctions are easy to remember if you think of the words "FAN BOYS":

For

And

Nor

But

Or

Yet

So

Examples of compound sentences include the following:

1.I like coffee, and Mary likes tea.

2.Mary went to work, but John went to the party, and I went home.

3.Iwant lose wieght yet i eat chocolate daily.

4.Dr mark said I could come to his office on friday or saturaday

5. Mary doesnt like cartoons because they're so loud so she doesnt watch them.

What Is Denotation?

The denotation of a word is the literal meaning of a word based on what the dictionary says about that word. More specifically, the denotative meaning of a word is its “dictionary definition.”

Consider, as an example, the word snake. If you look up this word in the dictionary, the literal definition is some sort of scaly, legless reptile with a long, cylindrical body.

No matter how you choose to use words, you need to understand that every word has an explicit meaning, also known as the denotation of the word. Some readers will think of that literal meaning when they read what you write.

Essay Title: The Importance of Recycling

Paragraph 1: The Introduction

Recycling is a vital practice for preserving the environment. It helps reduce the amount of waste in landfills, conserves natural resources, and reduces pollution. For example, recycling paper products reduces the number of trees that need to be cut down, while recycling plastic conserves petroleum, a non-renewable resource. Additionally, recycling can also create jobs and stimulate economic growth.

Paragraph #2 Example #1: Reducing Waste in Landfills

The most obvious benefit of recycling is the reduction of waste in landfills. Landfills are not only eyesores, but they also release methane, a potent greenhouse gas, into the atmosphere. By recycling, we are able to divert waste from landfills and reduce the negative impact on the environment. In fact, according to a report by the Environmental Protection Agency, recycling and composting prevented 87.2 million tons of material from being disposed of in landfills and incinerators in 2017.

Paragraph #3 Example #2: Conserving Natural Resources

Recycling also helps conserve natural resources. When we recycle materials like aluminum, paper, and glass, we are able to reduce the need to extract virgin materials from the earth. This not only conserves resources, but it also reduces the environmental impact of extraction and processing. For instance, recycling aluminum saves 95% of the energy required to make new aluminum from raw materials.

Paragraph #4 Example #3: Creating Jobs and Economic Growth

Recycling also has economic benefits. It creates jobs in the recycling and manufacturing industries, which in turn stimulates economic growth. According to the Institute for Local Self-Reliance, for every job created in a landfill or incinerator, recycling creates ten times as many jobs. Additionally, recycling also saves municipalities money by reducing the need for new landfills and incinerators.

Paragraph 5: Conclusion

In conclusion, recycling is a crucial practice that helps preserve the environment, conserve natural resources, and create jobs and economic growth. By recycling paper products, we can reduce the number of trees that need to be cut down. By recycling plastic, we can conserve petroleum, a non-renewable resource. And by recycling in general, we can reduce waste in landfills and create jobs and economic growth. It is important for individuals and communities to take an active role in recycling and make it a part of their daily lives.


20. Examples of declarative sentence,interrogative sentence and exclamatory sentence...at least two examples


Declarative sentences are the most common, typical sentences.
Manila is the capital of the Philippines.
Black is my favorite color. 

Interrogative sentences are simply the questions and end with a question mark.
What time it is?
Could you close the window?

Imperative sentence is a command or request. It ends with an exclamation mark or a full stop.
Please close the window.
Don't forget to walk the dog!

Exclamatory sentences express emotions and end with an exclamation mark.
You didn't do your homework again!
I'm so sorry for what I have done!
