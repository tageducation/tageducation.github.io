Here are my personal expectations from learning math as a subject

It is a common secret already that math is a "frightening" subject for average students in all levels of education. Students who excel in math, or commonly referred to as "math wizard" seem to be idolized by many, and they are deemed to be more competent in other subjects as well. Math often times does not only involve numbers, but also alphabets, such as in the case of algebra.

However, there are numerous effects of learning math. And these effects should be ingrained in students' mind so that they can be motivated to learn math. They do not have to be particularly good at it, because just understanding the basic is enough. Besides, there is nothing wrong with getting a good grades in math. I think it would work better to get the students motivated by knowing about what to expect by learning math.

Below are my personal expectations by learning math:

It would help me with my problem solving skill. Students are usually tasked to solve math problems. In the long run, they will find it easier to solve problems in daily life because the mind is already trained thanks to math.My critical thinking skill will be better after I learn math as a subject. Math also requires a degree of critical thinking. For example, in reading a story problem in math, I have to identify the problem, search for the solution, and then solve it myself.Learning math will help me to try new things through trials and errors. Trials and errors are specially apparent when I happen to forget the right way to solve a math problem, hence I will have to use other ways that may solve the problem.
