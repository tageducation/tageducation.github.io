3. what are the barriers to communication mentioned in the article?​


Technically, an obstacle is anything that can distort a message, either in terms of sending or receiving a message. Communication barriers are all forms of disturbances that occur in the process of delivering and receiving messages between individuals, which are generally caused by environmental, physical, and psychological factors of the individuals involved.

There are three forms of communication barriers, namely technical barriers, semantic barriers, and human barriers.

Technical barriers are obstacles in the form of limited facilities and communication equipment. For example, the uneven development of technology, and damage to communication tools.

Semantic barriers are communication barriers in the context of delivering messages effectively. Semantics is more directed at expressing something through language and words. In the communication process, semantic barriers are defined as erroneous interpretations or misunderstandings in capturing a meaning sent by the communicator or communicant.

Human barriers are barriers caused by various human factors, such as emotions, personal prejudices, perceptions, incompetence, abilities or incompetence, and so on.

Communication barriers can be divided into five, namely:

Barriers from the sender of the message For example, the message sent by the communicator is not clear according to the communicant.Barriers to encoding or symbols, for example, the use of language that is too difficult, ambiguous words, and different meanings of symbols between communicators and communicants.Media barriers for example, interference with radio signals so that the sound is interrupted and listeners cannot listen to the information conveyed.Barriers in coded language for example, the communicant interprets the message or information he receives differently from the actual context conveyed by the communicator.Barriers from the recipient of the message for example, the communicant pays less attention and listens to the message sent by the communicator, and the communicant responds to the message incorrectly without seeking further information.

What is a communication barrier?

- a communication barrier is an entity that disrupts effective communication between at least two parties who are exchanging communication with one another.

-This will typically cause misinterpretation, misunderstanding, and confusion amongst the parties. This might prove to have a negative result if it goes too far.

Factors That May Cause Communication Barriers:

JargonEmotional BarriersLack of attentionDifference in viewsDifference is perceptionLanguage differencesPhysical BarriersAnd more....

Situations that show communcation barriers:

1.) The student could not understand a concept from the professor's syllabus as he was not listening, but instead looking at his phone while the professor was discussing. (Lack of Attention)

2.) A University of the Philippines freshman was confused as to what "TBA" meant in his letter, and was fooled by some seniors when they deliberately changed the meaning of the acronym. (Jargon)

3.) The Russian national could not understand what the American was saying as he was not familiar with the English language. (Language Differences)
