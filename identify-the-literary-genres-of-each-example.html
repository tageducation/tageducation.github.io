Q: This type of drama is usually full of exaggerations and characterized by the use of slapstick humor with its sole purpose of making the audience laugh

A: Farce

Q: The use of sensational incidents and spectacular staging coupled with the music and singing are what makes this drama wonderful to watch on stage

A: Theme

Q: This is a type of dialogue which helps reveal the private thoughts and emotions of the character for the audience to know

A: Soliloquy

Q: Instead of dialogue and acting, this drama makes use of dancing and singing to tell the story

A: Musical

Q: A literary genre meant for performance by actors and actresses in theaters, televisions, films and even on radio

A: Drama

Q: In the field of drama, this element is also called storyline

A: Plot

Q: This element of drama need to interact with each other to create a

motion in the storyline called actions

A: Character

Q: This kind of drama typically shows human limitations against the

forces of destiny

A: Tragedy

Q: This is the form into which a drama is written

A: Script

Q: The ancient Greeks employed this type of drama as a comic relief in between acts of a tragic drama

A: Satyr Play (Satire)

Q: In today’s contemporary period, this word has become synonymous to the word ‘drama’

A: Play

Q: This refers to the shaping of dramatic materials, settings, or costumes for the audiences to understand more about the play as

seen on stage through the perspectives of the playwright, the

directors and the producers

A: Style

Q: This element helps create the effect of interest, excitement, and

tension in dramas

A: Suspense

Q: This element plays a significant role as it helps to convey clues to

the characters’ feelings, personalities, backgrounds and even shifts

in personalities

A: Language (Verbal + Body)

Q: This character in a tragedy eventually suffers the downfall at the

end

A: Tragic hero

Q: It is the universal truth that the drama would like the audience to realize after watching its totality

A: Melodrama

Q: This element of drama brings characters to life by revealing their personalities and by showing what they are thinking and feeling through lines of conversations

A: Dialogue / Dialog

Q: This is a very important element because its absence in the drama would mean no movement and no narrative drive at all

A: Conflict

Q: The professional term used in calling the writers of dramas

A: Playwright(s)

Q:  William Shakepeare’s “A Winter’s Tale” is a very good example of

this type of drama

A: Comedy
