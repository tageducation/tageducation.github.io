5. Two covalent bonds form between the two oxygen atoms because oxygen requires two shared electrons to fill its outermost shell. 20. 2. Which type of compound has a higher melting point? What does this tell you about the strength of ionic bonds? 3. Are ionic compounds harder or softer than covalent compounds? What does this tell you about the strength of ionic bonds?​


Answer:

2. Ionic compounds typically have higher melting points compared to covalent compounds. This is because ionic bonds are much stronger than covalent bonds, and it requires more energy to break the bonds between the ions in an ionic compound.

3. Ionic compounds are generally harder than covalent compounds. This is because the ionic bonds in an ionic compound are stronger than the covalent bonds in a covalent compound. The strength of ionic bonds is due to the electrostatic forces between the positively and negatively charged ions. In contrast, covalent bonds are held together by the sharing of electrons, which are not as strong as the electrostatic forces in ionic bonds.
