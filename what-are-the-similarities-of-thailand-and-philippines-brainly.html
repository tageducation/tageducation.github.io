2. What are the similarities of Thailand and Philippines?


Listed below are the similarities of Thailand and Philippines.

- Both countries are in the continent of Asia, specifically in Southeast Asia.

- Both countries have tropical, wet and dry climate.

- Both countries are close allies of the United States.

- Both countries have tropical fruits.

- During the 1970s and 1980s, there was a surge in the occurrence of overseas workers flying to other countries because of low salaries in Thailand and Philippines.

- Both countries have a lot of beaches to boast.

- Both countries consist of islands.

- Both countries have informal sellers or what we commonly know as street vendors.

- Both countries have rice as staple food for their meals.

- Filipinos and Thai look alike in terms of their skin color and physical build.

- One similarities of Thai and Filipino beliefs is their strong religious faith.

- Part of the similarities of Thailand’s culture and Philippine culture is their ability to smile despite challenges, and their nature of being polite.

10. what are the similarities of thailand and philippines in terms, beliefs and way of life?


Similarities

Common Look (may it be the coutryside or the Thai Chinese, they look the same with Filipino and Chinoys (Chinese Pinoys). Well we came from the same region

Love for Malls - Bangkok Siam CEN is like Makati CBD and Ortigas to Manila.. Malls are of high class. Siam Paragon can compete with the likes of Greenbelt and Rockwell in Makati

Almost same food taste preference - when I say almost, you can omit the spicy for Filipinos but the love for Sour, Salty and Oily is the same. Both are mango lovers but I will debate that Philippine Mangoes are miles better than any in Southeast Asia (not bragging, but I tried the ones in Indonesia, Thailand and Malaysia but nothing is sweeter than Guimaras mangoes)

Transportation - we may lack on this department but Thailand has songthaews as Filipinos have Jeepney.

TV Drama - I personally watch Thai Lakorns and it is like a better version of Filipino Teleserye. With better budget, HD, cinematography and length (one lakorn can finish max 3 months while Teleserye is minimum 6 months). Filipinos like our Teleseryes as how Thais watch their Lakorns and Cable dramas.

Currency - Baht and Peso share the same denomination and almost the same value. (1:1.4). There is no adjustment in terms of money since it is almost the same. Unlike Rupiah which has 100,000 and VND of 500,000.

Differences

Language - one ask if Filipino is a tonal language, and it is NOT. It may sound Thai to others but no Thai is a whole new world. Filipino syntax and lexicon is one of the most difficult in Southeast Asia even we share the same letters with English. Thai on the other hand has their abugida system. We may sound the same due to the pitch but for Filipinos, we solely use it to intensify or elaborate while tone/pitch which is way higher in Thai is part of the language that you need to learn. (Learning Thai by the way and it is harder that Bahasa Indonesia/Malaysia)

Religion - Filipinos are majority Catholic and Christian while Thailand is a majority Theravada Buddhist.

General Attitude to Foreigners - due to the western upbringing (colonized by Spain for 333 years, Americans for 50+ years), Filipinos are more gregarious and friendly to foreigners (Which at times, backfires to us). We do speak English as we are raised to be ESL since Preschool. We do standout when with other nationalities. Not saying Thais are not, Thais are more reserve due to their Buddhist and more Asian upbringing and values. I really like that to their society, reserved. However, the western mix to the Filipinos actually is a double edged sword to the Filipino society. We are torn between “I do not care what they think, I will do what i want” attitude to “What would other people think?” attitude which is the Asian in us… Sounds not so great but this what makes Filipinos the nickname, Asian Latinos. (Many would dispute this,, haahahah). Again, no two country is better on this.

Tourist Price - not only in Thailand but in Indonesia, Malaysia, Cambodia and Laos as well. Philippines does not have this. I do not have to pay 10x more than what local wants. Imagine going to the National Museum of the Philippines, which is free by the way, locals or tourist, while I went to Muzium Negara for a price 2x the local, Grand Palace for 500 Baht which is free for locals. I am not complaining but why there is always a tourist rate, can we have an ASEAN rate? just a suggestion. I paid 39 USD for Angkor Wat. As a Filipino who has lived and work in Thailand for 3 years, these are my observations about the similarities and differences between the Thais and Filipinos.

People: Thais and Filipinos are both friendly people but I think the latter warms up easily to strangers than the former. Also most Thais are gentler and reserved, but Filipinos are generally more gregarious. Both love to party and drink and they will definitely let their hair down once the alcohol kicks in.

Culture: Very different. Most Thais are Buddhists which probably explain their gentle nature. Filipinos on the other hand have a very Catholic upbringing that’s why many foreigners think that Filipinos are much more similar to Latinos than Asians. Thais are more relaxed and they don’t like to be stressed but Filipinos, due to the American and Spanish influences, are a little bit more Westernised in their attitude.

Food: Thai food relies heavily on herbs and spices and is reknowned for using a lot of chili peppers. Regional dishes exists but generally you can say their food is fiery and spicy. Filipino food uses a lot of pork and seafood and almost every dish uses vinegar and other souring agents like calamansi, citruses and sour fruits to flavor their food. Influences are varied too from Malay to Polynesian, Indian to Chinese, and Spanish to American.

Language: The Thai language is tonal and based on ancient languages like Khmer and Sanskrit. They have their own writing unlike the Philippines which has adapted the western alphabet. More Filipinos can speak, read and write English too than Thais and the Filipino language, just like the food, has varied influences.
