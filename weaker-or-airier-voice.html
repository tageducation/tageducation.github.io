18. a weaker and more airy voice


FALSETTO

The reason falsetto has an airy tone is because of how it's produced by the vocal cords. In falsetto, the tiny vocal folds are coming close enough to one another to cause the edges to vibrate as the air flows between them, but they are not making contact with one another.

Falsetto

Falsetto is a vocal register that sits slightly above the modal voice to our register and overlaps it by around an octave. Because of this it will be caused by the partial or complete vibration of the ligamentous margins of the vocal cords. The term "falsetto" refers to a voice that uses only a portion of our vocal equipment in our throats than the full vibratory sound employed in normal singing and speaking. The vibrations of our vocal folds make the regular vocal sounds that we make (or vocal cords).
