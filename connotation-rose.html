connotative meaning of sea-

sea is a huge body of salt water which is bigger than river and lake but bigger smaller than an ocean. The Mediterranean sea and the the Caribbean sea are the most suitable examples to understand a sea.

Sea is used in different connotations which are also termed as the figurative sense. Most of the people don't have experience of natural disasters. The waves of the sea are symbolised as the fury of Nature ( which has been called Prakriti by the Indian philosopher sage Kapil in Sankhya philosophy) . Since it's limitless, the sea is utilised in the sense of limitless in volume and vast in quantity. You can use the phrase ' the sea of people' that signifies endless persons. It is also a part and parcel of Nature in visible form. The sea is employed in the sense of seriousness and depth of knowledge. It is a scientific truth that life came out of water , therefore sea is the symbol of life. The language of tides communicate lots of things which are inspirational for human beings such as rhythm of life, no politics before Nature, no dishonesty and betrayal.
