8. What do people living in hot countries wear?​


People in hot countries stay cool by wearing light-colored clothing and living in white-painted homes. Since white is a good reflector and therefore a bad absorber of radiant heat, this decreases the amount of heat consumed. In cold areas, on the other hand, black cloths and dark colored clothing and furnishings are favored.  

The absorption of solar energy is directly proportional to the color of skin. This is why, in hotter temperatures, people choose lighter-colored clothing. Wearing darker colors causes you to get colder more quickly, while wearing lighter colors allows you to remain cooler for longer.  

Houses are another example of the same principle. Houses in hotter climates are frequently white or light-colored. In a warm climate, you can hardly see a dark home. This is because the house can trap the sun's heat, making the interior very hot.
