14. Identification: This compound is formed by a cation and an anion.


Answer: Ionic compound

Explanation:

When ions, particularly positively charged (cations) and negatively charged ions ( anions) are held together by an electrostatic bonds called ionic bonding, it forms an ionic compound.

A common example is the table salt, NaCl or sodium chloride. It is an ionic compound between sodium which is a cation and chlorine which is an anion.

25. what kind of element forms a cation


Cations (positively-charged ions) and anions (negatively-charged ions) are formed when a metal loses electrons, and a nonmetal gains those electrons. The electrostatic attraction between the positives and negatives brings the particles together and creates an ionic compound, such as sodium chloride.
