1. can we really stop people from committing crime? Yes or no?​


Unfortunately, I think the answer is no. We can't really stop people from committing crimes.

Explanation details:

Crime is an activity in which a person violates the law. Broadly speaking, there are two types of factors that influence a person to commit a crime.

1. The first is internal factors or factors that come from within humans. For example:

A person's psychological condition or mental state and a person's experiences. Psychological conditions make a person instinctively driven to commit crimes.Envy or hatred towards others triggers the desire to commit criminal acts, such as theft and robbery, to murder.

2. The second is external factors or factors that come from outside humans.

For example:

The act of low education makes criminals not think long about the negative impacts of committing crimes.Factors of economic difficulties that make criminals commit crimes to meet their needs.

From the factors that cause crime above, we can see that these factors are not factors that we can suppress in order to stop someone from committing a crime. For example, we may be able to bring people with psychological conditions who tend to steal to do counseling so that their mental health conditions can be treated, but it is very difficult for us to suppress other feelings felt by that person so that they commit crimes.

Besides that, there are still many other factors beyond our reach that cause people to commit crimes. Therefore, we can't really stop people from committing crimes if it doesn't come out of their will.
