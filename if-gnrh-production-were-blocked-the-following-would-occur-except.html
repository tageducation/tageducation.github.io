14. 1. What hormone stimulates the production of GnRH?________________________________________________________________2. What hormone inhibits the production of GnRH an LH?________________________________________________________________3. What organ produces the Gonadotropin Releasing Hormone or GnRH?_____________________________________________​


What hormone stimulates the production of GnRH?

Kisspeptin signals directly to the hypothalamic GnRH neurons via the kisspeptin receptor to release GnRH into the portal circulation, which in turn stimulates the anterior pituitary gonadotrophs to produce LH and FSH (121,141).

What hormone inhibits the production of GnRH an LH?

A negative feedback system occurs in the male with rising levels of testosterone acting on the hypothalamus and anterior pituitary to inhibit the release of GnRH, FSH, and LH. The Sertoli cells produce the hormone inhibin, which is released into the blood when the sperm count is too high.

What organ produces the Gonadotropin Releasing Hormone or GnRH?

Gonadotrophin-releasing hormone (GnRH) is produced from cells in the hypothalamus. It is then released into small blood vessels that carry the hormone to the pituitary gland. As a consequence, the pituitary gland produces luteinizing hormone (LH) and follicle-stimulating (FSH) hormones.

16. what would happen if testosterone levels get too high?a. testosterone feeds back to directly inhibit GnRH productionb. testosterone feedback to directly inhibit FSH productionc. FSH production increases to a counteract high testosterone levelsd. LH production increases to counteract high testosterone levels​


Answer:

A. Testosterone feeds back to directly inhibit GnRH production

Explanation:

What Would Happen if Testosterone Levels Get Too High

The correct answer among the choices above to the question, what would happen if Testosterone levels get too high is letter A. This is because high levels of testosterone feeds back to suppress the production of GnRH in the hypothalamus. When GnRH production is subdued, the production of both Luteinizing Hormone (LH) and Follicle-stimulating Hormone (FSH) is also halted. This explains why letter B is incorrect; high testosterone levels indirectly inhibit FSH as well as LH.

Further Explanation on What Would Happen if Testosterone Levels Get Too High

Here are further explanations regarding the choices above and what would happen if testosterone levels get too high.

The primary role of FSH in men is to control the production of sperm. It does not directly affect testosterone. Lh stimulates the production of testosterone. This is positive feedback. So, if the LH level increases, the level of testosterone also increases. In summary, high testosterone levels have direct negative feedback on the level of GnRH while they have indirect negative feedback on LH and FSH levels.

29. what might be the negative effects of non production of GnRH on the male and female reproductive system?​


Answer:

Any deficiency, whether total or partial, in the production of GnRH can lead to a failure of puberty which in turn can lead to a lack of sexual development and infertility. Pubertal failure might be total or partial depending on the degree of GnRH deficiency. GnRH deficient conditions can affect both men and women
