If there is no gravity and friction, the behavior of objects would be drastically different from what we observe in our everyday lives. Here are some possible outcomes:Objects would move in straight lines at a constant speed: In the absence of friction, an object in motion would continue to move in a straight line with the same speed forever, unless acted upon by another force.There would be no concept of weight: Without gravity, there would be no force pulling objects towards the center of the earth. Objects would not have weight, and there would be no need for supports or structures to bear the weight of objects.Objects could not stay in orbit: In the absence of gravity, there would be no force to keep objects in orbit around a planet or a star.There would be no atmosphere: Gravity is the force that holds the atmosphere close to the earth's surface. Without gravity, the atmosphere would simply float away into space.Energy would be conserved: Without friction, energy would be conserved, and objects would continue to move perpetually without losing energy

Overall, the absence of gravity and friction would result in a very different physical universe than the one we observe today.
