Chemical reactions  

Substances involved in chemical reactions are reactants and products.

Reactants are compounds that cause chemical reactions, usually located to the left in the reaction equation. Product is a compound produced from the reaction in the reactants. In this problem, it is shown that the reaction involves 2 reactants which is a combination reaction.

Further explanation  

There are several chemical reactions  

1. Combination / Synthesis  

The combination from two-element  

A + B ⇒ AB

2. Decomposition  

One compound breaks down into 2 components  

AB ⇒ A + B  

3. Single displacement

One element replaces another element in a compound  

A+BC ⇒ AC + B

4. Double-Replacement reactions  

Two elements in two compounds replace each other to form new products  

AB + CD ⇒ AD + CB

5. Combustion  

Reaction with oxygen which produces CO₂ and water  

CxHy + O₂ ⇒ CO₂ + H₂O

Then the reactants needed in the above reaction:

1. MgO + H₂O⇒Mg + (OH)₂ ⇒Mg(OH)₂

2. 2Na + Cl₂ ⇒ 2NaCl or Na + Cl ⇒ NaCl

3. H₂O + SO₃ ⇒ H₂SO₄

4. Al + (PO₄)₃ ⇒ Al(PO₄)₃

5. S + O₂ ⇒ SO₂
