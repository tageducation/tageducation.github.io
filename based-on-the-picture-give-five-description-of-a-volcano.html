1. Based on the picture,give five description of a volcano


Answer:

Description of a volcano:

1. A volcano is a mountain that opens downward to a pool of molten rock below the surface of the earth. When pressure builds up, eruptions occur.

2. A cone-shaped hill or mountain, wholly or chiefly of volcanic materials, built up around the vent, usually so as to form a crater.

3. Volcanoes are usually associated with plate boundaries but can also occur within the interior areas of a tectonic plate.

4. The shape of a volcano is directly related to the type of magma that flows from them—the more viscous the magma, the steeper the sides of the volcano.

5.  Some volcano burst to life in explosive eruptions, like the 1991 eruption of Mount Pinatubo, and others burp rivers of lava in what's known as an effusive eruption, like the 2018 activity of Hawaii's Kilauea volcano.

How do volcanoes form?

 Subduction zone- The majority of volcanoes in the world form along the boundaries of Earth's tectonic plates massive expanses of our planet's lithosphere that continually shift, bumping into one another. When tectonic plates collide, one often plunges deep below the other in. Hotspot volcanism-  In this situation, a zone of magmatic activity or a hotspot in the middle of a tectonic plate can push up through the crust to form a volcano. Although the hotspot itself is thought to be largely stationary, the tectonic plates continue their slow march, building a line of volcanoes or islands on the surface.

3. Based on the picture,give five descriptions of a volcano.​


Five Descriptions of a Volcano

As seen in the picture, one of the five descriptions of a volcano discussed here is that it usually has steep slopes that are symmetrical or cone-shaped. Second, it is an opening on the earth's crust where gases, lava, and volcanic ash escape from underneath. The moment these contents escape, an eruption occurs. In addition, a volcano has a magma chamber within it containing a pool of underground molten rock. Fourth, its topmost part usually has a circular depression called a crater formed during eruption. Lastly, the ash cloud coming from the opening is made up of pulverized rocks, minerals, and volcanic glasses.

Additional Five Descriptions of a Volcano

Here are additional five descriptions of a volcano:

The lava - the fiery liquid expelled from a volcano - is composed of liquefied silicate rocks.When lava flows downhill, it cools and hardens which contributes to the form of the volcano. It has a main vent where magma flows from the chamber to the surface. The uppermost section of the main vent is called the throat. 3 types of volcano: stratovolcano, cinder cone, and shield volcano.

16. ypes of Volcanoes and Volcanic Eruption Directions Based on the picture, give at least five descriptions or characteristics of a volcano. Present your answer in a concept map as shown below. (5 points)​


Answer:

A volcano is a mountain that opens downward to a pool of molten rock below the surface of the earth. When pressure builds up, eruptions occur.[1]

In an eruption, gases and rock shoot up through the opening and spill over or fill the air with lava fragments. Eruptions can cause lava flows, hot ash flows, mudslides, avalanches, falling ash and floods.[2]

The danger area around a volcano covers about a 20-mile radius.[3]

Fresh volcanic ash, made of pulverized rock, can be harsh, acidic, gritty, glassy and smelly. The ash can cause damage to the lungs of older people, babies and people with respiratory problems.[4]

Volcanic lightning occurs mostly within the cloud of ash during an eruption, and is created by the friction of the ash rushing to the surface. Roughly 200 accounts of this lightning have been witnessed live.[5
