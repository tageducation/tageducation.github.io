Hypothesis

A hypothesis is a statement of fact, or a concept that is made for the sake of debate and then evaluated to see if it is correct.

A hypothesis is usually a speculative assertion or proposal that is made only for the purpose of being tested.

2 Types of Hypothesis

Null Hypothesis: The "no difference" hypothesis is another name for the null hypothesis. The null hypothesis is useful for experiments since it is easy to refute.

Alternative Hypothesis: In a standard statistical experiment, the alternative hypothesis is a statement. It is symbolized as Ha or H1 and is in direct opposition to the null hypothesis.

Example of Hypothesis:

If you get at least 6 hours of sleep, you will do better on tests than if you get less sleep.

There is no significant change in a person’s health during the times when they drink green tea only or root beer only.

Hyperactivity is unrelated to eating sugar.

If you drink coffee before going to bed, then it will take longer to fall asleep.

The growth of the plant improved during the times when it received vitamin-rich water only, as opposed to distilled water only.
