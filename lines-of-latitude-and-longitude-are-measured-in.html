Latitude and Longitude

Any point on Earth can be defined by the intersection of its lines of latitude and longitude. It is measured as the angle from the equator to the Earth’s center, to your position on the Earth’s surface. It is expressed as degrees north or south of the equator (0 o), with the poles at a latitude of 90 o.

Answer:

Lines of latitude are called parallels. Maps are often marked with parallels and meridians, creating a grid. The point in the grid where parallels and meridians intersect is called a coordinate. Coordinates can be used to locate any point on Earth.

Answer:

Lines of latitude are called parallels.

Maps are often marked with parallels and meridians, creating a grid.

The point in the grid where parallels and meridians intersect is called a coordinate.

Coordinates can be used to locate any point on Earth.

The imaginary lines of latitude and longitude intersect each other, forming a grid that covers the Earth. The points of latitude and longitude are called coordinates, and can be used together to locate any point on Earth. Straddling the Equator in Ecuador.

Imaginary Lines of Earth

What are Latitude Lines?

Latitude lines are the imaginary lines from the east to west which divides the northern and the southern hemisphere. These lines were parallel to the equator line. The Earth has a total of 180 latitude lines. An imaginary ring that links all points and shared a parallel is called a circle of latitude.  

What are Longitude Lines?

Longitude lines are also imaginary lines just like the latitude lines but it divides the western and southern hemisphere. The lines were extended from the north to south poles. Prime Meridian line is considered as a longitude line.
