1. What is meant by Jele jele bago quiere and how is this demonstrated in Noli me tangere? Can you cite at least three examples from the novel.


"Jele jele, bago quiere."

Translation

The phrase is a colloquial term used to refer to a person or an attitude demonstrated by a person who is trying to play coy or uninterested.

Jele or hele is the Tagalog word for lullaby or to lull one into submission or sleep.

Bago quiere is a combination of Tagalog and Spanish words. Bago is Tagalog for before while quiere is Spanish for like or want.

A person may try to say that they don't want something but in truth, they want it as much but want more prodding or convincing from the person offering before finally agreeing.

In Tagalog, this can be translated as aayaw-ayaw, pero gusto naman.

Noli Me Tangere, Chapter III: The Dinner

This attitude was presented by certain characters in the novel, Noli Me Tangere. In the third chapter, we recall that a dinner party was thrown by Kapitan Tiyago.

Since Ibarra was sat at the head of the table, it is assumed that he is the guest of honor. However, sits at the other end is considered the second most important guest in the room. As it is customary during the period, Spanish friars are often hailed as important guests. So it was expected that either one of the two friars present will take this seat. Here is where the first example of Jele jele, bago quiere is demonstrated.

In an attempt to seem humble, Padre Sibyla and Padre Damaso insisted on one another to take the seat. In reality, Padre Damaso wanted the seat for himself because he is the family's confessor but Padre Sibyla was the local Parish Friar.

During this back and forth between the two friars who wanted to seem humble, it seemed that Padre Sibyla was about to give in. But at the last minute, he again plays the game of Jele jele, bago quiere by offering the seat of honor to the Lieutenant. The Lieutenant who was genuinely uninterested, unlike the two friars, politely declined.

The third instance of Jele jele, bago quiere was demonstrated by the host himself, Kapitan Tiyago. With the guests no longer paying him mind during the dinner, he was left out of the dinner table and the conversation. Ibarra was the only one who noticed and respectfully invites Kapitan Tiyago to dinner. Kapitan Tiyago, playing the gracious host, declines and tells Ibarra not to worry about him.

25. Get the definition of the following words and then use in sentencels.1. envy 2. victorious3. quiered 4. proclamation 5. repentance​


Envy

-The feeling of wanting to have what someone else has

E.g.

Their exotic vacations inspired envy in/among their friends.

Victorious

-Having won a victory

E.g.

A victorious army

Quiered

-Quiered is use to ask questions, especially in order to check if something is true or correct

E.g.

A few students have queried their marks

Proclamation

-A public or official announcement, especially one dealing with a matter of great importance.

E.g.

Eisenhower signed a proclamation admitting Alaska to the Union

Repentance

-The action of repenting; sincere regret or remorse

E.g.

Each person who turns to God in genuine repentance and faith will be saved
