Directions: Solve the problem showing a complete solution.

1. A 1300-kg car moving at 20m/s hits the fence and comes to a stop in 2 s. what is the average forced acted to the car?

Given:

Mass: 1300-kgAcceleration: 20m/s

Find: F?

Solution: To get the answer use this formula

[tex]\boxed{\sf F=ma}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\sf F=1300kg ×20m/s}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\sf F=26000 N}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\sf F=26000N÷2s }[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\sf F=\red1\red3\red0\red0\red0N}[/tex]

Therefore the the average forced of a car is 13000N

2. A lady tennis player hits an approaching ball with a force of 750 N. If she hits the ball in 0.002, how much impulse is imparted to the tennis ball?

This is a problem with Impulse. The change in momentum of an object caused by an outside or external force acting on it is referred to as an impulse. Impulse = Force and Impulse = Change in Momentum (time). The magnitude of the force experienced by the object is represented by force, while the object's impact time is represented by t.

Given: F= 750N t = 0.002s

Find : Impulse =?

Solution:

Impulse = Force × time

750 N×0.002 s = 1.5 Ns

Therefore the impulse of the tennis ball is 1.5 Ns
