SOLVE THE QUADRATIC EQUATION (SQUARE ROOT METHOD)

The solution that satisfies the quadratic equation (k+7)² = 289 are k = 10 and k = -24.

Definition of Quadratic Equation

A quadratic equation is an equation with a variable to the second power as its highest power term.

The Standard Form of a Quadratic Equation:

ax² + bx + c = 0

where:

x represents an unknown (variable)a, b, and c represent known numbers, where a ≠ 0

How could you solve such a quadratic equation?

There are three basic ways to solve the quadratic equations:

to factor the quadratic equationto taking the square rootsto use the quadratic formula

Usually, you will not be told which method to use. You will have to make that decision yourself.

However, here are some guidelines as to which methods are better in different situations.

Try Factoring first. If the quadratic factors easily, this method is very quick.Look at the side of the equation containing the variable. Is that side a perfect square? If it is, then you can solve the equation by taking the square root of both sides of the equation. Don't forget to include a ± sign in your equation once you have taken the square root.Finally, use the Quadratic Formula. Any quadratic equation can be solved by using the Quadratic Formula.

From the given question, it is asked to use the square root method.

The equation

(k+7)² = 289

Take the square root of both sides of the equation

√(k+7)² = √289

Then, k+7 = ±17

k + 7 = 17 ⇒ k = 17 - 7 ⇒ k = 10k + 7 = -17 ⇒ k = -17 - 7 ⇒ k = -24

Therefore, the following values of s satisfy the equations k = 10 and r = -24.

 Understanding Quadratic Equations

 Also called a polynomial or polynomial equation, a quadratic equation is an equation with a maximum power of two or the order of two. One of the easiest examples of quadratic equations is usually directly practiced during learning such as 2x² + 4x + 3 = 0. The conditions are very different from linear equations with the highest power of one.

 The quadratic form contains the equation with the highest power of two, which is why this type of equation is called a quadratic equation. Square means a number to the power of two, the square root in mathematics of the number x is equal to the number r so that r2 = x. This means that the number r squared is the product of the number itself equal to x.

 

Application of Quadratic Equations to Life

 The form of applying quadratic equations in everyday life is very easy to find, various aspects of life even apply quadratic equations. Like a parabola, a curve to a curve. A rainbow is an example of a graphical form of a quadratic equation, or like an arrow when released, here are some of them.

 -Rainbow Shape

 Being one of the phenomena that until now can amaze people, appears as one of the amazing natural processes. A rainbow appears when it rains, the shape of a rainbow that resembles a curve or a parabola shows that one of God's masterpieces can be applied to a quadratic equation.

 -Kick Ball Direction

 If you pay close attention when watching a football match, you will definitely see how the movement of kicking the ball goes far. The process of the ball sliding in that direction forms a curve and can also be called a parabola. The movement is one of the applications of the quadratic equation, the force of the ball kick is an influential variable.

 -Bow Arrow Movement

 Archery is necessary and requires high concentration when doing it, because it can't be done randomly. When released and if observed properly it will be very visible to form a curve until it hits the target. The direction of the arrow that is released is one form of a quadratic equation.
