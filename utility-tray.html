TOOL USED TO DRAIN WATER

Answer:

B. Colander

The tool used to drain excess water after washing vegetables is Colander. A colander is one of the utensils use in the kitchen for cooking. The colander is a metal bowl where in it has small holes that helps to drain water to rinse vegetables and drain pastas.

Explanation:

The Colander is very useful for the cook because it helps the cook to drain water easily in rinsing vegetables or in pastas. It is a kind of bowl but made in metal with many small holes. Colander can also be made by stainless, plastic, silicon and ceramic. The Colander is somehow related to the strainer but the difference is, colander has more largely holes than the strainer.  

History of Colander

The early other spelling of collander are "colyndore and cullinder". The word "Colander" came from the Latin word "colum" which means sieve or filter. And from the word colum, a verb word colare came, which means "flow, strain and filter".

24. Describe each of the following according to makeDutch Oven ________________________________________________________Grater ________________________________________________________Utility Tray ________________________________________________________Tube Center Pan _______________________________________________________​


Answer:

A Dutch oven (not to be confused with masonry oven) is a thick-walled cooking pot with a tight-fitting lid. Dutch ovens are usually made of seasoned cast iron; however, some Dutch ovens are instead made of cast aluminium, or ceramic. Some metal varieties are enameled rather than being seasoned, and these are sometimes called French ovens. Dutch ovens have been used as cooking vessels for hundreds of years.

A grater is a kitchen utensil that is usually made from metal (and sometimes ceramic or even wood), with sharp perforations or protrusions used to shred food. Graters come in various sizes: from those with larger perforations which are often used to shred cheese and vegetables, to the very fine graters and micro planes that can be used to zest citrus fruit. Micro planes are preferred by many for their efficiency in producing finely grated food. Fresh ginger or wasabi graters are usually made from porcelain or bamboo, and use spikes or ridges to tear those fibrous rhizomes (they are not clogged with fibres like conventional graters).
