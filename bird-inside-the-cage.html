1. a bird inside the cage. what do you think is the difference if the bird is inside the cage and if it is outside?​


Answer:

Is your question based on the feelings of the bird or their awareness? All birds are aware that they are in a cage, however how they feel about being in a cage is the issue. I have been given wild birds that have been injured and the trauma of being caged is as much a threat to their health as the actual injury. I can tell this is the case because as time passes and the bird realizes that it is safe, it’s anxiety diminishes. On the other hand, birds that are raised in a cage view the cage as “safe territory” and this is why they voluntarily go back in . My various birds wait close by while I am cleaning their cages and once I am finished they zoom back in there. I always have treats placed around the cage for them to enjoy. In order to maintain this perception of safe territory, always move slowly when placing food or water inside their space.

16. caged bird critique​


Answer:

"Caged Bird" was published in Maya Angelou's 1983 poetry collection Shaker, Why Don't You Sing? The poem describes the opposing experiences between two birds: one bird is able to live in nature as it pleases, while a different caged bird suffers in captivity. Due to its profound suffering, the caged bird sings, both to cope with its circumstances and to express its own longing for freedom. Using the extended metaphor of the two birds, Angelou paints a critical portrait of oppression in which she illuminates the privilege and entitlement of the un-oppressed, and conveys the simultaneous experience of suffering and emotional resilience. In particular, the poem's extended metaphor can be seen as portraying the experience of being a member of the African American communit.
