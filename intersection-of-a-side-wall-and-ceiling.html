22. The ceiling in a hallway 10m wide is in the shape of a semi-ellipse and is 9m high at the center and 6m high at the side walls. Find the height of the ceiling 2m from either wall.​


[tex] \large \mathcal{ANSWER:} [/tex]

[tex] \boxed{8.4\textsf{ meters}} [/tex]

[tex] \large \mathcal{SOLUTION:} [/tex]

[tex] \begin{array}{l} \textsf{Let the center of the hallway be at the origin.} \\ \\ \quad\circ\:\textsf{Center of the semi-ellipse:} (0, 6) \\ \quad \circ\: \textsf{Width of the semi-ellipse or length of the} \\ \quad \quad \textsf{horizontal major axis: } 2a = 10 \Rightarrow a = 5 \\ \quad \circ \: \textsf{Distance from the center of semi-ellipse to} \\ \quad \quad \textsf{the co-vertex or highest point on the} \\ \quad \quad \textsf{ceiling: } b = 9 - 6 = 3 \\ \\ \textsf{Equation of ellipse with center at }(0, 6): \\ \\ \quad \dfrac{(x - h)^2}{a^2} + \dfrac{(y - k)^2}{b^2} = 1 \\ \\ \quad \dfrac{x^2}{5^2} + \dfrac{(y - 6)^2}{3^2} = 1 \\ \\ \textsf{At }x = 3, \\ \\ \quad \dfrac{9}{25} + \dfrac{(y - 6)^2}{9} = 1 \\ \\ \begin{aligned} \quad \dfrac{(y-6)^2}{9} &= 1 - \dfrac{9}{25} \\ \\ \dfrac{(y-6)^2}{9} &= \dfrac{16}{25} \\ \\ \sqrt{\dfrac{(y-6)^2}{9}} &= \sqrt{\dfrac{16}{25}} \\ \\ \dfrac{y - 6}{3} &= \pm\dfrac{4}{5} \\ \\ y - 6 &= \pm \dfrac{12}{5} \end{aligned} \\ \\ \quad \quad \:\:y = 6 \pm \dfrac{12}{5} \\ \\ \textsf{We want the value of }y\textsf{ to be greater than 6 so } \\ \quad y = 6 + \dfrac{12}{5} = 8.4\textsf{ m. (Answer)} \end{array} [/tex]
