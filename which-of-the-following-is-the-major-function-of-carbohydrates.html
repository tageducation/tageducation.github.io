FUNCTIONS OF BIOMOLECULES:

Carbohydrates:

1.Disaccharides in carbohydrates ,mainly glucose is used in the process of producing energy which meet the requirements of our body. It is through the process of glycolysis(break down of glucose into to pyruvate molecules) and the energy released is in the form of ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate)

2.Pentose sugars(ALSO CARBOHYDRATES) like Ribose and deoxy ribose are involved in the formation of nucleic acids such as RNA and DNA.

Lipids:

Storage of energy for long-term use (e.g. triglycerides)

Hormonal roles (e.g. steroids such as oestrogen and testosterone)

Insulation – both thermal (triglycerides) and electrical (sphingolipids)

Protection of internal organs (e.g. triglycerides and waxes)

Structural components of cells (e.g. phospholipids and cholesterol)

Proteins:

Histone protein helps in the coiling of DNA during interphase.

Rhodopsin or visual purple in the eyes helps in converting messages into neural signals and send it to the brain thhrough opticakl nerve.

Actin,Myosin,Tropomyosin and Meromyosin helps in muscle contraction and locomotion.

Proteinized enzymes acts as catalyst in many bio-chemical reactions and also helps in lysis(breaking).

Antibodies are proteinaceous substances,which help in defense mechanism .

Collagen is a structural protein which is present inside the bones .

Nucleic acids:

Nucleic acids are DNA and RNA.The former conveys genetic information from one generation to another and the latter reads the code from DNA and passes the messages to respective functional sites(m-RNA).

28. which of the following is the major function of carbohydrates?1.Structural framework2.storage3. energy productiona.1 only. b. 2 only. c. 3 only. d.1&3 only​


Of the given options, the major function of carbohydrates is c. 3 only.

Details of explanation:

Carbohydrates are organic compounds that the body needs in large amounts.

Based on the length of the digestion process, there are two types of carbohydrates:

1. Simple carbohydrates are carbohydrates that are digested quickly by the body. These carbohydrates will quickly send glucose into the bloodstream and provide energy quickly to the body.

Examples of simple carbohydrates are sugars, both natural and added sugars in foods.

2. Complex carbohydrates are carbohydrates that take a longer time to digest. Complex carbohydrates will provide energy to the body slowly and steadily, not as fast as simple carbohydrates.

An example of a food that contains complex carbohydrates is brown rice.

Based on their origin, there are three types of carbohydrates:

1. Fiber is a complex carbohydrate from plants. Examples of foods that contain fiber are nuts and processed foods from wheat.

2. Starch is a complex carbohydrate that also comes from plants. Examples of foods that contain starch are cassava, tubers, and bananas.

3. Sugar

All sugars are carbohydrates. There are two types of sugar, namely natural sugars contained in foods such as fruits, and added sugars that are added to foods during the manufacturing process, such as cakes and syrups.

Carbohydrates have many functions. However, the main function of carbohydrates for the body is as a source of energy production. Carbohydrates that enter the digestive process will be broken down by the body and provide energy to do activities.

Every 1 gram of carbohydrates will produce 4 calories for the body. A calorie is a unit of energy to measure the amount of energy produced by food.

Based on the explanation above, the correct answer to the question of the main function of carbohydrates is c. 3 only, energy production.

Carbohydrate is actually also useful as a reserve energy store for the body. However, option number 2, storage, does not explain what storage is meant.
