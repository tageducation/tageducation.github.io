4. Describe the religious developments during the 19th century philippines as explained by schumacher.


Answer:

The following are the developments that I have read from the work written by Schumacher. The Philippines exported great amounts of rice due to the increase in demand which resulted in great profits to provinces that focuses on agriculture which flourished the economy of the country. Filipinos were deprived of career positions because of the rampant graft and corruption within the bureaucracy. In line with the corrupt governance, they were unable to provide the basic needs for some sectors as a result of the officials mostly pocketing the funds. The taxation system also affected the modernization of the infrastructures as well as some equipment needed for agricultural progress. The cultural developments in the country greatly benefitted from the educational improvements due to the return of the Jesuits instituting primary and secondary schools for the Filipinos. Having educational access for the Filipinos has enlightened them about literature science and philosophy as well as principles in human dignity, justice, and equality, thus, opening their eyes to the reality of their situation under Spanish control. Due to the rise of nationalism among the ilustrados, they have become anti-friar or against the Catholic religion that was run by friars who were abusive of their authority. Most of the great personalities or heroes during the 19th century had their conflicts against the Spanish friars that have led to the execution of GomBurZa. However, later on, Filipino priests were then handed the authority to the parishes due to the lack of friars by the end of the century which gave way to the rights of the Filipino clergymen, serving them justice.

7. describe the 19th century Philippines understanding?​


Answer:

The 19th century

By the late 18th century, political and economic changes in Europe were finally beginning to affect Spain and, thus, the Philippines. Important as a stimulus to trade was the gradual elimination of the monopoly enjoyed by the galleon to Acapulco. The last galleon arrived in Manila in 1815, and by the mid-1830s Manila was open to foreign merchants almost without restriction. The demand for Philippine sugar and abaca (hemp) grew apace, and the volume of exports to Europe expanded even further after the completion of the Suez Canal in 1869.
