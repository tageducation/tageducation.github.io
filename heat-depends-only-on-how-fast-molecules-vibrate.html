25. True or False1. Heat depends only on how fast molecules vibrate2. Joule is a unit of heat energy3. We measure temperature with a thermometer4. The faster molecules vibrate, the more heat they give off5. Temperature measures the average kinetic energy per molecule​


Heat

1. Heat depends only on how fast molecules vibrate

False, because the three characteristics that affect heat are the change in temperature, the mass of the system, and the substance's phase.

2. Joule is a unit of heat energy [True]

3. We measure temperature with a thermometer [True]

4. The faster molecules vibrate, the more heat they give off [True]

5. Temperature measures the average kinetic energy per molecule​ [True]

The energy that is transferred from an object with a higher temperature to an object with a lower temperature is called heat. While the temperature states the level of heat of an object. Objects have a certain level of heat because they contain thermal energy. As you have done in the investigation activity that a glass of water and a bucket of water at the same temperature have different heat energy.

In general, the temperature of an object will increase if it gains heat. On the other hand, the temperature of the object will decrease if heat is released from the object. Hot water if left for a long time will cool to near room temperature. This indicates that some of the heat is released by the object into the environment.

You already know that the increase in temperature by heat is affected by the mass of the object. To raise the same temperature, 200 g of water requires more heat than 100 g of water.

As a form of energy, in SI heat has units of joules (J). The popular units of heat (often used in the field of nutrition) are calories and kilocalories.

Heat TransferConduction is the transfer of heat through a material without the movement of particles of the material.Convection is the transfer of heat from one place to another along with the motion of the particles of the object.Radiation is the transfer of heat without the need for a medium.
