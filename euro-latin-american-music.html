1. Euro-latin American music


Answer:

Characterized the music of euro latin americanThere are musics that characterized the euro latin american music.

The musical traditions of Mexico, Central America, and the Spanish and Portuguese-colonized portions of South America and the Caribbean are referred to as Latin American music. These traditions reflect the region's distinct mix of Native American, African, and European influences, which have evolved over time.

Carnivals, both colorful and exotic, as well as a range of dancing styles, have had a tremendous impact on Latin American music. Fanfarras, brass instruments associated with fanfare, and nearly invariably a samba band can be found at carnivals.

The term "folk and popular music" in Latin America refers to a variety of musical styles and genres that have developed over time in certain countries or areas. These styles are rooted in Latin America's indigenous, European, and African cultures; the exact mix of influences varies by country and region.
