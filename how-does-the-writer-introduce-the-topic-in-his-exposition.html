12. In which part of the exposition does the writer usually put his/her stand?A.IntroductionB.BodyC.ConclusionD.Opinion​


In the exposition text the author places his stance and opinion in the thesis section which is the introductory part, so the answer is

A. Introduction

Further Explanation:

Exposition Text

Exposition text is a form of text or writing that contains information and knowledge. In general, the exposition text itself has the aim of providing an explanation or description of an idea, main idea, opinion, information, or knowledge to the reader without intending to influence it.

Thesis or Introduction

Thesis or introduction is part of the structure of the exposition text. This section is the first part as well as the opening of the exposition text. This section is called the opening and contains the author's opinion.

Thesis can also be referred to as a statement of opinion. Before starting to write an exposition text, the writer must determine the purpose of the exposition text first. The thesis itself can be understood as a part that has a function to present an introduction to the issue, problem, main idea, to the author's general view of the topic or problem being discussed.

Explanation of other answers:

B. Body

In the exposition text the body or body of the text is called an argumentation. The series of arguments can be understood as opinions in the form of logical reasons, information according to the findings, facts that occur, to statements of experts. Arguments support statements or opinions with arguments based on strong facts and data.

C. Conclusion

In the exposition text, the conclusion section is called reaffirmation. The last part of the structure of the exposition text is reaffirmation. After the writer conveys the issue or problem and it has been supported by various reasons or opinions that are in accordance with the facts, the next conclusion or conclusion will be presented. The purpose of the reaffirmation itself is to provide confirmation of the initial opinion as well as to present conclusions and suggestions on the overall problem discussed.

D. Opinion

The Opinion section in the exposition text is part of the thesis where in that section there are opinions or opinions that are the subject of discussion in an exposition text.
