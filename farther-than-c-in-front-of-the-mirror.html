Concave and Convex Mirrors

A spherical mirror that is concave has a reflecting surface that is curled inward, whereas a convex mirror has a reflecting surface that is bulged outward.

8. A concave mirror may form an image which is following is incorrect?

A. real, erect and magnified

Supporting details:

Concave mirrors can create both actual and fictitious pictures. They can be upright (if fictitious) or inverted (if genuine), behind the mirror (if fictitious), in front of the mirror (if real), or even the exact same size as the item.

9. The image in a convex mirror is always

A. real, erect and diminished

Supporting details:

Regardless of the object's position, the image created by a convex mirror is always upright, fictitious, and decreased.

10. The image formed in a plane mirror is

A. at the same distance in front of the mirror as the object is

mirror.

Supporting details:

The object and its image appear to be equally distant from the mirror because the image created by a flat mirror is imaginary, upright, and the same size as the thing.

Additional Information:

Concave Mirrors

The picture is reversed and, in the center, when the object is far from the mirror. Light truly focuses at the place of the image, therefore it is real.

The image is at infinity when the focal point is that object. The picture behind the mirror becomes virtual and upright as the object travels nearer the mirror inside the focal point.

At first, the virtual picture is much larger and farther away than the actual thing, but as the object gets closer to the mirror, the virtual image becomes closer as well and gets smaller.

Convex Mirrors

Convex mirrors always provide a virtual picture that is situated behind the mirror. The reflection is erect and in the center when the object is far from the mirror.

The picture increases until it is the same height as the object as it moves toward the mirror with the object.

19. in an concave mirror, what is the orientation of a farther than a center of curvature?​


Answer:

If the object is at a point farther from the mirror than the center of curvature, the image is real (i.e., it is formed directly by the reflected rays), inverted, and smaller than the object. If the object is at the center of curvature, the image is the same size as the object and is real and inverted.
