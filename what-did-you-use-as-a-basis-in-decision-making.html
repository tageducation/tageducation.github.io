1. Yes, I am in favor with Anti Cyber-Bullying Act of 2015.

My stand is to support this act or program. This is to help young people participate in creating a positive internet environment. In the Philippines itself, bullying is growing with the development of the internet. If in the past bullying was done verbally or physically, now it is rife in students on social media which in the end will affect the child's goal of going to school.

2. I see the important basis of decision-making is the guide of every social media. For example, to launch the Anti Cyber-Bullying Act, Tik Tok and the community have launched a guide that informs what bullying is, what steps need to be taken if you see bullying, tips for using the internet smartly, and taking advantage of the security features available on various websites. internet application to ensure the safety and convenience of users.

3. Some of my advices that I would give those people commenting on social media posts are:

Don't let your finger go faster than your mind, THINK BEFORE CLICKDon't use harsh, provocative, or racist / discrimination words on social mediaDon't post fake articles or statuses or hate speechDo not copy and paste articles or images that have copyright, and leave relevant comments.Don't interfere in other people's lives, don't judge others as if you know someone's life better.

KNOWING THE ACT OF ANTI CYBER-BULLYING:

The Anti Cyber-Bullying Act 2015 in the Philippines is a law that is implemented on students at schools in the Philippines, which covers what is said on social media. This law aims to protect students or children in kindergartens, primary and secondary schools, and learning centers (collectively, “Schools”) from acts of bullying. This law requires schools to adopt policies that can address the presence of bullying in their respective educational institutions.
