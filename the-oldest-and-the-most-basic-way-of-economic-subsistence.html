21. What are the distinguishing characteristics of the economic systems labelled subsistence, commercial, and planned? Are they mutually exclusive, or can they coexist within a single political unit?


Answer:

What are the distinguishing characteristics of the economic systems labeled subsistence, commercial, planned, and transitional? Are they mutually exclusive, or can they coexist within a single political unit?

1. Subsistence: Goods and services are created for the use of the producers and their kinship groups

2. Commercial: Producers or their agents freely market their goods and services, and market competition is the primary force shaping production decisions and distributions

3. Planned: Associated with communist-controlled societies, producers or their agents dispose of goods and services through governmental agencies that control both supply and price

-None of these three economies are mutually exclusive

4. Transitional: Economies in formerly communist countries that are in the process of shifting away from central planning towards free market exchange

-This is the only economic system that is independent

How is intensive subsistence agriculture distinguished from extensive subsistence cropping?

-Intensive subsistence agriculture: Involves the cultivation of small land holdings through the expenditure of great amounts of labor per acre

-Extensive subsistence agriculture: Involves large areas of land and minimal labor input per hectare
