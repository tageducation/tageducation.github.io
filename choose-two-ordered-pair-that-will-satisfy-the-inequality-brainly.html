Step-by-Step Explanation:

How To Determine If An Ordered Pair Is A Solution To A Linear Inequality?

An ordered pair contains x-coordinate which is known as abscissa and y-coordinate which is known as ordinate. The two values are written in a fixed order inside the pair of parenthesis. It is used to locate a point on the Cartesian plane.

To determine if an ordered pair is a solution to a linear inequality, you have to plug in the given values of x and y into the inequality and simplify. If it satisfied the inequality, then the ordered pair is a solution. If not, then it is not a solution.

Solution:

Let us now find out the ordered pairs that satisfy the inequality above.

1. y < x + 3

(2,5) = 5 < 2 + 3 = 5 < 5

(5,2) = 2 < 5 + 3 = 2 < 8 ✔

(-5,-5) = -5 < -5 + 3 = -5 < -2 ✔

(4,0) = 0 < 4 + 3 = 0 < 7 ✔

2. 3x + y > 10

(-1,2) = 3(-1) + 2 > 10 = -3 + 2 > 10 = -1 > 10

(1,10) = 3(10) + 1 > 10 = 30 + 1 > 10 = 31 > 10 ✔

(3,1) = 3(3) + 1 > 10 = 9 + 1 > 10 = 10 > 10

(3,3) = 3(3) + 3 > 10 = 9 + 3 > 10 = 12 > 10 ✔

3. 2y + x ≥ 5

(1,2) = 2(2) + 1 ≥ 5 = 4 + 1 ≥ 5 = 5 ≥ 5 ✔

(0,4) = 2(4) + 0 ≥ 5 = 8 + 0 ≥ 5 = 8 ≥ 5 ✔

(2,2) = 2(2) + 2 ≥ 5 = 4 + 2 ≥ 5 = 6 ≥ 5 ✔

(-1,-5) = 2(-5) + (-1) ≥ 5 = -10 + (-1) ≥ 5 = -11 ≥ 5

4. 3x - 2y ≤ 10

(5,1) = 3(5) - 2(1) ≤ 10 = 15 - 2 ≤ 10 = 13 ≤ 10

(0,-5) = 3(0) - 2(-5) ≤ 10 = 0 + 10 ≤ 10 = 10 ≤ 10 ✔

(-1,-6) = 3(-1) - 2(-6) ≤ 10 = -3 + 12 ≤ 10 = 9 ≤ 10 ✔

(10,2) = 3(10) - 2(2) ≤ 10 = 30 - 4 ≤ 10 = 26 ≤ 10

5. 5x + 3y > 15

(2,1) = 5(2) + 3(1) > 15 = 10 + 3 > 15 = 13 > 15

(-3,1) = 5(-3) + 3(1) > 15 = -15 + 3 > 15 = -12 > 3

(0,6) = 5(0) + 3(6) > 15 = 0 + 18 > 15 = 18 > 15 ✔

(2,2) = 5(2) + 3(2) > 15 = 10 + 6 > 15 = 16 > 15 ✔
