13. What is the specific lesson or issue in your track and strand that has piqued your interest the most?​


The specific lessons or issues in my track and strand that have piqued my most interest are:

1. Tired of crying, I learned to be happy with simple things

2. I have no one to rely on but myself

3. I am appreciated if I can give, not beg

EXPLANATION:

1. Tired of crying, I learned to be happy with simple things

From the beginning I realized that I didn't have much to be happy about. Therefore, I only have two choices. Waiting to have more things such as wealth and more harmonious family relationships or learning to be happy just as it is. The first is not an impossible choice. However, there are times when I get tired of hoping. Therefore, it is quite logical that I choose the latter.

2. I have no one to rely on but myself

Actually, I'm not anti to asking others for help. It's just that, I will do it only after there is no other way. I only take it when I really have to. Why? Because the obstacles in my life are far more numerous and heavier than other people. Therefore, I don't want to trouble them too much. Not sure if they will be able to help me. I don't want to be disappointed because I have high hopes on other people.

3. I am appreciated if I can give, not beg

This is my biggest motivation to achieve success in the future. I have experienced for myself how asking for help from others often leads to disaster for myself. Some help by expecting something in return. Some help accompanied by endless ridicule. There were also those who didn't even reach out, they only cursed. Even so, of course there are those who sincerely do good and are sincere in helping. However, it is different if I can be the party that provides assistance. People will appreciate me more.

Wondering what track to take to achieve your dream course? If you have this in mind, you’re probably an incoming senior high school student. So, here’s a piece of advice: choose your track and strand wisely. Doing this will urge you to pursue college or to start your career shortly after graduation. Are you more of the latter? If yes, here’s the best tip for you: take the Technical-Vocational-Livelihood or TVL track.

So, what’s the TVL track?

The subjects under TVL track strictly follow TESDA’s rules, ensuring workplace-ready students. In fact, every TVL strand helps students acquire job-ready skills. The TVL track does not only guarantee skills but also ensures employment, backed by TESDA certifications: Certificate of Competency (COC) and National Certifications (NC). So, for students who want to get hired right away in the field of agriculture, electronics, or trading, TVL track is for you.
