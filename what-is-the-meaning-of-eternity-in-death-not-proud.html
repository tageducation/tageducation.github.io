8. DEATH BE NOT BE PROUD SUMMARY


Answer:

Death be not proud, though some have called thee

Mighty and dreadful, for thou art not so;

For those whom thou think'st thou dost overthrow

Die not, poor Death, nor yet canst thou kill me.

From rest and sleep, which but thy pictures be,

Much pleasure; then from thee much more must flow,

And soonest our best men with thee do go,

Rest of their bones, and soul's delivery.

Thou art slave to Fate, Chance, kings, and desperate men,

And dost with poison, war, and sickness dwell;

And poppy, or charms can make us sleep as well,

And better than thy stroke; why swell'st thou then?

One short sleep past, we wake eternally,

And death shall be no more; Death, thou shalt die.

13. What makes the poem "Death, be not proud" unique in terms of the way it was written?​


Answer:

"Death, be not proud" is a sonnet written by John Donne that is considered to be one of the most powerful and unique expressions of the theme of death in English literature. The poem is part of a series of holy sonnets that Donne wrote, which express his religious beliefs and the struggle between the human desire for immortality and the finality of death.

One of the unique features of the poem is the way Donne uses the metaphor of death as a person or an entity that can be defeated. He uses strong and powerful language to assert that death is not something to be feared but rather something that can be overcome through faith in God. This is a unique perspective as death is often portrayed as something inevitable and final.

Another unique aspect of the poem is the way Donne uses vivid imagery and metaphor to convey his message. He compares death to a "quaint and curious" slave, a "bare, forked animal" and a "slave" to the soul. These comparisons serve to demystify and diminish the power of death in the minds of the readers.

In terms of its form, it's a sonnet, which is a traditional poetic form that is characterized by 14 lines and a specific rhyme scheme. However, Donne's use of religious themes and his unique perspective on death make this sonnet stand out from the traditional love sonnets of the time.

Overall, "Death, be not proud" is a unique and powerful poem that explores the theme of death through vivid imagery, metaphor, and a unique perspective on death that is both personal and religious.
