1. 1-4 years old help rendered in the family and community​


1-4 years old help rendered in the family and community​:

1. Obey parents so that the house is orderly

2. Assist in Simple Tasks Both at Home and Community

3. Be Polite to All Family Members and Community

4. Respect Both Parents and Love the Younger Ones

EXPLANATION:

The role of children aged 1-4 years in the family and society is certainly not the same according to their age level in each family rule. This is what every child needs to understand, considering that they will grow and adjust outside the home. Rules in the family help children to understand the basic rules of good behavior and which are not good or violate the rules at home and in society.

1. Obey parents so that the house is orderly

The first role of children under five in the family is to obey the leadership of their parents. This is very natural, because in every family member there must be one top leader. But usually the holder of the leadership. All important decisions for children will be determined by parents. The leadership style in the family can usually also affect the role of children in the family. Most parents may continue to make decisions for their children into adulthood or even after adulthood.

2. Assist in Simple Tasks Both at Home and Community

The role of children in the family is no less important is to help do some simple things that can lighten the burden on parents. Call it starting from helping parents keep the house clean by helping sweep or clean their own room. Thus, indirectly the little one is trained to be responsible from an early age.

3. Be Polite to All Family Members and Community

The role of children in the family that is no less important is to be polite not only to parents, but also to all family members. A polite attitude towards family members shows that your little one respects his family, especially those who are older. On the other hand, children who are able to be polite hold the values ​​and norms that are taught well.

4. Respect Both Parents and Love the Younger Ones

Moms and Dads have spent a lot of energy and time raising their little ones. So, a child should respect his parents. The role of children in the family is not only limited to both parents. More than that, every child also needs to respect and be responsible to other family members, whether older or younger. For example, children need to maintain good relations and get along with their older siblings.

Training our children with the right manners is the first step in being a good role model. A child will always learn from the adult so we should keep in mind that whatever actions we do will always be a reflection of our children.

1 - 4 Years Old (Infants, Toddlers, Preschoolers)

Skills such as taking the first step, smiling for the first time, and waving “bye-bye” are called developmental milestones. Developmental milestones are things most children can do by a certain age. Children reach milestones in how they play, learn, speak, behave, and move

5 - 8 Years Old (Middle Childhood)

Middle childhood brings many changes in a child’s life. By this time, children can dress themselves, catch a ball more easily using only their hands, and tie their shoes. Having independence from family becomes more important now.

9 - 12 Years Old (Middle Childhood and Young Teens)

Growing independence from the family and interest in friends might be obvious by now. Healthy friendships are very important but peer pressure can become strong during this time. Children who feel good about themselves are more able to resist negative peer pressure and make better choices for themselves.

13 - present (Teenagers to Adulthood)

This is a time of many physical, mental, emotional, and social changes. Hormones change as puberty begins. Most boys grow facial and pubic hair and their voices deepen. Most girls grow pubic hair and breasts and start their period.
