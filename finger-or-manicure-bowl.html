5. Discription of Finger bowl ​


Answer:

A finger bowl is a small bowl used for rinsing the fingers, typically between courses of a formal meal. It is usually made of glass, crystal, or porcelain and filled with warm water and a slice of lemon or other fragrant herb. The bowl is passed around the table after the main course and before dessert, and each guest dips their fingers in the water to rinse off any residue. The finger bowl is then removed, and guests dry their hands on a napkin. Finger bowls are considered a formal table setting tradition and are still used in some formal dining occasions, although they are not commonly used in casual or modern dining.
