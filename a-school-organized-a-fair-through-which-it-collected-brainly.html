24. Fair discrimination definition brainly?


Fair discrimination refers to generally acceptable kinds of discrimination laws.

These are the four types of fair discrimination:

1. Discrimination based on affirmative action

This is designed to promote fairness or equity in the employment of groups or individual in terms of race, gender, and disability.

2. Discrimination based on the inherent requirements of a job

This refers to the inherent nature of the job and its corresponding qualifications.  For example, individuals who have poor eyesight are not qualified to become a pilot.

3. Fair compulsory discrimination by law

The law prohibits the employment of children who are under 15 years, and pregnant women who are near there giving of birth.

4. Discrimination based on productivity

The law allows the employer’s fair giving of performance merit based on productivity.
