3. What happens to the temperature of air when altitude increases? *


Answer:

Temperature decreases

Explanation:

As you increase in elevation, there is less air above you thus the pressure decreases. As the pressure decreases, air molecules spread out further (i.e. air expands), and the temperature decreases. If the humidity is at 100 percent (because it's snowing), the temperature decreases more slowly with height.

Why is it that air temperature decreases as the altitude increases?

The basic answer is that the farther away you get from the earth, the thinner the atmosphere gets. The total heat content of a system is directly related to the amount of matter present, so it is cooler at higher elevations.

8. What happens to the temperature of air when altitude increases?


What happens to the temperature of air when altitude increases?

Air pressure is  inversely proportional to altitude.  In other words, as the  altitude increases, the air pressure decreases.

This is due to the fact that air molecules are continuously  being drawn down (to the middle of the Earth) by gravity.  As a result, air molecules are closely packed near  the surface of the earth, relative to some higher altitudes.  

One of the best real-time situations we can observe related to  this is that mountaineers bleed as they hit a very high altitude.  This is because, as the pressure decreases, the veins inside the body burst  due to the difference in pressure from inside the body and outside the atmosphere.
