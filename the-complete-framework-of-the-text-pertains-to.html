29. what aspect of multimodal text pertains to volume, pitch and sound effect?​


Multimodal text refers to the use of multiple modes, or forms, of communication in a text, such as written language, images, and audio. The aspect of multimodal text that pertains to volume, pitch, and sound effects is the audio mode.

Volume refers to the loudness of a sound, and it can be used in multimodal text to convey different emotions or emphasis. For example, a loud sound can be used to indicate anger or excitement, while a soft sound can indicate sadness or whispering.

Pitch refers to the highness or lowness of a sound, and it can also be used to convey different emotions or emphasis. For example, a high-pitched sound can indicate surprise or a question, while a low-pitched sound can indicate a statement or authority.

Sound effects are non-verbal sounds that are added to a text to enhance the meaning or create a specific atmosphere. For example, a sound effect of a car honking can indicate a busy city scene, or the sound of a bird chirping can indicate a peaceful countryside.

In multimodal text, these aspects of sound can be used to convey meaning and create a specific atmosphere, in addition to written language or images. They can also be used to enhance the overall meaning of the text, making it more engaging and memorable for the audience.

In written texts, these aspects of sound are often represented through onomatopoeias, such as "beep" "buzz" "ring" etc. These words that imitate sounds, are specifically used to indicate the sound in the written text, but it's different when it comes to the multimodal text, where the sound is audible.

In summary, volume, pitch, and sound effects in multimodal text pertain to the audio mode of communication, which can be used to convey emotions, emphasis, and atmosphere, and enhance the overall meaning of the text.
