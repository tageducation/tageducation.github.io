There can be two ways in measure the strength of your upper extremity – first id the Jamar dynamometer and the other is the push-up test. Jamar dynamometer is recommended for measuring grip strength, for other muscle groups, handheld dynamometry is recommended. Manual muscle testing may also be used in case of limited wrist strength or for total upper limb muscle strength.  

The push-up test is a basic fitness test used by all coaches, trainers, and athletes all over the world in order to assess upper body fitness and to monitor their or their trainee’s progress during strength and fitness training. This test will help you compare your own upper body muscular endurance to others of your same age and gender and track your fitness program over time.

The most widely practices exercises for upper and lower body muscular strength testing are the bench press and leg press. Other active strength tests include the arm curl, latissimus dorsi pull down, knee extension and knee curl.
