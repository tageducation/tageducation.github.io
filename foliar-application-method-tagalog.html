1. foliar application method explanation tagalog po. EPP po yan ​


Answer:

Filipino Translation:

Ang isang foliar application ay nangangahulugan na ikaw ay nagpapakain ng mga halaman sa pamamagitan ng paglalagay ng likidong pataba nang direkta sa mga dahon kumpara sa sa lupa. Ang pagsipsip ay nagaganap sa pamamagitan ng kanilang stomata at kanilang epidermis. Ang transportasyon ay kadalasang mas mabilis sa pamamagitan ng stomata, ngunit ang kabuuang pagsipsip ay maaaring kasing lakas sa pamamagitan ng epidermis.

English Translation:

A foliar application means that you are feeding plants by applying liquid fertilizer directly to the leaves as opposed to in the soil. The absorption takes place through their stomata and their epidermis. Transport is usually faster through the stomata, but total absorption may be as great through the epidermis.

2. ANO ANG IBIG SABIHIN NG BASAL APPLICATION METHOD BROADCASTING METHOD FOLIAR APPLICATION METHOD RING METHOD & SIDE-DRESSING METHOD ANSWER IN TAGALOG


Answer:

Basal Application Method - Paglalagay ng abono sa pamamagitan ng paghahalo ng pataba sa lupa bago itanim ang halaman. Kapag ang halaman naman ay itatanim sa paso, ang pataba ay inihahalo muna sa lupa bago itanim ang halaman.

Broadcasting Method - Ang Pataba ay ikinakalat sa ibabaw ng lupa. Kadalasang ginagawa ito sa mga palayan at maisan.

Foliar Application Method - Ito ay ginagawa sa pamamagitan ng pagdidilig o pag-iispray ng solusyong abono sa mga dahon ng halaman.

Ring Method - Humukay nang pabilog sa paligid ng tanim na may layong kalahati hanggang isang pulgada mula sa puno o tangkay. Ilagay ang pataba sa lugar na hinukay. Takpan ng lupa ang pataba.

Side-Dressing Method - Ang pataba ay ilalagay sa lupa na hindi gaanong malapit sa ugat ng halaman sa pamamagitan ng isang kagamitang nakalaan para rito. Ito ay kadalasang ginagawa sa mga halaman na nakatanim nang pahilera at hindi paisa-isa.

21. foliar application method​


Answer:

foliar application means that you are feeding plants by applying liquid fertilizer directly to the leaves as opposed to in the soil. Foliar applications are definitely good for plants, but they're not a good substitute for healthy soil. The best time to use foliar applications is when a plant is lacking in nutrients.

The absorption takes place through their stomata and their epidermis. Transport is usually faster through the stomata, but total absorption may be as great through the epidermis. Plants are also able to absorb nutrients through their bark.

The purpose of foliar feeding is not to replace soil fertilization. Supplying a plant’s major nutrient needs (nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium) is the most effective and economic via soil application. However, a foliar application has proven to be an excellent method of supplying plant requirements for secondary nutrients (calcium, magnesium, and sulfur) and micronutrients (zinc, manganese, iron, copper, boron, and molybdenum), while supplementing N-P-K needs for short or critical growth three-stage periods. However, a foliar application has been shown to avoid the problem of leaching-out in soils and prompts a quick reaction in the plant.

For all landscape and garden plants, the major pathway for nutrient uptake is by way of the roots. Leaves have a waxy cuticle, which restricts the entry of water, nutrients, and other substances into the plant. To a limited extent, nutrients applied to leaves can be absorbed and used by the plant, but for the major nutrients (nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium) the quantity absorbed at any one time is small relative to plant needs. That means that foliar application of these three nutrients can only supply a tiny fraction of the total needed by the plant, so a foliar application should be considered a supplement to regular soil application of these nutrients.
