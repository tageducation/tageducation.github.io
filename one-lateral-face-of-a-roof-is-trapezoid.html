2. One lateral face of the roof of the school building is trapezoid in shape. One of thebases of this trapezoid is 4 m longer than the other base. Find the length of the twobases if the median measures 18 m.


One lateral face of the roof of the school building is trapezoid in shape. One of the  bases of this trapezoid is 4 m longer than the other base. Find the length of the two  bases if the median measures 18 m.

The answers are:

Shorter Base or Base 1 = 16

Longer Base or Base 2 = 20

Solution:

Given:

Median = 18 m

Let us set variables for the bases:

Shorter Base or Base 1 =  x

since "One of the  bases of this trapezoid is 4 m longer than the other base", then:

Longer Base or Base 2 = x + 4

Find x, to get Base 1 and Base 2

Equation:

Formula:

[tex]Median = \frac{Base 1 + Base 2}{2}[/tex]

Substituting the given we have:

[tex]18 = \frac{x + (x+4)}{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]18 = \frac{2x + 4}{2}[/tex]

Then, we cross multiply,

18 × 2 = 2x + 4

36 = 2x + 4

2x = 36 - 4

2x = 32

Dividing both sides by 2,

[tex]\frac{2x}{2} = \frac{32}{2}[/tex]

x = 16

We now go back to Base 1 and Base 2

Base 1 => x = 16

Base 2 => (x + 4) = 16 + 4 = 20

Final Answer:

Base 1 = 16

Base 2 = 20

8. One lateral face of a monument is trapezoid in shape. the base is 4 dm more than the other. If the median of the trapezoid measures 10 dm, find the length of the longer base​


Answer:

Let's denote the length of the shorter base by "x". Then, we know that the length of the longer base is "x + 4", since it is 4 dm more than the other base.

We also know that the median of the trapezoid has a length of 10 dm. The median of a trapezoid is the line segment connecting the midpoints of the two non-parallel sides of the trapezoid. In this case, the two non-parallel sides are the two bases of the trapezoid.

Since the median of the trapezoid has a length of 10 dm, we can set up an equation involving the lengths of the two bases and the median. Using the formula for the length of the median of a trapezoid, we have:

median = (1/2) x (shorter base + longer base)

Substituting the expressions for the two bases, we get:

10 = (1/2) x (x + x + 4)

10 = (1/2) x (2x + 4)

10 = x + 2

x = 8

Therefore, the length of the shorter base is 8 dm. The length of the longer base is 4 dm more than the shorter base, so it is:

x + 4 = 8 + 4 = 12

So the length of the longer base is 12 dm.

10. Apply the concepts of direct and joint variations in solving the following problems:An area of a trapezoid roof is jointly proportional to the height and the sum of its bases. If the area is 80 m² when the height is 4 cm and the bases are 4 cm and 10 cm, what is the area of a trapezoid roof if the height is 6 cm and the bases are 5 cm and 8 cm, respectively?​


Answer:

To solve this problem using the concepts of direct and joint variations, we can start by using the formula for the area of a trapezoid:

A = (1/2) * h * (b1 + b2)

Where A is the area, h is the height, and b1 and b2 are the lengths of the two bases. We can see that the area of the trapezoid is jointly proportional to the height and the sum of the bases.

Given that the area is 80 m² when the height is 4 cm and the bases are 4 cm and 10 cm, we can write an equation using the formula:

(1/2) * 4 * (4 + 10) = 80

We can solve for the proportionality constant k:

k = (1/2) * 4 = 2

Next, we can use the formula with the new values for height and bases:

A = k * h * (b1 + b2) = 2 * 6 * (5 + 8)

Finally, we can evaluate this expression to find the area of the trapezoid with a height of 6 cm and bases of 5 cm and 8 cm:

A = 2 * 6 * (5 + 8) = 2 * 6 * 13 = 156 cm²

So the area of the trapezoid roof with the height of 6 cm and bases of 5 cm and 8 cm is 156 cm².

To find the area of one of the triangular faces, we can use the formula for the area of a triangle: A = (1/2)bh, where b is the base of the triangle and h is the height.

In this case, the base of the triangle is 9 feet (the length of the roof) and the height is 6 feet (the slant height of the roof). Plugging these values into the formula, we get:

A = (1/2) * 9ft * 6ft = 27 sq ft

Since there are four triangular faces on the roof, the total lateral area of the roof is 4 * 27 sq ft = 108 sq ft.

Therefore, the lateral area of Michelle's square pyramid-shaped roof is 108 sq ft.

Answer:

The lateral area of a square-based pyramid is given by the formula:

Lateral area = 1/2 * perimeter of base * slant height

Since the base of the pyramid is a square, the perimeter of the base is 4 times the length of one side:

Perimeter of base = 4 * length of one side = 4 * 10 ft = 40 ft

The slant height is given as 5√2 ft.

Therefore, the lateral area of the roof is:

Lateral area = 1/2 * 40 ft * 5√2 ft = 100√2 sq.ft.

So, the lateral area of the roof is 100√2 square feet.
