20. in terms of the consequences on the earth's lithosphere, how will you differentiate this type of convergent plate boundary with the other two?


The difference between convergent, divergent, and transform lies in the direction of plate motion. Convergence is the movement of plates that collide with each other. Divergence is the movement of plates away from each other. While the transformation is the movement of plates that intersect, The three plate movements occur due to the influence of convection currents or heat currents located in the asthenosphere layer (earth's mantle).

 

Definition of Plate Tectonics

If interpreted one by one based on the constituent words "tectonic plates," plates are the uppermost constituent parts of the earth's material. Most of the Earth's plates have a thickness of up to 100 km. While tectonic is a process of movement in the earth's crust that causes indentations, folds, and faults that result in the high and low of the earth's surface.

From the explanation above, the notion of plate tectonics is closely related to the earth's lithosphere. The lithosphere is the upper part of the earth that consists of the earth's crust and mantle. Both have rigid and solid properties.

Therefore, this part of the lithosphere undergoes a process so that it becomes tectonic plates. Thus, plate tectonics is the upper part of the earth that undergoes a process of movement, causing the formation of the high and low parts of the earth's surface. This affects the appearance of the earth's surface, which is more dynamic.

 

Plate Boundaries

The movement between one plate and another adjacent plate forms an interaction, so that it is divided into three types based on the type of movement, namely:

1. Divergent:

Plate movement where the plates move away from each other where the force acting on this motion is a pulling force (tension). his divergence causes magma to rise from the earth's core, forming the ocean floor or oceanic crust.An example is MOR (Mid Ocean Ridges) at the bottom of the Atlantic Ocean.

2. Convergent:

Plate movement where the plates move closer to each other where the force acting on this motion is a compressional force.

3. Transform:

Plate movement in which the plates move past each other.This movement is parallel and not perpendicular, which results in a horizontal fault of the Strike Slip Fault type. An example is the San Andreas fault in the United States.

Three types of Convergent boundary

Continental to oceanic convergenceOceanic to oceanic convergenceContinental to continental convergence

Continental to oceanic convergence

Oceanic plate will subduct beneath the continental plate since it is more denser. Several geological features will formed because of this convergence. Philippine Islands are also a product of continental to oceanic plate convergence, wherein Eurasian plate collides with Philippine sea plate which produces volcanic islands creating Philippines Island.

Oceanic to oceanic convergence

The geological features formed in this convergence is similar to continental oceanic convergence.

Continental to continental convergence

This type of collision will not produce a trench and volcanoes, but it produces mountain ranges and earthquake. Earthquake will occur in all type of converging plates.

Here are the list of geological feature when two continental plate converged

EarthquakeMountain Ranges

Earthquake

Grinding of the two oceanic plate as they collide produces earthquake. The type of earthquake produce is tectonic earthquake since its origin is the movement of the plate. When earthquake is produce because of Volcanic activity, we called them as Volcanic earthquake.

Types of Earthquake

Tectonic EarthquakeVolcanic Earthquake

Tectonic Earthquake

Tectonic earthquake is the result of the movement of the plate which produces tremendous amount of energy that travels in all direction away from the epicenter causing shaking on the surface.

Volcanic Earthquake

Volcanic Earthquake occurs due to the volcanic activity of erupting volcanoes. Volcanic activity due to the rising of magma on the surface of the earth causes shaking in surrounding areas known as volcanic earthquake.

Mountain Ranges

Are formed between between the collision of continental-to-continental convergence. Collision zone is created in this type of collision, no trench, no volcanoes but only large group of tall mountains are created known as mountain ranges. One very good example is the Himalayas mountain.

Convergent boundary is also known as destructive boundary. As the subducted plate reaches the mantle, it will be destroyed. This type of boundary also explains most of the rock cycle. The plate will move in a certain direction because of the convection current of the mantle. Convection current in the mantle are produce because of heat in the inner core caused by radioactive decay of uranium which releases tremendous amount of heat.
