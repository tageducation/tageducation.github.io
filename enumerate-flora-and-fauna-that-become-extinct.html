2. Enumerate flora and fauna that become extinct and the reasons for their inexistence​


Answer:

Extinction of Flora and Fauna

Extinct Flora:

Glossopteris – a plant that used to cover the southern continents, Glossopteris became extinct due to changes in the world’s climate. Cooksonia – these are believed to be the first land plants, but have gone extinct in the Early Devonian period due to a shift in the world’s climate.

Extinct Fauna:

Dinosaurs – dinosaurs used to rule the world during the Mesozoic, from 251 to 65.5 million years ago. However, the asteroid impact 65.5 million years ago ended their reign. Many species of dinosaurs died during the impact and the subsequent chaos that followed. Wooly Mammoth (Mammuthus primigenius) – the wooly mammoth is one of the largest animals to roam the land. Their population started to dwindle during the Last Ice Age because of overhunting by humans. Dodo Bird (Raphus cucullatus) – the dodo bird became extinct during the 17th century as colonists landed in the island of Mauritius. The rats, dogs, and cats that they introduced on the island killed off the birds and destroyed their eggs.

Explanation:

FLORA

Waitakere Scurvy Grass Lepidium amissum

This plant was under the Brassicaceae family, it was only discovered when it is already extinct around early 1900s and was made official by the IUCN in 2014. The environment condition that cause its extinction is the coastal degradation.

St. Helena Heliotrope Heliotropium pannifolium

This heliotrope plant is a flowering shrub native to the remote island of St. Helena, Southern Atlantic, thus naming the flower as St. Helena Heliotrope. The introduction of some animals, such as goats contributed on the plant's extinction. And with the high competition with invasive plants were likely have destroyed its specie. It was declared extinct in 2016.

Wood’s hau kuahiwi Hibiscadelphus woodii

Animals such as wild goats and pig and Invasive plants caused the Wood's hau kuahwi's extinction. This shrub were bright yellow flowers used to grow on the Hawaiian island of Kaua’i. It is thought to be totally extinct, not until in 2019, when some researchers discovered three specimens on a rocky outcrop of the island.  

FAUNA

Capricorn rabbit-rat Conilurus capricornensis

Scientists believed that this specie went extinct because of the introduction of cats that becomes their predator, and the changes in land use. The small nocturnal rodent were native in Australia, belongs to the Conilurus genus, this rabbit-cat was described thanks to the retrieval of its fossilized remains.

Barbados racer Erythrolamprus perfuscus

Brabados racer is a kind of snake that is native in Barbados, it hasn’t been observed since 1963. It probably went extinct due to intense urban development, particularly the land clearance and pesticide use that causes habitat loss.

15. ano ang flora and fauna


ANO ANG FLORA AND FAUNA

• Ito ay ang mga hayop at mga mga bulaklak na nabubuhay sa isang particular na rehiyon .

• Ang flora ay tumutukoy sa mga bulaklak na nabubuhay sa isang particular na lugar samantalang  

• Iba ang mga flora o bulaklak sa mga disyerto, iba din ang mga flora sa mga tabi ng ilog.

• Ang fauna naman ay tumutukoy sa buhay ng hayop na nabubuhay sa isang particular na lugar.

• Ito ay ang mga halaman at mga bulaklak na nakatira sa isang particular na lugar at sila ang nagpapaganda ng mundo.

• Ang flora and fauna ay naghahati-hati ng pagkaka-iba ng mga halaman at mga bulaklak sa iba’t-ibang uri o species. Ito ang malawak na salitang ginagamit sa mga hayop at halaman na nabubuhay sa isang particular na lugar , rehiyon, klima at oras.

Halimbawa ng mga FLORA

1. Native flora

• ito ang mga indigenous na uri ng mga bulaklak na nabubuhay sa iisang lugar. Halimbawa ang mga bulaklak sa tabi ng ilog. Ang mga bulaklak na ito ay natural na nabuhay sa tabi ng ilog at iyon talaga ang kanilang likas na tahanan.

2. Weed flora

• Ito ang mga bulaklak na nabubuhay sa isang lugar kahit hindi tinanim. Halimbawa nito ay ang mga damo na mayroong magagandang bulaklak. Subalit kadalasang dinadamo ito at ayaw ng tao na mabuhay sa kanilang lugar.

3. Horticultural flora

• Ito naman ang mga bulaklak na sinadyang itanim ng mga tao upang magamit din ng tao. Ito ay tinatawag na mga agricultural plants.

Halimbawa ng Fauna

1.Megafauna

• Ito ay tumutukoy sa mga malalaking hayop na nakatira sa isang lugar at hayop.  

2.Infauna

• Ito ang mga hayop na nabubuhay sa ilalim ng tubig at kadalasang nililibing ang kanilang katawan sa oceanic sediments

3. Cryofauna

• Ito naman ang mga hayop na nabubuhay sa mga malalamig na lugar tulad ng may mga snow o ang tinatawag na Arctic tundra.

Extinct Flora & Fauna

Flora and fauna refer to the plant and animal species in a specific area. Every day there more and more flora and fauna are becoming extinct or are driven close to extinction due to various reasons that are influenced by human activities.

Extinct Fauna and the Reason for Extinction

Desert bettong or Bettongia anhydra – their extinction was declared back in 2016. It is a marsupial animal that was believed to be extinct due to the introduction of mice and foxes in their habitat. Lesser stick-nest rat or Leporillus apicalis – due to the arrival of wild cats, these rats were not found during the 1930s to 1940s and therefore were declared extinct in 2016.Pagan reed-warbler or Acrocephalus yamashinae – a specie of bird that lives in the Northern Mariana Islands. In 1981 a volcanic eruption destroyed their habitat and resulted in the decline o their population, they are declared extinct in 2016.

Extinct Flora and the Reason for Extinction

Kaua’i flatsedge or Cyperus rockii – this plant was last observed in 1916 along the streams of Kaua'i at Hawaiian islands. The reason for their extinction in 2016 was the arrival of pigs and other invasive plant species. St. Helena heliotrope or Heliotropium pannifolium – the introduction of goats on the island of St. Helena was the reason for the extinction of this flowering shrub. It was announced extinct in 2016.Adams mistletoe or Trilepidea adamsii – is a type of shrub that was last seen back n 1954 around New Zealand's North Island. The degradation of its natural habitat affected the pollinators of this plant and thus resulted in the extinction declaration in 2011.

21. What is flora and fauna??​


FLORA AND FAUNA

Flora is the various plant life that grows in a certain area or during a given time period. Fauna is the various animal life that lives in a certain area or time period.

Let’s know furtherly about flora and fauna.

A. Flora

Flora is the various plant life that grows in a certain area or during a given time period. It is commonly divided on the basis of region. Plants that grow in a river will be so much different from the plants that grow in a desert. We will take an example, cactus and water lily live in a different environments. Cactus lives in a desert, this plant does not require much water. Water lily lives in a river, this plant requires so much water.

B. Fauna

Fauna is the various animals that live in a certain/specific region. Fauna is distinguished in many different ways. It is much more complex than flora because fauna is divided based on their forms. We will take an example, Avifauna which refers to birds, and piscifauna which refers to fish. Fauna also covers small organisms such as bacteria and viruses.

Thus, we conclude that flora is the various plants that live or grow in a certain area and are divided or categorized based on their region, on the other hand, fauna is the various animals that live in a certain region or specific region and are divided or categorized based on their forms.
