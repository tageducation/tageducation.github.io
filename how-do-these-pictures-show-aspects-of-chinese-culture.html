9. Should you accept a compliment in Chinese culture? Why or why not? Is age a good indicator of how much respect to show in Chinese culture? Explain your answer. Why is building guanxi important?​


Answer:

In Chinese culture, it is considered polite to accept a compliment with humility and gratitude. When receiving a compliment, it is common to respond with a phrase such as "过奖了" (guò jiǎng le) which translates to "you are too kind" or "you flatter me." This phrase is used as a way to show gratitude while also downplaying the compliment, as it is considered impolite to boast or brag about one's accomplishments.

Age is considered an important indicator of respect in Chinese culture, with older individuals generally receiving more respect and deference than younger ones. This is reflected in the use of honorific titles and terms of address, with older individuals often being referred to using more formal or respectful terms.

Building guanxi, which refers to building relationships or connections, is considered important in Chinese culture as it is believed that strong relationships can lead to business and personal success. Guanxi can be built through a variety of means such as by showing generosity, offering help and support, and maintaining regular communication. In Chinese culture, it is believed that a strong network of relationships can provide access to resources, opportunities, and support. Building guanxi is considered important as it can help to establish trust, open doors, and create opportunities for mutually beneficial cooperation.

In Chinese culture, humility is highly valued, and accepting a compliment with grace and humility is considered more appropriate than boasting or showing pride. When receiving a compliment, it is common to respond by downplaying or deflecting the compliment, such as saying "it was nothing" or "I am not that good", rather than accepting it outright.

Age is considered a significant indicator of respect in Chinese culture. It is important to show respect to those who are older, or in positions of authority, such as parents, grandparents, and elders in the community. This includes using formal titles and addressing them politely.

Building guanxi, or personal relationships, is vital in Chinese culture. It is through building strong relationships that trust and social networks are established. Guanxi can be beneficial in many aspects of life, particularly in business, as it can lead to more opportunities and help to resolve conflicts. Guanxi is built by investing time, energy and resources into building trust and mutual understanding with others. Strong guanxi can also be useful in finding jobs, getting promotions, and resolving disputes.

~ According to legends, the founder of music in Chinese mythology was Ling Lun who, at the request of the Yellow Emperor to create a system of music, made bamboo pipes tuned to the sounds of birds including the phoenix. A twelve-tone musical system was created based on the pitches of the bamboo pipes, the first of these pipes produced the "yellow bell pitch, and a set of tuned bells were then created from the pipes.

~ Archaeological evidence indicates that music culture developed in China from a very early period. What makes the Chinese New Year festival unique?

Chinese New Year, or the Spring Festival, is the most important celebration observed in China, with cultural and historic significance. The festival signals the beginning of spring, and the start of a new year according to the Chinese lunar calendar.

Why is Chinese Spring Festival important?

Chinese New Year is the most important holiday in China. Tied to the Chinese lunar calendar, the holiday was traditionally a time to honor household and heavenly deities as well as ancestors. It was also a time to bring family together for feasting.
