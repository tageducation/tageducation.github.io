21. How to find the sum of 3/2a and 1/6a in simplest form​


Addition of Dissimilar Fractions:

The sum of 3/2a and 1/6a is 1 2/3a.

Solutions:

Since the given set of addends are dissimilar fractions, they cannot be added at once.

Express the given fractions into similar fractions by using their Least Common Denominator (LCD). The LCD of 2 and 6 is 6. Thus,

3/2 = (3)(3)/6 = 9/6 where the LCD 6 is divided by the old denominator 2 and the quotient is multiplied by the old numerator 3.

1/6 = (1)(1)/6 = 1/6 where the LCD is divided by the old denominator 6 and the quotient is multiplied by the old numerator 1.

Since they are now expressed as similar fractions, proceed with the addition.

9/6a + 1/6a = 9 + 1a/6 = 10/6a

Express the answer in simplest form by dividing the numerator 10 with its denominator 6.

10/6a = 1 4/6a. Note that 4/6 is not yet in the simplest form since they can still be divided by 2.

1 4/6a ÷ 2/2 = 1 2/3a

Therefore, the sum of 3/2a and 1/6a is equals to 1 2/3a in its simplest form.
