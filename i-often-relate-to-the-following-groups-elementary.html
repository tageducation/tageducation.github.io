2. Reflect on how your relationships and roles in society have changedthroughout your lifePeriodsEarly Childhood ElementaryHigh School I often relate to thefollowing groupsI performed thefollowing roles​


Early childhoodFamily and childhood pals are two groups with which I identify most frequently.

By following my parents' or elders' instructions, I played the following roles. In order to support my old pals, I offer them my spare snacks.

ElementaryI frequently connect with the following social groups: family, friends, and teachers.

I played the following parts by complying with my parents' request that I leave for school early. I have errands to run after school, including cooking dinner, washing dishes, and pitching water. Then, I assisted my classmates with their weekly assignments inside the classroom and showed respect for the teachers by using phrases like "Good morning," "Good afternoon," "Goodbye," and "Thank you, Ma'am."

High SchoolI frequently identify with the following groups: my family, best friend, classmates, teachers, other members of my religious church, my crush, and my cousins.

I played the following parts according to my parents' rigorous instructions. I support my best buddy in her efforts to sell meals and T-shirts to our classmates. After that, I assisted my teachers by keeping our classroom tidy and organized. My religious church friends inspire me to treat others well and to respect seniors, and they teach me how to have confidence in God. My school crush inspires me to put out my best effort, showcase my talents, and achieve decent grades. My ability to go outside of my hometown, spend the night with my pals, and go shopping on my own without informing my parents is all because of my cousins. Briefly, I'm liberated.

1. Significant  changes  that I noticed in my relationships : 

The biggest changes I've observed in my interactions with others over the course of my life are that I've grown to be a godly lady who is kind and respectful of the elderly. As a result, I also earn the respect of my loved ones, close friends, classmates, churchmates, and best buddies. assisting others who require my assistance by tutoring them on their tasks, projects, or other responsibilities. It gives me more self-assurance, defines who I am, and makes me really content and happy.

2. Society's role :  

My belief is that society contributes to these changes by allowing me to make acquaintances with others I met at work, church, and school. They assist me in discovering the genuine worth of my existence in our hometown. Without them, I would not be the person I am now.

22. my weakness when i was elementary ​


Weakness

All of us might have it; it can find you challenging since you want to correct and improve your weaknesses. And these are the downsides that we have because we are not all perfect. But we can get through with it in time, so take it to step by step.

My weakness when I was an elementary studentFear of failure

I always wanted what my parents to see was the best of me. In terms of academics, when I was an elementary student, I pursued myself to be on the list of honors. And that leads me to fear failure, which is that I need to strive more as hard as possible even though it exceeds my limitations as a kid. But this weakness makes me more stressed, wherein if I fail, I would see myself as a worthless child. My parents wanted me the best at school with high expectations; nevertheless, it resulted in some weakness in my life that gave me anxiety and stress in my mind.

Nowadays, we might struggle to cope with our weaknesses in life. We do our best so that we can get through with it. And we see those weaknesses as a strength if we overcome them step by step. It might be hard on the first part, but it is all worth it in the end. If you face all those weaknesses you have in life, you may see yourself as a growing person and go for the best you can to get rid of them. Sometimes, it might not let you go, but it lessens that before.

Remember:

Do not worry if you have weakness in life, and it is all normal. And do not see it as a negative part ourselves but a challenge to overcome to improve more. Do not let yourself be down on this weakness, and focus on the strengths you have now.
