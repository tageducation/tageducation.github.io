29. What are the other soft wares used in digital sculpting


Digital Sculpting is a form of art which uses software to manipulate digital objects to look like it is made from real-life substance such as clay. It is a way of bringing any shape of object in to life. There are many different softwares used in digital sculpting. Some of these are enumerated below:

ZBrush - most popular digital sculpting program. It offers the world's most advanced tools, Its price is around 795 USDMudbox - biggest competitor of ZBrush and owned by Autodesk. Its price is 10USD/month or 80USD/yearMeshmixer - free 3D-sculpting based CAD program by Autodesk and a beginner-friendly application3D Coat - has a super powerful texturing, UV mapping, and painting tools. It is by Pilgway and its price is from 99USD (amateurs) to 379USD (professionals) Sculptris - a free digital sculpting tool by Pixologic. This shows the basic of ZBrush since the latter is also created by the same company.Blender - a free tool which workflow is similar to ZBrush and Mudbox.Cinema 4D - has a big range of brushes, stencils and stamps and from Maxon. Its price is 3,500USDMODO -  Price: 1,799 USDMaya - customers complain that the workflow is buggy and sluggish. It is from Autodesk and the price is 242USD/month or 1,936USD/yearSculptGL - free, doesn’t need a download/installation and doesn’t require you to sign up. It is by Stephane Ginier
