1. how to fill up the withdrawal slip


Answer:

The order of information on withdrawal slips varies from bank to bank, but the general requirements are similar. Fill in the blanks with the necessary information:

the current date

your checking account number

your name in printed letters

the amount you wish to withdraw

your signature

Some banks require you to write the amount in both words and numbers, similar to how you write a check.


2. how to fill up deposit slip​


Answer:

To fill out a deposit slip:

List the amount of money you want to deposit. Most slips have separate lines for cash and checks.

For example, add $30 (cash) and $450.55 (check).

Enter the subtotal. In this case, it's $480.55.

Enter any amount you want back. In this case, it's 0.

Enter the Total. In this case, it's $480.55.

Sign the deposit slip.

Take the slip and the money you want to deposit to a teller at your bank. You also can make deposits using an ATM if you have an ATM card. You'll learn about using an ATM later in this lesson.

12. 1. The movement is up or down and parallel to the dip of the inclinedfault surface.a, dip-slip faultb, strike slip faulte, oblique faultd. fault​


Answer:

D. Fault

Explanation:

Fault, in geology, a planar or gently curved fracture in the rocks of Earth’s crust, where compressional or tensional forces cause relative displacement of the rocks on the opposite sides of the fracture. Faults range in length from a few centimetres to many hundreds of kilometres, and displacement likewise may range from less than a centimetre to several hundred kilometres along the fracture surface (the fault plane). In some instances, the movement is distributed over a fault zone composed of many individual faults that occupy a belt hundreds of metres wide. The geographic distribution of faults varies; some large areas have almost none, others are cut by innumerable faults.

Answer:

11. A. Dip- Slip fault

12. A. Strike slip fault

13. B. Oblique fault

Explanation:

11. Dip - slip fault are those in which the primary motion is parallel to the dip of the fault plane. A normal fault is a dip-slip fault produced by tension that stretches or thins Earth's crust. At a normal fault, the hanging wall moves downward relative to the footwall.

12. Strike-slip fault, also called transcurrent fault, wrench fault, or lateral fault, in geology, a fracture in the rocks of Earth's crust in which the rock masses slip past one another parallel to the strike, the intersection of a rock surface with the surface or another horizontal plane.

13 A fault is a fracture or zone of fractures between two blocks of rock. ... Faults which move horizontally are known as strike-slip faults and are classified as either right-lateral or left-lateral. Faults which show both dip-slip and strike-slip motion are known as "oblique-slip" faults .
