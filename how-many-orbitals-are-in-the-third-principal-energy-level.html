3. How many orbitals are in the third principal energy level *?


Answer:

There are 9 orbitals in the third principal energy level

Further explanation

In an atom, there are energy levels in the shell and subshell

Atoms have a nucleus composed of protons and neutrons and are surrounded by electrons which have a greater volume of space than the volume of space from the nucleus

The volume of space from these electrons is divided into regions called principal energy levels starting with numbers 1, 2, 3, 4,. etc calculated from the nucleus ..

Each maximum energy level can be filled with electrons in the formula 2n²

For example for the first level energy n = 1, the maximum electron = 2 (1²) = 2; the second 2 (2²) = 8; the third, 2 (3²) = 18 etc.

This energy level is expressed in terms of electron configurations.

Writing the electron configuration starts from the lowest to the highest subshell's energy level. There are 4 sub-shells / sub levels that have orbitals, namely s, p, d, and f. The maximum number of electrons for each subshell is

s: 2 electrons

p: 6 electrons

d: 10 electrons and

f: 14 electrons

The greater the value of n (the atomic shell, the main quantum number,principal energy level), the greater the energy level

In normal circumstances, electrons fill the shell at the lowest energy level starting from the shell K, L M and then N

The first principal energy level only has one s orbital

the second has one s orbitals and three p orbitals.

The third energy level has one s orbitals, three p orbitals, and five d orbitals

The fourth level has one s orbitals, three p orbitals, five d orbitals, and seven f orbitals
