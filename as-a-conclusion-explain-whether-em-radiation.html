What are the effects of electromagnetic waves in our environment?

Not much apart from their intended effects, usually. Radio and TV broadcasts fill the air but we don’t perceive them and are not affected by them - unless we use appropriate equipment to receive their data comtent.

Some transmitters radiate very high power EM waves. These can heat you up, as in a microwave oven. Care is taken to exclude people from the dangerous areas. (But the myth is that ready-cooked ducks fall out of the beam of over-the-horizon radars!)

Some consumer equipment intentionally transmits EM energy - WiFi and mobile phones for example. There has been some concern that radiating energy from mobile phones held so close to the head can cook your brain. But the power is too small and any minor heating will be dissipated by the blood circulation. Many studies have shown no discernable harm. If this worries you, use a headset so the transmitter is away from your body by a couple of cm.

Caution - some body parts do not have enough blood circulation for this to work. Famous examples are eyeballs and the other balls. So don’t use your mobile held next to those bits!

WiFi routers are normally far enough away that heating effects are less than negigible.

As well as ‘intentional transmitters’ there are unintended sources of EM energy - industrial welders, heaters, medical diagnostic kit etc. Their use is regulated to keep the public safe, slightly less safe for their operators.

But what about effects other than heating? After all this is ‘radiation’? Well, EM waves up to light frequency do not have enough energy per photon to damage (ionise) matter. Infra-red radiation may heat you but ultra-violet destroys skin cells, for example. Radio waves are much less energetic than infra-red so are not a risk.
