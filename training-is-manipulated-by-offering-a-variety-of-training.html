Situation 1

It is clear from this case of media manipulation that the insurance firm is altering or changing data in order to increase sales.

Situation 2

In this instance, it is clear that media manipulation has taken place because the individual with political aspirations is hiring an advertising agency to enhance his or her public image.

Situation 3

A product's credibility with the buying public shouldn't rely on the endorsement of well-known people.

Situation 4

One example of manipulating the media is saying that a product is the best. No product is deemed to be the best on the market if it is not backed up by proof.

Situation 5

A victim of media manipulation is the woman who is readily seduced by the "Buy One, Take One" advertisement. There can be a catch to the "Buy One, Take One" promotion regarding the discounted item.

The media has been quite successful in spreading news, promoting politics, and other things. However, when the media is controlled or misused, it helps spread false information.
