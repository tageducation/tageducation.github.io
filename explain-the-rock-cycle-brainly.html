1. Explain the rocks cycles. Describe the processes and product formed in the rocks cycle​


explanation:

The three main rock types are igneous, metamorphic and sedimentary. The three processes that change one rock to another are crystallization, metamorphism, and erosion and sedimentation. Any rock can transform into any other rock by passing through one or more of these processes. This creates the rock cycle.

The rock cycle is a cyclic process that involves the transition of rocks from one form to another. It entails the following steps: Igneous rocks are formed when hot lava cools. After that, igneous rocks are broken down into tiny particles, which are transported and deposited.


3. explain the rock cycle of Ineous rock​


Explanation:

Igneous rock can change into sedimentary rock or into metamorphic rock. Sedimentary rock can change into metamorphic rock or into igneous rock. Metamorphic rock can change into igneous or sedimentary rock.

Igneous rock forms when magma cools and makes crystals. Magma is a hot liquid made of melted minerals. The minerals can form crystals when they cool. Igneous rock can form underground, where the magma cools slowly. Or, igneous rock can form above ground, where the magma cools quickly.

20. explain the process of rock cycle​


The Six Rock Cycle Steps

Weathering & Erosion

On the surface of the planet, igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic rocks are continually being eroded by wind and water. Sand carried by wind scrapes bits of rock off like sandpaper. The rough edges of rocks are worn away by flowing river water, pounding waves, and precipitation, leaving behind smooth rocks or pebbles. Mountain rocks fracture as water penetrates into their crevices and freezes there.

Large rocks are reduced to tiny fragments. Weathering is the process of rock fragments breaking off and remaining in one place. Erosion occurs when the particles are transported to another location.

Transportation

Rock fragments that have been eroded are carried away by the wind, rain, streams, rivers, and seas.

Deposition

Rivers' currents slow down as they deepen or enter the ocean, causing the rock and soil particles to settle and form a layer of silt. In many cases, the silt accumulates faster than it can be swept away, leading to the formation of tiny islands and the splitting of the river into several channels in a delta.

Compaction & Cementation

The weight and pressure of the bottom layers compacts them as the sediment layers build up (above or below water).

Minerals that have been dissolved fill up the minute spaces between particles, then solidify to serve as cement. Sediment becomes sedimentary rock after years of compaction and cementation.

Metamorphism

Sedimentary or igneous rocks can get deeply buried during very long geologic epochs, typically as a result of plate tectonic activity. These rocks undergo metamorphosis underground because to the intense heat and pressure they are subjected to. The pressure of the plates tends to crush the rock that has been cooked by the hot magma below where tectonic plates collide.

Rock Melting

Can you see a rock that is "rock hard" melting? In the earth's core, they carry out activities like that. Underground metamorphic rocks melt to form magma. Magma pours from a volcano as it erupts. (Magma that has reached the earth's surface is known as lava.) The lava solidifies and transforms into igneous rock as it cools. The processes of weathering and erosion occur as soon as the new igneous rock is created, restarting the entire cycle.
