Answer:

The Kyrgyz carpet Shyrdak

Explanation:

The Kyrgyz carpet Shyrdak is a traditional Kyrgyz thick felt carpet that can be laid out in a yurt, or simply on the grass. They are often found in Kyrgyz homes, either on the floor, or sometimes on divans, or even as a wall hanging.

24. meaning of the Kyrgyz felt rugs


Kyrgyz 'mosaic' felt rugs, or shyrdak, are made from two sheets of white felt which have been dyed, cut into identical shapes and reassembled into a mosaic-like textile of interlocking positive and negative patterns.
