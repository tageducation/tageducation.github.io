16. what is research?Subject: Practical research 1​


Research is a systematic process of collecting and analyzing information to answer a specific question or solve a problem. It involves the application of various scientific methods and techniques to gather data and evidence, interpret and analyze the results, and draw conclusions based on empirical evidence.

Research can be conducted in a variety of fields, including science, engineering, social sciences, humanities, and business. The research process typically involves several steps, including defining the research question, reviewing the relevant literature, selecting an appropriate research methodology, collecting data, analyzing the data, and presenting the findings.

The goal of research is to expand our understanding of the world around us and contribute to the existing body of knowledge. Research findings can be used to inform policy decisions, improve practices, and develop new technologies or products.
