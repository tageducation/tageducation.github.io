The answer is B, Review of Related Literatures

A review of related literature (RRL) is a thorough review of current literature on the subject of a thesis or dissertation. In an RRL, you discuss current literature information and findings related to your subject. If you see holes or contradictions in the current literature, you will explore them in your analysis, as well as how you plan to correct these gaps or overcome these conflicts through your research, if appropriate.
