19. How does dehydration cause hyperthermia?​


Answer:

Dehydration causes surface blood vessels to constrict, effectively halting sweat production to conserve blood volume. This leads to a dramatic increase in core temperature, breakdown in the thermoregulatory system, and concurrent increase in body temperature.
