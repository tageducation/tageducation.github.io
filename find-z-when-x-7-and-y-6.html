16. If z varies directly as x and inversely as y, and z=28, when x=7 and y=8, find z when x=9 and y=6​


Problem:

If z varies directly as x and inversely as y, and z = 28, when x = 7 and y = 8, find z when x = 9 and y = 6.

Solution:

To translate into variation statement a relationship involving combined variation between two quantities.

The statement, "z varies directly as x and inversely as y" translated into combined variation is z = kx/y where k is the constant of variation.

Solve if z is 28 when x is 7 and y is 8. So, find the constant using the equation of a combined variation.

z = kx/y(28) = k(7)/(8)28 = 7k/828 × 8 = 7k224 = 7k7k/7 = 224/7k = 32

The constant of the variation is 32. In equation of variation.

z = 32x/y

Find z when x is 9 and y is 6. Substitute the equation using the constant of the variation that you obtained.

z = 32x/yz = 32(9)/(6)z = 288/6z = 48

Answer:

∴ Therefore, the value of z is 48 to the combined variation.

When the value of a variable changes, it is said to vary. If one variable increases while the other decreases, they are said to vary inversely. This means that the product of the two variables is constant.

If we let k be the constant of proportionality, we can write the equation x=kyz. Plugging in the values we know, we have 7=2k(4), from which we can find that k=0.875. This means that when y=3 and z=6, we have x=0.875*3*6=22.5.

In summary, when y=3 and z=6, the value of x is 22.5.
