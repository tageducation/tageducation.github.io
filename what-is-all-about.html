1.Rational Numbers:

Rational numbers are numbers that can be expressed as the ratio of two integers. This means that rational numbers can be represented as a fraction or a decimal with a finite or repeating pattern. Rational numbers include whole numbers, integers, terminating decimals, and repeating decimals.

2.Integers:

Integers are whole numbers, including zero, and their negatives. They can be expressed without fractions or decimals. Integers include positive numbers, negative numbers, and zero.

3.Whole Numbers:

Whole numbers are numbers that are greater than or equal to zero, and do not have any decimal or fraction parts. Whole numbers include all natural numbers and zero.

4.Natural Numbers:

Natural numbers are positive integers or counting numbers that do not have a fractional or decimal part. They are numbers used to count objects or things. Natural numbers start from one (1) and go up to infinity.

5.Irrational Numbers:

Irrational numbers are real numbers that cannot be expressed as the ratio of two integers. This means that irrational numbers cannot be expressed as a fraction or a decimal with a repeating or terminating pattern. They include numbers such as the square root of 2, pi, and e.

3. Whole numbers are a set of non-negative integers, including zero and all positive integers. They are used to count objects or represent quantities that cannot be divided into smaller parts. Whole numbers are often used in arithmetic operations such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

4. Natural numbers are a set of positive integers, including 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, and so on. They are used to count objects or represent quantities that can be divided into smaller parts, such as apples, people, or cars. Natural numbers are often used in mathematics to define sequences, series, and other mathematical structures.

5. An irrational number is any real number that cannot be expressed as a ratio of two integers, such as the square root of 2, pi, or e. Irrational numbers are often represented as non-repeating, non-terminating decimals and cannot be expressed as a fraction or a ratio of two integers.
