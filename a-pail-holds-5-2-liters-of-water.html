Tub = 62.1 L


First statement:

3.4 L * 4 = 13.6 L                        2/9 of a tub can hold 4 pails.

13.6 L = 2/9 of tub.                    So, 2/9 of tub can hold 13.6 L of water.


Second statement:

13.6 L + 13.6 L = 27.2 L             Another 4 pails were poured.

27.2 L = 4/9 (2/9 * 2)                4/9 of tub was filled in 8 pails of water (27.2 L)

Missing = 5/9                            The part that was not filled is 5/9

5/9 = 2 tanks                             That 5/9 part is equal to two tanks.

5/9 = ?

If 13.6 L = 2/9, then..

Solution to get the 5/9: 13.6 L / 2 * 5

The answer is 34 L which is equivalent to 2 tanks.

34 L / 2 = 17 L


Therefore, 17 L is the amount of water can a tank hold.
