1. bee and flower relationship: mutualism

2. fish and seaweeds relationship: commensalism

3. an orchid and a tree: parasitism

4. bird and tree relationship: commensalism

1. the bee benefits from the pollen the flowers produce and the flower benefits by the pollination the bee performs later on.

2. the fish benefits from using seaweed as a hideout while the seaweed does not gain anything from the relationship.

3. the orchid depends on the tree for its survival, stealing nutrients from it while the tree benefits nothing from it.

4. the bird gains a place to build a nest and stay for a while but the tree neither benefits nor is it at a disadvantage from the relationship.

Here Are 5 Most Popular Fruit Bearing Trees And Their Benefits

1. Java Plum Tree or Jamun Tree

The Java Plum is an evergreen tropical tree which typically originated in India and is also grown in other Southeast countries like Pakistan, Nepal, Bangladesh, Indonesia and Sri Lanka. The tree is known to live for about a 100 years. Java Plum commonly known as Jamun is a substantial berry generally oval and elongated with a tarty flavour. The fruit is initially green in color but it tends to change colour as it ripens. Its deep color is known to turn the tongue purple. The best season to enjoy jamun is in the months of May and June. The fruit is known to be a natural blood purifier since it is rich in iron.  It helps to control high blood pressure levels. It is also rich in Vitamin and Vitamin C and therefore, is known to be great for your skin.

Sapotas are not very vibrant and appealing to the eyes but are extremely delicious and sweet much like the taste of brown sugar. The fruit has a grainy flesh containing black glossy seeds. Sapota  seed oil is best used for damaged hair and dry scalp.
