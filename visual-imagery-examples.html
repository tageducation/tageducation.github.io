what is tactile imagery?

-In this form of poetic imagery, the poet appeals to the reader's sense of touch by describing something the speaker of the poem feels on their body. It may include the feel of temperatures, textures, and other physical sensations.

what is auditory imagery?

-Auditory imagery pertains to sounds, noises, music, or the sense of hearing. (This kind of imagery may come in the form of onomatopoeia). Olfactory imagery pertains to odors, aromas, scents, or the sense of smell. ... Tactile imagery pertains to physical textures or the sense of touch.

what is OIfactory imagery?

-Olfactory imagery pertains to odors, aromas, scents, or the sense of smell. Gustatory imagery pertains to flavors or the sense of taste. Tactile imagery pertains to physical textures or the sense of touch.

what is visual imagery?

-A mental image or mental picture is an experience that, on most occasions, significantly resembles the experience of visually perceiving some object, event, or scene, but occurs when the relevant object, event, or scene is not actually present to the senses.

14. Define the following terms and provide 2 examples each. - Auditory Imagery - Visual Imagery - Tactile Imagery - Gustatory Imagery - Olfactory Imagery PA HELP HO RITO PLESSS NONSENSE = REPORT


Answer:

Auditory Imagery: Imagery that involves sound and evokes aural sensations. Examples: “The clang of the bell echoed through the empty hallway” and “The gentle ripple of the stream lulled me to sleep.”

Visual Imagery: Imagery that involves sight and evokes visual sensations. Examples: “The sky was a deep sea of stars" and “The sun shone through the trees like a thousand diamonds.”

Tactile Imagery: Imagery that involves touch and evokes tactile sensations. Examples: “The rough surface of the wood scratched my skin” and “The velvet material was soft against my fingertips.”

Gustatory Imagery: Imagery that involves taste and evokes gustatory sensations. Examples: “The sweet taste of the candy lingered on my tongue” and “The sour taste of the lemon made my face pucker.”

Olfactory Imagery: Imagery that involves smell and evokes olfactory sensations. Examples: “The aroma of freshly baked bread filled the kitchen” and “The smell of smoke hung heavy in the air.”

18. Define the following terms and provide 2 examples each. - Auditory Imagery - Visual Imagery - Tactile Imagery - Gustatory Imagery - Olfactory Imagery


Answer:

Auditory Imagery: is a literary device used to create a sound experience within the reader's mind. Examples: “The music was as sweet as a child’s laughter.” “The crack of thunder echoed through the night sky.”

Visual Imagery: is a literary device used to create visual representations of situations, people, or objects in the reader's mind. Examples: “She had a face that could light up a room” “The sun was setting, painting the sky with a brilliant array of colors.”

Tactile Imagery: is a literary device used to create a sense of touch in the reader's mind. Examples: “The grass was soft and cool beneath my feet.” “The velvet dress was as smooth as silk.”

Gustatory Imagery: is a literary device used to create a sense of taste in the reader's mind. Examples: “The soup was as salty as the sea.” “The cake was as sweet as honey.”

Olfactory Imagery: is a literary device used to create a sense of smell in the reader's mind. Examples: “The fresh-cut grass was fragrant and inviting.” “The room was filled with the smell of freshly brewed coffee.”
