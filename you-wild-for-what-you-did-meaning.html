1. what is the explanation for "you wild for what you did"​


"YOU WILD FOR WHAT YOU DID" means human nature that resembles a wild animal.

EXPLANATION:

The more we know humans, the more we understand that we are actually talking animals. Animals that can be tame like sheep, or wild like lions. Basically it is the nature that becomes the parameter for us whether we are worthy of being called "noble human beings" or worse than the animals themselves.

What distinguishes us from other creatures is reason and conscience. God created the mind to think and contemplate the signs of the Creator's power. Thinking that everything has a purpose and learning, this happened when Adam and the angels were asked by the Creator about the names of creatures and all of their creations. Intellect is an important tool that humans have to reason and consider our actions, reason distinguishes us from animals to think that what we do now or in the future will bring benefits or harm ourselves or others.

Because with reason we have standards and ways to deal with problems and take lessons. That's what makes us different from animals, animals live just to live while humans live to pursue their purpose in life. So from here the role of reason arises, reason processes all our activities into experience and knowledge from observing, hearing, feeling, and thinking.

Indeed, reason is the difference between us and animals, but reason can get lost at the crossroads of good and bad. So that apart from reason, God created a partner from reason called conscience. Conscience becomes a guide and a reminder when reason goes beyond its limits. In addition, conscience translates meanings that cannot be translated by reason.

So reason and conscience are two heirlooms possessed by humans, inheritances that make us avoid the nature of wild animals that pounce on each other with words and actions. If we lose both, then there is no difference between me, you, and all those who claim to be humans and animals. Have no goals and have no rules in living the rest of life on this earth.

29. THE CALL OF THE WILDwhat did they expect to find in africa?​


The African wild dog (Lycaon pictus) is a canine which is a native species to sub-Saharan Africa. It is the largest wild canine in Africa, and the only extant member of the genus Lycaon, which is distinguished from Canis by dentition highly specialised for a hypercarnivorous diet, and a lack of dewclaws. It is estimated that about 6,600 adults (including 1,400 mature individuals) live in 39 subpopulations that are all threatened by habitat fragmentation, human persecution and outbreaks of diseases. As the largest subpopulation probably consists of less than 250 individuals, the African wild dog has been listed as endangered on the IUCN Red List since 1990.
