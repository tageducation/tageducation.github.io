11. Why do this health issues concern the public?Its about "Indigenous people rights act of 1997" pls po paki sagot kung meron po kayong answer​


Answer:

The Indigenous People's Rights Act of 1997 (IPRA) is a law in the Philippines that recognizes the rights of Indigenous Peoples (IPs) and provides for the promotion and protection of these rights. This law is important because IPs in the Philippines have long been marginalized and have suffered from discrimination, displacement, and loss of their ancestral lands and resources. By recognizing their rights and promoting their welfare, the IPRA aims to improve their living conditions and preserve their culture and traditions.

In terms of health issues, IPs are often vulnerable to diseases and health problems due to their living conditions, lack of access to health services, and limited knowledge on health and hygiene practices. The IPRA seeks to address these concerns by providing for the promotion of IP health and wellness, including the provision of adequate health services and the promotion of traditional health practices.

The health issues concerning IPs are important to the public because they affect a significant portion of the Philippine population and have implications for the overall health and well-being of the country. By addressing these issues and promoting the rights and welfare of IPs, the public can help to improve the health and well-being of all Filipinos and contribute to the development of a more just and equitable society.

13. RA 6969 why does this health issue concern the public ​


Answer:

The Republic Act No. 6969, or the Toxic Substances and Hazardous and Nuclear Wastes Control Act of 1990, is a Philippine law that aims to protect the public from the harmful effects of toxic substances and hazardous and nuclear wastes. These substances and wastes can pose a serious threat to human health and the environment, and it is important for the public to be aware of the potential dangers they pose. The law aims to regulate the production, importation, and use of these substances and wastes, and to ensure that they are properly disposed of in a way that minimizes the risk to public health and the environment.
