3. in my community i am a


In my community, I am a general member. But as a member, there are a lot of things to do.

Being a member of a community means being part of a group of people who share common interests or goals. As a general member, you are not a leader or decision-maker, but you are still an important part of the community. Your participation and contributions help to shape the direction and culture of the community, and your actions can have a positive or negative impact on others.

To be a good member of a community, you should follow the rules and guidelines set by the community leaders or administrators. This may include things like respecting the opinions of others, avoiding divisive or controversial topics, and not engaging in disrespectful or abusive behavior. By following the rules, you can help to maintain a positive and productive environment for everyone in the community.

In addition to following the rules, you should also strive to contribute to the community in a positive way. This can involve participating in discussions, sharing your knowledge and expertise, and helping out other members who may need assistance. By providing value to the community, you can help to make it a more welcoming and engaging place for everyone.

Finally, it's important to be respectful and considerate of others in the community. This means treating everyone with kindness and empathy, and avoiding behavior that could be hurtful or offensive. By being respectful and considerate, you can help to create a more harmonious and inclusive community where everyone feels welcome and valued.

Improving Family Relationships with Emotional Intelligence

Looking to improve your relationships with your family members? Learn how emotional intelligence (EQ) is your most effective tool for overcoming rifts and strengthening bonds.

Old man sitting, smiling at person whose hand rests on his as he grips the handle of his cane

Emotional intelligence in the family

There’s nothing like family. The people we’re related to by blood and marriage are expected to be our closest allies, our greatest sources of love and support. Too often, however, our interactions with family are filled with misunderstanding and resentment, bickering and badgering. Those we should know and be known by best, end up feeling like adversaries or strangers.

Family is where our first and strongest emotional memories are made, and that’s where they keep appearing. And this is why emotional intelligence (EQ) succeeds where other efforts at family harmony fail. Active awareness and empathy—the ability to be aware, accepting, and permanently attuned to ourselves and others—tells us how to respond to one another’s needs.

EQ is incredibly powerful in the family because it puts you in control of your relationships with parents and children, siblings, in-laws and extended family. When you know how you feel, you can’t be manipulated by other’s emotions; nor can you blame family conflict on everyone else. Most of the techniques for improving family relationships are therefore centered on communicating your feelings to those you care about, as close relationships are centered around feeling.
