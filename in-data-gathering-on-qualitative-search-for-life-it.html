In your thesis or dissertation, you will have to discuss the methods you used to do your research. The methodology chapter explains what you did and how you did it, allowing readers to evaluate the reliability and validity of the research. It should include:

The type of research you did

How you collected your data

How you analyzed your data

Any tools or materials you used in the research

Your rationale for choosing these methods

The methodology section should generally be written in the past tense.

29. A biology class is studying the effect of temperature on the amount of growth of duckweed in an aquarium. What type of data will they need to collect, and which tools will most likely be used?They will gather qualitative data, using a thermometer and a hand lens.They will gather qualitative data, using a meterstick and a graduated cylinder.They will gather quantitative data, using a thermometer and a balance.They will gather quantitative data, using a beaker and a timer.


When the biology class is studying the effect of temperature on the amount of growth of duckweed in an aquarium, they will gather quantitative data using a thermometer and a balance.

 Let us discuss what they are doing.

In the activity that the biology class is doing, we have two variables that we consider. These are the effect of temperature and the amount of growth of duckweed.

 Since they are measuring the effect of temperature on the amount of growth of duckweed, they are gathering the numerical data that can show them the results. In this situation, they are concern on the quantity that will prove their statement. Thus, this is a quantitative data.

 We already know that this is a quantitative research, then the next step is how they are going to do this and what are they going to use to conduct this activity.

 Since they are dealing with the effect of temperature, so they need to gather the data about the temperature in the aquarium and to do this, they use the thermometer.

 Next, they are going to measure the amount of growth of the duckweed. To measure this, they need the balance to weigh the duckweed. The result will show how much weight does the duckweed gain that will prove its growth.

 Therefore, the type of data they use is a quantitative data and the tools that they use are the thermometer and balance.
