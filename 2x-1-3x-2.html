18. Simplify the expression: (2x^2 + 3x - 5) - (3x^2 - 2x + 1)


Answer:

x^2 + 5x - 6.

Step-by-step explanation:

First, we need to distribute the negative sign to the second expression inside the parentheses:

(2x^2 + 3x - 5) - 3x^2 + 2x - 1

Then we can combine like terms:

2x^2 - 3x^2 + 3x + 2x - 5 - 1

Simplifying further:

-x^2 + 5x - 6

Therefore, the simplified expression is -x^2 + 5x - 6.
