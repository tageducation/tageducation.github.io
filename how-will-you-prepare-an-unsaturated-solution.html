SATURATED SOLUTION

- a saturated solution is a kind of solution that have or contains the maximum amount of solute (soluble compound) dissolved in a solvent - forming a solution. For example, take 100 grams of table salt (NaCl also known as Sodium Chloride) at room temperature, 20 degrees Celsius that is dissolve at 500 milliliters of water, all 100 grams of table salt were dissolved. Then you added 40 grams more of table salt to the existing solution, in which 36 grams of newly added table salt were dissolved, and the remaining 4 grams remain undissolved, thus the NaCl or table salt solution is now saturated.

Solubility of solution are..

Solubility is the formation of new bonds between solute and solvent molecules. In terms of quantity, solubility is the maximum concentration of a solute that will dissolve in a solvent of known concentration at a given temperature. Solutes are classified as highly soluble, sparingly soluble, or insoluble based on the concentration of the solute dissolved in the solvent. Solutes that are 0.1 g or more dissolved in 100 mL of solvent are soluble. It dissolves in solvents at concentrations of less than 0.1g, but is said to be sparingly soluble. Solubility is therefore meant to be a quantitative expression expressed in units of grams per liter (g/L).

Different types of solutions are obtained depending on the solubility. A saturated solution is a solution in which a specific amount of solute is completely dissolved in a solvent at a specific temperature. On the other hand, a supersaturated solution is one in which the solute begins to salt out or precipitate after dissolving a certain concentration at the same temperature.

Unsaturated Solution

A solute must be added to the solvent to form a solution. First, the solute dissolves in the solvent to form a homogeneous solution. Such solutions in which solutes dissolve are called unsaturated solutions. Basically, a solution consists of her two types of particles: solutes and solvents. Most often water is used as the solvent (which is one of the reasons why water is also called the universal solvent). An unsaturated solution has the ability to dissolve more solutes until it reaches its saturation point. After reaching the saturation point, solutes are no longer soluble in the solvent and they become unsaturated solutions. Therefore, it is said that all solutions are mostly unsaturated in nature and are eventually transformed into saturated solutions by the addition of solutes.

In our daily life, we come across many examples of unsaturated solutions. Some examples of this are listed below:

Even salt or sugar dissolved in water is an unsaturated solution if the amount of dissolved salt/sugar is below the saturation point.

Iced coffee is another example of an unsaturated solution. Each solute has different solubility rules and solutes also dissolve in solvents. These solutions are considered unsaturated solutions. A solution of tea and sugar is a good example of an unsaturated solution because it dissolves more sugar. When such a solution reaches the saturation point, it transforms into a saturated solution.

A gas solution has a gas as a solvent and a solute can be in solid, liquid or even gas state. Air, smoke, and fog are also good examples of unsaturated gaseous solutions.

In unsaturated solutions, the concentration of a solute is much lower than its solubility equilibrium. All solutes have limitations associated with their particular solvent. A dissolved substance therefore has a certain value for its solubility in a solvent.
