Answer:

The answer to this problem is letter D.

Step-by-step explanation:

First of all, let me clarify that the formula for converting Fahrenheit to Celsius is [tex]C = \frac{5}{9}(F-32)[/tex].

Next, we will go to the details of the problem. It is stated in the following choices the possible scenarios when increasing the temperature. Our goal here is to prove each scenario and filter what is true from what is false.

Scenario 1: A temperature increase of 1 degree Fahrenheit is equivalent to a temperature increase of 5/9 degree Celsius.

Let us try an example to prove this

Let say our Temp 1 = 50 °F (Using the formula above, this equivalent to 10°C)

Then we increase it to Temp 2 = 51°F (Equivalent to 10.56°C)

Then we find the difference in both units

Temp 2 - Temp 1 = 51°F-50°F = 1°F

Temp 2 - Temp 1 =10.56°C - 10°C = 0.56°C

Thus, we can say that 1°F change is equivalent to 0.56°C change (or 5/9 °C). Therefore Scenario 1 is TRUE.

Scenario 2: A temperature increase of 1 degree Celsius is equivalent to a temperature increase of 1.8 degrees Fahrenheit.

Let us try another example to prove this

Let Temp 1 = 30°C (Equivalent to 86°F)

Then we increase to Temp 2 = 31°C ( Equivalent to 87.8°F)

Then we find the difference in both units

Temp 2 - Temp 1 = 31°C-30°C = 1°C

Temp 2 - Temp 1 =87.8°F - 86°F = 1.8°F

Thus, we can say that 1°C change is equivalent to 1.8°F change. Therefore Scenario 2 is TRUE.

Scenario 3: A temperature increase of 5/9 degree Fahrenheit is equivalent to a temperature increase of 1 degree Celsius.

Let us use another example to prove this

Let Temp 1 = 60°F (Equivalent to 15.56°C)

Then increase to Temp 2 = 60.56°F (Equivalent to 15.87°C)

Then we find the difference in both units

Temp 2 - Temp 1 = 60.56°F-60°F = 0.56°F (or 5/9 °F)

Temp 2 - Temp 1 =15.87°C - 15.56°C = 0.31°C

Thus, we can say that 5/9°F change is NOT equivalent to 1°c change. Therefore Scenario 3 is FALSE.

Since only I and II are true, then Letter D must be the answer.
