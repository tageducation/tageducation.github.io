Answer:

Answer:

Introvert and Extrovert

Introvert

According to Psychologist Carl Jung, the first person to characterize these two personality extremes, he described an introvert as an individual who prefers minimally stimulating environments, they're the individuals who need time alone to recharge. Social engagements and activities can leave introverts feeling drained and exhausted. They're often reserved in their manners and interaction with others.

Extrovert

While introverts despise social engagement, extroverts are the exact opposite of it. Jung described them as individuals who get energized by crowds and social interaction. They thrive in social situations and seek out social stimulation. Too much alone time drains their natural energy and their way of recharging is by being around other people.
