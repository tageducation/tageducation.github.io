When we raise our left hand, we will see our right hand in the image reflected in the mirror.

A mirror is a very useful tool that we use in daily life. If you happen to ever observe yourself looking at it, you might notice almost immediately that the body part that is reflected in the mirror is the counterpart to the actual part that we currently have or placed.

For example, if we raise our right hand while standing or sitting in front of a mirror, then we would see the left hand reflected in the mirror. And, it is practically impossible to see the same bodyparts reflected in the mirror, since it is how reflection works.

21. When you raised your left hand in the mirror, what hand was your image raising? Why do you think this happened?


Answer:

When I look in the mirror and raise my right hand, my image raises its left hand. And yet both our heads are still on top. Why does a mirror reverse things right to left, but not top to bottom?

The mirror shows on its right side what is on its right side (as if it is looking at you) which is your left side.

The mirror when looking at your head, when you’re standing upright, shows what is at the top - which is your head.

It is difficult to explain but if you are able to stand with your back to the mirror and look over your shoulder (or even use another mirror to look backward) you should be able to see that your left hand and your mirror image’s left hand are both showing on the same side of the mirror.
