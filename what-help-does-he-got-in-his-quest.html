1. What is the “dreadful oracle” that was delivered to King Acrisius?

Acrisius, the king of Argos, was told by the oracle of Delphi that his own grandson, born to his daughter Danae, would kill him one day. This lead the King Acrisius to lock his daughter Danae in a tower. However, the god Zeus was still able to came in and he impregnated Danae with Perseus.

2. What adventure does Polydectes suggest that Perseus undertake?

King Polydectes, who wanted Danae to become his wife, gave Perseus a dangerous mission. Knowing that he would not be able to get Danae for himself as long as Perseus was there to protect her, the king made a plan to send Perseus not only far away but also to a risky task. Polydectes told Perseus to kill Medusa and to prove it by handing him over its head.

adventure.

Perseus’ encounter with Graeae- The Graeae were three old sisters, who shared a single eye between them. The Graeae did not initially want to help Perseus, but he managed to steal their eye. Perseus refused to give back the eye until they agreed to help him.  Perseus’ encounter with the Nymphs- The Graeae told Perseus about the nymphs and where to find them. When Perseus found them, they significantly assisted him with three important gifts:  winged sandals, which would allow him to fly through the air;  a cap of darkness, which would make him invisible;  and a special metallic pouch for Medusa’s head.

3. Perseus immediately fell in love with her. Using his sword and winged sandals, Perseus killed the monster to stone by showing it Medusa’s head.
