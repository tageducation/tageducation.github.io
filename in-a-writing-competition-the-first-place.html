24. Identify the error and write the correction in the sentence Parvati was delightful with her prize for coming first in the Mathematics competition.


Answer:

Adjective use

Explanation:

The error in the sentence is the use of adjective.The proper adjective to be used is delighted, not delightful.Delightful was an obsolete adjective.In the sentence "Parvati was delightful with her prize for coming first in the Mathematics competition", it was the prize that gave Parvati the delight.The sentence aims to tell us that the prize gives delight to Parvati.

Another error is the use of for.In the sentence, "from" mus be used instead of "for".For is a preposition telling us the purpose of something.In the sentence, the preposition for is not used correctly because the sentence should be telling us from what is Parvati delighted.

To correctly write the sentence, use delighted instead of delightful, and use from instead of for.Erroneous Sentence: Parvati was delightful with her prize for coming first in the Mathematics competition. Corrected Sentence: Parvati was delighted with her prize from coming first in the Mathematics competition.

29. 10 swimmers training swimming competition in how many ways they can arrange the first second and third places​


[tex] \large\underline \mathcal{{QUESTION:}}[/tex]

10 swimmers training swimming competition in how many ways they can arrange the first second and third places.

[tex] \large\underline \mathcal{{SOLUTION:}}[/tex]

[tex]\textsf{ We will use the permutation formula:}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{P(n,r)=\frac{n!}{(n-r)!}}[/tex][tex]\textsf{Given that n=10 , r=3}[/tex]

[tex]\textsf{ Substituting the values we get:}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{P(n,r)=\frac{n!}{(n-r)!}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{P(10,3)=\frac{10!}{(10-3)!}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{P(10,3)=\frac{10!}{(7)!}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{P(10,3)=\frac{10×9×8×7!}{7!}}[/tex]

[tex]\textsf{ Cancel 7!}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{P(10,3)=\frac{10×9×8×\cancel{7!}}{\cancel{7!}}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{P(10,3)=10×9×8}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{P(10,3)=720}[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline \mathcal{{ANSWER:}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{There are 720 ways}[/tex][tex]\large \bold {ANSWER}[/tex]

[tex]\small\textsf{Using the permutation formula} \\ \\ \small\sf{_{n}P_{r}} = \frac{n!}{(n - r)! } \\ \\ \small\sf{_{10}P_{3}} = \frac{10!}{(10 - 3)! } \\ \\ \small\sf{_{10}P_{3}} = \frac{10!}{7! } \\ \\ \small\sf{_{10}P_{3}} = \frac{10 \times 9 \times 8 \times \cancel{7! }}{\cancel{7! }} \\ \\ \small\sf{_{10}P_{3}} = 10 \times 9 \times 8 \\ \\ \small\boxed{\green{\sf{ _{10}P_{3} = 720 \: ways}}}[/tex]
