1. When do we innovate products


Answer: We innovate products when there is a need to improve the product or explore new ways to reach out new customers

Explanation:

Innovation is the process or method in which an existing product or process is changed or modified by added something new to the product or service. This will allow new customers to purchase the product or service or keep existing customers engaged and satisfied.

For example, cellphones have been innovated in the recent decade by adding new features such as GPS, music players, cameras, and even mobile games that were added on top of the original purpose of the cellphone. These new features made the cellphone a ubiquitous item that everyone uses because it is not just tool for communication anymore. Today, cellphones are used by everyone not just as a way to talk to each other, but also produce amazing works of art, navigate around the world, or even entertain and socialize with other people.

Innovations can also be done when existing processes are not effective anymore and must be altered to meet the standards of the society. For a time, fossil fuels and coal are the main sources of energy and electricity for decades. However, a growing concern over the environmental impact, the exponential rise in demand for power, and finally the possible dwindling of non-renewable energy sources pushed scientists and energy companies to think and develop new sources of energy that can not only sustain the growing needs of the economy but also maintain a steady and effective supply of fuel. This propelled the research for "green energy" such as photovoltaic cells that utilizes solar energy, geothermal plants, biofuels, wind turbines, and even the shift of nuclear energy research into nuclear reactors that are now meeting the energy demands of highly-industrialized countries.

Innovation of products and services comes from the demand of the society to continually improve itself or when an update is needed to meet the current standards and expectations of the society. There is always an opportunity to add something new or to fix what is wrong about a product or service that is introduced.
