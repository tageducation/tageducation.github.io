4. What is Quota sampling ?


Answer:

Quota Sampling?

Explanation:

Quota sampling means to take a very tailored sample that’s in proportion to some characteristic or trait of a population. It is simple to do yet an effective way in the initial phases. The researchers can select two variables in a population so that they can study the particular group. It is also considered as a researchers judgement and a non-probability sampling. It is easy to administer, fast to create and complete most especially it is inexpensive. It can be use if the probability sampling techniques are not possible. Also, it is used in the companies especially when the person whose research topic is limited and when time is short. It is also use when details accuracy of the research is not that important.  Researchers can add other sub-points to the data set according to their research requirements. It also improves the representation of any particular group ensuring that they are not over presented.

26. How quota sampling different from stratified sampling?​


Answer:

The main difference between stratified sampling and quota sampling is that stratified sampling would select the students using a probability sampling method such as simple random sampling or systematic sampling. In quota sampling, no such technique is used

What is Snowball Sampling?

In sociology and statistics research, snowball sampling is a nonprobability sampling technique where existing study subjects recruit future subjects from among their acquaintances. Thus the sample group is said to grow like a rolling snowball
