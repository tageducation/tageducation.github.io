1. from what bodies of water do typhoons originate?​


Answer:

Typhoons and tropical cyclones usually originate from warm bodies of water. Most of the time they originate from the ocean, including the Pacific Ocean. Countries that are near the Pacific Ocean are usually affected by typhoons.

Answer:

In the Pacific Ocean

Explanation:

A typhoon is a hurricane in the western Pacific and it starts like hurricanes anywhere. The water is warm from stored solar energy and a storm comes along and kicks up spray which vastly increases the surface area of the water. That increases the rate of heat exchange from the water to the air many fold. The heated air, laden with moisture now expands and is lighter than the colder surrounding air. The heavier surrounding air pushes down and the lighter air gets pushed up like an inverted bucket of air in a swimming pool.

Typhoons are extremely large, powerful, and destructive storm that occurs especially in the region of the Philippines or the China Sea.

In some areas around the world, it is called Hurricane, Whirlwind, and Storm.

Basically, typhoons need warm air temperature to form and some other factors that can contribute to it's strengthening.

Typhoons originate in warm bodies of water. An example of this is the Ocean. And the area near the equator is the warmest part of the Earth which therefore means that most devastating and strong Typhoons originates in the Oceans near the equator. An example of this is the Pacific Ocean where most typhoons that struck the Philippines and other Asian countries come from.
