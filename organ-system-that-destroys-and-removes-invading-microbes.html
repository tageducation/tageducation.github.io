1. organ system that destroys and removes invading microbes​


Answer:

The immune system is a complex network of cells and proteins that defends the body against infection. The immune system keeps a record of every germ (microbe) it has ever defeated so it can recognise and destroy the microbe quickly if it enters the body again.


2. 3 organ system that destroys and removes invading microbes ​


Answer:

•The immune system- is made up of special organs, cells and chemicals that fight infection (microbes). The main parts of the immune system are: white blood cells, antibodies, the complement system, the lymphatic system, the spleen, the thymus, and the bone marrow.

•Enzymes in tears and saliva- break down bacteria. The stomach produces acid which destroys many of the microbes that enter the body in food and drink.

•1.)Primary lymphoid organs- These organs include the bone marrow and the thymus. They create special immune system cells called lymphocytes.

•2.) Secondary lymphoid organs: These organs include the lymph nodes, the spleen, the tonsils and certain tissue in various mucous membrane layers in the body (for instance in the bowel).

-White blood cells: Serving as an army against harmful bacteria and viruses, white blood cells search for, attack and destroy germs to keep you healthy.

-Lymph nodes: These small glands filter and destroy germs so they can't spread to other parts of your body and make you sick.

The tasks of the immune system:

1.) To fight disease-causing germs (pathogens) like bacteria, viruses, parasites or fungi, and to remove them from the body,

2.) To recognize and neutralize harmful substances from the environment, and.

3.) To fight disease-causing changes in the body, such as cancer cells.

19. the. are specialized white blood cells that identify and destroy invading bacteria and viruses​


Phagocytes are a type of white blood cell that move by amoeboid action. They send out pseudopodia which allows them to surround invading microbes and engulf them. Phagocytes release digestive enzymes which break down the trapped microbes before they can do any harm. This process is called phagocytosis.

Lymphocytes (Specifically T-Lymphocytes)

Explanation:

Lymphocytes is a type of WBCs which is only slightly larger than a red cell and sometimes found in the blood. Lymphocytes plays a vital role in the process of immunity.

There are two types of lymphocytes: T-cells and B-cells

T- cells are a type of lymphocytes which is originated in the red bone marrow and diffentiated in the thymus gland. Hence, it is called T-cells because it undergo a special process and produces thymus derived lymphocytes. The main function of the T-cells is to provide an important defense against many viral infections such as viruses and foreign bacterias.

B- cells on the other hand, originated in the bone marrow and differentiated at the fetal liver. This type of lymphocytes is responsible for antibody mediated immunity and secrete antibodies into the body fluids.
