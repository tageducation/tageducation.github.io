Algebra Problem #1

The square of a number equals nine times that number. Find the number.

[tex]\begin{aligned} &\sf x^2=9x \\&\sf x^2-9x=0 \\&\sf x(x-9)=0 \\&\sf x=0 \ , \ x-9=0 \\&\sf x_1=0 \ , \ \purple{x_2=9} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Answer:

The number is [tex]\boxed{\bold{\purple{9}}}[/tex].

[tex]\\[/tex]

Problem #2

Suppose that four times the square of a number equals 20 times that num-

ber. What is the number?

[tex]\begin{aligned} &\sf 4x^2=20x \\&\sf 4x^2 \div 4=20x \div 4 \\&\sf x^2=5x \\&\sf x^2-5x=0 \\&\sf x(x-5)=0 \\&\sf x=0 \ , \ x-5=0 \\&\sf x_1=0 \ , \ \purple{x_2=5} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Answer:

The number is [tex]\boxed{\bold{\purple{5}}}[/tex].

[tex]\\[/tex]

Problem #3

Twice the square of the sum of a number and 3 is 98. Find the number.

[tex]\begin{aligned} &\sf 2(x+3)^2=98 \\&\sf 2(x+3)^2 \div 2=98 \div 2 \\&\sf \sqrt{(x+3)^2}=\pm \sqrt{49} \\&\sf x+3=\pm 7 \\&\sf x+3=-7 \ , \ x+3=7 \\&\sf x_1=-7-3 \ , \ x_2=7-3 \\&\sf x_1=-10 \ , \ \purple{x_2=4} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Answer:

The number is [tex]\boxed{\bold{\purple{4}}}[/tex].

27. 6. A television station is considering the sale of promotional videos. It can have the videos produced by one of two suppliers. Supplier A will charge the station a set-up fee of $1,200 plus $2 for each DVD; supplier B has no set-up fee and will charge $4 per DVD. The station estimates its demand for the DVDs to be given by Q =1,600 -200P, where P is the price in dollars and Q is the number of DVDs. (The price equation is P= 8 - Q/200.) a. Suppose the station plans to give away the videos. How many DVDs should it order? From which supplier? b. Suppose instead that the station seeks to maximize its profit from sales of the DVDs. What price should it charge? How many DVDs should it order from which supplier? (Hint: Solve two separate problems, one with supplier A and one with supplier B, and then compare profits.
