Given:

[tex]\bold{Formula : } \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \\ \boxed{ \rm \: Mole = \dfrac{Mass}{Molar \: Mass} \: }[/tex]

Substitute the given values into the above formula and solve for mass:

[tex]\sf:\implies{Mole = \dfrac{Mass}{Molar \: Mass}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies{1.23 = \dfrac{Mass}{100.09}}[/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies{Mass = 1.23 \times 100.09}[/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies\bold{Mass = 123.11 \: grams} [/tex]

Therefore, the mass of CaCO₃ is 123.11 grams.
