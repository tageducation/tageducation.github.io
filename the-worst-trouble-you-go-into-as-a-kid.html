4. Why do you think children go back to their parents ỉn moments of trouble?


Answer:

Children often turn to their parents in times of trouble because of the strong emotional bond that exists between them. Parents are typically seen as a source of safety and security for children, and children often feel more comfortable and secure when they are near their parents. Additionally, parents are often the first people that children turn to for support and guidance, as they have a wealth of life experience and knowledge that they can share with their children.

Another reason why children may turn to their parents in times of trouble is because of the sense of trust and understanding that they have developed with their parents over time. Children feel that their parents understand them and will be there to help and support them, even in difficult situations.

Lastly, parents are the first role models that children have, they have been with them since they were born, and they have a deep understanding of the child's personality and needs. Therefore, children trust their parents’ judgement and guidance more than anyone else.

In summary, children tend to turn to their parents in times of trouble because of the strong emotional bond, sense of trust, and understanding that exists between them, as well as the parents’ life experience and knowledge.

27. 800 kids 12% go how many kids is 12%


Answer:

96

Step-by-step explanation:

Write 12% as

[tex] \frac{12}{100} [/tex]

Since, finding the fraction of a number is same as multiplying the fraction with the number, we have

[tex] \frac{12}{100} \: \: of \: \: 800 = \frac{12}{100} \times 800[/tex]

[tex] \frac{12 \times 800}{100} [/tex]

[tex] \frac{9600}{100} [/tex]

[tex]96[/tex]

Therefore, the answer is 96

If you are using a calculator,

simply enter 12÷100×800

which will give you 96 as the answer.
