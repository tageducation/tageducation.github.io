1. A player scores 21 points. (table tennis)


Table tennis is governed by the worldwide organization International Table Tennis Federation (ITTF). The table tennis official rules are specified in the ITTF handbook. Rules are also reviewed by ITTF, and if necessary changes are needed it is normally implemented with effect from the 1st of January of the following year.

Since 2000, there have been changes in the rules of table tennis. One of it is the change in point scoring system. Before, table tennis is played with a 21-point scoring system. Now it is changed to 11-point scoring system. The first player to reach 11 points wins the game, however a game must be won by at least a two point margin. A match is played best 3 of 5 games (or 4/7 or 5/9). If both player scores 10, it will be a deuce. The change in the point scoring system became effective in September 2001.

The answer is letter B deuce. Here are the explanation.

GAMES UP TO 11 POINTS OR 21 POINTS Until 2001, up to 21 points were played in a table tennis  match. But the rules of table tennis have been changed to make the game more exciting, have more  "crucial" points and make it more appealing to television viewers.  

A table tennis game is currently played with up to 11 points. The winner is the player or pair who scores 11 points first, so that both players or pairs score 10 points (called deuce). Then, the first player or pair must  win a game to earn a two-point advantage.

HOW MANY SERVES?

When table tennis rules were changed to make each game up to 11 points instead of 21, the number of  serves for each player changed as well.  

Now each player has two serves each, while each player had five serves in the old 21 point system.  

Service keeps alternating between opponents until one player scores 11 points, whether both players or  pairs score 10 (called deuce) points.  If both players hit 10 points, then after each point, service alternates until one player gains a lead of two.
