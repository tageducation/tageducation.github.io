About animal locomotion

Animals move from one place to another using their limbs, such as legs, fins, wings, or even their abdominal muscles.

The locomotion of animals is adapted to their habitat.

Animals move for various purposes, for example, to find food or prey, avoid enemy pursuit, or carry out the process of reproduction.

There are two types of locomotion in animals, namely muscles (active locomotion) and bones (passive locomotion). Both of them will work together to carry out the movement needed by the animal to sustain its life. Apart from bones and muscles, this movement is also supported by joints, the three of which then form a movement system. All primate species have legs that are used for walking or climbing up trees. However, feet are also found in many other animals, such as frogs (used for jumping) and various types of reptiles (used for walking).

In addition, almost all mammal species also have legs, for example, horses, elephants, zebras, cats, dogs, lions, deer, bears, and so on.Birds and bats also have legs, which help them get to high places such as the ceiling of a cave or tree branches.
