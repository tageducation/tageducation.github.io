14. Eugene wants to buy jeans at a store that is giving $10 off everything. The tag on the jeans is marked 50% off. The original price is $49.98


Answer:

$14.99

Step-by-step explanation:

Here are the three things we need to consider:

1. The jeans originally cost $49.98.

2. The jeans are to be marked down to 50%.

3. Everything in the shop will be sold $10 less.

This problem requires two steps to arrive at the final price the jeans.

First, we consider how much the jeans would cost if we apply the discount or the percentage markdown. That is finding 50% of $49.98 and subtracting it from the price.

Discounted Price = Original Price ÷ Discount Percentage

Discounted Price = 49.98 ÷ 50%

Discounted Price = 49.98 ÷ 2

Discounted Price = 24.99

Now we have the discounted price of the jeans, we need to find the final amount to be paid at the cashier when the store-wide discount is applied.

Final Price = Discounted Price - Store Discount

Final Price = 24.99 - 10

Final Price = 14.99

Answer:

the original price of the item was PHP 5,050.00

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the original price of the item, we need to work backwards and undo the effects of the discounts. If the item was marked down by the same percentage for three years in a row, then the first year it was marked down by 50/3 = 16.67%. This means the price of the item after the first year was 100 - 16.67 = 83.33% of its original price.

After the second year, the price of the item was marked down by another 50/3 = 16.67%, so the price after two years was 83.33 - 16.67 = 66.66% of its original price. Since the price of the item after two years was 50% off its original price, this means that the price after two years was 50/100 = 0.50 times the original price.

Since the price of the item after two years was PHP 2,525.00, we can set up an equation to find the original price of the item:

0.50 * original price = PHP 2,525.00

Solving for the original price, we get:

original price = PHP 2,525.00 / 0.50
