20. Why do you think your physical activities are healthy/ unhealthy?​


Answer:

Physical activity or exercise can improve your health and reduce the risk of developing several diseases like type 2 diabetes, cancer and cardiovascular disease. Physical activity and exercise can have immediate and long-term health benefits. Most importantly, regular activity can improve your quality of life.


21. physical inactivity and an unhealthy diet are risk factors for heart disease


Answer:

Unhealthy diets (especially those which have a high content in fats, free sugars and salt) and physical inactivity are among some of the leading causes of noncommunicable diseases (NCDs) including cardiovascular diseases (CVD), type 2 diabetes and certain cancers.

Answer:

Benefits of regular physical activity

If you are regularly physically active, you may:

reduce your risk of a heart attack 

manage your weight better 

have a lower blood cholesterol level 

lower the risk of type 2 diabetes and some cancers 

have lower blood pressure 

have stronger bones, muscles and joints and lower risk of developing osteoporosis 

lower your risk of falls 

recover better from periods of hospitalisation or bed rest 

feel better – with more energy, a better mood, feel more relaxed and sleep better.

30. Why is lack of physical activity unhealthy​


Answer:

Not getting enough physical activity can lead to heart disease—even for people who have no other risk factors. It can also increase the likelihood of developing other heart disease risk factors, including obesity, high blood pressure, high blood cholesterol, and type 2 diabetes.
