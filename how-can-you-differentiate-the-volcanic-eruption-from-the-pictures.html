Eruption is the process of releasing material from a volcano into the atmosphere and onto the surface of the Earth in an erratic amount. It can also be interpreted that an eruption is a volcanic eruption, or a burst of hot oil and steam from the bowels of the Earth. Based on its form, the material that comes out of the eruption is divided into solid, liquid and gaseous materials.

Solid materials such as large stones, small stones, gravel, sand, and volcanic ash). Liquid materials such as lava, hot lava, and cold lava. While gaseous materials, include carbon dioxide, sulfide acid, sulfuric acid, chloride, water vapor, and hydrochloric acid.

Eruption Types

The types of eruptions based on their strength are divided into three types, namely Hawaiian, Strombolian, and Vulkanian.

Hawaiian eruptions

The Hawaiian type of eruption is characterized by the eruption of molten lava originating from the crater for quite a long time.

Strombolian eruptions

The characteristics of the Strombolian type eruption come from gas and magma fragments or flakes. Erupted material falls back into the crater or around the crater rim.

Vulcanian eruptions

The eruption is explosive and the magma that forms a vulcanian type eruption is between basic and acidic (from andesite to dacite). Volcanian eruptions occur because the crater hole is closed by plugs of lava or magma that freezes in the magma pipe after the eruption.
