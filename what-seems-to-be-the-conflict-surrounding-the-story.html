CHECHNYA CONFLICT

In the 1990s, the most recent battle between the Chechnya​ and the Russian government occurred. As the Soviet Union dissolved in 1991, Chechnya​ proclaimed its independence. Late in 1994, the First Chechnya​ War broke out, and two years later, in December 1996, Russian forces withdrew from the region.

Russia fought two wars with Chechnya between 1994 and 2000. In both wars, Russia bombarded Chechnya mercilessly, leveling Grozny and killing tens of thousands of civilians. Russia launches a massive bombardment assault. Urban areas are reduced to ruins.

Since 1992, the discussion in Chechnya over independence has led to a small-scale civil war in which the Russians have attempted secretly to overthrow the government of Dzhokhar Dudayev. 1994 marked the beginning of the First Chechnya War when Russian soldiers entered Chechnya to restore constitutional order.

Attempts to defeat the Chechnya independence movement in post-Soviet Russia produced the extraordinarily deadly and savage First Chechnya War, which lasted from December 1994 to August 1996. The war concluded with a humiliating defeat for the Russian Armed Forces, but Chechnya was also devastated (Hodgson, 2003).

Estimates of Second Chechnya War mortality range significantly from 25,000 to 200,000 civilian deaths and 8,000 to 40,000 Russian military deaths.
