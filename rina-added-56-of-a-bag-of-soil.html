3. rina added 5/6 of a bag of soil


Answer:

a. What is asked?

The amount of soil that Jessa added more than Rina.

b. What are given?

⅚ and 1 ⅛

c. What is the operation to be used?

Subtraction

d. What is the number sentence?

1 ⅛ - ⅚ = N

e. What is the answer?

Jessa added ‌7⁄24‌ bags of soil more than Rina.

Explanation:

How to Subtract Dissimilar Fractions

The given fractions above are examples of dissimilar fractions. Dissimilar fractions are fractions with different denominators. The problem is asking for the amount of soil that Jessa added more than Rina. The clue words in the given word problem above is "how much more". This means that we have to subtract their bags of soil.

These are the steps in subtracting dissimilar fraction.

Find their LCD or least common denominator.

Make the fractions similar by dividing the LCD by the denominator, times the numerator.

Subtract the numerators and copy the denominator.

Simplify, if possible.

Solution:

Step 1: LCD

1 ⅛ = 8, 16, 24

⅚ = 6, 12, 18, 24

Step 2: LCD ÷ denominator × numerator

1 ⅛: 24 ÷ 8 × 1 = 3 -> 1 ‌3⁄24‌

⅚: 24 ÷ 6 × 5 = 20 -> 20⁄24‌

Step 3: Subtract

1 ‌3⁄24‌ - 20⁄24‌ = 27⁄24‌ - 20⁄24‌ = 7⁄24‌

Jessa added ‌7⁄24‌ bags of soil more than Rina.

b. Given: 5/6 -  bag of soil Rina had added to her garden

             1 1/8 - bags of soil Jessa had added to her garden

c. Operations: subtraction

d. Number Sentence: 1 1/8 - 5/6 = N

e. Answer: 7/24 bags

Solutions:

Express 1 1/8 as an improper fraction.

1 1/8 = (8)(1) + 1/8 = 8 + 1/8 = 9/8

Express 9/8 and 5/6 as similar fractions using their Least Common Denominator.

8 - 8, 16, 24

6 - 6, 12, 18, 24

Therefore, the least common denominator will be 24.

9/8 = (3)(9)/24 = 27/24

5/6 = (4)(5)/24 = 20/24

Subtract 20/24 from 27/24.

27/24 - 20/24 = 7/24

Therefore, Jessa have 7/24 bags of soil more than that of Rina.

23. Rina added 5/6 of bag of soil to her garden her neighbor jessa added 1 1/8 bags of soil to her garden how much more did jessa add than rina


Answer:

Jessa has added [tex]\frac{7}{24}[/tex] of a bag of soil more than Rina.

Step-by-step explanation:

We have two different fractions. One is a proper fraction and the other one is a mixed number. Take note that we can combine easily if the fractions are similar, which means that they should have the same denominator.

Different Types of FractionsProper FractionImproper FractionMixed Number

The solution that we need to do is to subtract the bag of soil added by Rina from the bag of soil added by Jessa. This is to clearly know how much difference of bag of soil is added.

Steps in Converting a Mixed number to an Improper FractionMultiply the whole number by the denominator. Add the product to the numerator.Form the fraction by making the sum from step 2 as the numerator and copy the denominator from the original fraction.

           [tex]1 \times 8 = 8[/tex]

           [tex]8+1=9[/tex]

     Then, the improper fraction is [tex]\frac{9}{8}[/tex].

Now, let's subtract [tex]\frac{5}{6}[/tex] from [tex]\frac{9}{8}[/tex].

Steps in Combining Dissimilar FractionsSolve for the least common denominator. Use the LCD to transform to similar fractionsSubtract the fractions.

The LCD of 6 and 8 is 24.

        [tex]\frac{24}{6} \rightarrow \frac{ \text{LCD}}{ \text{Denominator of the First Fraction}}=4[/tex], so [tex]\frac{5 \times 4}{6 \times 4}=\frac{20}{24}[/tex]

       [tex]\frac{24}{8}\rightarrow \frac{\text{LCD}}{\text{Denominator of the second fraction}} = 3[/tex], so [tex]\frac{9\times 3}{8 \times 3}=\frac{27}{24}[/tex]

    Then,

                           [tex]\begin{aligned}\frac{27}{24}-\frac{20}{24}&=\frac{27-20}{24}\\&=\frac{7}{24}\end{aligned}[/tex]

This means to say that Jessa has added [tex]\frac{7}{24}[/tex] of a bag of soil more than Rina.
