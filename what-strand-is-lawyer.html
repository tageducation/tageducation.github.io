15. What educational qualification is required for lawyer? ​


Education

To become a licensed and practicing lawyer, most students will need to complete four years of undergraduate study and three years of law school. While no specific undergraduate course of study is necessary, students can prepare for the practice of law by taking courses in areas such as philosophy, history, English and other humanities studies, in addition to mathematics and science classes. To be admitted to law school, students who have completed or will soon complete an undergraduate degree need to complete a standardized test and application process. Admission to law school can be quite competitive, and the required tuition can be expensive.

In the DNA base pairing between the two strands of DNA,

   *A (adenine) is pair with T (Thymine) vice versa

   *G (Guanine) is pair with C (Cytosine) vice versa

  A    A     T    G    C    C    A   G   T   G    G    T

  ↓     ↓     ↓    ↓    ↓     ↓    ↓    ↓   ↓   ↓     ↓     ↓

  T     T     A    C   G     G   T    C   A   C    C    A

Here are the list of nitrogenous bases of DNA

   1.Adenine

   2.Thymine

  3. Guanine

   4.Cytosine

These sequence of nitrogenous bases is very important in making or synthesizing a protein needed for the body. Segment of DNA which codes for protein or genetic materials is known gene. The gene sequence is necessary in the body in maintaining its genetic instruction. Problem with miss pairing of the nitrogenous bases would result to some gene mutation which is accompanied by medical or genetic disorders.


29. Is it possible to be a lawyer if you're a teacher? Please answer my question. I'm having hard time right because I don't know what strand to choose in Senior High School and what course to take in college. I'm not sure about my future and I really need advices. ​


If you're asking if you could switch careers later on from a teacher to a lawyer, then yes, of course! It could be a little harder though depending on what subject you're thinking of teaching, but it's still very much doable!

Of course, before becoming a lawyer you're going to have to take into account that after a Bachelor's, you'll also need to apply and go to law school, and then take the bar exam.

I'm less familiar with the process of becoming a teacher, but I do know a lot of them begin teaching right after college.

For choosing your Senior High strand, I say to think about what you want to take up for college; and see which strand will help you get there. If you're still unsure of what to take up for college, there's nothing wrong with choosing GAS/General Academic Strand (if your school offers it).

Remember, you're still young and still have the rest of your life! And there's nothing wrong with changing your mind and plans later on.

Good luck!
