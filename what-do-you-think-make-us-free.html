The advertiser also emphasizes that the product has an oil-control formula, is hypoallergenic, and dermatologically tested to be mild even on sensitive skin.
In terms of language used, the propaganda appears to have achieved its purpose of convincing the customer to buy the product. The advertiser used glittering generalities, which are designed to appeal to people's emotions and desires, rather than to their reason and logic. The advertiser used positive and appealing words and phrases to make the product seem like a miracle cure that can solve all of the teenager's skin problems in just seven days.

It is important to be cautious and do research before buying any product, especially those that make extraordinary claims.

Understanding the different propaganda techniques can help people identify when they are being manipulated and make more informed decisions.
