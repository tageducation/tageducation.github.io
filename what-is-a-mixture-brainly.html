8. What makes pure substance different from mixture brainly


Answer:

a pure substance consists only of one element or one compound. a mixture consists of two or more different substances, not chemically joined together.

Explanation:

A pure substance is made up of only one kind of particle. For example, table sugar only contains sugar particles and distilled water contains only water particles. Mixtures are made up of pure substances combined together. A mixture is composed of at least two different kinds of particles.

Examples of Mixture

Seawater: A mixture of salt and water

Air: A mixture of gases like oxygen, carbon dioxide, nitrogen, and others

Vinegar: A mixture of water and acetic acid

Mud: A mixture of water and dirt

Vegetable Salad: A mixture of various kinds of leafy vegetables like lettuce, cucumber, and tomatoes

Mixture

A mixture is a kind of matter that is made up of two or more substances that are not combined chemically. Mixtures can be separated by physical methods, such as filtration and distillation.

General Properties of Mixture:

The components of the mixture keep their original form.

The components of it can be separated physically.

It contains a variable composition.

Types of Mixture

1. Homogeneous - A mixture that contains two or more uniform components. It has only one visible phase.

Example: Alcohol in water, air, and a mixture of salt and water

2. Heterogeneous - Unlike homogenous, it does not have a uniform appearance. The components of it can be seen separately.

Example: A mixture of sand and soil, oil and water, and fruit salad
