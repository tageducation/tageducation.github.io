A snake is not considered a worm. Worms are invertebrate animals that belong to the phylum Annelida, which includes segmented worms such as earthworms, ragworms, and leeches. Snakes, on the other hand, are vertebrate animals that belong to the class Reptilia and are known for their elongated, legless bodies and ability to slither on the ground. 11. which of the following is not considered a worm A.ascaris B. fluke C. planaria D.millipede​


The correct answer is letter D-millipede.

Millepede belongs to arthropods since it has a segmented body and jointed legs. Ascaris, fluke, and planarian belong to helminth or worm with an elongated, flat or round body. Ascaris is a parasitic worm in the intestine. Fluke is a parasitic flatworm infecting the bile duct. Planaria is a non-parasitic worm living in freshwater.
