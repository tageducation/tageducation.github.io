28. 1. Which of the following temperature is warmer?A. 20 °FB. 10 °CC. 150 °KD. They are the same​


TEMPERATURE CONVERSION

The warmer temperature is 10 °C (B).

FURTHER EXPLANATION

The definition of a temperature conversion formula is one that changes a temperature value from one unit to another. There are numerous unique methods for converting temperatures. The most widely used temperature scales are Kelvin, Celsius, and Fahrenheit. The formulas for converting temperatures are as follows:

Celsius to Kelvin: K = C + 273.15Kelvin to Celcius: C = K - 273.15Fahrenheit to Celcius: C = (F - 32) (5/9)Celsius to Fahrenheit: F = C(9/5) + 32Fahrenheit to Kelvin: K = (F - 32) (5/9) + 273.15Kelvin to Fahrenheit: F = (K - 273.15) (9/5) + 32

UNIT OF TEMPERATURE

The temperature can be recorded in a variety of units. Temperature is measured in three main ways: Celsius (°C), Fahrenheit (°F), and Kelvin (K). The generally used scales for measuring temperature are Fahrenheit and Celsius, whereas the SI unit for doing so is Kelvin.

GIVEN

Which of the following temperature is warmer?

A. 20 °F

B. 10 °C

C. 150 °K

D. They are the same​

Solution

20 °F = ... °C

C = (F - 32) (5/9)C = (20 - 32) (5/9)C = (-12) (5/9)C = -60/9 C -6,67 °C

10 °C = 10 °C

150 °K = ... °C

C = K - 273.15C = 150 - 273.15C = -123.15 °C

Then, -123.15 °C < 10 °C

Therefore, the warmer temperature is 10 °C (B).
