30. What do volcanoes and mountains have in common?Explian your answer.​


Answer:

Some mountains are volcanoes, others are not.

Some volcanoes form mountains, others do not.

In Earth science (geology, ecology, meteorology, etc.) the term "mountain" is a descriptive term while "volcano is a genetic term.

What this means is calling a landform feature a "mountain" is simply describing its size and shape, thus "descriptive." Many volcanoes can be described as mountains, such as Mt. Fuji (Japan) or Mt. Rainier (USA).

Calling a feature a "volcano" implies you know there have been eruptions of lava or ash at this location. Since naming a feature a volcano tells everyone how the feature came to be formed, volcano is a "genetic" term.

Many volcanoes do not form mountains, such as the Laki fissure or the Ukinrek Maars.
