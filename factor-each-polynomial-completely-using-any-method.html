A Polynomial Function

A combination of the Greek poly, meaning "many", and the Latin nomen, meaning "name", forms the word polynomial. Simply by switching out the Latin bi- for the Greek poly-, the word binomial was born. To put it another way, it means the addition of several different phrases (many monomials). In the seventeenth century, the term polynomial was first used.

A mathematical statement of one or more algebraic terms, each of which consists of a constant multiplied by one or more variables raised to a non-negative integral power (such as a + bx + cx²) is called a polynomial function.

An expression involving one or more variables, coefficients, and exponents of variables that are non-negative integers is called a polynomial. Algebraic expressions having the same value are said to be equal when the sign "equal to" is placed between them in a mathematical statement.
