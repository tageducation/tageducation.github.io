28. What is a bias? What is a prejudice? What is the difference of the two? Why are these two behaviors not acceptable in our society?​


Answer:

Bias - A personal and sometimes unreasoned judgment.

Prejudice - An unjustified or incorrect attitude (usually negative) towards an individual based solely on the individual's membership of a social group.

Why are these two behaviors not acceptable in our society?​

Such biases can potentially cause us to make decisions that are neither wise nor fair. It can also cause us to make discriminatory decisions. Prejudice can become dangerous if it is spread on a large scale. Negative prejudices that are common in a society can cause tension between groups, or result in groups of people being discriminated against or treated unequally on the basis of, for example, their background, skin color, or religion. This is called discrimination.
