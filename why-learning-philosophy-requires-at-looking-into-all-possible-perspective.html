PHILOSOPHY

1. The study or discipline that uses human reason to investigate the ultimate causes, reasons, and principles which govern all things is called Philosophy.

2. The Greek term for Philosophy is called Philosophia, which is from the two greek words Philo (love) and Sophia (wisdom). Philosophy in literal translation means "love of wisdom".

3. Reflection is the activity that requires a person to examine his or her thoughts, feelings, and actions and learn from experience.

4. A perspective that considers the "bigger picture" when dealing with a particular situation or problem is called holistic perspective/thinking. According to Aristotle, a holistic perspective is when the whole is taken more into consideration rather than only parts of it. It considers all perspectives and not only focuses on one.

5. Quite opposite of the holistic perspective/thinking is the partial perspective/thinking. It focuses on the specific aspects of a situation, just like how a person focuses on certain areas of a problem in order or him to understand it.
