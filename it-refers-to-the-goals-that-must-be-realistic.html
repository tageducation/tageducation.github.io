What are some examples of realistic goals?

Realistic goals are strategic goals with certain characteristics that help you easily achieve them. As an example, let’s take an unrealistic goal of yours is going to mars. Why it is unrealistic? Because you don’t know how you are going to do it, you have no knowledge, resources, or a plan on how you are going to do it. You can make it realistic by planning the detailed steps on how you are going to do it.

A simple example of a realistic goal will be reading a book.

1. First, can you read?

2. Which book you will be reading?

3. It is really what you what to do?

4. Why do you want to do it?

5. If you are clear about what and why you want to do it. You should start by planning how many chapters/pages you will read in a day/week/month.

What makes it a realistic goal? You know why, when, and what you are doing. To make a goal realistic, you need to have a detailed plan and enough information about what you want. Take your goal as a semester project and divide it into parts, and then further divide those parts into sub-parts until you get a list of milestones. If you can divide your goals into milestone, it is a realistic goal. You can use spreadsheets, sticky notes, notepad, or anything you think will assist you during the process.
