7. What are the products of photosynthesis and cellular respiration? why are these two process important in plants?​


Answer:

Water, carbondioxide, glucose.

Explanation:

1)The photosynthsis and cellular respiration are opposite to each other. Photosynthesis is an anabolic process, while the respiration is a catabolic process.

2)In photosynthesis, carbondioxide and water combines to form energy rich glucose. Light energy is stored in chemical energy.

3)In cellular respiration, the glucose is oxidiesd in presence of oxygen into water and carbondioxide. The ATP is released as source of energy.

Answer:

why are these two process important in plants?

Photosynthesis converts carbon dioxide and water into oxygen and glucose. Glucose is used as food by the plant and oxygen is a by-product. Cellular reaspiration converts oxygen and glucose into water and carbon dioxide. Water and carbon dioxide are by- products and ATP is energy that is transformed from the process.
