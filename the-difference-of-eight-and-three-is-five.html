Five less than twice the difference of five and a number is thirty-eight. The quotient of the sum of a number and six and the difference of twice the number and three is thirteen.


Answer:

Twice the product of a number and three is eleven: 2(3x)=11

Five times the difference of four and a number is fifteen: 5(4-x)=15

Five added to the difference of a number and four is equivalent to the sum of the number and one: 5+x-4=x+1

Half the sum of twice a number and three is equal to sixteen: 1/2(2x+3)=16

Eleven diminished by half of a number is seventeen: 11-(1/2)x=17

Three more than twice the sum of a number and six is eighteen: 3+(2(x+6))=18

Five less than twice the difference of five and a number is thirty-eight: 5+(2(5-x))=38

The quotient of the sum of a number and six and the difference of twice the number and three is thirteen: (x+6)/(2x-3)=13
