18. why do you think microbiology and parasitology is included in the curriculum of nursing course ?​


Explanation:

Microbiology and parasitology are important components of the nursing curriculum because they provide nurses with essential knowledge about infectious diseases and parasites. This knowledge gives nurses the ability to identify and diagnose potential health issues, as well as develop effective plans of care and treatment. Additionally, nurses need to understand the transmission of infectious diseases in order to provide appropriate preventive measures and interventions. Finally, nurses must be aware of the various medical treatments available for infectious diseases and parasites in order to appropriately refer patients for diagnosis and treatment.

30. Why do you think it is important to learn home nursing practices?​


HOME NURSING PRACTICES

Infections that patients are exposed to in hospitals can be prevented by nursing care at home. Patients who have had surgery or received critical care may be more vulnerable to recurrence and infection. Because the patient is less exposed to infections, in-home nursing care can help the patient recuperate faster and better.

A patient may require emergency treatment that necessitates a number of different procedures on a daily basis. Because it is not cost-effective to keep a patient in a hospital for a long time, in-hospital care is usually substantially more expensive. In-home care is a type of medical care in which a patient receives hospital-quality medical care in the comfort of their own home.

Long-term confinement in a hospital setting can have a severe impact on a person's mental health. As a result of their surroundings, hospitals are frequently associated with "something wrong" and can leave patients burdened and stressed. Negativity can also result from seeing doctors, nurses, and patients on a regular basis.

In most cases, hospital care is a straitjacket, and everyone receives the same treatment. As a result, there isn't much flexibility for adjusting to the patient's unique needs and independence. Patients may become dissatisfied in one way or another as a result of this.

Bathing, grooming, cooking, and cleaning are all things that some people find difficult to do on their own. In-home care from a professional might also make it easier to carry out these daily tasks.
