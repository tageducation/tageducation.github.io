3. a violent attack threat or attempt to harm a person


Answer:

A violent attack, threat or attempt to harm a person is called ASSAULT.

An assault is the act of giving physical or in some cases physical contact that's unwanted. An assault in legal terms can also come to mean as a threat to do such an action. It is against the law to assault someone and is considered a criminal or civil liability depending on the case.

Types of Assault

Simple Assault

This type of assault occurs when a person threatens or attempts to cause physical harm to another person. No weapons have been used and that the victims suffered relatively minor injuries due to the assault.

Aggravated Assault

This type of assault happens when : 1. a weapon is used 2. the assault happened  together with another crime and 3. when the force used in the assault is great.

Aggravated assault occurs when a weapon is used, when assault occurs during another crime, or when increased force is used. In some states, the victim must suffer physical injuries before an assault can be considered aggravated.

Sexual Assault

A sexual assault happens when a person attempts to commit a sexual act without the other person's consent.  In a sexual assault, the criminal  sodomizes, molests, rapes or commits any other sex crimes.
