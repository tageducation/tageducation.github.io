1. wrong send or wrong sent


The Use of Adverbs

None of them are right. The correct one is wrongly send/wrongly sent. It is because wrong is adjective and adjective does not modify verb at all. the only one which can modify the verb is the adverb. So the correct one is to put adverb and verb together. wrongly as an adverb and sent as the verb.

To answer correctly, we must know what is adverb.

Adverb

An adverb is a word that describes a verb, an adjective, an adverb, and a whole sentence. Commonly, an adverb is a word that is ended with -ly, but some of them are not.

Example:

Describe a verb

"She eats slowly", 'Slowly' is the adverb and it describes eat (verb),

Describe an adjective

"He is very handsome.", 'very' is the adverb that describes handsome (adjective)

Describe an adverb

"Anna shows it too vividly.", 'too' is the adverb describes vividly (another adverb)

Describe Whole sentence

"Yesterday, I went to City Park." 'Yesterday' is the adverb that describes 'I went to City Park' sentence

How to Use Adverbs

1. Adverbs can be placed at the beginning of a sentence, it modifies a whole sentence. "Luckily, I made it this time"

2. Adverbs can be placed at the end of a sentence, modifying a verb.

" You draw beautifully"

3. Adverbs can be placed in the middle of a sentence and modifies an adjective.

"Jennie is remarkably smart"

In conclusion that we have to remember is adjectives can not modify or describe a verb. Adjectives only can modify a noun. The only thing that can modify a verb is an adverb.

19. What is correct wrong send or wrong sent?


With the question, "Which is correct, wrong send or wrong sent?", I believe neither is right. These phrases are examples of a localized English usage. Taking the grammatical rules in English, the best possible way to say the phrase is "wrongly sent" which is the incomplete form of "The message was wrongly sent." Adverbs modify verbs and we are making use of an adverb of manner here. Therefore, we are supposed to say "wrongly" instead of "wrong" because the sentence is modifying the verb "to send". Adverbs of manner answer "how" the verbs happen.

The sentence is also done in the Passive Voice form. Passive Voice is used to emphasize that the object of the sentence is the one that experiences the action rather than the subject doing the action. The sentence "The message was wrongly sent." can be done either in Simple Past Passive or Present Perfect Passive Voice (The message has been wrongly sent.) form because we are trying to talk about a "finished" action.
