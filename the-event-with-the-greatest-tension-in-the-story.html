Answer:

In every story, there is a corresponding argument or conflict that needs resolving by the main characters. And through the journey in finding clues and other stuff to help the hero do so until he reaches the point where there is a great tension between opposing forces where readers will gain excitement and thrill to read what happens next.

Examples of Climax in a story:

When the protagonist and the antagonist are engaged in an intense conflict with one another When the natural forces opposing the protagonist reaches its peak When someone or something gets in the way on accomplishing the character’s goal

30. it creates tension and builds suspense in the story​


Answer:

Conflict

Explanation:

Conflict is one of the key ingredients of suspenseful writing. Conflict automatically creates tension in scenes, so naturally, it's an author's best friend. What makes conflict even more important is its versatility. There are different types of conflict too.

Four factors are necessary for suspense—reader empathy, reader concern, impending danger and escalating tension. We create reader empathy by giving the character a desire, wound or internal struggle that readers can identify with. The more they empathize, the closer their connection with the story will be
