6. i have known that critical thinking character communication ?I realized that critical thinking character communication?​


Answer:

Truth is the compatibility between knowledge and objects, it can also be interpreted as an opinion or act of someone that is in accordance with others and does not harm oneself. Truth is the opposite of error which is an object and knowledge is incompatible. True knowledge is knowledge that corresponds to the object, that is, objective knowledge. Since an object has many aspects, it is difficult to cover all aspects (try to cover the whole truth of the object) Truth is the correspondence between statements and reality. A statement is said to be true if there is empirical evidence to support it.

These are how you tell the truth to others:

1. Telling the truth with valid evidence

Before you start telling the truth, there are a few examples of opening sentences that you can use. Next you need to understand that the target in conveying facts is to convince someone else. So, when you want to convey the truth, you must include evidence that can corroborate the truth you convey. The facts you quote will lead your interlocutor into the topic you raise. There are several techniques you can use. Gale Carnegie describes it with the principle of “DEFEATS”, namely:

Demonstration, by demonstrating the example directly in front of the audience. Examples, by giving real examples, can be by showing via video or telling events that recently happened.

Facts, namely by using facts that can be easily verified, not confusing, and cannot be debated. You can use it by quoting words from experts or important figures related to the topic. Or the results of other people's research that has been verified.

2. Using a Simple Analogy

To make it easier for the opponent to understand the facts you convey, every fact you quote or analogy you convey, must be clearly related to the truth you want to highlight. In this case, it is very important to pay attention to whether our analogy is apple to apple or not. You have to compare something with a comparable measure.

3. Pay attention to the intonation of speech

This is very important because it ensures that the truth is valid. Do not rush to set according to the rhythm that suits you. Don't stutter, especially in a high tone, remember that emotions when conveying the truth will have a negative effect. In addition, being carried away by emotions will also make other people feel uncomfortable.

4. Using polite words

Submission of something logical and factual but without packaging in polite language will make the audience disrespect or even hurt by what you say. So, keep an eye on the dictions you convey.
