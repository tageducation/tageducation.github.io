2. From viewing, cite the important components needed to produce an Opera?


Answer: The opera is made up of 5 different components: Overture, Recitative, Aria, Chorus, and Ballet.

Explanation:

Opera, a staged drama set to music in its entirety, made up of vocal pieces with instrumental accompaniment and usually with orchestral overtures and interludes. It is also an extended dramatic composition, in which all parts are sung to instrumental accompaniment.

5 components of an Opera:

Overture it is an instrumental composition intended especially as an introduction to an extended work, such as an opera or oratorio.Recitative it is a little break from the more complicated singing.Aria is a song for one of the leading singers in an opera or choral work.Chorus is a group of singers or a refrain in a songBallet is a classical dance form demanding grace and precision and employing formalized steps and gestures set in intricate, flowing patterns to create expression through movement. Ballet came to be associated with story-telling.

13. What is components of an opera


An opera is a form of theater that has a combination of different theatrical elements of drama such as music, dance, costume, scenery, lighting, and a lot more.

The opera has different components. These are Overture, Recitative, Aria, Chorus, and Ballet. Each of them has a significant function in the play as a whole.

The Overture is the introduction of the opera. This component arranges the music and prepares the audience for ready. The Recitative is the part where the actors' speech takes place.The Aria is the component in which singers express a song.The Chorus is where the opera is in complete rhythm. Finally, the Ballet. It is the last part where the actors finish their story and the performance ends.

30. what are the important features and description that compose the theatrical components of an opera​


Answer:

Opera is a form of theatre in which music is a fundamental component and dramatic roles are taken by singers, but is distinct from musical theatre.[1] Such a "work" (the literal translation of the Italian word "opera") is typically a collaboration between a composer and a librettist[2] and incorporates a number of the performing arts, such as acting, scenery, costume, and sometimes dance or ballet. The performance is typically given in an opera house, accompanied by an orchestra or smaller musical ensemble, which since the early 19th century has been led by a conductor.
