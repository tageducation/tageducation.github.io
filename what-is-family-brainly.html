Answer:

As the family is considered the smallest basic unit of the society, the family members are bound with the duty in primarily shaping the person’s desirable traits and good manners to be possessed in order to grow as a person who is accountable and responsible towards the consequences of his/ her actions during the course of childhood. Aside from this, the family creates the environment or the atmosphere in the everyday lives of the household, which is observed and adapted by the child; theobservations may become the child’s means towards interacting and socialization to other people outside the family. A person’s personality and characteristics is molded through the discipline applied during his/her childhood, which comprises of the principles to abide to all the time, along with the consequences of going against the house rules implemented. Moreover, the family teaches a child of the customary beliefs and traditions to conform with, hence affecting the philosophy of a growing individual. In terms of speaking with the individual’s life, the parents in the family train their offspring of chores and other skills to be able to equip them of learnings in order for them to become independent as they develop and grow into more mature individuals. Through their guidance and advice, they keep an individual on the right path of life. With their strong influence, they first-hand molded most of the behavior and characteristics an individual portrays as he/she grows.
