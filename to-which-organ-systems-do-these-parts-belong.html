22. to which organ system do these parts belong


Answer: INTEGUMENTARY SYSTEM

Integumentary system includes skin, hair, subcutaneous fat, nails, eyes, some mucus membranes, as well as the pores. These are what parts of the human body do you see when enumerating the organs of integumentary system of human body.

We have numbers of organ systems. Can you identify these organ systems? Circulatory system, respiratory system, muscular system, skeletal system, digestive system and our topic, the integumentary system. Army and rescue team are how do these organ system work together.

Integumentary system is the organ system that composed of your skin, hair, nails, and exocrine glands. Skin is a few millimetres thick but it is also the largest part or largest organ of the human body. The average human body’s skin weighs almost 10lbs and it has surface area of 20 sq. ft.  

The most superficial layer of the skin the epidermis as it covers almost our entire human body surface. Skin is providing protection to the tissues underlying beneath it and it is a protection from pathogens, physical and mechanical damages, as well as against the ultraviolet light.

However, hair is the so-called accessory organ of the skin. Hair is actually made of tightly packed columns of dead keratinocytes. Hair is found in the most regions of our human body.  

Nails, on the other side, are also accessory organs of the skin. Nails are made of hardened keratinocytes’ sheets and they are found on ends of our toes and fingers. Our fingernails and toenails are reinforcing and also guarding ends of digits as they are also used to manipulate small objects and to scrap things. Our hair and our nails extend from our skin to actually reinforce skin and shield it from the environmental damages.

Next part of integumentary system is the sweat glands. These are sudoriferous glands. Sweat glands are the exocrine glands that are found in the dermis of human body’s skin while the sebaceous glands are the ones that are found in dermis of our skin that can produce sebum.

The other exocrine glands are the ceruminous glands that are only found in dermis of our ear canals. They produce the cerument, it is the waxy secretion inside our ear to protect its canals as it lubricates our eardrums.

The exocrine glands are known as the integumentary system’s part that produces oil, sweat, and wax on our externals to moisturize, cool, and look after the surface of our skin.
