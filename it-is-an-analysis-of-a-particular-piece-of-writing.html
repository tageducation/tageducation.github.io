He suggests that essays can be studied most effectively within a three-poled frame of reference. First is the personal and the autobiographical (i.e., the essayists write fragments of reflective autobiography and look at the world through anecdotes and descriptions). Second is the objective, the factual, and the concrete particular (i.e., the essayists do not speak directly of themselves, but turn their attention outward to some literary or scientific or political theme. Essayists pass judgment and draw general conclusions from the presentation relevant data. Third, is the abstract-universal (i.e., the essayists do their work in the world of high abstractions).
