28. The purpose of a position paper is to get potential funders. Select one: True False


The correct answer is false.

Position papers are not written to persuade people or organizations to provide funding for your stance or project.

A position paper is written to convey the side or stance that the writer, or the organization he represents, takes for in an issue or debate. It does not just contain the reasons why you are for or against a certain subject matter but it also must detail the ways on how you plan to arrive at a solution.

No matter how short or lengthy a position paper is, one must abide by logic reasoning and the rules of argument. While you may be passionate about your beliefs, you must be able to provide facts such as historical data or statistics to back you up. It does not help to simply rely on emotional and fiery arguments.
