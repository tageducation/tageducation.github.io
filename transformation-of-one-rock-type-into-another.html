10. 5. Igneous and metamorphic rock can be buried and undergo tremendousheating and stress. What is the process of transformation of one rock typeinto another?A. Compaction B. Lithification C. Metamorphism D. weathering​


Answer:

Metamorphism

Metamorphism is the process of transforming from one rock type to another, hence, the answer is letter C. Rocks that undergo this process are referred to as metamorphic rocks. Igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic rocks can undergo metamorphism if the right pressure and temperature conditions are met. The original rock is then referred to as the protolith.

Explanation:

Here are some of the examples of metamorphic rocks and their protolith:

Shale (sedimentary rock) becomes slate if there is enough heat and pressure. Slate (metamorphic rock) can further metamorphose into phyllite, schist, and gneiss. Sandstone (sedimentary rock) can metamorphose into quartzite. Diorite (igneous rock) can metamorphose into gneiss.

Compaction is different. It is the hardening of the sediments because of applied pressure. Lithification is the process of sediments becoming a rock after being compacted for a long time. Weathering is the breaking down of rocks due to physical or chemical means.

Metamorphism is the change of minerals or geologic texture (distinct arrangement of minerals) in pre-existing rocks (protoliths), without the protolith melting into liquid magma (a solid-state change).
