10. One of the basic tenets of nonviolent direct action is to make the oppressor face the inconsistency between the oppressors' ideals and the oppressors' actions. How did the Selma march use this principle?


The Selma to Montgomery Marches were organized by nonviolent activists to fight racial injustice. They used this inconsistency principle by showing the government the irony of protecting people but only the selected ones. They demonstrated the desire of African-American citizens to exercise their  constitutional rights to vote. Millions of African-Americans suffered as minorities because of the discriminatory policies of the government. The march was successful and historic as their rights were finally recognized.
