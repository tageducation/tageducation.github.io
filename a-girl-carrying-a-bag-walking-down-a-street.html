Further explanation:

Work in physics is the energy that is transferred to or from an item when a force is applied along a displacement. It is frequently described in its most basic form as the result of force and displacement. When a force is applied, it is said to have a component that moves the point of application, and this is referred to as positive work. If the component of a force at the point of application is in the opposite direction of the displacement, the force is said to do negative work.

A force is an effect that can alter an object's motion according to physics. An object with mass can change its velocity, or accelerate, as a result of a force. An obvious way to describe force is as a push or a pull. A force is a vector quantity, since it has both magnitude and direction.

A girl pulling her cart

A young woman was pushing her cart backward. The Earth is exerting a reaction force on the girl in the forward direction in accordance with Newton's third law of motion. The effect is that the cart advances.

Objects in contact with each other exert a force on one another known as contact force. Since a force must be given to the cart in order to pull it, So, this is an illustration of Contact force.

A man lifting a box to be placed on a table

When you exert force on an object, and it lifts off the ground, you have simply done positive work on that object because you have moved it, and the amount of work is equal to the weight of the moved object times the displacement.

Every time we lift weights, we apply force to the barbell. The formula f = ma, which states that force is equal to mass times acceleration, is used to calculate the force in physics. This formula is based on Newton's Second Law of Motion. Either increasing mass or accelerating must be done in order to produce additional force (or both).

A mango fruit failing from a branch

This is the classic one, it's the same with an apple falling in some guy's head. The downward force of gravity is the action that causes a mango to fall (F1). The power the apple puts on the earth has the opposite effect (F2). The term "curvilinear motion" refers to motion in which the body follows a curved route. Because it will fall vertically downhill in the direction of gravity that follows a straight line, a mango falling from a tree will serve as an illustration of straight-line motion.

24. a girl carrying a bag walking down on a street is there work done? explain


No, there is no work when a girl carrying a bag walks down a street.

Because there is no work done on carrying a bag walking horizontally since the bag wasn't displaced upwards. Work is done when a force is applied to an object and the object is moved through a distance.

In physics, work is a measure of the energy transfer that occurs when an object is moved a certain distance by an external force at least partially applied in the direction of displacement. Work is energy that is transferred to or from an object through the application of a force throughout the displacement.

The work done on an object is achieved not only by moving the object as a whole from one place to another, but also, for example, by compressing gases, by rotating shafts, and even by causing the invisible motion of particles within the body by external magnetic forces. .

For example, when a ball is held on the ground and then dropped, the work done by the force of gravity on the ball as it falls is equal to the ball's weight (force) times the distance to the ground (displacement).

When a force acts on an object causing the object to move, this is called work on the object. For a force to qualify for doing work on an object, there must be displacement and the force must cause displacement.

Work is closely related to energy. The work-energy principle states that the increase in the kinetic energy of a rigid body is due to an equal amount of positive work being done on the object by the resultant force acting on the object.

In contrast, the decrease in kinetic energy is caused by an equal amount of negative work done by the resulting force.

There are several examples of good work that can be observed in everyday life. For example, a horse pulls a plow in a field, a man pushes a grocery cart down the aisle of a grocery store, a freshman lifts a backpack full of books over his shoulders, or a weightlifter lifts a barbell over his head.

In each of the cases described here, a force is exerted on an object to cause it to be displaced. In general, for work to occur, a force must be exerted on an object that causes it to move.
