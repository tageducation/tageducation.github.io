Diversity helps to protect the decision-makers from taking an approach which is too linear, from extrapolating too readily from the past and making simplistic assumptions about the future. 29. why is genetic diversity important?​


Answer:

Genetic diversity is important because it helps maintain the health of a population, by including alleles that may be valuable in resisting diseases, pests and other stresses. Maintaining diversity gives the population a buffer against change, providing the flexibility to adapt.

Explanation:

Answer:

Genetic variation is an important force in evolution as it allows natural selection to increase or decrease frequency of alleles already in the population.
