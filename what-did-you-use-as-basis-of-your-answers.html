Common Good

The common good approach refers to actions or policies that are implemented to benefit not only a specific group of people, but society as a whole. It is regarded as the best decision that will result in positive and beneficial outcomes.

Why is it important to put the common good into action?

They enable not only collective decision-making but also the ability to put decisions into action once they have been made. They allow for the peaceful resolution of disagreements. They are intended to deter tyranny, whether by individuals or groups, and to provide a voice for all.
