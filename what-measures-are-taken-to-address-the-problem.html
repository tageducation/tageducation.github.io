5. Guide Questions:1. What adverse effect can acid rain pose on living organisms and its environment?2. Who should be responsible for cleaning up the pollution problem?3. What measures are taken to address the problem?​


Answer:

1) The ecological effects of acid rain are most clearly seen in aquatic environments, such as streams, lakes, and marshes where it can be harmful to fish and other wildlife. As it flows through the soil, acidic rain water can leach aluminum from soil clay particles and then flow into streams and lakes.

2)All humans must be responsible for cleaning up the pollution problem. Since we use the environment for our own needs and personal goals, it is just but fair that we take care of it and maintain its cleanliness.

3) There are 5 ways to address the problem:

• Reduce amount of sulphur dioxide and oxides of nitrogen released into the atmosphere.

• Use cleaner fuels.

• Flue Gas Desulphurisation (FGD).

• Use other sources of electricity (i.e. nuclear power, hydro-electricity, wind energy, geothermal energy, and solar energy)
