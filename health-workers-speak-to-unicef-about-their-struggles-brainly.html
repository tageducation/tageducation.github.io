3. Comparison of the poem if we must die to the health workers speak to UNICEF about their struggles


Answer:

The poem "If We Must Die" by Claude McKay and the health workers speaking to UNICEF about their struggles are two very different works and cannot be directly compared. "If We Must Die" is a poem written by McKay during the Harlem Renaissance that addresses the experience of African Americans in the United States at the time. It is a powerful and emotional call to action for African Americans to resist oppression and fight for their rights.

On the other hand, the health workers speaking to UNICEF about their struggles are real people discussing their experiences working on the frontlines of the COVID-19 pandemic. They are healthcare workers who are facing challenges and risks as they work to care for and protect the public during the pandemic.

While these two works are very different, they both address important issues and challenges facing their respective communities. "If We Must Die" speaks to the struggle of African Americans against oppression, while the health workers discuss the challenges they face as they work to protect the public during the pandemic. Both works highlight the resilience and determination of the people they represent.
