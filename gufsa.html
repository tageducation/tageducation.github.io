1.

Problem:

A jeepney from rest accelerates uniformly over a time of 3.25 second and covers a distance of 15m. Determined the acceleration of the jeepney.

Given Data:

d (distance) = 15 m

Vi (initial velocity) = 0 m/s (from rest)

t (time)  = 3.25 s

a (acceleration) = unknown

Solution:

We can use the formula  (d = Vit + 1/2 at² ) and  to find a (acceleration) we can use the formula    a = d / 1/2 (t²) since Vi = 0 m/s.

a = d / 1/2 (t²)

 = 15 m / 1/2 (3.25 s)²

 = 15 m / 1/2 (10.56 s²)

 = 15 m/  5.28 s²

 = 2.84 m/s²

15. During exercise,lactic acid (C3H6O3), forms in the muscle causing muscle cramps. If 5.0 of lactic acid (C3H6O3) concentrate in your leg muscles, how many moles of lactic acid (C3H6O3) are causing you pain? solve using gufsa​.


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of C₃H₆O₃.

molar mass = (12.0 g/mol × 3) + (1.008 g/mol × 6) + (16.0 g/mol × 3)

molar mass = 90.048 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of molecules of C₃H₆O₃.

[tex]\text{number of C₃H₆O₃ molecules = 5.0 g C₃H₆O₃} × \frac{\text{1 mol C₃H₆O₃}}{\text{90.048 g C₃H₆O₃}} × \frac{\text{6.022 × 10²³ C₃H₆O₃ molecules}}{\text{1 mol C₃H₆O₃}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{number of C₃H₆O₃ molecules = 3.3 × 10²² molecules}}[/tex]
