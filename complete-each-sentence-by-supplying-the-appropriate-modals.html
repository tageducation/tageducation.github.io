Modals

Modal verbs are very common and useful in English. We use them to talk about ability, permission, obligation, requests, offers, suggestions and more. To give suggestions and advice we can use the modal verbs ‘could’, ‘should’, ‘ought to’, ‘had better’. One of the most difficult grammar points for students to grasp. The pattern for modals in English is: Modal + Base Verb.

Learning Task 1:Answers:ShallCanwouldMayshould

Things to Remember:Modal verbs are always followed by a base form of a verb – an infinitive verb without to.Should’ is used when the speaker wants to make a strong suggestion.Could’ is not as strong as ‘should’ and is normally used to give mild advice or to suggest a solution to some problem.Never use an infinitive verb with a modal.
