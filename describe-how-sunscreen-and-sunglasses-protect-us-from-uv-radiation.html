29. describe how sunscreen and sunglass protect us from UV radiation​


How do sunscreen and sunglasses protect us from UV radiation?

Sunscreen protects our skin, and it works by blocking and absorbing UV radiation to our skin that is considered an important part. It is through a combination of having particles like chemical and physical. Some physical particles that help reflect UV radiations are zinc oxide and titanium dioxide.

On the other hand, sunglasses protect us from UV radiation by preventing its reflection. It prevents us from penetrating our eyes, saving our lives from vision loss or lifetime damage. Having a good pair of sunglasses will help protect our eyes that reduce UV rays by the anti-reflective coating on the lens that cuts down the reflection of UV radiation and light.

What is the meaning of UV radiation?

It is a form of electromagnetic radiation which comes from the sun and even artificial sources. And it is invisible. The different types of it depend on how much energy they have.

Three types of UV radiation: UVA rays- it is considered to have the least amount of energy. And it can cause our skill cells to age which can result in wrinkles. But it this ray is thought to have a huge role in skin cancers.

UVB rays- it has more energy than the first one. It can damage our skin cells directly, which causes sunburns. And this ray is thought of having the cause of most skin cancer.

UBC rays- it has more energy than the rest. It is not a risk factor for skin cancer since it doesn’t reach the ground. But it can be present from artificial sources like welding torches, UV sanitizing lamps and light that kills bacteria, and even mercury lamps.

Some health problems caused by UV radiation: Sunburn Premature aging of our skin Eye problems Weakens immune system
