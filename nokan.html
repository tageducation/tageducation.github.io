Dizi

The dizi, is a Chinese transverse flute. It is also sometimes known as the di or héngdi, and has varieties including Qudi, Bangdi, and Xindi. It is a major Chinese musical instrument that is widely used in many genres of Chinese folk music, Chinese opera, as well as the modern Chinese orchestra.

The dizi is a side-blown (transverse) edge aerophone (flute) of the Han Chinese. In various forms it is used today in many regional and national forms of Chinese music for solo, small ensemble, and orchestra performance.

The body of the flute has six finger-holes at measured distances. It also features an extra hole between the blow-hole and finger-holes which is covered by a thin membrane of reed and gives the dizi a bright, resonant, and slightly humming tone.
