13. The first four terms of an arithmetic sequence are 16, 28, 40, 52... Calculate the sum of the first 69 terms.


✒️SERIES

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM}:} [/tex]

The first four terms of an arithmetic sequence are 16, 28, 40, 52... Calculate the sum of the first 69 terms.

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \LARGE \: \rm{S_{69} = 29,\!256} [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

» Each term will add 12 to get its next term. Thus, the common difference is 12. Find the sum of the first 69 terms using the arithmetic series formula.

[tex] \begin{align} & \bold{Formula:} \\ &\: \boxed{\rm S_n = \frac{\,n\,}{2} \big[ 2a_1 + d(n-1) \big]} \end{align} [/tex]

[tex] S_{69} = \frac{69}{2} \big[ 2(16) + 12(69-1) \big] \\ [/tex]

[tex] S_{69} = \frac{69}{2} \big[ 2(16) + 12(68) \big] \\ [/tex]

[tex] S_{69} = \frac{69}{2} \big[ 32 + 816 \big] \\ [/tex]

[tex] S_{69} = \frac{69}{2} \big[ 848 \big] \\ [/tex]

[tex] S_{69} = 69 \big[ 424 \big] \\ [/tex]

[tex] S_{69} = 29,\!256 \\ [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] The sum of the first 69 (noice) terms of the sequence is 29,256
