18. What I Have LearnedDirections: Differentiate gender stereotyping to culture stereotypingGender stereotypingCulture stereotyping​


Answer:

Gender related Development Gender stereotypes are the beliefs that people have about the characteristics of males and females. The content of stereotypes varies over cultures and over time. These expectations are often related to the roles that the sexes fulfill in the culture.

A gender stereotype is, at its core, a belief and that belief may cause its holder to make assumptions about members of the subject group, women and or men. In contrast, gender stereotyping is the practice of applying that stereotypical belief to a person.

Gender is a cultural construct and perceptions of men and women differ from culture to culture or even during different periods even in a single culture or society.

Explanation:

Cultural Stereotypes. Generalizations become stereotypes when all members of a group are categorized as having the same characteristics. Stereotypes can be linked to any type of cultural membership, such as nationality, religion, gender, race, or age. Also, stereotypes may be positive or negative.

Gender stereotyping is defined as an overgeneralization of characteristics, differences and attributes of a certain group based on their gender. Gender stereotypes create widely accepted biases about certain characteristics or traits and perpetuate the notion that each gender and associated behaviors are binary.

27. Write 5 examples of each common stereotypes Age stereotype Racial StereotypesGender StereotypesSocial Class Stereotypes​


Answer:

Age stereotypes:

1. Older people are forgetful and unable to learn new things.

2. Teenagers are rebellious and irresponsible.

3. Children are naive and innocent.

4. Middle-aged people are boring and unadventurous.

5. Young people are entitled and self-absorbed.

Racial stereotypes:

1. African Americans are lazy and dishonest.

2. Asian Americans are good at math and science.

3. Latinx people are passionate and fiery.

4. Native Americans are lazy and dependent on government assistance.

5. White people are privileged and entitled.

Gender stereotypes:

1. Men are not good at household tasks.

2. Women are emotional and irrational.

3. Girls are passive and delicate.

4. Women are not good at math and science.

5. Men are strong and capable, while women are weak and vulnerable.

1. Poor people are lazy and do not want to work.

2. Rich people are greedy and selfish.

3. Upper class people are snobbish and pretentious.

4. Working class people are uneducated and uncultured.

5. Middle class people are boring and conformist.

It is important to note that these stereotypes are harmful and not based in reality. Every individual is unique and cannot be accurately judged or categorized based on their age, race, gender, or social class.
