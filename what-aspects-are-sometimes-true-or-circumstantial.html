8. what are the factors that circumstantial evidence may be used to prove conviction?​


Answer:

Circumstantial evidence is sufficient for conviction if: (1) There is more than one circumstance; (2) The facts from which the inferences are derived are proven; (3) The combination of all the circumstances is such as to produce a conviction beyond a reasonable doubt.

22. explain Circumstantial or moral evidence ?​


Answer:

Circumstantial evidence is evidence that relies on an inference to connect it to a conclusion of fact—such as a fingerprint at the scene of a crime. By contrast, direct evidence supports the truth of an assertion directly—i.e., without need for any additional evidence or inference.

Answer:

Circumstantial evidence is evidence that relies on an inference to connect it to a conclusion of fact—such as a fingerprint at the scene of a crime. By contrast, direct evidence supports the truth of an assertion directly—i.e., without need for any additional evidence or inference

MORAL EVIDENCE. That evidence which is not obtained either from intuition or demonstration. It consists of those convictions of the mind, which are produced by the use of the senses, the testimony of men, and analogy or induction. It is used in contradistinction to mathematical, evidence.
