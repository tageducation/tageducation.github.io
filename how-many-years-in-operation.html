11. Suppose that your business is operating at the 4.5 sigma quality level. if projects have an average improvement rate of 50 annually, how many years will it take to achieve six-sigma quality?


Needs 11.75 years or 11 years 9 months to achieve six-sigma quality.

Explanation :

If we ignore the fact that "Six-sigma" quality means the error rate corresponds to about -4.5σ (3.4 ppm) then we will use the reference ...

P(z ≤ -6) ≈ 9.86588×10^-10

and

P(z ≤ -4.5) ≈ 3.39767×10^-6

this error rate ratio is about 0.000290372.

In the next step we will multiply the error rate by 0.5 (representing each year), so as to calculate the result to the power of 0.50 which gives this value:

0.50^t = 0.000290372

t•log(0.50) = log(0.00290372) . . . . take logarithms

t = log(0.000290372)/log(0.50) = -3.537045/-0.301030

t = 11.75

That is, it takes about 11.75 years or 11 years and 9 months to reach Six Sigma quality (error rate 0.99 ppb).
