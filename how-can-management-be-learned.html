3. how can management be learned?Cite concrete situation or example.​


Management can be learned through exposures to people, experiences, and real life situations that need decision making for self and/or a team or group.  It is broad in itself which requires a wide array of learning and experiencing.  In this manner, your skills will be developed, improved, and evaluated over time.

For example, management in a classroom setting would require someone, who may be the class president, to be empowered, equipped, and skilled in handling people, that is his or her classmates.  Classroom management will need a president who knows how to discipline, direct and lead his or her classmates since he or she is the immediate authority other than the class adviser.  In this regard, he or she should have the ability to earn and practice his or her authority.
