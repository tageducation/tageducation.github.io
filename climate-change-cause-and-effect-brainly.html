Natural causes of climate change:

The earth's climate is influenced and changed through natural causes like volcanic eruptions, ocean currents, the Earth's orbital changes, solar variations and internal variability. is short-term cooling. Volcanic eruptions pump out clouds of dust and ash, which block out some sunlight.

Man-made Causes of climate change:

Humans are increasingly influencing the climate and the earth's temperature by burning fossil fuels, cutting down forests and farming livestock. This adds enormous amounts of greenhouse gases to those naturally occurring in the atmosphere, increasing the greenhouse effect and global warming.

Effects on plants of climate change:

Climate change has many consequences for plants, be it heat waves, increased flooding, or droughts. Besides these knock‐on effects of global warming, rising carbon dioxide concentrations and temperatures directly affect plant growth, reproduction, and resilience.

Effects on Animals of climate change:

Humans and wild animals face new challenges for survival because of climate change. More frequent and intense drought, storms, heat waves, rising sea levels, melting glaciers and warming oceans can directly harm animals, destroy the places they live, and wreak havoc on people's livelihoods and communities.

2. Cause of climate change Effect of climate change and Recommendation​


Answer:

The cause of climate change is primarily due to human activities that release greenhouse gases into the atmosphere. These gases, including carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide, trap heat from the sun and warm the planet, causing global temperatures to rise.

The effects of climate change are far-reaching and can be seen in many areas of the world, including rising sea levels, more frequent and severe weather events, shifts in migration patterns of plants and animals, and more. These changes can result in significant economic, social, and environmental impacts, including increased food and water insecurity, displacement of communities, and damage to critical infrastructure.

To address the issue of climate change, there are several recommendations that can be made. Firstly, reducing greenhouse gas emissions through reducing energy consumption and transitioning to clean energy sources such as wind, solar, and hydropower. Secondly, increasing investment in research and development of new technologies that can help mitigate and adapt to the effects of climate change, including better water management systems, more resilient infrastructure, and new agricultural practices.

In addition, individuals and governments must work together to create policies that promote sustainability and reduce carbon footprint, such as energy-efficient buildings, green transportation options, and reforestation initiatives. Lastly, raising awareness about the importance of addressing climate change and engaging the public in efforts to reduce their carbon footprint can help bring about systemic change and ensure a sustainable future.

Natural phenomena, like volcanic eruptions and changes in solar radiation.

Effects of climate change:

Rising global temperatures leading to more frequent heatwaves, droughts, and natural disasters.

Melting of glaciers, causing sea level rise and increased risk of coastal flooding.

Changes in precipitation patterns, resulting in water scarcity in some regions and increased flooding in others.

Decline in biodiversity and loss of ecosystems, threatening the survival of many species.

Adverse impacts on agriculture and food security.

Recommendations to mitigate climate change:

Reduce greenhouse gas emissions by transitioning to renewable energy sources, such as wind and solar power, and increasing energy efficiency.

Implementing carbon pricing and regulation to incentivize low-carbon practices.

Promoting reforestation and sustainable land use practices.

Encouraging sustainable transportation, such as public transit, cycling, and electric vehicles.

Raising awareness and promoting individual action, such as reducing waste, using less energy, and supporting climate-friendly policies.
