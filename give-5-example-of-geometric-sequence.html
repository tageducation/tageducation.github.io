Term in Arithmetic and Geometric Sequences

What is the difference between Arithmetic and Geometrical Sequences?

The difference lies in the operation, that is, if the Arithmetic sequence uses addition and subtraction operations, while the Geometry sequence uses multiplication and division operations.

The nth term in the Arithmetic sequence is formulated as:

[tex]a_{n} =a+(n-1)b[/tex]

where a = first term, and b = difference/difference between terms. And the value of b is obtained by [tex]a_{2} -a_{1}[/tex] or  [tex]a_{3} -a_{2}[/tex] or [tex]a_{11} -a_{10}[/tex].

Meanwhile, to determine the nth term in the geometric sequence, it is formulated:

[tex]a_{n} =a.r^{n-1}[/tex]

Where a=first term, and r is the ratio obtained by dividing [tex]\frac{a_{2} }{a_{1} }[/tex] or [tex]\frac{a_{3} }{a_{2} }[/tex] or  [tex]\frac{a_{11} }{a_{10} }[/tex] and so on.
