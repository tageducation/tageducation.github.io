4. 3. As you go away from the focus, what happens to the intensity of the earthquake? a. Both A and B b. It decreases. c. It increases. d. It does not change.


Answer:

B. It decreases

Explanation:

Since intensity decreases with distance from the earthquake, a large deep earthquake, which solely because of its depth is far from Earth's surface, produces small shaking intensity. Thus we cannot use intensity to accurately compare the relative size of shallow and deep earthquakes.

Earthquake Intensity

Earthquake intensity is the amount of damage caused by an earthquake in a certain location and the effect it has on people and infrastructure. The intensity is determined based on the strength of the earthquake, the distance between the earthquake and the epicenter and local geological conditions.

The intensity of the earthquake is the level of damage felt at the location where the earthquake occurred. To what extent is the level of damage to an earthquake area, that is the intensity of the earthquake.

The number of intensity is determined by assessing the damage it produces. The effect on objects, buildings, land, and consequently on people. This intensity scale is also called MMI (Modified Mercalli Intensity) introduced by Giuseppe Mercalli in 1902.

Magnitude is an earthquake parameter that is measured based on what occurred in a certain area, due to earthquake shocks at the source. The unit used is the Richter Scale. This scale was introduced by Charles F. Richter in 1934 (Badan Geologi, ESDM).
