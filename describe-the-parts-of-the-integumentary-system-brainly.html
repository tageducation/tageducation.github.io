Parts of the integumentary system

1.The SKIN is the largest organ of the body, with a total area of about 20 square feet. The skin protects us from microbes and the elements, helps regulate body temperature, and permits the sensations of touch, heat, and cold.

2.HAIR is a protein filament that grows from follicles found in the dermis. Hair is one of the defining characteristics of mammals

3.A NAIL is a claw-like keratinous plate at the tip of the fingers and toes in most primates. Nails correspond to claws found in other animals

4.A GLAND is an organ which produces and releases substances that perform a specific function in the body. There are two types of gland. Endocrine GLANDS  are ductless glands and release the substances that they make (hormones) directly into the bloodstream.

5.The NERVOUS SYSTEM has two main parts: The CENTRAL NERVOUS SYSTEM is made up of the brain and spinal cord. The PERIPHERAL NERVOUS SYSTEM is made up of nerves that branch off from the spinal cord and extend to all parts of the body.

The integumentary system consists of the skin, hair, nails, glands, and nerves. Its main function is to act as a barrier to protect the body from the outside world. It also functions to retain body fluids, protect against disease, eliminate waste products, and regulate body temperature.

The parts of the integumentary system are:

Skin- The skin is a vital organ that covers the entire outside of the body, forming a protective barrier against pathogens and injuries from the environment. It is the body's largest organ. Moreover, it consists of three layers which are the epidermis, dermis, and the subcutaneous fat.Hair- is a protein filament that grows from follicles found in the dermis. It protects, regulates body temperature, and facilitates in evaporation in perspiration. It also acts a sense organ by transmitting the disturbances the hair root plexus senses to the central nervous system, which can then respond by activating the skeletal muscles of your eyes.Nails- are claw-like keratinous plate at the tip of the fingers and toes in most primates. They protect the sensitive tips of fingers and toes, support the tips of our fingers and toes, and help in picking up small objects.Secretory Glands- are small oil-producing glands present in the skin of mammals. They are usually attached to hair follicles and release a fatty substance, sebum, into the follicular duct and thence to the surface of the skin.Subcutaneous tissue- is the deepest layer of your skin. It's made up mostly of fat cells and connective tissue. It acts as a layer of insulation to protect your internal organs and muscles from shock and changes in temperature.

☆ hair folliclesHair follicles hold the root of the hair fibers.

☆ sweat glandsSweat glands help regulate body temperature and water content by sccreting sweat.

☆ sensory nerves (ending for touch)The sensory nerves in the integumentary system receive impulses that enable the body to recognize sensory stimuli.

☆ epidermisThe epidermis is a protective covering for all of the internal and external surfaces of the body.

☆ dermisThe dermis contains connective tissue, blood, and lymph vessels, and nerve fibers (also contains hair follicles, and the sebaceous and sweat glands.

The integumentary system is made up of several organs and structures including the skin, hair, nails, glands, and nerves. The primary function of the integumentary system is to protect the inside of the body from elements in the environment—like bacteria, pollution, and UV rays from the sun.
