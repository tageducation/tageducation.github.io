7. 2. There are the same remainder 3 when 135, 111, and 87 iscertain number. Find the largest possible value of this number.A. 11b. 12C. 13d. 15​


b. 12

The largest possible value is 12.

Step-by-step explanation:

Finding the Greatest Common Factor

The question above is asking for the largest possible value of the number which always has a remainder 3 when divided by the numbers 135, 111 and 87. There are two steps to find the answer.

Subtract 3 from each number since it always has a remainder of 3.Find their greatest common factor by prime factorization method.

Solution:

Step 1: Subtract 3

135 - 3 = 132

111 - 3 = 108

87 - 3 = 84

Now, the new numbers are 132, 108 and 84. Find their greatest common factor.

Step 2: Prime Factorization

In prime factorization, you have to break down the number until you end up with its prime factors.

132 = 3 × 2 × 2 × 11

108 = 3 × 2 × 2 × 3 × 3

84 = 3 × 2 × 2 × 7

3 × 2 × 2

G C F = 3 × 2 × 2 = 12

(I attached the solution for the prime factorization method.)

Final Answer:

12

Checking:

111 ÷ 12 = 9 r 3 ✔

87 ÷ 12 = 7 r 3 ✔
