23. What was the first bank in the Philippines? source


The first bank in the Philippines was the Bank of the Philippine Islands.

Explanation:

Founded in 1851, Bank of the Philippine Islands is the first bank in the Philippines and in the Southeast Asian region. BPI is a versatile bank that, together with its subsidiaries and branches, offers a wide range of financial products and solutions catering to both individuals and businesses. BPI's services include retail and lending banking, wealth management, payments, insurance, securities brokerage and distribution, foreign exchange, leasing, corporate and commercial' investment banking. .

The bank has a network of over 800 branches in the Philippines, Hong Kong, and Europe, and is close to 3,000 ATMs and CAMs (cash accepting machines). The establishment of BPI, originally known as El Banco Español Filipino de Isabel II, opened up the Philippine banking and financial industry. The bank performed many functions at the time, from granting credit to the national treasury to printing and issuing currency, effectively making it the nation's leading central bank. Today, BPI is proud to continue this tradition, funding a variety of private and public sector initiatives and businesses to support economic growth and nation building.

The royal decree establishing Banco Espaol-Filipino also gave him the power to print Philippine currency, the first time Philippine pesos were minted in the country; prior to 1851, multiple currencies were used, most notably the Mexican peso. They were originally called Philippine pesos fuerte (PF), or "strong pesos". First minted on May 1, 1852, they are convertible to face value Mexican gold or silver coins. The first deposit to the bank was also made that day by a man named Fulgencio Barrera. Three days later, a Chinese man named Tadian became the bank's first borrowing customer after the bank discounted a ten thousand peso fuertes note for him.

On September 3, 1869, after the revolution that overthrew Isabella II, the name was changed to Banco Español-Filipino. In January 1892, the bank moved from the Royal Custom House in Intramuros to the new business area Binondo after learning that Intramuros was becoming "economically inactive". It moved to the corner of 4 Plaza Cervantes Juan Luna Street which was at that time the main property owned by Dominican friars.
