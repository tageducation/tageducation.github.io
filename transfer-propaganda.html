12. give 5 transfer of propaganda technique.​


Here are 5 transfer propaganda techniques:

Glittering Generalities: This technique involves using vague, broad statements that have positive connotations in order to create a positive association with a particular idea or product. For example, using words such as "freedom," "equality," and "justice" to promote a particular political ideology.Testimonial: This technique involves using endorsements from famous or respected individuals to promote a product or idea. For example, a celebrity endorsing a product in a commercial.Fear-mongering: This technique involves using fear to persuade people to support a particular idea or agenda. For example, using fear tactics to sway public opinion on a political issue.Bandwagon: This technique involves creating the impression that everyone is doing or supporting something, and therefore, the individual should also join in. For example, using slogans such as "everyone is doing it" to promote a particular product or idea.Name-calling: This technique involves using derogatory or negative names or labels to discredit an opposing viewpoint or person. For example, labeling an opposing political candidate as "unpatriotic" or "dangerous."

28. Give 3 examples of transfer Propaganda​


Answer:

Transfer propaganda is a type of propaganda that seeks to associate a particular idea, person, or group with a symbol, such as a flag or a logo. The goal of transfer propaganda is to create an emotional connection between the symbol and the idea, person, or group it represents. Here are three examples of transfer propaganda:

1. The Nazi Swastika: The swastika was originally an ancient symbol of good luck and prosperity. However, during World War II, the Nazis adopted the swastika as their official emblem. The swastika became synonymous with Nazi Germany and was used to promote Nazi propaganda. By associating their ideas and values with the swastika, the Nazis were able to create a powerful emotional connection with their followers.

2. The American Flag: The American flag is a powerful symbol of the United States and its values. It is often used in propaganda to promote patriotism and support for the government. For example, during times of war, the American flag is used to rally support for the military and the war effort.

3. Coca-Cola: Coca-Cola is one of the most recognizable brands in the world. Its logo and advertising campaigns have been used to promote American values, such as freedom and happiness. By associating its brand with these values, Coca-Cola has been able to create a strong emotional connection with its customers.
