Answer:

Fast-food chains and fine dining restaurants are similar in two grounds. First, they sell the food they have expertise in. Second, they need to establish a good reputation to remain and compete in the food industry.

Explanation:

As to the first ground, business owners have to sell the food they know they are good at in order to stand out. They cannot just invest or start a business without doing research on the food they want to offer to the public; otherwise, their business will not prosper.

As to the second ground, both fast-food and fine dining restaurants have to make a good impression on the customers. For example, Jollibee is known for their chicken joy while Mang Inasal is known for their chicken inasal. Both fast-food chains are known in how they cooked their chicken which has allowed them to remain in the competition. The same goes for fine dining restaurants. They have to come up with an outstanding menu, and at the same time offer an excellent fine-dining experience to customers.
