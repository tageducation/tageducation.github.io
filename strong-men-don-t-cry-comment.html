25. #wallexplain this statement"Strong men don't cry"​need ko lang cno pwdy mksagot comment kyo


The statement "Strong men don't cry" comes because the stigma that exists in society makes boys reluctant to cry.

Some people, especially men, find it uncomfortable to cry. They may fear being judged by others or think that crying makes them seem weak or vulnerable.  It could be that the man adopts another form of emotional release besides crying because he is not used to it.

In addition to stigma in society, parenting can also make boys hide their emotional feelings or sadness in front of others. Young children are often scolded when crying can make them not used to it. Or, they think that it's wrong for men to cry.

When someone in the family feels secure attachment and openness, it becomes one of the factors that help men or women to open up to their emotions, including crying.

Based on research, the most common reason adults, both women and men, cry is as a form of stress or an outlet for anger. Some of them said crying made them feel better, although it didn't solve the problem. Others also said they felt relieved when they released the emotions they were holding back by crying.

However, based on research by Professor Ad Vingerhoets from the Netherlands, women do cry much more often than men. Women are known to cry between 30-64 times a year, while men only cry 6-17 times a year. This means that women cry more often and easily than men.

There are sociological and physiological reasons why men cry less than women. According to him, men have much lower levels of prolactin, the hormone associated with emotional tears, than women. Allegedly, this is what causes men to not cry easily.
