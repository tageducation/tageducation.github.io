RHYTHMIC ACTIVITIES

1. Fundamental Rhythms. Fundamental rhythms include walking, running, skipping, and other fundamental movements

2. Creative Rhythms. Creative rhythms involve movement that initiates animals or man. Some animals that move in groups create specific rhythms and sounds as they move.

3. Dramatic Rhythms. This could be based on a story and idea, a poem and emotion or a song

4. Rhythmic Movements. Involves timely coordination, frequently observed through musical units. Explore the elements of rhythm and the types of skills that contribute to various rhythmic movement activities.

5. Rhythmic activities improve physical and mental health. Aside from increased physical confidence, rhythmic activities can also improve mental functions because rhythm regulates the brain and prevents cognitive decline.
