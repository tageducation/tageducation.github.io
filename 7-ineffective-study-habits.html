7. what the 7 effective and ineffective study habits​


Answers and Explanations:

Effective

1. Plan your study

- By planning your study time and progress, you can estimate how much time is needed for you to understand the lessons. it also helps you manage your time.

2. Test yourself

- Testing yourself helps better retain information.

3. Set specific goals for your study times.

- The same as study plans, it helps you grasp the time needed for your study and focus on a specific topic.

4. Start studying when planned.

- Procrastinating ruins your plan of study and schedule  

5. Use pictures or drawings

- You can absorb information better if you have visual references.  

6. Review your notes

- By rerea ding notes you are refreshed of your previous lessons.

7. Set a limited time for studying

- It is good to limit your study time to prevent exhaustion of your brain.  

Ineffective

1. Procrastination

- It does not help you absorb lessons as you are rushing to complete deadlines.

2. Studying too long

- Your brain also gets tired. If you study without break, yo umay not be able to absorb all the necessary information .

3. Playing with gadgets while studying

- it distracts you from your real purpose  

4. Watching TV while studying

- it also distracts you

5. Copying everything

- when notetaking, only copy important info to avoid overwhelming yourself with useless infomation

6. Not asking the teacher for help

- They may give you more references or better explanations that can help you

7. Just memorizing

- memorizing but not understanding a topic will only lead to a short-term memory of that topic. understanding helps you better grasp it and remember it more.
