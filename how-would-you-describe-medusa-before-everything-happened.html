17. Describe the lines, colors, shapes and the texture of the raft of the Medusa.​


The Raft of the Medusa – originally titled Scène de Naufrage – is an oil painting of 1818–19 by the French Romantic painter and lithographer Théodore Géricault. Completed when the artist was 27, the work has become an icon of French Romanticism.

The Raft of the Medusa use of line

Use of lines:

Directional lines (horizontal, vertical, and diagonal): there are strong diagonal lines in the composition. The lines of the raft form a base as an implied diamond shape on which the drama unfolds. This is countered by the mast and rigging.

Géricault utilized a somber, dramatic color palette that was characteristic of Romantic painters. As the focus of the painting is the mass of corpses, flesh tones are present in abundance. Warm shades such as ochre, burnt sienna, umber and deep brown were used in contrast to the deep blue of the stormy sea.

The action is arranged in two distinct pyramidal shapes. The diagonal lines lead the eye to two key peaks: the wave that may or may not engulf the survivors on the raft, and the flag in the top right corner that is raised in a last gesture of hope to the ship that may or may not rescue them.

The Myth Of Medusa

Medusa, before everything happened, or as we can say before she was cursed by Athena, is an ineffable woman and Athena’s priestess. She is a hopeless and fragile creature that cannot refuse god’s will (Poseidon).

Let’s dig more into the story of Medusa before and after she was cursed!

Medusa is Athena’s priestess. Athena is the goddess of war and wisdom, moreover, she is the only virgin goddess. To become Athena’s priestess, Medusa had to promise to remain a virgin while in service. It means Medusa cannot have intercourse with anyone but someone she was married to. As Athena’s priestess, Medusa had a duty to gather water for service in the temple, unfortunately, came Poseidon and saw her beauty.

After had seen Medusa’s beauty, he wanted her and took her. Medusa ran to the temple of Athena seeking protection. Unluckily, she was raped by Poseidon on the floor of Athena’s temple. Out of a sudden, Athena came and saw that her sacred temple was defiled. She took the blame on Medusa and left Poseidon as innocent.

Medusa’s sister came to Athena and tried to defend her sister, but Medusa’s sister was punished by Athena. Athena cursed Medusa with a familiar look that we knew now, a head full of snakes and eternal life. Because of her curse, Medusa was exiled by the world of man and she could not near any man at all.
