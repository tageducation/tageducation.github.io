11. 1. What I know about research question?​


Research questions are questions that must be answered by researchers in a research project, where the answers to research questions will help solve the research problem.

To create a research question, the researcher must first determine what type of research will be carried out, whether it is qualitative, mixed,or quantitative research. the factors that can influence the research question are the timing of the research, how the research will be carried out, methodological approach,and research funding.

 

Problems in making research question

The problem that often occurs when making research questions is that the questions asked are less specific, unclear, and often not critically worthwhile as research questions.

Although the topic or research issue raised is very interesting, if the questions are not good and are too trivial, the research will lose meaning for others.

If this happens, the benefits or impacts that will be given from the research will be reduced in value. Keep in mind that the most basic thing about research is how research can have an impact on other people. In this case, research means it must be interesting so that other people will be interested in reading it.

Requirements for research questions

In essence, research questions can be formulated by knowing the contradictions that occur between them.

-What is needed and what is provided

-What should happen (prescriptive) and what actually happened (descriptive)

-What is desired and what is obtained

The first thing that is often present in research statements is the existence of a problem arising from a particular event or indication. However, all existing problems do not have to be used as research problems.

There are conditions that can be considered research problems. Based on a review of previous research such as books, journals, etc.

There are conditions for the applicable research problem, namely:

-There is information/data to solve the problem.

-The data and information collection used scientific methods, namely observation, investigation, survey, interview, questionnaire, documentation, testing, and participation.

-The conditions for authenticity can be met. There are previous research studies.

-Science can receive theoretical support from the problem under study.

-Is a problem or topic that is being discussed

-Problems or problems that exist require immediate handling solutions. Many people need it.

-Researchers must be aware of the ability to pose problems. Do not let the researchers overdo it.
