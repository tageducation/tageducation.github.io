2. Directions: Think of another helpful expression for each of the followingexpressions below to avoid communication breakdown. Put your answer in eachThe gaining EspressionGive Positive Remark or CommentAsk for a RepetitionCheck for Understanding​


Answer:

Use the following expressions to avoid communication breakdown

I. Time-gaining expressions: This type of expression (often referred to as a "filler") allows the speaker to think.

Example: “Well, let me see…” “Oh, well, you know, I don’t know, really" “I need some time to take this in—-can I get back to you?"

II. Give Positive Remark or Comment: A positive response to something indicates agreement, approval, or encouragement.  

Example: My goodness, how impressive Great job Very professional

III. Ask for a Repetition: You ask someone to repeat what they have said when you have not heard them or when you have not understood them.

Example: Excuse me? ( said like a question) Pardon me? ( rising intonation) What did you say (your name was)? Did you say . . . ? Can you repeat that? Could you say that again please? What was that again? Sorry, I didn't catch that.

IV. Check for understanding: It means that you want to check if you both understand the message that had been sent.

Example: I'm not sure I got your point.  I beg your pardon  but I don't quite understand.

To prevent a breakdown in communication, use the following phrases.

I. Time-gaining expressions:

The speaker can think while using this kind of expression, sometimes known as a "filler."

Example: "Well, let me see.""Oh, well, you know, I really don't know.""I'll need some time to process this—-can I get back to you?"

II. Make a Positive Remark or Comment:

A positive reaction to anything denotes support, encouragement, or agreement.

Example: Wow, that's really impressive.Superb workProfessional in every way.

III. Request a Repeat:

When you haven't heard someone or don't understand what they've said, you ask them to say it again.

Example:Excuse me? (said as a question)Pardon me? (in a rising tone)What did you say—what was (that again)?Did you mention..?Can you say that again?Could you repeat that please?What was that once more?I'm sorry, I missed it.

IV. Check for understanding:

This means that you want to make sure that both of you grasp the message that was conveyed.

Example:I'm not sure I understand your point.I beg your pardon.but I'm not sure what you mean.
