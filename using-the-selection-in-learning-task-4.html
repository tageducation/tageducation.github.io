Explanation:

What I notice while reading - - - there seems to be a lot more detail from the story that might give readers a slight feeling of disinterest especially in the first few paragraphs where the name James Dillingham Young was given a lot more emphasis than needed. The story has these dispondent characters from the very start that almost give off a little bit of an eerie feel considering it is a Christmas story. Bottomline though, the plot of the story saves the loneliness of the most parts and the lesson it gave to the readers about giving and receiving to and from your loved ones is spectacular.

What I wonder about while reading - - - - while reading, I wondered if the plot will save the dejected feel throughout the story and thankfully it did. The author gave the characters life and clearly depicts a pair of lovers in despair but with a love so strong. Its almost uncanny that the author gave that kind of ending but for me, the real lesson there was the characters' lack of communication that made them have the decision of giving gifts which might eventually not be of use but the thought of it though is way bigger and what readers should pay attention to.

How I connect to the text------ personally, I am a giver by nature and I relate to the characters of this text. Although one might mistakenly look at giving as means to receive something in return. For me.. In giving is where you receive. God pays attention one might not realize. In the text, there love for each other is so big that they display the meaning of the word "selfless".
