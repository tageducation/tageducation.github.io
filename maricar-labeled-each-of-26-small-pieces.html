If she will askher brother, Paulo, to randomly pick a piece of paper from the box, what is the probability that it is:1) a vowel or a consonant?2) a consonant or w?3) a vowel or e?4) a consonant oro?5) a vowel ork​


PROBABILITY

ANSWER:

1. The Probability that a vowel or a consonant will be picked is 1.

EXPLANATION

     To solve the problem we use the formula of union of two events which is, P(A U B)=P(A)+P(B)-P(A ∩ B). Let's assume that event A is a vowel and event B is a consonant.

The probability that a vowel will be picked is 5/26 because out of 26 letters in the alphabet, 5 of them are vowels which are A, E, I, O, and U.The probability that a consonant will be picked is 21/26 because out of the 26 letters in the alphabet, 21 of them are consonant which are (B,C,D,F,G,H,J,K,L,M,N,P,Q,R,S,T,V,W,X,Y).SOLUTION:

   EVENT A:{A,E,I,O,U}

  P(A):5/26

   EVENT B:{B,C,D,F,G,H,J,K,L,M,N,P,Q,R,S,T,V,W,X,Y}

   P(B):21/26

   But since there is no intersection between event a and b. we will use this formula P(A U B)=P(A)+P(B).

         [tex]\frac{5}{26} +\frac{21}{26}=\frac{26}{26}[/tex] or 1

Step-by-step explanation:

To calculate the Economic Order Quantity (EOQ) for the Maricar Company, we need to know the demand for the product (shirts), the holding cost per unit, and the fixed cost to place an order.

Based on the information provided, the demand for shirts is 1,000 units per year, the holding cost is P5 per unit per year, and the fixed cost to place an order is P2. We can use this information to calculate the EOQ using the following formula:

EOQ = √(2DS/H)

Where D is the demand for the product in units per year, S is the ordering cost per unit, and H is the holding cost per unit.

Since the fixed cost to place an order is a one-time cost and does not depend on the quantity of shirts ordered, it is not included in the EOQ formula. Instead, it is typically added to the total cost of the order to determine the total cost of acquiring the inventory.

Plugging in the values provided, we get:

EOQ = √(21,0002/5)

= √(2,000/5)

= √(400)

= 20

Therefore, the EOQ for the Maricar Company is 20 shirts. This means that the optimal order quantity for the company is 20 shirts, which will minimize the total cost of ordering and holding inventory.

It is important to note that the EOQ is based on several assumptions, including that demand is constant over time, that lead times are constant.
