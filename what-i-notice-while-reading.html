26. What I NOTICE while reading The Gift of The Magi


MY NOTICE ABOUT THE GIFT OF THE MAGI

The short story "The Gift of the Magi" by O., The story of Henry centers on a couple's effort to find important holiday gifts for one another. Due to their limited funds, the couple sacrifices a prized possession to buy a gift for one another.

The message of "The Gift of the Magi" is that love is more important than physical attractiveness. In this short novella, each character surrenders his or her most prized possession. Jim gives up his watch, which elevates his status in the eyes of others; Della gives up her hair, which causes other women to covet her.

They are compared to the Magi because the Magi were the wise men who gave gifts to the infant Jesus. They are compared to these wise men not because their actions were wise but because they had offered the most precious gift of all: love.

In the story's climax, it is revealed that Jim has sold his pocket watch to purchase hair combs for Della, while Della has sold her long hair to purchase a watch chain for Jim, so illustrating the situational irony. It is paradoxical that each individual's sacrifice has rendered the sacrifice of the other meaningless.
