9 Types of Software Platform

1.Technology Platforms: Technology Platforms include Amazon Web Services, Microsoft Azure, and Twilio. Technology platforms provide building blocks or services that can be reused in a wide variety of products. Third-party developers embed these building blocks and services in their products using permission-less innovation, increasing platform adoption.

2.Computing Platforms: In contrast to Technology Platforms, Computing Platforms enable interactions between platform users and third-party developers. The developer "owns" the one who is used in Technology Platforms. The platform "owns" the one who used in Computing Platforms.

3.Utility Platforms: Platforms entice users by offering a useful, usually free service. Once a critical mass of users has been reached, the platform becomes available to the second type of participant.

4.Interaction Networks: The fact that this type of platform facilitates interactions between specific participants (people and/or businesses) is a defining feature. A message, voice call, image, or money transfer are all examples of digital interactions.

5.Marketplaces: Marketplaces facilitate transactions between demand-side (buyers) and supply-side (sellers) (sellers). The supply-side participants set the prices for the goods and services offered on the platform. Not to mention, there is a high sensitivity to the variety of services/products offered on the platform — in general, the more variety offered on the platform, the better. Marketplaces have a network effect between buyers and sellers. Sellers entice buyers, who in turn entice more sellers, and so on.

6.On-demand Service Platforms

7.Content Crowdsourcing Platforms

8.Data Harvesting Platforms

9.Content Distribution Platforms
