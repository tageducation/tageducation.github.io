1. What must happen for a chemical reaction to take place


According to the Collision Theory, reactants must collide in order for a chemical reaction to take place. Take note that energy is needed to break the bonds between the atoms of the reactants. The collision between these molecules will provide the necessary kinetic energy needed to break the bonds between the reactants so that a new bond can be formed.

For instance, suppose you have molecules AB reacting with C to form CA and B, as shown below:

AB + C → CA + B

This reaction requires AB to collide with C on the A side of molecule AB, so that CA will be formed. In this case, the energy brought by the collision of AB and C will be enough to break the bond between A and B, which will allow the bonding of A and C.
