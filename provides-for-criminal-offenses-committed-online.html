To answer the question above, we have to know what is an offense or violation committed by the police officer and its example.

What is Police Misconduct?

Police misconduct is illegal or inappropriate action that is conducted by a police officer, including a violation of state law, federal law, or police department rules and regulations.

Examples of Police Misconduct

It is already a common thing in the news when we hear a report of police misconduct. Most often is the use of force, brutality, racist act, etc. This act can bring much harm to people and violation to our constitutional rights.  In fact, the range of actions of police misconduct is broad, including false reports, unlawfully destroying property, and using a seized property.

The Penalties for Police Misconduct

The penalties for police misconduct can be classified in many forms, such as disciplinary action, policy reforms, criminal charges, and community action.  The intention of these penalties is to decrease police misconduct and improve police services. The victim of police misconduct violated their person's civil right and can sue the officer and the department that employed the officer. The victim can seek compensation for their injuries.

The real case of police misconduct

A Chicago police commander, Jon Burge, was arrested for lying about his involvement in the torture and physical abuse of suspects in police custody back in 1980s. Burge was using a violation to obtain confessions.

Police misconduct is an act that is conducted by an officer harms a law.  An officer is able to receive some penalties from their office. Explanation:

In certain situations, private individuals have the power to make an arrest without a warrant. These types of arrests, known as citizens' arrests, occur when ordinary people either detain criminals themselves or direct police officers to detain a criminal.

While arrests by private citizens are subject to fewer constitutional requirements than an arrest by law enforcement officers, there are still rules that apply. Failure to abide by these rules can result in civil and criminal liability for the arresting individual.
