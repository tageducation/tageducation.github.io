20. find the least common denominator of 4/15 and 3/12 then rename as similar fractions show the solution​


LEAST COMMON MULTIPLE (LCM)

LCM of denominator 4/15 and 3/12

The prime factors of 15 and 12 in exponent form:

15: [tex]3^1 \times 5^1[/tex]12: [tex]2^2\times 3^1[/tex]

The product of only those factors between these that have the highest powers.

[tex]2^2 \times 3^1 \times 5^1[/tex] = 60

Therefore, LCM (15, 12) = 60

FURTHER EXPLANATION

What is Least Common Multiple?

In mathematics, the value that is equally divided by the two supplied numbers is known as the LCM of any two. Least Common Multiple is the phrase's whole form. Another name for it is the Least Common Divisor (LCD). As an illustration, LCM (3, 5) = 15. In this case, the LCM 15 may be divided by both 3 and 5, hence these two numbers are referred to as the divisors of 15.

The relationship between LCM and fraction

When the denominators of two fractions are different, LCM can also be used to add or subtract the fractions. LCM is used to make the denominators common when doing any arithmetic operations using fractions, such as addition and subtraction. The simplifying process is facilitated by this procedure.

How to find LCM?

There are three key approaches we can use to determine the LCM of two or more numbers. As follows:

Listing the MultiplesPrime Factorisation MethodDivision Method

What we discuss next is the Prime Factorization Method

LCM by Prime Factorization Method

The LCM of the supplied numbers can be determined by applying the prime factorization method. The following steps are used to determine the LCM of two numbers using the prime factorization method:

Step 1: Use the repeated division method to identify the prime factors of the supplied numbers.Step 2: Format the integers in exponentiation. Only those prime factors' products with the highest power should be chosen.Step 3: The LCM of the provided numbers is the product of these factors with the highest powers.

Example:

Using prime factorization, get the 30 and 60's least common multiple (LCM).

Solution:

The prime factors of 30 and 60 are as follows in step one

30: 2 × 15 60: 2 × 2 × 15

These prime factors can be written as follows in exponent form:

30: [tex]2^1 \times 15^1[/tex]60: [tex]2^2 \times 15^1[/tex]

Then, find the product of only those factors between these that have the highest powers. We get

[tex]2^2\times 15^1[/tex] = 2 × 15 = 30

Therefore, LCM (30, 50) = 30.
