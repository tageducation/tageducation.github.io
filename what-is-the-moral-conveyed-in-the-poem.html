1.Moral of the poem 'The Road Not Taken' is 'Take your own decisions without any regrets.'

"The Road Not Taken" is one of the most celebrated poems of Frost.

*The poem primarily celebrates the precipitous individualism and the difficulty which the poet faces in choosing a wise option when life offers him such opportunity.

*The moral lesson that Frost conveys through the poem is that whenever life gives us choices, we must make the decisions wisely.

*He also says that the decisions must be taken independently without fearing its consequences as it is what 'makes all the difference.'

*Frost suggests that one needs to take the challenges and the responsibility of these decisions and never regret it because it is the only way to have a contented and happy life
