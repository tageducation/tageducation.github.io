Examples of artifacts include stone tools, pottery vessels, metal objects such as weapons and items of personal adornment such as buttons, jewelry and clothing.  

Venus of Hohle Fels Country of Origin:  Germany

The Venus of Hohle Fels figurine is the oldest sculpture depicting the human figure. It is the oldest Upper Paleolithic sculpture of a woman and dates back to about 40,000 years ago.  

Lion-man of the Hohlenstein-Stade Country of Origin:  Germany

The Löwenmensch figurine is the oldest known piece of figurative art in the world. It is an ivory sculpture of a lion headed human that is between 40,000 years old

Skhul Cave Beads Country of Origin:   Israel

The shell beads from Skhul Cave in Israel are thought to be the oldest pieces of jewelry discovered that are created by humans.  

Olmec Colossal Heads Country of Origin: Central America

The Olmec Collosal Heads portray men with almond-shaped eyes, flat noses and plump lips. Each head wears a unique visage, expression and headdress, giving the notion that each carved boulders identify different particular leaders.

King Tut’s Funerary Mask Country of Origin: Egypt

King Tut’s Funerary Mask is a 24-pound facial replica capped the wrapped mummy of the Egyptian king, who died in 1323 B.C. at the age of 19.

Terracotta Army Country of Origin: China

The Terracotta Army are thousands of life-sized clay warriors which are lined in trenches in what seems to be a military formation.
