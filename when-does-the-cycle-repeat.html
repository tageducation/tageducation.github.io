1. when does the cycle repeat?


The refrigeration cycle repeats when the water vapor transforms to liquid and can be recycled again. Once it becomes liquid, it can now be subjected to evaporation and then compression which continues the cycle. In a single-stage vapor compression refrigeration cycle, Freon enters the compressor. Freon or any liquid refrigerant is present in any refrigerator unit.  

What happens inside the refrigerator?

The cooling agent or refrigerant enters the compressor and undergoes pressure. When there is pressure, the liquid heats up and it produces heated water vapor. The water vapor then enters the condenser and is cooled to become liquid. The heat is dissipated to the environment. This is why when you touch the back of the refrigerator there is heat.

In this process, heat is removed from the equation and cooled air is retained. This is now what cools the entire refrigerator. The expansion valve, meanwhile regulates the whole process. When the liquid from the compressor passes through the expansion valve, t he pressure significantly drops, thereby decreasing the temperature further. The mixture of liquid and vapor moves to the evaporator and is vaporized. The vapor then returns to the inlet of the compressor repeating the cycle.

When there is not enough Freon or there is a break in the cycle, it will result in cooling being ineffective. In many cases, you just need to add Freon (as in the case of car aircons) but when the compressor is damaged, then it is better to replace the whole refrigerator.

2. when does the cycle repeat?


When does the cycle repeat?

A cycle repeats itself whenever the process ends and goes back to the starting point. This is observable everywhere, and here are some examples:

The life cycle of a frog, the animal starts out from eggs and goes through the process of metamorphosis to later become frogs themselves. Once matured, they will mate and release eggs into the water which restarts the cycle all over again.  

The water on earth goes through the cycle of turning into vapor and then collects as clouds. They will then fall back to earth in whatever form of precipitation possible in the environment. They flow into canals, streams, rivers, and others. They would then make their way back into the seas and oceans, thereby ending the cycle. It is here that it goes back to the starting point. The whole process repeats endlessly in a perpetual cycle.

When does the cycle repeat?

A cycle repeats whenever it is done with the last stage and reaches the starting point. Cycles are found everywhere, in industries and especially in nature.  

We can pick the metal aluminum as a great example of a resource that is perpetually recyclable. Every bit of this metal can be melted, molded and reused as a product and go through the same process over and over again.

 

In nature, water may be the best example. The familiar water cycle has been happening for billions of years nonstop.

5. when does the cycle repeat on refrigerator?


Answer:

The Vapor - Compression Cycle or otherwise known as Refrigeration Cycle.

The process that takes place inside the refrigerator is the "Refrigeration Cycle"

For us to be able to know better when and on what part of the refrigerator is the cycle repeating, let us first define terms:

fluid refrigerant - cooling medium or the chemical inside the tubes of the system. Its main property should have a low boiling point, Sensitive to heat absorption, Pressure of evaporation is higher than atmospheric pressure and high evaporation rate.compressor - It is the heart of the system as it is keeps the refrigerant flowing. condenser coils - the black coils found at the back of the fridge. It cools and condenses the refrigerant.evaporator coils - the coils on the inside of the fridge.expansion device - devices used to control the refrigerant flow in a refrigeration system. They help to facilitate the change of higher pressure of liquid refrigerant in the condensing unit to lower pressure gas refrigerant in the evaporator.refrigeration cycle - Is a continuous cycle of vapor-compression of a circulating liquid refrigerant as the medium which absorbs and removes heat from the space to be cooled and subsequently rejects that heat elsewhere.

The refrigeration cycle starts as it enters the Compressor.

(To imagine clearly the compressor, it is usually the black cylinder found at the bottom of the refrigerator. If you can't see it that is the refrigerator component that you usually you hear whenever nearby a refrigerator.)

But since it is a cycle, basically it is just the starting point of the process.

In the compressor the refrigerant is compressed and heats up. (Temperature is increasing, Pressure increases)The refrigerant then goes through the condenser coils where its temperature is being lowered as it passes through the coils with air fins. (air fins help in the cooling down of the fluid as it divides the surface area of the coils into smaller ones making it more efficient to release heat into the air) (Temperature decreases, Pressure decreases)The high pressure liquid refrigerant then flows through an expansion valve or capillary tubes to reduce its pressure, and regulate its flow into the evaporator.  (In this point the refrigerant is cooler as heat is already released as it passed through the condenser coils, and becomes much cooler as it passes through the expansion valve. At this stage also in the refrigeration cycle, high pressure liquid refrigerant will flow down the liquid line, through a filter-drier that is designed to prevent contaminants from flowing through the system, and on to the metering device.) (Temperature decreases, Pressure decreases)In the evaporator, the low pressure liquid refrigerant absorbs heat from the fridge interior, changing to a low pressure gas in the process, and then flows on again to the compressor.

This process will continue throughout the lifespan of the refrigerator, this is also the cycle used in air conditioning systems or anything that requires the removal of heat in a certain space.

1.HOW REFRIGERATOR WORKS?

Refrigerators work by causing the refrigerant circulating inside them to change from a liquid into a gas.

2.What happened to the hot gas produced by compression?

The hot gas produced by compression in the refrigeration cycle is transferred to the condenser. The condenser then dissipates the heat from the refrigerant to the outside air. What remains is the liquid. The refrigerant in effect turns the vapor into hot liquid as a result of the high pressure and reduction in temperature. More details can be found below.

3.When does the cycle repeat?

The refrigeration cycle begins by the heat being absorbed by the refrigerant, cooling the air inside the evaporator (low pressure). The cooled air is then transferred to the compressor (high pressure). When air is compressed, it heats up. The greater the heat, the more high pressure gas is produced. This is the gas that is described in the process above. Now, as refrigerant passes through high pressure to low pressure, the expansion valve acts a regulator to the process.
