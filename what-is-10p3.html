1. What is P(8,5)? What is 10P3?


Answer:

1. P(8,5)=6720

2. 10P3=720

Step-by-step explanation:

1. P(n,r) = n! / (n-r)!

P(8,5) = 8! / (8-5)!

P(8,5) = 8! / (3)!

P(8,5) = 8×7×6×5×4×3! / (3)!

P(8,5) = 8×7×6×5×4 (cancel 3!)

P(8,5) = 6720

2. P(n,r) = n! / (n-r)!

P(10,3) = 10! / (10-3)!

P(10,3) = 10! / (7)!

P(10,3) = 10×9×8×7! / 7!

P(10,3) = 10×9×8 (cancel 7!)

P(10,3) = 720

» Using the Permutation Formula to indicate the number of permutations.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \bold{\color{lightblue}Formula:} \\ & \boxed{\: \rm _nP_r = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!} \:} \end{aligned} [/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned} \rm P(10,3) = \frac{10!}{(10 - 3)!} \end{aligned} [/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned} \rm P(10,3) = \frac{10!}{7!} \end{aligned} [/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned} \rm P(10,3) = \frac{10 \cdot 9 \cdot 8 \cdot \cancel{7!}}{ \cancel{7!}} \end{aligned} [/tex]

[tex] \rm P(10,3) = 10 \cdot 9 \cdot 8 [/tex]

[tex] \rm P(10,3) = 720[/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] The number of permutations is 720.
