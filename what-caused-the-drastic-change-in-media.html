3. What caused the drastic change in media?


THE CAUSE OF DRASTIC CHANGE OF MEDIA:

1. The existence of online media rather than print media

2. Psychological effects of new media

EXPLANATION:

1. The existence of print media in the midst of online media

The global community has shifted to digital. The existence of the internet network has changed the way people use media even around the world. Changes in the form of delivering messages from print to online have an impact on the future of the media itself. The shift in the habit of readers to consume media using an internet connection and start leaving print media is a threat to the existence of print media. Publishing tabloids, newspapers and the like is becoming increasingly difficult because people's reading habits have changed. Especially when the price of paper is getting more expensive and the print media industry is becoming more and more difficult to survive, the choice for print media is inevitably to transform themselves to participate in mastering and adapting to the development of existing digital technology.

2. The advantages of media from a psychological perspective

If you compare print media like the newspapers, magazines, tabloids, etc with the online media, the news presented from newspapers can still be accounted for because they are made through an editing process to the editorial team. While online media, it is rather difficult to be held accountable because there are still many who do not understand the journalistic code of ethics, so that it can allow information providers to make mistakes. Print media is considered more capable of preventing inappropriate information and presenting news more accurately. Print media is still interesting because published information can be stored without having to do "recording" as in broadcast media, and the information can still be retrieved if one day it is needed.

6. What caused the drastic change in media


The cause of the drastic change in media is the digitalization.

Further Explanation:

It is undeniable that broadcast media with digital platforms have many advantages over analog or conventional media. In addition to opening up two-way communication channels, the new media of the information age has better impression quality and wider distribution. The last character that is carried is Audience Generated, that new media allows audiences to distribute content that they collect themselves.

In the era of information society, the mass media industry inevitably has to transform from analog to digital form. Because the characteristics of technological products in this era offer productivity, efficiency, speed and cross-border. Text, audio and visual communication devices that were previously separate are now integrated and converged in a single transmission device that combines the functions of old broadcast media into one new media platform. Everything is supported by the global network of the Internet, where mass media, computers, and telecommunications networks are integrated with each other or later commonly referred to as media convergence.

It should also be noted that the emergence of the Internet cannot be seen partially as a result of technological evolution but also a cultural aspect that sees technology in terms of goals, values, code of ethics, belief in progress, awareness and creativity. The presence of Internet technology can be interpreted as a tool that changes the order of production and distribution of information, in which the audience also has a significant role, not only organizers or practitioners of the media industry.

Communication devices that are increasingly convergent also have a big role in changing the current order of information distribution. The camera integrated in the wireless communication device makes the collected information more complete because it is supported by visualization of the scene. Society or the public can distribute information that has news value, faster than even a journalist. If this is the case, practically the news media industry players must think again in order to maintain their existence as presenters of factual information following the development of communication technology and the lifestyles of the people who accompany it.
