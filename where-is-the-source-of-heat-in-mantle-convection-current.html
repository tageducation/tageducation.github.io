15. 11. Where is the source of heat in a mantle convection current?A. the SunB. the crustC. the coreD. the moon​


The core is the source of heat in a mantle convection current.

Mantle convection currents is the process of circulating magma under the earth while transferring core heat to the lithosphere so that the layers in the earth's crust move. The mantle is heated from below, cooled from above, and the overall temperature decreases over a long period of time.

 

This causes plate movement in the crust because these convection currents carry large amounts of energy from the heat of the Earth's core, which can move the tectonic plates above the mantle. The Indo-Australian plate moves northward and infiltrates the Eurasian plate.

 

What is the convection current?

Convection currents are generated by differences in fluid density due to temperature gradients. The activity resulting from the continuous replacement of the hot fluid in the heat source area by the current of nearby cooling fluid is called natural convection currents.

 

What are natural convection currents?

When the current is simply generated by a temperature-derived density difference in a fluid, it is known as natural convection. When convection currents are caused by external factors such as pumps or fans, this is called forced convection. The faster the fluid is moved, the faster the rate of convection.

Convection currents are flowing fluids that move because there is a difference in temperature or density within the material. Because the particles in solids stay in place, convection currents are only seen in gases and liquids. The difference in temperature causes a transfer of energy from a high-energy area to a low-energy area.

 

An example of a convection current

A thunderstorm can be the best example of a convection current. The warm water in the air rises to the top and turns into saturated water droplets that form clouds. Hence, the larger clouds are formed when the smaller clouds bump into each other. Storm clouds, or cumulonimbus clouds, form to reach the final growth stage.

✏QUESTION: What is the main source of heat for mantle convection?

✏️ANSWER:

The primary sources of thermal energy for mantle convection are three: (1) internal heating due to the decay of the radioactive isotopes of uranium, thorium, and potassium; (2) the long-term secular cooling of the earth; and (3) heat from the core.
