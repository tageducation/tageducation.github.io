Example of Illocutionary

Illocutionary is a type of speech act that has a hidden meaning so that the speech partner takes certain actions desired by the speaker. The examples of illocutionary are:

Beware there is fierce dog!

The illocutionary meaning in the sentence above is: everyone must be aware of the presence of a fierce dog near them.

I think the garbage should not be left lying on the side of the road.

The illocutionary meaning in the sentence above is: the garbage on the side of the road should not be left alone, but should be thrown into its place, namely the trash can.

Is complaining, your situation will change just like that?

The illocutionary meaning in the sentence above is: we should not just complain. Because, complaining will not be able to change our situation just like that.

Will constantly cheating on your life will continue to be happy?

The illocutionary meaning in the sentence above is: continuously cheating will not bring lasting happiness. On the contrary, it actually brings loss and regret.

Watch out, there are a lot of crazy people here!

The meaning of the illocutionary in the sentence above is: everyone must be alert when passing the place referred to in the sentence above, because in that place there are many crazy people.

Example of Perlocutionary:

Perlocutionary is a speech act that relates to the presence of another person's speech in relation to the non-linguistic attitude and behavior of that other person. The examples of perlocutionary are:

The clothes you are wearing are wrinkled.

When people hear the sentence above, then that person will immediately tidy up his wrinkled clothes. Or, it could be that the person replaces his wrinkled clothes with neater clothes.

For the time being, this road is being repaired.

When people hear or see the writing above, that person will immediately turn around and look for other roads that can still be passed.

Special zone for children.

If adults see the writing above, then they will refrain from entering the zone. If they are found to be bringing children, they will include them in the area or zone.

Your hairdo is so messy.

When someone (especially the one with messy hair) hears the sentence above, he will immediately straighten his hair.

This book is not for sale.

When someone sees the writing on a book, then they will not sell the book. Even if they finally want to know the contents of the book, they will definitely choose to borrow the book at a place that provides the book.

30. Locutionary to illocutionary to perlocutionany Observation​


Answer:

J. L. Austin's three-prong distinction between locutionary, illocutionary and perlocutionary acts is discussed in terms of D. Davidson's theory of action. Perlocutionary acts refer to the relation between the utterance and its causal effects on the addressee. In contrast, illocutionary and locutionary acts are alternative descriptions of the utterance. The possibility of conceiving of locutionary acts as expressing propositions under a certain mode of presentation is discussed. Different ways to define illocutionary acts without encroaching on the locutionary or perlocutionary territory are considered.
