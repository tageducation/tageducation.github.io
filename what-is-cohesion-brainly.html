22. What is coherence and cohesion


Coherence and cohesion are two important concepts in writing that help to make a text more understandable and effective.

Coherence refers to the logical connections between ideas within a text. A coherent text is one where the ideas are presented in a clear and logical manner, with each idea building on the previous one in a way that makes sense. Coherence is achieved through the use of transitional words and phrases, such as "therefore," "however," and "on the other hand," which help to signal the relationship between ideas and ensure that the text flows smoothly.

Cohesion, on the other hand, refers to the way that individual sentences and paragraphs are connected to each other within a text. Cohesion is achieved through the use of grammatical and lexical devices, such as pronouns, conjunctions, and repeated key words, which help to create a sense of unity and coherence in the text. Cohesion helps to make the text more readable and understandable by creating a sense of continuity between ideas.

In short, coherence is about the logical connections between ideas in the text, while cohesion is about the connections between individual sentences and paragraphs. Together, they help to create a well-organized, effective piece of writing that is easy to follow and understand.
