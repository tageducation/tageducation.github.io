19. What activities are included in the pyramid?


"Here are activities that are included in the activity pyramid.

The activity pyramid, or what is commonly referred to as the physical activity pyramid, is a set of guidelines for adults in doing their obligation of exercising. According to humankinetics website, this comprises of ranges of activities as well as their categorization of being relatively intense activities to replenishing back the energy being lost during the exercise.

Activities in the activity pyramid:

Moderate physical activity. This one is also phrased as lifestyle activities, you could do this by ironing, washing the clothes (manual), and sweeping the floor. Vigorous aerobics. The closest example of this is jogging. It's fairly free and you could get other benefits as well. Vigorous sport and recreation. Basketball and soccer are instances of these activities because they require you to move around. Muscle fitness exercises. Rock climbing and jumping are two examples of exercises that would seriously train your muscle. Though, this would be felt in long term. Flexibility exercises. According to the same website, yoga is one example of an exercise that trains one's flexibility. This is in parts due to the fact that we do many kinds of positions in yoga. Avoiding inactivity. Inactivity is defined as not being active. Few examples of this include sitting for hours playing computer of laying down with your phones. We are suggested at least of 60 minutes light activities each day, so don't stay in one place too much. Balancing energy. The energy lost during activities that you have done in a day must be replenished. This is to avoid being starved that could lead to you being sick. You might want to take a note of what you eat every day in terms of its calorie count as well as the nutrition and vitamins.
