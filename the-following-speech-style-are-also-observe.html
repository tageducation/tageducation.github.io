Speech Styles

Speech styles refer to the type of language used by the speaker, which is distinguished by the level of formality.

5 Types of Speech Styles

1. Intimate

It is used in conversations between two people who are very close. It is characterized by a lack of words and a high likelihood of nonverbal communication. There is also a free and easy participation of both speaker and listener, similar to casual.

2. Casual

It is also known as informal style and is typically used between friends or insiders who have something to share. Both the speaker and the listener can freely and easily participate in this type.

3. Consultative

It is essentially unplanned speech because the speaker relies on the listener's participation and feedback. The speaker will provide background information, while the listener will continue to participate.

4. Formal

This style employs formal words and expressions and is more commonly seen in writing than in speaking. It also prohibits using ellipsis, contractions, and qualifying modal adverbials.

5. Frozen

It is the highest form of communicative style, also known as fixed speech, and is frequently used in respectful situations or formal ceremonies such as Shakespearean plays, weddings, and funerals, among others. It employs complex grammatical sentence structure and vocabulary that only experts in that field are aware of.

These styles are important in public speaking because they will help you choose the best approach for specific situations. Choosing the appropriate speech prevents misunderstandings and conflicts.
