6. Two billiard balls approach each other, if ball one is said to be at rest, what is its total velocity before the collision? A. Zero B. Doubled C. Change D. Constant


Answer:

D.constant

Explanation:

“Elastic collision” means you know conservation of momentum and (kinetic) energy.

Let their initial speed be given by v , and their masses be m1 and m2 .

Conservation of momentum: m1v−m2v=m1v1f+m2v2f

Conservation of energy: m1v2/2+m2v2/2=m1v21f/2+m2v22f/2

You should solve these equations to get your answer, in general.

If m1=m2 , (probably a reasonable assumption given the phrasing), then the first equation says −v1f=v2f . Plugging this expression for v2f into the second equation (and simplifying) says v2=v22f . This tells you that |v1f|=|v2f|=v .

This tells you that, after the collision, the balls move in opposite directions with respect to each other, with the same speeds.

Contrary to other answers, in general, this is all you know without more information about the collision.

9. two Billiard balls approach each other with the same speed. if they collide in a perfectly elastic collision, what would be their velocities after collision??? a. zero. b.same in magnitude and direction. c.same in magnitude but opposite direction. d.different magnitude and opposite direction


Answer:

The answer is letter C: Same in magnitude but opposite direction.

Explanation:

Collision

Collision is the sudden change in route of a moving body/ bodies because of interaction with another body/ bodies.

The magnitude and direction of the speed (or velocity) of the colliding objects is subjected to change during collision. The force experienced by the object during collision acts only for a very short period of time.

There are two conservation laws applied in collision, the law of conservation of linear momentum and the law of conservation of energy.

Types of CollisionElastic collision - both the momentum and kinetic energy of the system are conserved. Inelastic collision - only momentum is conserved and not the kinetic energy.

Law of Conservation of Linear Momentum

It states that the linear momentum of a system (composed of objects colliding) is conserved given that there is no external force acting on the system.

Mathematical Expression:

m₁v₁ + m₂v₂ = m₁v₁' + m₂v₂'   → equation 1

where

m₁ = mass of object 1

m₂ = mass of object 2

v₁ = initial velocity of object 1

v₂ = initial velocity of object 2

v₁' = final velocity of object 1

v₂ = final velocity of object 2

Law of Conservation of Energy

It states that energy can neither be created nor destroyed, it is rather converted from one form to another.

Mathematical expression:

For this the equation for kinetic energy is used since we are dealing with momentum and kinetic energy in collisions.

[tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex] m₁v₁² + [tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex] m₂v₂² = [tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex] (m₁)(v₁')² + [tex]\frac{1}{2}[/tex] (m₂)(v₂')²   →  equation 2

(Please refer above for the meaning of the variables)

In the problem, we are given with 2 billiard balls with the same speed. We can assume that both of their masses are also the same.

If m₁ = m₂ and v₁ = v₂ = v, then equation 1 will be −v₁' = v₂'. This will be our third equation.

−v₁' = v₂'  →  equation 3

Substituting equation 3 for v₂' in equation 2 then simplifying, we'll have

v² = (v₂')²

therefore, |v₁'| = |v₂'| = v

This last equation tells us that, the balls, with the same magnitude of velocity, will move in opposite directions with respect to each other after the collision.
