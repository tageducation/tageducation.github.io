12. Habilitation is concerned with people who have acquired disabilities


The correct answer is False.


It is not habilitation; rather, it is rehabilitation which is concerned with people who have acquired disabilities. Rehabilitation helps an individual regain his/ her skills, abilities, or knowledge that may have been lost or compromised because of acquiring disabilities or due to a change in one’s disability or circumstances. Examples, skills or functioning lost due to stroke, head injury, illness or other cause.


On the other hand, habilitation is concerned with developmental disabilities. Its services are for those who may not have ever developed the skill, such as a child who is not talking as expected for his or her age.  Also, adults can also benefit from habilitative services, particularly those with intellectual disabilities or disorders such as cerebral palsy who may benefit from services at different points in their life to address functional abilities.

23. what is the developmental issue that concerned with whether an individual is a product of his/her genes or culture?​


DEVELOPMENTAL ISSUE ON PRODUCT O HIS/HER GENES OR CULTURE:

The nature-nurture controversy in child development is concerned with whether a person is a product of his or her genes or culture.

In actuality, a child's surroundings and experiences shape their genetic composition in a variety of ways. According to the child development website First Five Years, the expanding science of epigenetics investigates how early experiences affect the chemical markers that build up in DNA and govern how much of a particular gene will be expressed.

The epigenome is made up of all of these markings; epigenetics is the study of these marks and how they affect both children and adults.

In the nature vs. nurture argument, "nature" is referred to as all genes and genetic elements that affect an individual's distinct physiology, personality, and outward appearance.

The term "nurture" refers to the various environmental factors that influence a person, such as their early life experiences, social and familial ties, culture, and community.

Empiricism and rationalism are philosophical ideas that are related to both nature and nurture.

empiricists, our sense experiences are the ultimate source of all ideas and knowledge.rationalists, conceptions and knowledge sometimes include more information than just sensory experience. They also think that some kind of reasoning is what gives us this new knowledge about the outside world.

Modern specialists agree that both "nature" and "nurture" have a role in psychological development and interact in complicated ways. Traditionally, the dispute over "nature vs. nurture" has been portrayed as one between those who argue for the superiority of one form of influence over the other.

Nature has less of an impact than nurture. Almost all respondents stressed the importance of nurturing whether discussing factors influencing an adopted baby, their own experiences, an angry youngster, or a violent young person.

29. why should we show help and concern to People with Disability?​


Answer:

People who have never interacted with a person who has a mental or physical disability may think of the exchange as intimidating or nerve-wracking. They might worry what to talk about or how to avoid staring. These concerns are understandable, but it’s important to realize people with disabilities should be treated the same as everyone else.

The most important part of interacting with someone who has a disability is seeing that person for whom he or she is, and not what disability that person has. What it boils down to is having a sense of disability awareness and disability etiquette.
