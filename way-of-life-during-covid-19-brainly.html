5. Priorities in life during covid 19​


Panic about the Covid-19 coronavirus outbreak occurs in almost every country. The virus that has spread in 118 countries has been the talk of the town since late December 2019 until now.

But behind the outbreak and feelings of panic, there are lessons or positive things that we can learn. 4 Priorities in life during covid 19​:

1. Time with family

During this pandemic, many workers have been laid off due to staff reductions to maintain economic stability and break the chain of transmission of Covid 19.  Students and students are now learning online. Of course, this causes learning patterns to be 180 degrees different from face-to-face schools. Parents also have difficulties as my aunt experienced when her elementary school child studied online. It is not the child who learns, but the mother who does the assignments while the child is busy playing games on the smartphone.

But we can take the positives and this is where we can spend more time with family. Those of us who are still students have more time to help our parents with their work. Those of us who used to gather only for breakfast can now gather for lunch, dinner, or just talk and joke every day. Not just on weekends.

2. Independent and creative

We can be more independent again, because now is a difficult time, everything is difficult, especially the economy and social relationships. We must be confused about how to keep earning income during this pandemic. The thing that comes to mind is 'how can I get an income without distressing my parents? One of the things we can do is utilize social media to sell online.

Many have discovered their new talents and hobbies during this pandemic, such as cooking because they have often seen cooking content on social media and then tried to practice it. Then we can also sell the food we make. those whose hobbies are drawing and writing can also take advantage of their time between learning activities to express their work, there are even websites that can pay for your paintings. So we can be independent and show our creativity while being able to produce something with our efforts.

3. Learn to save money

Consciously or unconsciously during Covid -19, we became more frugal because the large-scale social restrictions (PSBB) that have been implemented by the government make us stay at home and not leave if there is nothing very important. In this way, we can save money that we usually use for traveling or hanging out with friends and use it for more useful things.

There are many online stores now. That makes it easier for us to shop so it will be more wasteful. Yes, many online stores sell various products, even new and unique products that make us want to buy. Even the price of products in online stores is cheaper than buying directly from the store. You can choose to shop at online stores that provide goods at these lower prices.

4. Maintain health

The current situation is very worrying because the transmission of the virus is very fast and can attack anyone. People affected by the virus can be asymptomatic and when tested it turns out that they are positive for the Coronavirus. Especially now that people seem to be tired of this situation and act as if there is no longer a virus, there are still many who go out, hang out, and public transportation is now crowded and there are still those who do not comply with health protocols.

6. Before COVID-19 During COVID-19 Way of life View of the world Priorities in life Relationships with others​


Explanation:In January 2020 the World Health Organization (WHO) declared the outbreak of a new coronavirus

disease, COVID-19, to be a Public Health Emergency of International Concern. WHO stated that

there is a high risk of COVID-19 spreading to other countries around the world. In March 2020,

WHO made the assessment that COVID-19 can be characterized as a pandemic.

WHO and public health authorities around the world are acting to contain the COVID-19 outbreak.

However, this time of crisis is generating stress throughout the population. The considerations

presented in this document have been developed by the WHO Department of Mental Health and

Substance Use as a series of messages that can be used in communications to support mental and

psychosocial well-being in different target groups during the outbreak.

Messages for the general population

1. COVID-19 has and is likely to affect people from many countries, in many geographical locations.

When referring to people with COVID-19, do not attach the disease to any particular ethnicity or

nationality. Be empathetic to all those who are affected, in and from any country. People who are

affected by COVID-19 have not done anything wrong, and they deserve our support, compassion

and kindness.

2. Do not refer to people with the disease as “COVID-19 cases”, “victims” “COVID-19 families” or

“the diseased”. They are “people who have COVID-19”, “people who are being treated for

COVID-19”, or “people who are recovering from COVID-19”, and after recovering from COVID-19

their life will go on with their jobs, families and loved ones. It is important to separate a person

from having an identity defined by COVID-19, in order to reduce stigma.

3. Minimize watching, reading or listening to news about COVID-19 that causes you to feel anxious

or distressed; seek information only from trusted sources and mainly so that you can take practical

steps to prepare your plans and protect yourself and loved ones. Seek information updates at

specific times during the day, once or twice. The sudden and near-constant stream of news reports

about an outbreak can cause anyone to feel worried. Get the facts; not rumours and

misinformation. Gather information at regular intervals from the WHO website and local health

authority platforms in order to help you distinguish facts from rumours. Facts can help to minimize

fears.

18. Life of Filipinos during time of covid 19​


Answer:

As COVID-19 strikes the Philippine nation, people rise together to counter it. At the forefront of the fight against the virus are our healthcare workers and various frontliners. Daily, they face the hazard of infection with their mantra: “We go to work for you. Please stay at home for us.”

Healthcare workers who directly work with COVID-positive patients are outnumbered; and due to their first-hand exposure, some staff require quarantine themselves - reducing their numbers. Another big challenge for frontliners is the lack of medical supplies including Personal Protective Equipment.

Community quarantine and social distancing has not hindered our kababayans in looking for ways to help with the battle. Different groups, individuals, classmates, colleagues - groups big and small - have come together, despite limited resources and movement. Showing everyone that we are all #InThisTogether.
