Ingredients

750 ml good-quality, interesting white wine such as Riesling, Muscat, a big oaky Chardonnay or dessert wine

180 ml live white wine or apple cider vinegar (more if the wine needs to be diluted)

1 large, clean 2l (68 fl oz) jar

1 clean tea towel or piece of jay cloth

1 strong elastic band

Details

Cuisine: British

Recipe Type: Sauce

Difficulty: Medium

Preparation Time: 20 mins

Cooking Time: 0 mins

Serves: 10

Step-by-step

Add the wine to the jar. If required, dilute the wine to 10% abv with water (see below for quick reference chart).

Work out 25% of the new total by dividing it by 4, then add this quantity of the live vinegar.

Do not add a lid. Instead, place the tea towel or cloth over the opening of the jar, and fix it in place with the elastic band.

Place the jar in a dry, dark place, ideally somewhere not too cold – warmth helps move the fermentation along a bit.

Taste it after 2 weeks to see how it is, then taste every week until it tastes strongly acidic.

Strain through a double layer of muslin and pour into sterilised bottles. Place the bottles in a cool dark place to mature for a few weeks, and pop your live vinegar ‘mother’ into whatever alcohol you want to ferment next.

When you come to use your vinegar, taste it and add a little water if you feel it necessary.

Understanding Nutrition Facts Label

The nutrition facts label of each food packaging has information that tells the buyer the nutrition content of the food that they are eating.

It serves as a guide to understanding a healthy, complete, and balanced diet.  Every packaged, or processed, product should have a label.

Here are some guides in understanding the Nutrition Facts Label:

Understand the serving proportion/ guideMake a comparison of carbohydrates depending on the individual's need.Percentage Value serves as the number guide of how much or how less is needed.Understand the Nutrition Terms used.Make sure to get enough and required Vitamins, Minerals, and Dietary Fiber.Take into consideration of the Additional Nutrients.

Here are more interesting facts about Nutritional Label:

Any kind of Food that has one more ingredient should be listed as well on the label. Ingredients are listed in descending order by weight. Those in the largest amounts are listed first. The Nutritional Label is a guide for that individual that has special food requirements and allergies.

The amount an individual usually eats at a given time and the number of servings in the package is the way to understand the serving's content.
