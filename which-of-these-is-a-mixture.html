1. Which of the following best describe heterogeneous mixtures? a. A heterogeneous mixture is a mixture which is not uniform in appearance. b. A heterogeneous mixture is a mixture which is uniform in appearance. c. A heterogeneous mixture is a mixture which has the same appearance. d. All of the above


Answer:

a. A heterogeneous mixture is a mixture which is not uniform in appearance.

Explanation:

It is because a heterogeneous mixture is a mixture that does not have uniform composition. It does not blend throughout. The individual substances of a heterogeneous mixture are recognizable or remain distinct. The phases or substances are all visible.

Examples of heterogeneous mixtures are:

- water and oil

- mixed nuts

- salad dressing

- fruit salad

- dalandan juice

- beach sand (since you can see different-colored particles)

- clouds (clouds contain tiny droplets of liquid water)

29. Colloids are heterogeneous mixture which of these mixtures is not a colloid ​


Answer:

A colloid is somewhere in between a truly homogeneous mixture and a heterogeneous mixture. A Lorri suggested, the individual particle in a colloid are small enough so that the thermal movement (Brownian motion) prevents them from settling to the bottom of a container through the action of gravity, although they can be separated by centrifugation (which is not possible for a true homogeneous mixture). On the other hand, colloids are usually not transparent, they disperse light because the particles are as large or larger than the wavelength of visible light.
