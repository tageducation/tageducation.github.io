1. D. it is a systematic gathering of data, investigation, and study of materials and sources to establish facts and reach is systematic

Further explanation:

Research is a process of collecting materials, systematically investigating by studying various materials and sources to establish facts and reach new conclusions.

2. D. It follows a procedure or process from identifying the problem to assessing the results of the investigation.

Further explanation:

Systematic in the scientific method means a process used in a research by using a logical way or step. To write a research result, the researcher must write it sequentially, structured and systematic.

In research systematics, it is a series of research reports that can be seen at the link. In general, systematics has a beginning, content and ending sequence.

3. B. A research can use any available sources of information even if it is not credible.

Further explanation:

That statement is not true. This is because the source of information in a study must be a reliable source. Reliable sources are sources that provide comprehensive and reasoned theories, arguments, discussions, and so on based on strong evidence.

4. A. Always true

Further explanation:

Researchers must rephrase a source of information or cite to avoid plagiarism is a true statement. Because by recopying an information, the sign of rearranging it without writing the source or author is included in the act of plagiarism against the original work or writing.

5. B. Introduction

Further explanation:

In scientific works, the introduction is the first part or chapter. The introductory section will help the reader to know the basic but important points of scientific work. Broadly speaking, the introduction of a scientific paper contains the background of the research carried out, the problems discussed, as well as the methods or mechanisms for solving problems that exist in scientific works.

6. D. Review of relate literature

Further explanation:

A related literature review is a summary of previous research on a particular topic. The literature review originates or is sourced from relevant research. The condition is that the literature review must mention, explain, summarize, evaluate objectively, and clarify previous research.

A literature review is a section that provides practical guidance on a particular topic. This review is especially helpful for those with limited research time. So that the literature review provides an overview of similar studies that will be conducted.

7. A. It explains how the research was conducted

Further explanation:

Research methodology is the science that explains how the research should and should be carried out.

Research methodology is a way to find out the results of a specific problem, where the problem is also called a research problem. In Methodology, researchers use a variety of different criteria to solve existing research problems.

8. C. it presents accurate data more simply and clearly without using many words

further explanation:

Graphs and tables in scientific writing serve to clarify the purpose of writing so that it is easy to read or interpret without having double meanings.

Presentation of data using graphs and tables is a method used to summarize, organize, organize or organize data so that the data is easy to understand and does not use a lot of words.

9. B. Respondent

Further explanation:

Respondents are sources of information that support research, namely people who are asked for responses to questions that have been structured or semi-structured to be a source of data in a study.

The type of questionnaire distributed to research respondents is in the form of representing individuals, couples, or organizations.

10. A. Abstract

Further explanation:

Abstract is defined as a summary of the contents of a scientific paper. Abstracts must be written accurately, easy to read, clear, and concise. The number of words is around 250-300 words. The purpose of the abstract is for the reader to quickly understand the content of the research.
