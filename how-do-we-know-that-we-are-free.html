3. how do we know that we are free​


The following are signs that a person has a true free spirit:

Appreciate Experience

Materialism doesn't affect you. You really care about your relationships with other people. You value life's experiences more than anything else. Traveling, exploring and adventuring is an exciting thing for you and it is the purpose of your life.

Learning New Things Interested

Your main desire is to know everything about the world. You have interests that are not limited to one area. It helps you connect the dots and create a picture of how the world really is. It's not uncommon for you to change your hobbies.

Other People's Opinions Will Not Affect Confidence

You respect other people's opinions but you are not one to question your own worth based on them. You're definitely not the kind of person to adjust your own life to your ideas of what other people are like. You will never be jealous of what other people are doing that may seem fun. You trust your own mind and instincts to make decisions about your life.

Solving Problems Well

Your logic looks perfect and you have the tools to solve any problem quickly. Free-spirited people have a deeper understanding of how things work and can make connections that just about anyone can make. This allows you to deal with problems that come your way with ease.

Don't think about the past

The things that happened in the past will always be a part of you. You realize that events in the past have a big impact on yourself today. A free spirit person has come to terms with the bad things that might happen to you. You don't dwell on the past and trouble yourself with what has happened in the past.

6. Based on the biblical passage, how do we know that a person has already gained true free-dom?​


Answer:

2 Cor 3:17

Explanation:

2 Cor 3:17 "And the Lord is the Spirit; and where the Spirit of the Lord is, there is freedom"

This verse states that the Lord is the Spirit. Then in chapter 4 verse 5 "... Christ Jesus as Lord..." This is a strong word in the Bible telling us that Christ is the Spirit. Only the Spirit who gives us the divine life as seen " the Spirit gives life" (3:6) . When the person receive the Spirit he gains freedom.

Also in John 8:32 " the truth shall set you free ". The "truth" in the original language which is Greek this is the same of the word "reality" in John 1:14,17

John 1:14 " ... full of grace and reality", verse 17 " ...grace came through Jesus Christ"

By this verse the truth is not the doctrine but the Lord Himself. In John 8:32 " the truth shall set you free " but as you read further in verse 36 " ... the Son sets you free ..."

This is another proof that the Lord is the truth.

In Col 2:9 " For in Him dwells all the fullness of the Godhead bodily" .

Since the Lord is the truth, the reality then He is the reality of what God is because what was in the Lord is the fullness of the Godhead. When the Lord comes into a person, the reality is imparted into us and is realized by us. And this reality operates to free us from the bondage of sin. Thus a person gains true freedom when the Lord comes as the Spirit into that person and receive Him.
