10. Five elements of Multimodal text​


The five elements of multimodal text are:

Language: The written or spoken words used to convey meaning.Images: Pictures, diagrams, or other visual representations that help convey meaning.Audio: Sound, music, or other auditory elements that help convey meaning.Video: Moving images that can include all of the other elements, as well as visual elements such as body language and facial expressions.Hypertext: Links to other texts or multimedia elements that provide additional context or information.

In the composition field, multimodal elements are commonly defined in terms of the five modes of communication: linguistic, visual, gestural, spatial, audio.

•Visual: colour, vectors and viewpoint in still and moving images.
