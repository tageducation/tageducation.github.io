9. How to cook rice using signal words in a fire wood?


How to cook rice in a fire wood?

Start by creating a level, low-temperature fire. Use a fire that can boil water, not one that is blazing hot and has licking flames. To keep the temperature as steady as you can, keep shifting the coals around. Bring water to a boil next. Use rice with four times as much water as rice. Due to boiling off, you will require more than the rice can absorb.

Then, add the rice and cook it uncovered for 12 to 14 minutes. While the rice will cook more quickly than on a stovetop due to the increased temperature, some of its nutrients and fluff will be lost. Finally, if the temperature rises too much, turn off the heat. The rice can continue to cook for a few minutes off the flames if you are having difficulties managing your fire, especially if you cover the pot.

Helpful Reminder in Different Conditions of Rice

1. Although the rice is ready to eat, there is still water in the pot. Using a wooden spoon to hold the rice in, drain the rice in a strainer or carefully pour most of the water out.

2. Even though the rice should be cooked, it is still chewy and firm. If the water has boiled off, add additional and simmer the food for a few more minutes.

3. Rice that has burned! To stop the rice from cooking, turn off the heat and wash the exterior of the pot with cold water. Take out the top-quality rice from the middle.

4. Rice usually burns. For 6 to 7 minutes, boil the rice uncovered. After that, completely turn off the heat, and cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. With no chance of burning, the rice should be fully cooked by the steam in 10 to 15 minutes.

11. ACTIVITY 3: Ask someone in your house who knows how to cook rice. Using the signal words, writedowndirections according to the steps he/she told you.How to cook rice​


1. Rinse the rice.

2. Use the right ratio of water. Add 2 parts water and 1 part rice to a large pot. For slightly firmer rice, use 1 part liquid to 2/3 parts rice.

3. Bring the water to a boil. Once it's boiling, add a big pinch of salt.

4. Maintain a simmer. Reduce heat to low, cover the pot with a tight fitting lid, and maintain a gentle simmer.

5. Cook without peeking or stirring. Cook until the water is absorbed, about 18 minutes. Try not to peek until the end of the cooking time so the steam doesn't escape. Whatever you do, don't mix the rice while it's cooking — this will lead to gummy rice.

6. Let the rice rest covered. Turn off the heat and let the rice sit, covered, for 10 minutes. During this time, the rice will steam for extra fluffy results.

7.Fluff the rice with a fork.

Answer:

Steps On How To Cook Rice Using The Signal Words

First, put some uncook rice in a pot. Second, rinse off the rice. Swish the rice gently with your fingers and rub lightly. Next, pour out the starchy water. You can repeat it for one to two times, depending on how starchy the rice is. After pouring out the water for the last time, add clean water to the pot. You can add 1 1/2 to 2 cups of water for one cup rice. Then, cover the pot and bring to a boil. Lower down the heat after it boils. Lastly, let the rice to simmer for 15 minutes.

Explanation:

What are signal words?

Signal words are words that we use to clearly show the sequence of events. These words are usually used in giving instructions or stating process.

Answer:

1. Measure and placed in a bowl the right amount of rice.

2. Optional: Rinse the rice to clear water.

3. Shift the rice to the inside of the rice cooker from the tub. A reusable rice napkin can also be used to make the cleaning easier afterwards.

4. Fill up the rice pan with water.

5. Place the bowl inside and close the top of your rice cooker. In this stage, you can add spices, oil and butter.

6. Stick on your cooker of rice and trigger your cooker. Most of the cookers are lit on the front of the device.

7. When the rice is cooked, the cooker notifies you that you turn off the light, switch up or make a sound. Let it stay for at least 15 minutes in Keep Warm before serving when your rice has finished cooking. This will evaporate excess moisture and allow your rice to absorb water completely.

8. To eat your rice, serve your disposable utensils or rice paddles.

Explanation:

The use of a rice cooker is easy and convenient, in particular when producing large amounts of rice, making them suitable for food stores. Furthermore, knowing the correct rice-to-water ratio will ensure you serve your guests the smoothest and most effective rice possible.
