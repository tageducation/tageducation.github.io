2. the research topic must be simple and specific enough for us to accomplish


Answer:

The first step in any research process is to think of a topic for research. The topic is obviously generated either from specific problems that you have encountered in your teaching or a wish to do something better. Your researchable idea can be generated from many sources:

You may have a particular area of interest that you have been thinking for quite some time, and would like to pursue further.

You may have generated your idea while discussing this issue with your colleagues/peers or in a seminar or while in a discussion in the classroom. Also, your existing interest may have been further crystallised in these situations.

You might have been reading journals, books or magazines on issues relating to teaching and learning in higher education; and, while reading you may have reflected on some issues and would like to pursue further thinking on it, maybe leading to a piece of research to find out about the topic.

Further reflection on our general interest, discussion with colleagues and teachers, whatever we read, and many such circumstances will facilitate focusing on the choice of our topic of research.

Any problem or idea for research that we think of is like a seed that has potential for further growth and development. You may like to consider some of the following questions while deciding to locate your topic of research:

Do you have sufficient interest in and background material on the area to be able to pursue it further with confidence?

Is the topic important to pursue, and can it make some contribution to the area that you are interested in investigating further?

Can you manage to locate and collect data with reasonable ease to pursue the piece of research? Are the required resources at your disposal? If not, where can you get them?

Do you have sufficient time at hand to pursue it; or, do you need to tone down the area of work so you can manage within a given time limit while maintaining the rigour of the work?

5. sample research topic of ETHNOGRAPHY with brief and simple explanation about why the topic belongs to that qualitative research type.


Answer:

Ethnography is a qualitative research method which involves a detailed study of a particular cultural group. The word ethnography comes from Greek words Ethnos meaning people and Graphein meaning writing. That is why Ethnography is also known as “culture writing”. It is a primary research method, but not limited to Anthropology which anthropologists often document in their fieldwork. Ethnography is one of the most distinctive characteristics of Anthropology. Socio-cultural anthropologists who write ethnographies are termed as ethnographers.

Explanation:

The methodology of Ethnography relies typically on participant observation and qualitative data collection. While conducting an ethnography, the anthropologists generally live in a culture different from their own. They get engaged in the daily lives of populations to be studied and deeply observe and record their findings. While conducting any research in Anthropology, the researchers try not be ethnocentric by using the tool of cultural relativism. :>

Effects of mobile gaming patterns on learning outcomes: A literature review

Within the past decade, a growing number of educational scientists have started to recognise the multifaceted potential that mobile learning games have as a tool for learning and teaching. This paper presents a review of current research on the topic to better understand game mechanisms with regard to learning outcomes. The purpose of this paper is twofold. First, we introduce a framework of analysis which is based on previous work on game design patterns for mobile games and on learning outcomes. The framework focuses on two aspects, motivation and knowledge gain. Second, we present a set of patterns which we identified in the literature and that positively influence these two aspects. Our results support the general assumption that mobile learning games have potential to enhance motivation. It reveals that game mechnisms such as Collaborative Actions or Augmented Reality provide incentive to get engaged with learning and/or a certain topic. With regard to knowledge gain, results are less comprehensive.
