21. What part moves the body tube and objectives up and down


Coarse Adjustment Knob- part moves the body tube and objectives up and down.

Parts of a microscope and their functions

1. Ocular lens or eyepiece: this lens close to the eye serves to magnify the image of an object (image properties: virtual, upright, enlarged). In general, the image formed can be magnified up to 6, 10 or 12 times.

2. Microscope tube: transmits light from the objective lens to the ocular lens.

3. Revolver: replaces the objective lens with the desired magnification.

4. Objective lens: the objective lens is the lens that is close to the object being observed and is used to magnify the image of the object. Depending on the manufacturer, the object magnification ranges from 10X, 40X, up to 100X and has an aperture value (NA). What is meant by aperture value is a measure of its ability to gather light and resolve fine specimen details when working at a fixed object (or specimen) distance. Higher numerical aperture values allow an increasingly oblique beam to enter the front lens of the objective, which produces clearer images and allows smaller structures to be visualized with greater clarity.

5. Object clamp: clamp the object glass.

6. Table microscope: a place to put the object.

7. The mirror consists of two mirrors, namely a concave mirror and a flat mirror. The concave mirror functions to collect, search for, and direct light to the object being observed. Flat mirrors function to reflect light when the light source is bright enough.

8. Diaphragm: adjust the amount of light.

9. Microscope leg: microscope stand.

10. Microscope arm: to hold the microscope.

11. Coarse adjustment knob: serves to clarify the shadow.

12. Fine adjustment knob:serves to sharpen shadows.
