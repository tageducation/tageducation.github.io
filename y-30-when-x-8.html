16. if Z varies Jointly as x and y , and z=8 ,when x=12 ,and y=10 ,find z when x =30 ,and y=12​


JOINT VARIATION

Direction: If z varies Jointly as x and y, and z=8 when x=12 and y=10, find z when x=30 and y=12

Answer:

[tex]\Large \blue{\bold{z = 24.12}}[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

Step 1: Write the correct equation. Joint variation problems are solved using the equation x=kzy if x varies jointly as z and y.

[tex]\large \bold{z = kxy}[/tex]

Step 2: Use the information given in the problem to find the value of k. In this case, you need to find k if z=8, x=12, and y=10.

[tex]z =kxy \\ 8 = k(12)(10) \\ 8=120k \\ \frac{8}{120} = \frac{ \cancel{120}k}{ \cancel{120}} \\ \bold{0.067 = k}[/tex]

Therefore, the constant of variation k is 0.067.

Step 3: Rewrite the equation from step 1 substituting in the value of k found in step 2.

[tex]\large \bold{z = 0.067xy}[/tex]

Step 4: Use the equation found in step 3 and the remaining information given in the problem to answer; in this case, you need to find z when x=30 and y=12.

[tex]z =0.067xy \\ z = (0.067)(30)(12) \\ z = 24.12[/tex]

Thus, the value of z is equal to 24.12.
