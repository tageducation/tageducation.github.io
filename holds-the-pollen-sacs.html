Answer:

Anther

Explanation:

Answer:

The typical anther is bilocular, it consists of two thecae. Each theca contains two microsporangia, also known as pollen sacs. The microsporangia produce the microspores, which for seed plants are known as pollen grains. If the pollen sacs are not adjacent, or if they open separately, then no thecae are formed.

1. Sepals are often green (as seen in the photo, at the base of each flower) but can also be nearly identical to the petals themselves. They serve as the outermost layer of a flower bud and then open to support the flower's reproductive structures.

3. Plants have a number of different means to attract pollinators, with bright, showy colors being one of the most common ways to maximize their visual effect. Hummingbirds prefer red, pink, fuchsia, or purple flowers. ... Butterflies enjoy bright colors such as yellow, orange, pink, and red.

4. The anther produces pollen (male reproductive cells). The filament holds the anther up. During the process of fertilization, pollen lands on the stigma, a tube grows down the style and enters the ovary.

5. Anther is the part of a stamen that contains the pollen.

6. The anther usually contains four pollen sacs which are responsible for producing pollen grains. Each pollen grain is a single cell containing two male gametes. Once mature, the anther splits open and pollen is released.

7. A slender stalk called the style often connects the ovary and stigma. Pollen grains germinate on the stigma, and the pollen tube must grow through the tissues of the style (if present) and the ovary to reach the ovule.

8. The pistil includes an ovary (where the ovules are produced; ovules are the female reproductive cells, the eggs), and a stigma (which receives the pollen during fertilization). A stamen consists of an anther (which produces pollen, the male reproductive cell) and a filament.

9. The pollen tubes grow out of the grains, becoming longer and longer, and move through the pistil toward the flower's ovules. Inside the tubes are the sperm cells that will fertilize the egg cells and cause them to develop into seeds.

10. Pistils are composed of three main parts: 1) the sticky top called the stigma, which catches pollen grains; 2) the style, a long neck that connects the stigma and the ovary; and 3) the ovary, in which ovules are produced.
