Types of Plates

Oceanic Plate - Thinner and denser than continental plate, and usally located under the large ocean water like the pacific plate.Continental plate - thicker and more less dense than oceanic plate.

The Earth's Lithosphere is divided into several segments called plate. These plates are moving in a certain direction as influenced by the flow of the convection current of the mantle.

The list below are the Major plates and the common minor plates around the World.

Major Plate

Pacific plate - Located in the Pacific ocean Australian Plate - Located in the Australian Continent Eurasian Plate - Located in the European and Asian continent African Plate - Located in the African continent North American Plate - Located in North American continent South American Plate - Located in South American continent Antarctic Plate - Located in Antarctic continent

Minor Plate

Philippine Sea Plate - East of the Philippines Indian Plate - Located in India Nazca Plate - Eastern Pacific ocean Somali Plate -  east coast of Africa Arabian Plate - Located in Arabian Peninsula Scotia Plate - Located between the Antarctic Plates and South America Amurian Plate - Eastern Asia Yangtze Plate -  bulk of southern China
