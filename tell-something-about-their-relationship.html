Explain each question.

1. What is a tsunami? What happens when tsunami enters shallow water?

Tsunamis are giant waves caused by earthquakes or volcanic eruptions under the sea. When tsunami enters shallow water, the wavelength decreases, the height increases, and currents intensify. Tsunamis are disasters that can kill people snd other living organisms as what happened in Japan. Very shocking.

2. Explain how convection current may be related to plate tectonics.

Convection currents are the result of differential heating. Convection currents are identified in Earth's mantle.

In places where convection currents rise up towards the crust's surface, tectonic plates move away from each other in a process known as seafloor spreading

3. Describe each of the three types of waves.

The threes types of waves are:

transverse waveslongitudinal wavessurface waves.

Transverse waves are like those on water, with the surface going up and down, and longitudinal waves are like of those of sound, consisting of alternating compressions and rarefactions in a medium.

4. Compare and contrast divergent and convergent plate boundaries.

Convergent boundaries, where two plates are moving toward each other, are of three types, depending on the type of crust present on either side of the boundary while divergent boundaries where new crust is generated as the plates pull away from each other.

5. What is a seismograph and how it works?

an instrument that measures and records details of earthquakes, such as force and duration.
