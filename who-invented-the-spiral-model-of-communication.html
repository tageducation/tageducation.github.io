1. WHO INVENTED THE SPIRAL MODEL OF COMMUNICATION /


Answer:

The Spiral Model of Communication was originally proposed by Frank Dance, an American communication professor. It is also called as the Helical Model of Communication. It introduces a concept wherein communication is taken as a dynamic process and progresses with age as the communicator’s experience grow and vocabulary increase.

Disadvantages of Helical / Spiral Model of Communication Some critics of the model do not take it as a fit model because of its many variables Critics say that it is not represented in a systematic and orderly way Critics say that continuity is not always feasible because of possible breaks to the process

16. Dance’s Helical Spiral Model of Communication key features


Answer:

The Helical Model of Communication, or Dance’s Helix Model, is a communication model in which communication is explained by means of a helix. The Helical Model of Communication is both linear and circular, and stands in contrast to exclusively linear models, or models based on circularity, such as the Artistotle communication model, or Berlo’s SBSW model.

The creator of the model, Frank Dance, emphasised the role of communication problems. To this end, he used the shape of a helix. This shape describes the communication process, and visualises communication as a spiral-shaped model. This approach also strongly highlights the evolutionary aspect of the communication model.

WHO INVENTED THE SPIRAL MODEL OF COMMUNICATION?

Frank Dance is the one who invented the Spiral Model of Communication or also known as "Dance's Helix Model". For a better communication process, Frank Dance proposed the communication model known as Dance's Helix Model in 1967. The word "helical" comes from the word "helix," which refers to a three-dimensional item with a three-dimensional shape, such as a wire wound uniformly around a cylinder or cone. Frank Dance explains that communication is a non-linear and dynamic process.

The difficulties of communicating were stressed in Dance's model. The communication process is described by Frank Dance in the shape of a Helix. Frank Dance based this idea on a basic helix that expands and contracts as it travels or expands. The evolving nature of a helical communication model is one of its most distinguishing features.

29. Who is the one who invented the Bohr model?


Niels Bohr came up with the Bohr model.ANSWER: Neils Bohr

Niels Bohr (1885-1962) was a young Danish physicist and a student of Rutherford. He believed Rutherford's model needed improvement. So in 1913 Bohr changed Rutherford's model to include newer discoveries about how the energy of an atom changes when it absorbs or emits light. He considered the simplest atom, hydrogen, which has one electron. Bohr proposed that an electron is found only in specific circular paths, or orbits, around the nucleus.
