Sources of information can be categorized into three. These are primary sources, secondary sources, and tertiary sources. They are ranked based on the originality of the sources and the hierarchy of work.

1. Primary Sources

These sources are received from the primary origin. these are also not altered, or edited and are received as is. They present originality and fresh information or data. Below are examples of primary sources.

JournalNewspaper Photographs MemoirsDocuments

The reason why we consider them as primary sources is that the pieces of information we can get from them are direct. for example, a diary. Diary is written by the owner who also happens to be the primary source of information. It is from his or her own perspective and the info he or she is writing is directly from her mind based on experience.

2. Secondary Sources

This is information that is being interpreted from primary sources. They become secondary sources because they are no longer the original copy and concept of the context. It may also alter, edit, summarize, develop, or expand the idea of the primary source. Below are examples of secondary sources:

Textbook Magazine articles Histories ReviewsIncycloped

If we take the review as an example, we can conclude that reviews are based on primary sources such as vlogs or documentaries. That is the reason why reviews are categorized as a secondary source because they cannot be made possible without the primary source of information.

3. Tertiary source

These are textual consolidations of the already published work. Reference materials can also fall under this category.

Dictionary Almanac Handbook BibliographiesChronollogics

The reason why they are categorized as tertiary sources is that they are only organized information. They are created to compile pieces of information.
