17. A) A die is rolled once. Find the probability of obtaining:a 3.b. a number divisible ly 2​


Answer:

Ahoy there matey ready to sail far down the seas

Answer:

The probability is given by:

[tex]P(\text{Desired Outcome})=\frac{\text{Number of Desired Outcomes}}{\text{Number of Total Outcomes}}[/tex]

Consider a.3:

A die has six possible outcomes: 1,2,3,4,5 and 6

Since there is only one (1) possible outcome where the number rolled is 3, the probability for a. is:

[tex]P(\text{Number rolled is 3})=\frac{1}{6}[/tex]

Consider b. a number divisible by 2:

A die has six possible outcomes: 1,2,3,4,5 and 6

Take note that numbers divisible by 2 from the possible outcomes are: 2,4, and 6.

This means that there are 3 desired outcomes and the probability is:

[tex]P(\text{A number divisible by 2})=\frac{3}{6}=\frac{1}{2}[/tex]

The answers are: a.1/6 and b.1/2

20. a die is rolled once. find the probability of obtaining 7​


Answer:

For each of the possible outcomes add the numbers on the two dice and count how many times this sum is 7. If you do so you will find that the sum is 7 for 6 of the possible outcomes. Thus the sum is a 7 in 6 of the 36 outcomes and hence the probability of rolling a 7 is 6/36 = 1/6.
