Speech is the skill of conveying messages in public verbally with a specific purpose. Speech objectives is to influence others, provide an understanding, make others happy and satisfied with the words conveyed in an entertaining way. The types of speech are:

1. Informative Speech

Informative speech is a type of speech that is used when conveying important information or new knowledge. Information or knowledge given to the communicant (listener). With the objective to make the audience can understand and receive the information.

Informative speech consists into 3 kinds, namely:

Oral Reports (Oral Reports), for example scientific reports, committee reports, committee reports, annual reports, project reports, and othersOral instruction report (Teaching), for example when the teacher explains the lesson, the boss explains the work.Informative Lectures, for example during public lectures, presentations in front of conferences, presentations of papers and recitations.

2. Argumentative Speech

Argumentative speech is speech that contains arguments, arguments, or data to support or reject a particular statement, opinion or belief. This type of sound evidence uses factual, statistical data, and proves to strengthen the acceptability of the arguments put forward.

3. Persuasive Speeches

Persuasive speech is a type of speech that aims to influence humans. The process of persuasion that uses policies, so that affected people can be influenced.

4. Recreational Speech

Recreational speech is speech delivered with a sense of kinship and brotherhood.

The Delivery Method of Speech consists of:

1. Manuscript Method (Manuscript)

In this method, the written script is written in full in accordance with what will be conveyed. This method is used to avoid errors in conveying the message or material conveyed, so accuracy is needed. The script method is usually used in official talks about politics, announcements, technical reviews.

2. Memorization Method (Memorial)

This method is an advanced method of the script reading method. In this method, the text that has been prepared is not read but memorized first and then pronounced in the opportunity to make a speech. This method demands the memory of the speaker (communicator) in mastering the material to be delivered to the audience.

3. Spontaneity Method (Improptu)

The spontaneity method is different from the two previous methods. In this method, the speaker does not prepare the script, or does not read the script. The speaker is only an idea of what problem will be raised. His speech was completely unprepared, because he was forcibly appointed to deliver the message (speech) in public.

4. Method of Outline (Extemporary)

In this method, the speaker will be more flexible in conveying his ideas. The communicator (speaker) can prepare the material well in the form of a speech framework. In this method, speaking determines the main contents of the stomach and then arranges it in the form of a speech framework.

In addition, the speaker takes special notes. For example, verses, laws, data or numbers that are difficult to remember. At the time of the speech, the brain framework that has been prepared in advance is developed directly.
