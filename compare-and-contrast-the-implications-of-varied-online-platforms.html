What are the similarities between print, broadcast, and online media?

Original question: What are the similarities between print, broadcast, and online media?

The biggest similarity between the three different versions is that they all use the “inverted pyramid” style of news writing.

From the time of the invention of the printing press (and possibly before, but I don’t know of any records of what “town criers” might have said) up until roughly the Spanish-American War (April to August 1898) newspapers generally used a narrative style of writing the news. That style describes news events in the sequence that they took place. At the time, the US news reporters covering the action in Cuba were sending reports back using a not-entirely reliable undersea telegraph cable, which caused the risk of interruption in the middle of a transmission. What this meant was that the editors back in New York might get the first half of a description of a battle, but fail to get the all-important information about “Who won?”

The solution to the problem was to create the style still used to this day: the inverted pyramid. The most important facts are reported first, the second most important facts reported next, and so on until all the contents have been transmitted.

For those unfamiliar with the two styles, here’s a quick example: I’m going to describe a house fire using both methods.
