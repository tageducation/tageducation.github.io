1. the instrument that can produce sound through air​


Answer:

aerophone

An aerophone (/ˈɛəroʊfoʊn/) is a musical instrument that produces sound primarily by causing a body of air to vibrate, without the use of strings or membranes (which are respectively chordophones and membranophones), and without the vibration of the instrument itself adding considerably to the sound (or idiophones).

Answer:

aerophone

Explanation:

Aerophones are one of the four main classes of instruments in the original Hornbostel–Sachs system of musical instrument classification, which further classifies aerophones by whether or not the vibrating air is contained within the instrument.

According to Ardal Powell, the flute is a simple instrument found in numerous ancient cultures. There are three legendary and archeologically verifiable birthplace sites of flutes: Egypt, Greece and India. Of these, the transverse flute (side blown) appeared only in ancient India, while the fipple flutes are found in all three. It is likely, states Powell, that the modern Indian bansuri has not changed much since the early medieval era.

Occasionally called "percussive aerophones", plosive aerophones are sounded by percussion caused by a single compression and release of air.An example of a plosive aerophone is the "scraper flute" which has tubes with ridged or serrated edges so that they can be scraped with a rod to produce sound.

The tongali is a four holed nose flute (one hole in the back) from northern Philippines and played by the Kalinga and other peoples of Luzon. The tongali is one of the few nose flutes in the world that is still actively taught, thanks to the work of Jose Maceda at the University of the Philippines and the ongoing effects of the music department of UP Quezon. The tongali is one of numerous traditional instruments that students can study at UP. SOUND ENERGYAnswer:

1. What is a sound?

• Sound, just like heat and light, is also an energy which is produced by vibration.

2. How do we produce sounds?

• Sounds can be produced by physical or psychological process. In the physical process, sound can be produced by physical means – the things that we can touch and see. Examples are banging of drums, clapping of hands, blowing of trumpet and playing a guitar. In the psychological process, sound occurs inside the ears and brain that converts the sounds that enter and can be interpreted into speech, music or noise.

3. Does sound travel/propagate through a medium?

• Yes, sound travels through a medium. Unlike light that can travel through a vacuum, sound needs a medium to propagate. This is the reason why light is faster than sound – lightning can be perceived first than thunder although they are created at the same time.

4. Describe the transmission of sound through solids, liquids and gases.​

• Sound travels the fastest in solids since the molecules are very compact to each other while it travels the slowest in gas (air).
