5. What are the TWO KINDS OF TRANSMISSION?


Transmission

In medical terms, transmission is a process of passing diseases from the infected individual to another uninfected individual or group. There are two kinds of transmission. The direct and indirect transmission. Other types of viruses, bacteria, fungi and parasites can cause infectious diseases. Tuberculosis, influenza and common colds are some examples of infectious diseases. But it can prevent  via frequently hand washing with soap.  

Direct transmission

Sanitation and hygiene is important. There are 5 ways the infected can directly transfer the disease to another.  

Directly physical contact. It involves touching the body of infected Indirectly physical contact. Touching or using the contaminated things of the infected  Droplet contact. Infection can be transferred through droplets of saliva. Airborne transmission. Some microorganisms can remain in the air for a long period of time. It can infect if  inhaled. Fecal transmission. It is a must to wash hands after using the toilet. Unwashed hands can contaminate the food and water.

Indirect transmission

Indirect transmission of disease is more widely spread. There is no person to person contact involved. Usually the carrier of  this are the organisms like tapeworm and insects like mosquitoes. Disease can be transferred if humans ingest improper cooking of food and/or bitten by a mosquito.

Ways to protect yourself from disease   Get vaccinated for immunization Frequently wash your hands with soap Prepare and cook the food safely Clean and disinfect your areas Cover your mouth before sneeze and cough

Answer:

Six factors are involved in the transmission of communicable diseases: the infectious agent, the reservoir, route of exit, mode of transmission, route of entry, and the susceptible host. A reservoir is a human, another animal, or a non-living thing (such as soil), where the infectious agent normally lives.

23. what kind of oil use for automatic transmission fluid​


Answer:

Commonly recommended manual transmission fluid formulations include SAE 80W, 75W-90, 80W-90 and SAE 90. In some cases, a multi-grade engine oil or automatic transmission fluid may be recommended. However, a manual transmission fluid is typically the most optimal for manual transmissions.

The vast majority of passenger car and light truck transmissions operate automatically, that is without the need to manually shift gears. Automatic transmission fluid is a critical component of any automatic transmission. Also referred to as ATF, this fluid ensures proper operation, performance and protection of the automatic transmission. Critical areas of performance include the following:

Heat resistance and thermal stability, helps prevent deposit and sludge from forming as the result of the high temperature operation of the transmission

Frictional Characteristics enables smooth gear changes by supplying the correct friction to the transmission clutches and bands

Extreme pressure/load carrying performance ensures anti-wear and gear durability

Viscosity stability helps maintain viscosity or proper fluid thickness over a wide temperature range

Low temperature flow ensures effective operation of hydraulics and electronics controls at low temperature
