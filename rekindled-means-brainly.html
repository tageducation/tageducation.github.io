rekindle

Use the verb rekindle when you're trying to get something started again, like a fire or a romance.

Definitions of rekindle

verb (used with object), re·kin·dled, re·kin·dling.

to excite, stir up, or rouse anew:efforts to rekindle their romance;comments that rekindled her anger.

    2. to cause to begin burning again; ignite again:

Never use gasoline to rekindle a fire.

verb (used without object), re·kin·dled, re·kin·dling.

    3. to begin to burn again; ignite again:

She is worried about a fire rekindling.

22. Give an essay about the theme"Rediscover, Rekindle and Relive English through communication"150 words up​


Answer:

English is a language that connects people from all over the world. It is the language of international communication, business, and education. However, as time goes by, some people may begin to lose touch with their English language skills. This is where the theme of "rediscover, rekindle and relive English through communication" comes in.

To rediscover English, individuals should seek out new ways to engage with the language. This could include reading English books, watching English movies or TV shows, or even taking an English course. By exposing oneself to different forms of English, people can learn new words, grammar, and sentence structures.

Once individuals have rediscovered their love for the language, they can then begin to rekindle their skills. This can be done through practice and repetition. By speaking, writing, and listening to English on a regular basis, people can improve their ability to communicate effectively in the language.

Finally, by reliving English through communication, individuals can not only improve their language skills but also connect with others who speak English. This can be done by joining an English-speaking club, finding a language exchange partner, or even communicating with native English speakers online.

In conclusion, by rediscovering, rekindling, and reliving English through communication, people can not only improve their language skills but also connect with others from around the world. It's a great way to expand our personal and professional horizons.


23. Therefore, the molds had to be once more prepared, and the fires (7) rekindled, and the metal remelted, and all the work tediously and (8) toilsomely repeated. (1) rekindled means _____ lighted again set on fire again stopped the fire (2) toilsomely means _____ with difficulty lightly unmindfully


Answer:

The answers are: rekindled - lighted again / toilsome - with difficulty

Explanation:

The word rekindled has the prefix 're' which means 'again' or 'to repeat'. The root word of rekindled is kindle which means 'to start (a fire) burning', so in this paragraph, the word rekindled simply mean 'lighted again'

While the toilsomely repeated means doing again the work with difficulty. Toilsomely is an adverb in which its root word is toilsome which simply mean 'marked by' or 'full of toil or fatigue'
