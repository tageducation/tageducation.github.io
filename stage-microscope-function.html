27. what is function of 1.fine adjustment knob2.nose piece3.stage4.diaphragm5.mirror6.eye piece7.stage clips8.course adjustment knobit is parts of microscope​


1. Fine Adjustment Knob

Function: This knob is inside the coarse adjustment knob and is used to bring the specimen into sharp focus under low power and is used for all focusing when using high power lenses.

2. Nose Piece

Function: A rotating turret that houses the objective lenses. The viewer spins the nosepiece to select different objective lenses. Objective lenses: One of the most important parts of a compound microscope, as they are the lenses closest to the specimen.

3. Stage

Function: Stages are often equipped with a mechanical device that holds the specimen slide in place and can smoothly translate the slide back and forth as well as from side to side.

4. Diaphragm

Function: This diaphragm has different sized holes and is used to vary the intensity and size of the cone of light that is projected upward into the slide. There is no set rule regarding which setting to use for a particular power.

5. Mirror

Function: Mirrors are sometimes used in lieu of a built-in light. If your microscope has a mirror, it is used to reflect light from an external light source up through the bottom of the stage.

6. Eye Piece

Function: The lens the viewer looks through to see the specimen. The eyepiece usually contains a 10X or 15X power lens. Diopter Adjustment: Useful as a means to change focus on one eyepiece so as to correct for any difference in vision between your two eyes.

7. Stage Clips

Function: Stage clips hold the slides in place. If your microscope has a mechanical stage, the slide is controlled by turning two knobs instead of having to move it manually. One knob moves the slide left and right, the other moves it forward and backward.

8. Course Adjustment Knob

Function: A rapid control which allows for quick focusing by moving the objective lens or stage up and down. It is used for initial focusing.
