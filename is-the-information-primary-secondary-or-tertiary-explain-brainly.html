1. Primary source of information

Source of information that is original and written in full. Usually a kind of book that is in the library, but it could also be in other sources. The status of an information center can dominantly determine the types of information it manages.

For example, the university library develops more information in the form of text books and journals, while the public library presents more general science books. Other examples include monographs, magazine articles, research results, and direct reports which are the results of interviews with someone, theses, theses, etc.

2. Secondary sources of information

A collection of information in the form of bibliographic data quoted from primary information sources and used to facilitate retrieval and search of primary information which is usually in the form of magazines or books. Examples of secondary sources of information are encyclopedias, dictionaries, bibliographies, index collections, abstracts, biographical sources and library catalogs.

3. tertiary sources of information

A collection of information that facilitates the search for secondary information, the tertiary information source is a summary of secondary information sources. An example is the abstract index which is a collection of abstracts and makes it easier for us to do abstract searches. Another example is in bibliographic lists published in magazine form.
