State of the Nation Address is a tradition for the Philippine presidents to give addresses every year. It is done to show the citizens the plans, actions, and objectives of the current government.

Here are the answers to your activity:

The information provided by the text above is the continuous growth of illegal crimes happening amidst the pandemic. Criminals are taking advantage of the situation and continue to do their operations despite the crisis and present situation of the country. The message also emphasizes the abnormal overpricing of the companies because they know the demand for their products.

The state of the Nation Address is an obligation of the president that is done every year. The main purpose of this tradition is to report and inform the public about the current economical situation of the country. It is also the chance for the president to showcase the plans, actions, and visions for the country in the present administration.

It should be the president of the Philippines who is tasked to deliver the State of the Nation Address. He is not allowed to pass the obligation to his spokesperson or any officer of the country.

As per the 1987 constitution, the State of the Nation Address should be done every fourth Monday of July. The speech should also be delivered at the Plenary Session Hall of the Batasang Pambansa Complex in Batasan Hills, Quezon City.
Here are the problems mentioned in the State of the Nation Address:
1. The lack of cooperation from the public. While the government is focusing on solutions to fight Covid-19, criminals are also seeing this as an opportunity to expand their illegal activities.

2. The amount of drugs seized during operations is very alarming.

3. The corrupt, grafters, overpricers, and profiteers are taking advantage of the situation which they see as an opportunity to victimize their fellow Filipinos.


The solutions were not clearly presented but the message emphasizes that the law would be the one responsible to punish these evil-doers. It is also mentioned that significant strides are being worked on over time.

The State of the Nation Address (SONA) is a constitutional requirement and annual tradition in which the chief executive reports on the nation's status, reveals the government's strategy for the upcoming year, and proposes specific legislative measures to Congress.

3. By mandate, The President of the Philippines is tasked by the constitution to deliver a SONA. The address of President Aquino III will be the 77th since 1935 and the 29th since the restoration of democratic rule under the Fifth Republic in 1987.

4. SONA should be delivered every fourth Monday of July at the Plenary Session Hall of the Batasang Pambansa Complex in Batasan Hills, Quezon City, Metro Manila (Mandated by the 1987 Constitution).

- Illegal Prescription
