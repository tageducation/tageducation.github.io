4. Over all sheen of minerals


Answer:

Lustre (British English) or luster (American English; see spelling differences) is the way light interacts with the surface of a crystal, rock, or mineral. The word traces its origins back to the Latin lux, meaning "light", and generally implies radiance, gloss, or brilliance.

A range of terms are used to describe lustre, such as earthy, metallic, greasy, and silky. Similarly, the term vitreous (derived from the Latin for glass, vitrum) refers to a glassy lustre.

Explanation:

Luster: A mineral’s luster is the overall sheen of its surface – it may have the sheen of polished metal, or that of an unpolished metal that is pitted by weathering – or it may have the sheen of glass, or look dull or earthy, etc. Luster should not be confused with color: A brass-yellow pyrite crystal has a metallic luster, but so does a shiny grey galena crystal . Quartz is said to have a glassy (or vitreous) luster, but its color may be purple, rose, yellow, or any of a wide range of hues

11. The overall shape or growth pattern of the mineral is?​


Answer:

Crystal Shape

Explanation:

Many minerals in an introductory geology lab do not exhibit their crystal form. If a mineral has space while it grows, it may form natural crystals, with a crystal shape reflecting the geometry of the mineral's internal crystal lattice. The shape of a crystal follows the symmetry of its crystal lattice.

30. The overall shape or growth pattern of the mineral determines its________​


Answer:

Crystal Habit

Explanation:

In mineralogy, crystal habit refers to the characteristic external shape of crystals. A single crystal's habit is the description of its general shape and its crystallographic forms. Habit is used as a method to identify a mineral as some of these patterns are characteristics to a specific mineral.\

The following are examples of crystal habits:

Acicular - needle-like and slender crystalsBladed - the crystals would look like blades as they are flat and slender\Cubic - cube shaped crystals, as in the ones seen in saltDodecahedral - twelve-sided crystalsFibrous - crystals formed are hair-like projectionsOctahedral - eight-sided crystals

Some crystals habits make them ideal for jewelry, such as the dodecahedral crystals of garnet and the octahedral crystals in diamonds. The slender structure in some crystals make them capable of damaging cells when inhaled such as the fibrous crystals in asbestos.
