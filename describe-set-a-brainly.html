8. Questions:1.What are real numbers?2. Rational and irrational numbers are disjoint sets, what does it mean?3.Rational numbers has 4 subsets, describe their relationship.Needs help just now!​Brainly makakasagot,po!.


Answer:

1. Real numbers can be defined as the union of both rational and irrational numbers. They can be both positive or negative and are denoted by the symbol “R”. All the natural numbers, decimals and fractions come under this category.

2. They do not overlap.

3.

a. The set of natural numbers N = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, . . .}

b. The set of integers Z = {. . ., –3, –2, –1, 0, 1, 2, 3, . . .}

c. The set of rational numbers Q = {x: x = a/b; a, b ∈ Z and b ≠ 0}

d. The set of irrational numbers, denoted by T, is composed of all other real numbers.

Thus, T = {x : x ∈ R and x ∉ Q}, i.e., all real numbers that are not rational.
