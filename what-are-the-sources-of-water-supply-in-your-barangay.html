TYPES OF WATER RESOURCES

Water is considered to be one of the necessities for any living thing to survive.

Water is accessible in any part of the Earth, as per a study that the Earth keeps 96% of its water in different parts such as the ocean, lakes, rivers,s, etc.

Getting proper accessibility to anyone to have safe drinking water to live in is a great help for anyone.

Water from a sink has several places and stages that go into before it even reaches the place where everyone can get a glass and drink.

Here are the Different Sources of Water:

Groundwater: goes into the little cracks in bedrock and beneath the surface is sand that creates contaminants tedious to filter out in big amounts. These sources also saturate the soil and contain so much sediment that the water must undergo a thorough filtration process to even become drinking.Storm Water: also known as rainfall water, this is the type of water that came from heavy storms, snow, and even hail.Salt Water Resources are commonly found in the big body of water which is the oceans. The salt content is a high amount that a human is not possible to consume.Surface Water- these are the fresh waters that can be found on the Earth’s surface and are accessible for anyone to use either for drinking or in everyday living. It can be found in rivers, lakes, streams, etc.

        These are also waters from natural resources of the environment, it              can be from top mountains in a form of Falls. It is also from melted snow blocks that floes into the streams and continuously flow to the ocean.

As being one of the necessities, Water also differs based on the way it is being used, it can be Domestic or Industrial types.

INDUSTRIAL PURPOSES- water is used for cooling and turning on the power generation. It also serves as an input for common manufacturing industries that creates food processing products, manufacturers of cola, chemicals, and textiles.

DOMESTIC PURPOSES-is are common for household chores such as washing, cleaning, drinking, and bathing.

. What are the sources of water as shown on the picture?

2. Name some of the places where water is used.

3. How is water used in these places?

Answer: Water can be used for direct and indirect purposes.
