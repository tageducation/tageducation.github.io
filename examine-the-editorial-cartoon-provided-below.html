COMPOUND SENTENCES

A compound sentence is one that joins two separate phrases with a conjunction, most frequently and or but. They perform nicely when integrating two or more distinct but connected sentences within one.

COMPLEX SENTENCE

One independent clause and at minimum one dependent clause make up a complex sentence. It performs admirably when you wish to expand on or change the core idea of any statement.

COMPOUND SENTENCE

1. The DepEd are working hard, but the budget and salaries are very low.

2. Students wants to finish until Senior High, yet they don't have enough budget for supplies.

3. The schools wanted to host an event, but it ended up having no funds for monthly dues and taxes.

4. The teachers wants to offer an online class; however, the budget is too short.

5. The National Budget has many debt and now, the employees are suffering the losses and cuttings.

COMPLEX SENTENCE

1. Although the budget is very low, the Teachers still works hard for a living.

2. The school is not having enough budget to repair classrooms, because the National had not released it to them.

3. Many workers have received their advanced budgetary; however, the DepEd did not.

4. Although the school is going great, the budget and funds are not enough to join any events outside the city.

5. They want to ask the government for budget, but they know that there are cuttings on budget.
