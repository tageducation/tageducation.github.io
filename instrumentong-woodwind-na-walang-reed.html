Answer:

Answer:

The clarinet uses a single reed made of one piece of wood, while the oboe and bassoon use a double reed made of two pieces joined together. Just as with the stringed instruments, the smaller woodwinds play higher pitches while the longer and larger instruments play the lower notes.

Answer:

The woodwind family of instruments includes, from the highest sounding instruments to the lowest, the piccolo, flute, oboe, English horn, clarinet, E-flat clarinet, bass clarinet, bassoon and contrabassoon.

Answer:

Ang mga instrumentong yari sa ihipang maqqqninipis na kawayan na tunatawag na reed ay kinabibilangan ng mga sumusunod:

1. Kudyapi - is a traditional two-stringed boat lute from the Philippines, made out of bamboo.

2. Bamboo flute - is a wind instrument made of bamboo and is a common instrument in traditional Philippine music.

3. Tongatong - is a traditional percussion instrument made of bamboo that originates from the Philippines

4. Kulingtang - is a set of bamboo tubes of different sizes and lengths, played by striking them with small sticks.

5. Suling - is a bamboo flute traditionally played in Southeast Asia, including the Philippines.

6. Salimbal - is a bamboo zither from the Philippines.
