18. what is an earthquake and it's different parts​


Answer:

There are three main parts to an earthquake: the focus, or origin of the event; the seismic waves; and the fault along which the earthquake occurs.

Answer:

→What is an Earthquake?

An earthquake is defined as sudden and violent shaking of the ground that is caused by movement within the earth's crust. The movement is caused by the release of energy within the earth, usually situated around a fault, or crack in the earth along which movement can occur.

The movement within the earth is caused by mechanical waves, or the propagation of mechanical energy through the layers of rock. This energy radiates out from the source in all directions. Scientists can measure the behavior of these waves by using delicate instruments called seismographs. Seismographs record the motion of the earthquake, or seismic, waves when they arrive at the location of the instrument.

→Parts of an Earthquake There are three main parts to an earthquake: the focus, or origin of the event; the seismic waves; and the fault along which the earthquake occurs.
