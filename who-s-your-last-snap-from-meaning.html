Here are Literary Approaches

Historical-Biological

Literary criticism that takes into account the author's historical setting is known as historical-biographical criticism. This method is predicated on the idea that a literary work's historical context is intrinsically tied to its relevance. For instance, historical-biographical critics consider Shakespeare's works in relation to the literature, history, and culture the late sixteenth and early seventeenth centuries.

Moral-Philosophical

Criticism that is morally and philosophically oriented looks at literature from an ethical perspective. Moral-philosophical critics assess a work of literature based on the moral assertions and conclusions the author and characters make throughout the narrative.

Sociological

Literature is judged according to how it relates to society in sociological critique. The sociological critique method looks at both the author's place in society and the impact the literary work had on its target audience inside that society. Marxist criticism is one type of sociological criticism that looks at whether a piece of literature supports or opposes oppression based on class.

Reader response

Reader-response criticism is founded on the notion that a reader's response to or interpretation of a text can be just as valuable as the text itself as a source for critical analysis.

Psychoanalytic Theory

Literature is examined using the psychological aspirations and phobias of the characters in a particular work of literature in psychoanalytic critique. Critics of psychoanalysis contend that a writer's unconscious thoughts are expressed in their writing.

Explanation of Incomplete Dominance

Incomplete dominance is an intermediate form of inheritance in which one allele for a particular trait is not fully expressed over its paired alleles. This generates a third phenotype in which the physical properties expressed are a combination of the phenotypes of both alleles. Unlike the inheritance of complete domination, one allele does not predominate or cover the other.

Explanation of Punnet Square

Punnett square is a square diagram that is used to predict the genotype of a particular crossing or breeding experiment. It is named after Reginald C. Punnett, who designed the approach. The diagrams are used by biologists to determine the probability of an offspring having a certain genotype. Punnett's Square is made from simple square which is divided into grids of 2x2 (or more)
