6. Methods of testing the quality and freshness of an egg​.


Answer:

How to Test the Freshness of Eggs

Place the egg in a bowl of water.

If the egg lays on its side at the bottom, it is still quite fresh.

If the egg stands upright on the bottom, it is still fine to eat, but should be eaten very soon, or hard-boiled.

If the egg floats to the top, it’s past its prime, and not good for eating.

Explanation:

Why this method is accurate

Eggshells are very porous. Over time air passes through the shell into the egg, and its shelf life diminishes as more air enters the shell. Also, the more air that enters the shell, the more buoyant the egg becomes.

13. what are the characteristics of quality fresh eggs?    


Characteristics of Quality Fresh Eggs

ColorWhen looking for quality fresh eggs, it is important to take a look at the color. The color it has should be dark and bright, not dull and cloudy. There should be no black spots that can be seen around the shell.Under the LightWhen checking the color of the egg under bright light, it should be a bright color and not cloudy. The position of the egg yolk should be in the middle, which means that the egg white it has is still thick. Quality fresh egg also has a smaller yolk compared to the old ones.ShellQuality fresh eggs should be in perfect condition and shape, there shouldn’t be any broken shells to be spotted.SmellThe egg should not be foul-smelling. A quality fresh egg gives off a neutral aroma, if not, there is a big chance that the egg is rotten.Inspect the Egg by Using the Floating MethodIt is stated that fresh eggs are heavier. 24. Steps on how to maintain fresh egg quality?


Explanation:

Keep nest clean. Maintaining clean nesting material will reduce microbial exposure when the egg is first laid. Replace nesting material as needed. Clean nesting material will also encourage the hen to use the nest rather than laying the egg on dirt or in weeds.

Collect eggs frequently. Eggs should be collected at least daily and preferably twice a day to prevent breakage and possible contamination from faecal material and dirt. The longer eggs are left in the hen house or pen, the more likely they are to be broken and exposed to bacteria.after collection can also improve egg quality if done properly. Water temperature for washing is very important. Eggs should be washed in water that is warmer than the eggs. Warm eggs washed in cool water will contract and draw bacteria into the egg. Temperatures ranging from 100 to 120°F are recommended. Wash water can become contaminated if used too long, therefore, the wash water should be changed as dirt and fecal material build up in the solution.

Egg drying. Once the eggs are washed they should be dried as soon as possible. Moisture provides a favorable environment for bacterial growth and a method of microbial entrance through the egg shell.

Use clean packaging materials. If egg flats or cartons are used for storage, these materials should be clean and free of contaminated matter such as egg yolk and albumen.

Storage conditions. After washing and drying, eggs should be stored within appropriate temperature and humidity conditions. The appropriate temperature is 45-55°F and relative humidity between 60-80%.
