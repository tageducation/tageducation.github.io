14. the following are representative particles of matter which among them represents a covalent compound


Answer:

A molecule is a representative particle of diatomic element. representative particle of covalent compound. A formula unit is a representative particle of ionic compound

What represents a covalent compound?

A covalent compound is made when two or more nonmetal atoms bond by sharing valence electrons. The shared valence electrons between two nonmetal atoms is called a covalent bond. Covalent bonds are formed when two atoms begin sharing electrons. The electrons are attracted to the positively charged nuclei of the atoms.

The types of representative particles that chemists generally work with are:

atoms – the smallest particle of an element.

ions – atoms with positive or negative charges.

molecules – two or more covalently bonded atoms.

formula units – the simplest ratio of ions that make up an ionic compound.

16. which of the following choices is not a representative particle?A.moleculeB.electronC.anionD.atom​


Based on the option gives, the correct answer is: C. ANION.

The representative particle is any kind of particle such as atoms, electrons, ions, molecules and formula units.The anion is NOT a representative particle.

FURTHER EXPLANATION:

Anions are negatively charged ions. Therefore, a cation molecule has a hydrogen proton without an electron, while an anion has an extra electron. Because of their opposite electrical charges, cations and anions are attracted to each other and readily form ionic compounds. Electrons, because of their small mass so that they occupy a large space, are considered as matter waves. It determines the entire size of the atoms and molecules that have electrons. Anions are larger than their parent molecules or atoms, because excess electrons repel each other thereby increasing the physical size of the ion, and its size is determined by its electron cloud.

Thus, in general, cations are smaller than their parent atoms or molecules because their electron cloud size is also smaller. The hydrogen cation has no electrons at all, so it only has a single proton, much smaller than a hydrogen atom. most anions are large, such as the earth's most common anion, oxygen. Based on this fact, it appears that most of the space in the crystal is occupied by anions and that cations are contained in the spaces between them.
