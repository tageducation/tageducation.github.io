1) Instead of demonstrating compassion, many individuals make fun of those with impairments.

It is vital that we educate those who make fun of persons with disabilities about why we should be understanding of them — namely, since they did not choose to have disabilities. As Martin Buber stated, it is essential that we embrace and respect them as fellow humans. Set a good example.

2) Regarding the rape problem, women are blamed for the clothing they wear.

Women are not things. Women are human beings just like males. We should view women as human beings, and it is not their fault that others objectify them based on the clothing they wear.

3) Many assert that the attitude of the impoverished causes their poverty.

Poor individuals are poor not because of their attitude, but because some are not afforded the same opportunities/head start in life as others born into wealthy homes. As stated by Karol Wojtyla, it is essential that we are able to share ourselves with others. Put oneself in the shoes of others in order to comprehend their perspective.

4) Indigenous peoples have a culture that others can not comprehend, and some individuals make fun of them.

The fact that other people have different culture, beliefs, and traditions does not make them lesser to you; therefore, no one has the right to mock them. According to Confucius, we should create harmony and love in this world. Call out those who make fun of other people's culture and assist them in imagining what they would do if they were the target of such ridicule.

5) Some people today believe that psychological issues are merely excuses people use to justify their weaknesses.

Psychological issues are equally as real as physical issues. Why would there be a need for psychiatrists if they are simply doctors? Consequently, the best solution is to educate people about psychological problems so that we have a better understanding of it and treat people with psychological problems as humans deserving of acceptance and sincerity.
