Definition of Literature

In general, the definition of literature is all written works that can be used as references or references in various activities in the field of education and other fields because they are considered to have lasting advantages or benefits.

Another opinion says that the meaning of literature is all sources of information that can be used as a reference by its users. In other words, literature does not have to be in the form of writing, but can also be in the form of films, recordings, LPs, laser discs, and other objects that can provide useful information.

Literature function

From the explanation of the definition, the following are some of the functions of the literature:

To assist users in finding the information they need. To strengthen the information generated from an analysis or hypothesis. To complete an information or additional information.

Types of Literature

In general, literature can be divided into three types. Referring to the definition of literature, there are several types of literature as follows:

Types of Literature Based on the Sharpness of the Analysis

1. Primary Literature

Primary literature is a type of literature that is created from the results of research where the contents have not been published by others before. This type of literature generally contains new ideas or theories in various disciplines.

Some examples of primary literature; theses, dissertations, papers, journals, patents, research reports, interviews, and others.

2. Secondary Literature

The definition of secondary literature is a type of literature whose contents are made using references from primary literature. Usually in this type of literature there are theories or ideas that have existed before and tend not to explain new findings.

Some of which include secondary literature; indexes, abstracts, encyclopedias, magazines, newspapers, manuals, and others.
