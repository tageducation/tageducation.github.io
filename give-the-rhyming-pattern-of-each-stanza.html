QUESTION:

give the rhyming pattern of each stanza.

ANSWER:

The patterns are encoded by letters of the alphabet. Lines designated with the same letter rhyme with each other. For example, the rhyme scheme ABAB means the first and third lines of a stanza, or the “A”s, rhyme with each other, and the second line rhymes with the fourth line, or the “B”s rhyme together.

9. What is the relationship between the stanzas and the rhyme scheme? How does the rhyming pattern connect and separate the sta


Answer in explanation:

___________1.What is the relationship between the stanzas and the rhyme scheme?

In order to answer both these questions we need to know what a 'stanza' and 'rhyme scheme' are;

The basic definition for a stanza is a group-- usually of two or more lines-- that forms a basic *reoccurring* pattern of rhyme and metrical scheme.

As for rhyme scheme: rhyme scheme commonly defines the pattern of rhyme [s] (recurring pattern of sound) at the end of a verse or stanza.

Rhyme schemes have the ability to change from line to line or from stanza to stanza. This means that a rhyme scheme often completes or sets a pattern for a stanza to follow.

'abab' is an excellent example of a relationship between rhyming scheme and stanza.

2.How does the rhyming pattern connect and separate the stanza?


Knowing that a rhyming pattern can vary from line to line, we understand that a rhyming pattern both separates and connect the stanza by changing or keeping the same pattern of sound. Depending on the frequency or if the rhyming pattern changes at all.
