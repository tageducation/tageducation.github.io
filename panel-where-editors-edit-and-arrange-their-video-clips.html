This is the panel where editors edit and arrange their video clips?

The Viewer panel, The preview window, also known as the preview window, is a standard feature in all video editing applications that helps you to see the video you're creating. The viewer panel can be used to preview how the numerous pieces of media you've inserted will appear in your final video, helping you to decide what functions and what needs to be modified.

The Viewer panel is a portion of the interface that cannot be omitted or obscured while customizing the workspace. The Viewer Panel includes the video preview - the player from which you can see the video you're working on You'll be able to see the effects of any adjustments you make to the timeline here. The options used in this panel are determined by the timeline settings you've chosen.Playback Controls

Loop Playback

This choice would cause your video to restart once the playhead hits the end of the timeline or the out point that you have specified. Loop Replay should be used in combination with the Set in Point and Set Out Point functions to loop a particular segment of your recording.Set in Point and Set Out Point You will determine the segment of your video to play by setting In and Out points. This functionality can also be used to export only a component of a project.

Playhead Control

The timeline's playhead helps you to manually jump to every frame in your recording. In addition to shifting the playhead around in your timeline, you can also alter the location of the playhead from the Viewer screen.

Viewer Playhead and the Duration Bar

The Period Bar displays the approximate duration of the project on the timeline. The work area is outlined in a light grey colour, and you can quickly adjust the range of your work area by using in and out points.

You can move the playhead on the timeline by moving the white vertical bar on the period bar left or right.
