Answer:

A typhoon is a type of tropical cyclone, which is a low-pressure weather system that typically forms over the ocean in the region of the tropics. Strong winds, heavy rainfall, and high waves characterize hurricanes. They usually develop from tropical disturbances or depressions that gain strength and organization as they move over warm, moist air near the ocean surface. Typhoons can cause significant damage and loss of life when they land due to the strong winds and heavy rainfall they bring.

A typhoon is a big storm that forms over the ocean near the equator. It has strong winds that can blow really hard, and it also brings a lot of rain. Sometimes a typhoon can be so strong that it can cause a lot of damage to buildings and trees when it hits land. It's important to be careful and stay safe during a typhoon.
