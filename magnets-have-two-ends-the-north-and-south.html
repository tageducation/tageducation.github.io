1. Magnets have two ends the north and south


Answer:

Yes

Explanation:

The magnetic field of a magnet is the area around it in which objects that are metal are affected. The area of the magnetic field where the magnet's power is the strongest is called the magnetic poles. If a magnet is hung so that it is free to turn, it will turn in the north-south direction. The end that faces the north is called the north-seeking pole, or north pole, of the magnet. The other end is called the south pole. When two magnets are brought together, the opposite poles will attract one another, but the like poles will repel one another. This is similar to electric charges. Like charges repel, and unlike charges attract. Since a free hanging magnet will always face north, magnets have long been used for finding direction. Thousands of years ago Chinese sailors used a magnetized needle floating in water to tell direction. This made a simple kind of compass. Columbus as well as other explorers also used magnetic needles as a compass to help them across the Atlantic Ocean.
