The color spectrum is part of visible light. Visible light is the electromagnetic spectrum that can be observed directly by the eye, it can also be referred to as white light. Visible light is made up of seven colors consists red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, and violet (ROY G. BIV). This was first stated by Sir Issac Newton in his book "Optics" in 1704.

According to Newton's theory of color spectrum, the source of color is light. He proved this with a study. Newton conducted an experiment, wherein dark room sunlight was shone through a small hole which then hit a prism and reflected colored light like a rainbow.

The wavelength of color that can be seen by the human eye is from 400-700 nanometers which are called visible light. Beyond the 400-700 nanometer range, the human eye is incapable of seeing it; For example, infrared light with a wavelength range from 700 nanometers to 1 millimeter.

The order of the electromagnetic spectrum from the smallest to the largest frequency in visible light measured in nanometers (nm) is as follows
