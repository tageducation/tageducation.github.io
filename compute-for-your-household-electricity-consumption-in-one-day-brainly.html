19. How is the cost of electrical energy consumption computed?


Answer:

Electricity is measured in kilowatt-hours on your bill, not watt-hours. One kilowatt is equal to 1,000 watts, so to calculate how many kWh a device uses, divide the watt-hours from the previous step by 1,000. Using our previous example, this means you would divide 375 watt-hours by 1,000, resulting in 0.375 kWh.

Answer:

The cost of electrical energy consumption is computed by using the following formula

Explanation:

To calculate the energy consumption and associated costs, you take the total number of hours you use a device, then multiply it with the wattage usage of that device per hour, and divide it by 1000, or  

(Operating Hours  x  Watt Usage per hour ) ÷ 1000 x  Electricity Costs per kWh = Electrical energy consumption cost  

                           

Dividing it by 1000 changes it from watt-hours to kilowatt-hours. The unit for energy consumption is kilowatt-hour or kWh.  

For example:  

We want to compute for the electrical energy consumption of a light bulb if the electricity cost is Php 10 per kWh.

Operating Hours  x  Watt Usage per hour) ÷ 1000 x Electricity Costs per hour= (100 watts x 730 hours) ÷1000 x  Php 10  

= Php73.0

Answer:

Ohms Law and Power

1. To find the Voltage, ( V ) [ V = I x R ] V (volts) = I (amps) x R (Ω)

2.To find the Current, ( I ) [ I = V ÷ R ] I (amps) = V (volts) ÷ R (Ω)

3. To find the Resistance, ( R ) [ R = V ÷ I ] R (Ω) = V (volts) ÷ I (amps)

4. To find the Power (P) [ P = V x I ] P (watts) = V (volts) x I (amps)

23. what do you call a device that measures the consumption of electricity used by your household as it passes into your home?​


Answer:

The device that measures the consumption of electricity used by your household as it passes into your home is called an "electricity meter" or "electric meter." Electric meters are typically installed by the utility company and are used to measure the amount of electricity used by a household or building for billing purposes. The meter usually displays the amount of electricity used in kilowatt-hours (kWh), which is the unit of energy used by utility companies to measure electricity consumption. Some modern electric meters may also have additional features, such as the ability to transmit usage data wirelessly to the utility company.
