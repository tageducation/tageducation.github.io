6. What were the basis of the introductory paragraph of the gospel of Luke?​


The basis of the introductory paragraph of the Gospel of Luke is the author's intention to provide an accurate and orderly account of the life and teachings of Jesus Christ. In this paragraph, the author, Luke, states that he has carefully researched the events that he is writing about and that he is presenting them in an orderly manner.

The introductory paragraph of the Gospel of Luke begins with a dedication to Theophilus, a person for whom the gospel is written. In this dedication, Luke states that he is writing to Theophilus so that he may have certainty about the things that he has been taught. This suggests that Theophilus is a believer in Jesus and that Luke is writing the gospel to provide further confirmation of the truths that he has already learned.

The rest of the introductory paragraph of the Gospel of Luke sets the stage for the main body of the gospel by describing the historical context in which Jesus lived and the events that led up to his ministry. Luke mentions the work of John the Baptist, who prepared the way for Jesus, and he describes the circumstances of Jesus' birth and the reactions of various people, such as the shepherds and the wise men. Overall, the introductory paragraph of the Gospel of Luke serves to provide a foundation for the rest of the gospel and to establish the author's credibility and reliability as a source of information about Jesus.
