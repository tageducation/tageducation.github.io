20. This are sacred signs and symbols, the language of our faith thatexpresses our awareness and experiences of God's grace.​


Answer:

Sacraments

Explanation:

The Catechism of the Catholic Church defines sacraments as "outward signs instituted by Christ, in order to give grace."

There are actually seven sacraments, and they are as follows:

1. Baptism - through baptism, we are being adopted or admitted into the Christian family. We become God's children and heirs of his kingdom.

2. Eucharist - through this sacrament, we receive Christ's body and blood through the bread and wine in the Eucharistic celebration. The Eucharistic Celebration reminds us of Christ's last supper, his mission, and his sacrifice on the cross.

3. Confirmation - through this sacrament we receive the gift of the holy spirit and we become soldiers of Christ, and defenders of our faith.

These first three sacraments are also called the Sacrament of Initiation.

4. Anointing of the sick - this sacrament is usually performed by the pouring of or anointing of oil on the forehead and on other parts of the body of a sick person. Its purpose is not only for bodily healing, but also for the forgiveness of sins.

5. Sacrament of penance - also called sacrament of reconciliation or confession. Through this sacrament, Christians confess their sins to God through the person of the priest. The priest listens to their confessions, absolves them of their sins, and gives them a form of penance.

The anointing of the sick and the sacrament of penance are called the sacraments of healing.

6. Matrimony - It is through this sacrament that the union of two people is being sanctioned and blessed by God. The goal of marriage in a Christian framework is primarily for reproduction.

7. Holy Orders - Also called the Sacrament of Ordination is specifically intended for people who vow to serve and offer their lives to God. This is the path to which the priests and the nuns have taken. They will live their lives serving God.
