Compression Stroke

Q19.What is the function of the spark plug?

Your spark plugs are what supply the spark that ignites the air/fuel mixture, creating the explosion which makes your engine produce power. These small but simple plugs create an arc of electricity across two leads which are not touching, but close enough together that electricity can jump the gap between them.

Q20. Describe the piston and the gases during power stroke

The high pressure of the gases acting on the face of the piston cause the piston to move to the left which initiates the power stroke. ... During the power stroke, the volume occupied by the gases is increased because of the piston motion and no heat is transferred to the fuel/air mixture.

Q21. In exhaust stroke, what happens to the piston and the mixture of gases ?

During the exhaust stroke, the mixture of gases that were ignited during the compression/power/expansion stroke is forced out from the cylinder through the exhaust valve. The piston, meanwhile, rises to force the gases out.

No. 100 percent efficient engine is not possible. In reality, heat engines are inefficient systems. ... Another is that not all thermal energy is used by the system, some of it exits the engine as heat, which is not used to produce the force or output that is needed.

Q 24. As a student, how can you help minimize the effects of thermal pollution?

Thermal pollution is the degradation of water quality due to changes in water temperature. A change in water quality affects oxygen supply and the ecosystem that is dependent on bodies of water. There are different factors why water temperature changes and one of these is due to the heat released by power plants.

As a student, there are only limited things that I can do to minimize the effects of thermal pollution:

Plant trees. By planting trees, it helps minimize the degradation of water temperature by blocking the sun's heat and absorbing carbon emissions. By absorbing carbon emissions, more oxygen is provided for the water.Reduce electrical consumption. Power plants are the major contributor of thermal pollution and by consuming less power, less power is also managed by the power plants. With less power, less water will be used as a coolant wherein it would lessen the heated water being sent back to where it came from.

In the end, there are only a handful of ways that I can do as a student to help solve thermal pollution. The best way is to educate other people of the effects of thermal pollution in order to have a collective effort in providing a solution to the problem. Take note that what we do will always have a domino effect on our environment therefore, we should do things accordingly.
