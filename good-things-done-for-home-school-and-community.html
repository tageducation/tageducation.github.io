Home

2. Every day, I clean my toilet.

3. My bookcases are well-organized.

4. My study room's floor is swept from time to time.

5. After each use, I store my shoes in a shoe rack.

School

1. Students should strive to give more than they receive every day.

Students should leave school every day with a sense of intellectual agitation and a sense of cognitive strain. Changed. And it must originate from students themselves, not from teachers, in order for this to be sustainable over time (a fundamental component of the Inside-Out Learning Model).

2. Students should ask more questions than they answer every day.

When it comes to pupils asking more questions than they answer, what are the reasons for this? The idea isn't quantity, of course. However, simply insisting on excellence isn't enough. More than anything else, a student's tendency to ask more–and better–questions on a regular basis is a sign of curiosity, ownership, autonomy, and hope, as well as'student involvement.'

3. Take what they've learned in class and apply it to their daily life.

What good is it if they don't? What good is it if they don't?

One strategy is to create learning journals that encourage students to think, in which they spend a few minutes each day or night reflecting on the transferability of what they've learned and/or their proclivity to do so.

4. Students should have a sense of accomplishment and hope every day.

With that in mind, it's time to expand.

There will not always be a clear line between progress and optimism. They may be sparse on some days. Anyone and everything who could have made things better for a pupil who lacks confidence, optimism, improvement, or growth is a failure.

Community

1. Plan an event for a charity.

Collaborate with a local charity, such as a food bank or a shelter for women. Then make a plea to consumers to bring in any items they need in exchange for a discount on their purchase.

2. Create a volunteer program for your employees.

Infusing a charity-oriented mindset into your company's culture through hiring personnel who appreciate the community.

3. Make a donation of your time and talents.

Doing what you do best is one of the most effective ways to give back to your community.

4. Other local enterprises should be favored.

You know how difficult it is to run a small business, so join forces with other local entrepreneurs to focus your efforts on each other and the community.

5. Make your space available for events.

Because many non-profits are on a small budget, they rely on donations, especially for venues, when planning a fundraising event.
