FURTHER EXPLANATION

Six Types of Triangles

In general, triangles fall into one of two categories:

Triangles are determined by the measurements of their sidesBased on their inner angles, triangles

Here, the definitions of these two triangle types and subtypes are provided.

Based on their Sides

1. Scalene

A scalene triangle has sides that are all measured differently. In such a triangle, no side will be the same length as any other side. Every interior angle in a scalene triangle is also unique. The scalene triangle is depicted in the figure below. You can observe that no two sides are the same length.

2. Isosceles

The lengths of two of the three sides in an isosceles triangle are equal. The angles that are opposite the equal sides are therefore equal. An isosceles triangle, in other words, has two equal sides and two equal angles. The isosceles triangle is depicted in the illustration below.

3. Equilateral

Each side of an equilateral triangle has an equal length. Each internal angle in this scenario will be 60 degrees in length. An equilateral triangle is often referred to as an equiangular triangle since its angles are equal. The triangle in the illustration below is equilateral.

Based on their Angles

1. Acute-angled

A triangle is considered acute if all three of its internal angles are acute. It is an acute-angled triangle, in other words, if all interior angles are smaller than 90 degrees. An acute triangle is shown in the diagram below.

2. Obtuse-angled

One of the three interior angles of an obtuse triangle has a measure greater than 90 degrees. In other words, a triangle is referred to be an obtuse-angled triangle if one of the angles is an acute angle. An obtuse triangle is depicted in the figure below.

3. Right-angled

A right triangle is a triangle with a 90° angle as one of its angles. The hypotenuse, which is the longest side of a right-angled triangle, is the side that forms a 90-degree angle with the right angle. It's possible to encounter triangle shapes with names like "right isosceles triangle" and similar combinations, however, this just suggests that the triangle has two equal sides and an interior angle of 90 degrees. The right triangle is depicted in the figure below.
