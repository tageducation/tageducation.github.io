12. 10 branches of physical science


1. PHYSICS--Basic principles of matter and energy.
2. CHEMISTRY--Matter, what is it made of and how it reacts.
3. ASTRONOMY--Study of universe or totality of matter, energy, space, and time.
4. METEOROLOGY--Atmospheric study including everything from the Earth's surface to the end of the Earth's atmosphere.
5. GEOLOGY--Study of the earth including its history and composition.
6. PHYSIOLOGY--The study of the mechanical, physical, and biochemical functions of living organisms.
7. RADIOLOGY--Branch of medicine dealing with the application of radiant energy, including x-rays and radioisotopes.
8. MICROBIOLOGY--The study of microorganisms, including viruses,prokaryotes, and simple eukaryotes.
9. GENETICS--The science of genes,hereditary, and the variation of organisms.
10. ASTROPHYSICS--The study of the physics of the universe.

14. What are the 10 branches of a physical science


Answer:

Physical Science:

The 10 branches of Physical Science are:

Astronomy

Physics

Astrophysics

Major Branches of Physical Science:

Astronomy is the Science that deals with the study of all extraterrestrial objects and phenomena.

Physics is the Science that deals with the structure of matter and the interactions between the fundamental constituents of the observable universe.

Chemistry is the Science that deals with the properties, composition, and structure of substances including elements and compounds, the changes they undergo, and the energy that is released or absorbed during these processes.  

Earth Science is the field of studies that concern the solid Earth, its waters, and the air that envelops it.

Sub - major Branches of Physical Science:

Astrophysics is the branch of astronomy that concerns primarily with the properties and structure of cosmic objects, including the universe as a whole.

Geology is the study of the composition, structure, physical properties, and history of the Earth's components, and the processes by which they are shaped.

Planetary Science is the study of planets, moons, and planetary systems particularly the Solar System and the processes that form them.

Meteorology is the branch of atmospheric Sciences which includes atmospheric Chemistry and atmospheric Physics, with a major focus on weather forecasting.

Geography is the Science that studies the terrestrial surface, the societies that inhabit it and the territories, landscapes, places or regions that form it.

Oceanography is the study of the physical and biological aspects of the ocean.

21. What are the branches of physical science and their definition?


acoustics- the study of phenomena related to sound propagation through air.
astronomy- the study of heavenly bodies by means of observations, using telescopes.
astrophysics- field of astronomy that deals with the constitution, physical properties and evolution of celestial objects.
biophysics- the study of biological structures and mechanisms.
calorimetry- series of techniques used to measure quantities of heat.
electromagnetism- the study of phenomena related to electrical and magnetic fields.
mechanism- the study of effects of forces on bodies.
optics- the study of light, it's laws and how they relate to vision.
solid state physics- the study of the physical properties of a solid.
fluid physics- the study of the behavior of fluids in motion.
nuclear physics- the study of atomic nuclei, elementary particles and nuclear reactions.
quantum physics- the branch of physics that uses and applies the quantum theory.
relativity physics- series of relations that assume that the laws of nature are the same in all spatial/temporal reference frames.
thermodynamics- the branch of physics that deals with the relation between heat and the transformation of matter.
