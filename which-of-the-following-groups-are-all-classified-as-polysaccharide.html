11. Which of the following groups are all classified as polysaccharides?


Answer:

Polysaccharides are complex long chain carbohydrates which are formed by dehydrate synthesis or polymerisation of more than 10 but generally very large number of units called monosacccharides. Starch, glycogen and cellulose are all polysaccharides. Starch is a glucosan homopolysaccharide which is the main reserve food of plants. Glycogen is also a glucosan homopoly- saccharide which is the major reserve food of fiingi, animals and some bacteria. It is also called animal starch. Cellulose is the structural polysaccharide of plant cell walls, some fungi, protists. It is a fibrous glucosan homopolysaccharide of high tensile strength.

13. Which of the following groups are all classified as polysaccharide? A. sucrose, glucose and fructose B. maltose, lactose and fructose C. glycogen, sucrose and maltose D. glycogen, cellulose and starch


Answer:

Glycogen, cellulose, and starch (letter D) are all classified as polysaccharide.

Explanation:

In biochemistry, carbohydrates are carbon compounds which contains large quantities of hydroxyl groups or also known as the ROH functional group. The simplest carbohydrates can contain a ketone or an aldehyde. Carbohydrates are classified as monosaccharide, oligosaccharide, and polysaccharide.

Monosaccharide - also know as simple sugar; the most basic form of carbohydrate

Examples of monosaccharide are the following:

GlucoseFructoseGalactose

Oligosaccharide - carbohydrate that contains a small number of monosaccharide repeating units

Examples of oligosaccharide are the following:

SucroseMaltoseLactose

Polysaccharide - much larger carbohydrate that contains hundreds of monosaccharide repeating units

Examples of polysaccharide are the following:

StarchGlycogenCellulose

Therefore, from the choices, letter D, glycogen, cellulose and starch are all classified as polysaccharide.

23. Polysaccharides triglycerides polypeptides and nucleic acids are classified as


Answer:

Please refer to the explanation below for the answer.

Explanation:

Biomolecules

From the word itself, biomolecules are biological molecules that are vital for the survival of living cells due to their different functions. They comprise of organic molecules such as carbohydrates, lipids, nucleic acids, and proteins.

Carbohydrates

This biomolecule consists of carbon, hydrogen and oxygen atoms. They are the starches, sugars, and fibers that can be found in grains, fruits, vegetables and milk products.

Sugars are classified as monosaccharides, disaccharide, and polysaccharides. Hence, polysaccharides are carbohydrates.

Lipids

This type of biomolecule consists of hydrocarbons, meaning compounds that have carbon and hydrogen. Lipids act as the building blocks of living cells structure. Fats, waxes, oils, vitamins are some examples of lipids.

Triglycerides are a type of lipid that can be found in a human blood that is released for energy.

Nucleic Acid

Nucleic acids are repeating units of nucleotide. It is the main reason why it is also called a polynucleotide.

For every unit of nucleotide, it contains a nitrogen-containing aromatic base attached to a five-carbon sugar, and this attachment is also attached to a phosphate group.

Proteins

A protein molecule contains hundreds to thousands of amino acids that are attached to each other in long chains of repeating units. This means that proteins are biomolecules that are formed by connected amino acids.

A peptide is composed of two or more amino acids bound together by peptide bonds. On the other hand, a polypeptide is a chain or repeating units of many amino acids, hence, polypeptide is classified as amino acid.
