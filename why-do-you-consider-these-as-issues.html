7. which part of the lesson SOCIAL ISSUES do you consider essential? why least essential? why?​​


It depends on what specific social issues are being discussed in the lesson. However, generally speaking, I would consider the understanding of the root causes of a social issue to be essential. Understanding the underlying factors that contribute to a social issue, such as poverty, discrimination, or lack of access to resources, is important in order to develop effective solutions.

On the other hand, I would consider the discussion of specific policy solutions or proposed legislation to be less essential. While these solutions can be important in addressing a social issue, they may not necessarily be the best fit for every situation and community. It's also important to remember that understanding the root causes and the impact of the social issue in the community is more essential than just proposing solutions without understanding the context.

Overall, it's important to have a holistic approach when discussing social issues, and to understand that a wide range of factors contribute to them and there is no one-size-fits-all solution. The most essential part is to understand the complexity and the nuances of the social issue and to be able to think critically about the possible solutions.
