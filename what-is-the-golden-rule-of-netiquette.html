3. what is the Golden rule of netiquette​


The golden rule of Netiquette is do unto others as you'd want them to do unto you.  It is consistent with the fundamental social norm that the majority of us learn as kids.

Here are things we can do to apply the golden rule of netiquette:

1. Be polite

We usually make an effort to avoid cursing while interacting with others in person. This self-control should be shown online as well. Always use a polite tone when interacting online, unless we're talking to small groups of people who don't mind rudeness.

2. Maintain our ethics

We should follow our personal ethics even in cyberspace. If we won't steal things from a stranger, why shouldn't the same principles apply when downloading pirated copies of software, movies, or music?

3. Take care your appearance

The impression we make online is crucial, just like how we present ourselves in public places. Ensure our grammar and spelling is correct since our quality of writing will be judged. Before posting information from a third party, ensure that we're knowledgeable in the subject.

4. Avoid fanning the flames

Online flaming has become a widespread phenomenon. Netiquette acknowledges that flaming is a part of internet communication heritage, but it forbids continuing "flame wars" in email, online forums, and social media feeds.

5. Respect people's privacy

Respect people's online privacy in the same way that you would respect their private in person. For example, don't ever consider looking through a coworker's drawers or peering through a neighbor's window.

24. The golden rule of netiquette "remeber the human" explain what his means in two or three sentences ​


Rule 1: Remember the Human

When communicating electronically, whether through email, instant message, discussion post, text, or some other method, practice the Golden Rule: Do unto others as you would have others do unto you. Remember, your written words are read by real people, all deserving of respectful communication. Before you press "send" or "submit," ask yourself, "Would I be okay with this if someone else had written it?"

Rule 2: Adhere to the same standards of behavior online that you follow in real life

While it can be argued that standards of behavior may be different in the virtual world, they certainly should not be lower. You should do your best to act within the laws and ethical manners of society whenever you inhabit "cyberspace." Would you behave rudely to someone face-to-face? On most occasions, no. Neither should you behave this way in the virtual world.

Rule 3: Know where you are in cyberspace

"Netiquette varies from domain to domain." (Shea, 1994) Depending on where you are in the virtual world, the same written communication can be acceptable in one area, where it might be considered inappropriate in another. What you text to a friend may not be appropriate in an email to a classmate or colleague. Can you think of another example?
