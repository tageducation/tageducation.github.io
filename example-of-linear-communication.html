Linear communication is a process of delivering messages from a communicator to the communicant directly or indirectly using various communication media. Where this communication only occurs in one direction without reciprocity of the message conveyed by the communicator. Where the communicator provides a stimulus and the communicant provides the expected response or response, without holding selection and interpretation.

This approach consists of several elements : source, message andreceiver. The linear model assumes that a person is only a sender or a receiver. Communicationis the process of delivering a statement by one person to another. This understanding explains that this communication involves a number of people with someone stating something to others and the people involved in the communication are called human communication.  

One-way communication is efficient, faster, but less precise. Usually this form of communicationis done when the communicator does not want his mistakes to be known to the communicant. Examples of linear communication that we often encounter in daily activities, for example, are communication on television, radio and various writings in the mass media. All of these things are linear aka one-way communication, because we as communicants do not give reciprocity.

One-way communication is communication that is carried out to convey messages or information without the need for feedback. So, there is no discussion between communicators and communicants in one-way communication. Only the communicator plays an active role in conveying the message, while the communicant listens to the content of the message.

One-way communication eliminates the opportunity for the communicant to obtain explanation and confirmation. Because, this form of communication only emphasizes the delivery of messages. Because it takes place in one direction, the communicant can only interpret or guess the meaning of the message he receives. So that message distortion, hearsay, or rumors are very likely to occur in this form of communication
