24. Enumerate and explain the WORSHIP AND OBSERVANCES of Mahayana Buddhism and relate them to your own religious affiliation's practices. (If you are a Christian/Muslim/etc., how do you relate the worship and observances of Mahayana Buddhism to the practices of Christianity/Islam/etc.?).​


Mahayana Buddhism is a tradition within Buddhism that emphasizes the bodhisattva path, the idea that one can attain enlightenment and become a buddha in order to help all beings achieve liberation. Here are some common practices and observances in Mahayana Buddhism:

Prostrations: This involves bowing down in front of a buddha image, stupa, or other sacred object as a way of showing respect and devotion.

Mantras: These are sacred syllables or phrases that are repeated as a form of meditation or devotion. The most famous mantra in Mahayana Buddhism is "Om mani padme hum," which is associated with the bodhisattva of compassion, Avalokiteshvara.

Offerings: Buddhists may make offerings of food, incense, flowers, or other items to the altar or to a monk or nun as a way of expressing devotion and making merit.

Chanting: Chanting is a common way to engage with the teachings of the Buddha and to cultivate a sense of devotion and mindfulness.

Meditation: Meditation is a central practice in Buddhism and involves cultivating mindfulness and concentration through various techniques, such as mindfulness of breath or the recitation of mantras.

Pilgrimage: Many Buddhists make pilgrimages to sacred sites, such as the temples of Lhasa in Tibet or the temples of Kyoto in Japan, as a way of expressing devotion and seeking spiritual nourishment.

As for how these practices relate to the practices of other religions, it depends on the specific tradition. Some religions, such as Hinduism and Daoism, have similar practices, while others, such as Christianity and Islam, may have different practices and observances. However, all religions have their own unique ways of expressing devotion and seeking spiritual nourishment, and it is important to respect and appreciate the diversity of practices within the world's different traditions.
