5. Which of the following can fight off bacteria?


antibiotics

Antibiotics take advantage of the difference between the structure of the bacterial cell and the host’s  cell.

They either prevent the bacterial cells from multiplying so that the bacterial population remains the same, allowing the host’s defence mechanism to fight the infection or kill the bacteria, for example stopping the mechanism responsible for building their cell walls.

An antibiotic can also be classified according to the range of pathogens against which it is effective. Penicillin G will destroy only a few species of bacteria and is known as a narrow spectrum antibiotic. Tetracycline is effective against a wide range of organisms and is known as a broad spectrum antibiotic.

15. how molecules of ascorbic acid does he need everyday to fight the cancer cells​


Answer:

Problem:

A cancer patient needs to increase his ascorbic acid (C6H8O6) intake to fight cancer cells. A.)how many moles of ascorbic acid does he need to complete the doctors prescription of 13.00 g of intravenous ascorbic acid everyday for one month?

Note:

Molar mass of C6H8O6 is 176.12 g/mol    (this means that in every 1 mole of ascorbic acid, its mass is 176.12 grams.

Given Data:

Grams of ascorbic acid given = 13.00 g

Moles of 13.00 grams of ascorbic acid = ? unknown

Solution:

The given grams (13.00 g) should be divided by the molar mass to find the moles of the 13.00 grams of ascorbic acid.

moles of ascorbic acid = 13.00 g ÷ 176.12 g/mol   (cancel the unit grams)

                                    = 0.07381 moles

                                    = 0.074 moles  (per day)

0.074 moles is needed everyday and in one month (let say 30 days)

0.074 moles × 30 days = 2.22 moles per monnth

Answer:

2.22 moles per monnth
