1. What helped you decide?


Decision making can be crucial when it comes to choosing between or among alternatives that reflect and can affect one’s values, preferences and beliefs.

In order to come up with a wise decision, you may consider the following:

1. Identify and acknowledge the problem.

Don’t dwell on why there is a problem, but focus on how you are going to solve the problem.

2. Gather necessary information and ask for an advice, if possible.

It is helpful in weighing the situation and consequences with the presence of a trusted person, a friend or maybe a parent.  Other people’s opinions may be relevant and take you to a new perspective.

3. Stick to your alternatives.

As you weigh your alternatives, visualize the possibilities of results when you take any of those.

4. Make an action.

Have that courage and do what you think is best to solve the problem or issue.  Prepare yourself for whatever your decision may bring you.
