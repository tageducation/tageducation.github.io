What is the difference between fact and opinion?

-FACT

A fact is a statement in the form of a real situation or an event that occurred. Facts contain something that really exists, and the statement of a fact is usually difficult for anyone to refute.

In a fact, one person's relationship with another must be the same because the incident is clear, undeniable, and can be verified.

-OPINION

An opinion is an attitude or opinion of someone about a situation that has or has not happened. Opinion is strongly influenced by the feelings, thoughts, perspectives, desires, attitudes, experiences, understandings, and beliefs of each individual.

So, opinions between one person and another tend to be different because they are influenced by the mindset, knowledge, and environment in which they respond to a problem.

 

Characteristics of Fact

1. It is verifiable.

 2. It contains data that is both quantitative (in the form of numbers) and qualitative (in the form of statements).

 3. Have accurate data for time, date, location, and event.

 4. Obtained from reliable sources

 5. It is objective, i.e., the actual data, not made up and equipped with object images.

 6. Usually, We can answer the question formulaically : 5W + 1H

 7. Mentioning an event that is currently or has previously occurred

 8. Information comes from actual events.

 

 

Characteristics of Opinions

1. Cannot be proven to be true

 2. Subjective and usually accompanied by opinions, suggestions, and explanations

 3. It does not have a source.

 4. Contains opinions about actual events

 5. Describes events that are unlikely to occur or will occur in the future.

 6. Represents a person's or group's thoughts or opinions

 7. The information submitted has no proof.

 8. Usually marked by the use of the words: "could be," "it seems," "maybe," "should".
