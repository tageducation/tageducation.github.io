9. Calculating Weighted Average: In a History course, a student's grade is composed of papers (40%), tests (40%) and a final exam (20%). The student has earned a 90 value on all papers and 80 value on all tests. What is the minimum score the student needs to earn on the final exam to achieve an overall grade of a B+ (87.0)?


Papers = 90 (0.40)
Tests = 80 (0.40)
Final exam minimum score required = x (0.20)  
Average = 87
For the minimum score, linear inequality is involved in the problem. Use symbol ≥ .

Weighted Average Equation:

[(90)(0.40)]  + [(80)(0.40)] + [(0.20) (x)] ≥  87

36 + 32 +  0.20x ≥ 87

68 + 0.20x ≥ 87

0.20x ≥ 87 - 68

0.20x ≥ 19

0.20x ÷ 0.20   ≥  19 ÷ 0.20

x ≥ 95          
Solution = {x/x ≥ 95}  ;  interval = [95, ⁺∞)

ANSWER: The student must earn a minimum value, or score of at least, 95 in the final exam to get a B⁺ equivalent to 87.

Check:

36 + 32 + (0.20)(95) ≥ 87
68 + 19 ≥ 87
87 ≥ 87

15. The final exam scores of 18 students are (in increasing order): 24, 58, 61, 67, 71, 73, 76, 79, 82, 83, 85, 87, 88, 88, 92, 93, 94, 97. find the upper quartile (q3).


Given :

The final exam scores of 18 students are 24, 58, 61, 67, 71, 73, 76, 79, 82, 83, 85, 87, 88, 88, 92, 93, 94, and 97.

Ask :

What is the upper quartile (q3) of the following numbers 24, 58, 61, 67, 71, 73, 76, 79, 82, 83, 85, 87, 88, 88, 92, 93, 94, and 97.?

Theory :

- Quartiles in data can be obtained by dividing the data sequentially into four parts that have the same value.

Quartile itself consists of three kinds, namely :

>Lower quartile (Q1)

>Middle quartile/median (Q2)

>Upper quartile ( Q3)

- Quartile for Odd data

To find the quartile for even data, we can use this formula :

Let x1, x2, x3, ..., xn is meaning the data in ascending order.

[tex]Q_1 = x_{\frac{n+1}{4} } \\Q_2 = x_{\frac{n+1}{2} } \\Q_3 = x_{\frac{3(n+1)}{4} }[/tex]

- Quartile for Even data

To find quartile for even data, we can using this formula :

x1, x2, x3, ..., xn means the data in ascending order.

[tex]Q_1 = x_{\frac{n+2}{4} } \\Q_2 = (x_{\frac{n}{2} } + x_{\frac{n+2}{2} })/2\\Q_3 = x_{\frac{(3n + 2)}{4} }[/tex]

Step to a solved problem :

24, 58, 61, 67, 71, 73, 76, 79, 82, 83, 85, 87, 88, 88, 92, 93, 94, and 97

Total data is 18, because 18 is even so me used the upper quartile for even data, and we get :

[tex]Q_3 = x_{\frac{3n+2}{4} } \\Q_3 = x_{\frac{3.18+2}{4} } \\Q_3 = x_{\frac{56}{4} } \\Q_3 = x_{14} \\Q_3 = 88[/tex]

To find the weighted mean of the student’s scores, the professor first assigns a weight to each score. In this case the professor could assign each of the test scores a weight of 1 and the final exam score a weight of 2. A student with test scores of 70, 80, and 85 and a final examination score of 90 has a weighted mean of? Solution must be attached.


WEIGHTED MEAN

If a professor counts the final examination score as a two-test score and to find the weighted mean of the scores, the professor assigns a weight to each of them, the weighted mean of a student who got test scores of 70, 80, and 85, and a final exam score of 90 is 83.

Note: Consider that the assigned weight for test scores is 1 and 2 for the final exam score.

Solving:

First, we must add multiply the scores by their assigned weight.70 (1) = 7080 (1) = 80 85 (1) = 8590 (2) = 180Then, we are going to add them all.70 + 80 + 85 + 180 = 415Lastly, we have to divide the sum by the weight of each score.[tex]\frac{415}{5}[/tex] = 83
