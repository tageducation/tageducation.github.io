Answer:As women grow older, their body goes to a gradual change in the balance of the sex hormones. It occurs when the a part of the woman's reproductive system (Ovary/Ovaries) starts producing as much of the hormone estrogen and progesterone which results into a complete stop to the production of unfertilized eggs.Menopause usually occurs when a women is at their middle age (40-60 years old)

Natural menopause comes gradually in these three stages:

1. Menopause Transition (Perimenopause)

Can take place on woman in their 30's or 40's

It is when the ovaries begin to produce lesser and lesser estrogen, transitioning to menopausal period due to age or hormonal imbalance:

Perimenopause symptoms:

InsomniaAbnormal interval between menstrual periodsIrregular periodsMood swingVaginal and bladder complicationDecreasing fertilityGradual change on cholesterol level

2. Menopause -This is the stage where a woman's egg production and menstrual period will gradually stop to an end

Menopause symptoms

Hot flushesInsomniaMemory lossIrritability.Menstrual period stoppage

3. Postmenopause - This is the stage where a woman can no longer get pregnant naturally, the symptoms of postmenopause can last up to years or decades depending on the woman's body itself. Women at postmenopausal stage are at a higher risk of Osteoporosis and Body aches

Post-menopause symptoms:

Hot flushIrritabilityInsomniaMood swingsStressOsteoporotic symptomsDepression

Why is it important to study the menstrual cycle

 

It is important to research the menstrual cycle for a woman to know her fertile period, particularly for  a married woman who is planning to separate her children and to keep her mother and children safe. The menstrual cycle is important to study since it predicts when women will be pregnant. Females are also advised to abstain from sexual activity. If the female does not wish to get pregnant as a result of sexual intercourse, it is recommended that you determine the days of fertilization and ovulation.

Menstruation

Menstruation is the elimination of the thickened uterine lining (endometrium) from the body through the vagina. Menstrual fluid includes fat, endometrial cells (uterine lining cells), and mucus. A normal duration lasts between three days and one week. Sanitary pads or Tampons are used to absorb menstrual blood flow. Pads and tampons to be replaced on a daily basis (at least every four hours). Tampon use has been linked to an elevated risk of toxic shock syndrome, a rare disease (TSS). The menstrual cycle is complicated and governed by a variety of glands and the hormones produced by these glands. The four phases of the menstrual cycle are menstruation, follicular process, ovulation, and luteal phase. Menstrual symptoms that are common include heavy or uncomfortable periods and premenstrual syndrome (PMS). Knowing where in a woman's menstrual cycle she is more likely to conceive will improve her chances of becoming pregnant.

The normal menstrual cycle lasts 28–29 days, although this varies between women and from cycle to cycle.From the first day of your period to the day before the next period starts, the menstrual cycle is measured. Girls usually have their first cycle (menarche) between the ages of 11 and 14 years. Other sexual traits, such as pubic hair and budding breasts, have evolved by this time.

Hormones and the menstrual cycle

The menstrual is the cycle is complex and is governed by several glands and the hormones produced by these glands. The hypothalamus, a brain organ, stimulates the adjacent pituitary gland to release certain chemicals, which stimulate the ovaries to produce the sex hormones oestrogen and progesterone. The menstrual cycle is a biofeedback mechanism, which means that the activity of one structure or gland influences the activity of the others.
