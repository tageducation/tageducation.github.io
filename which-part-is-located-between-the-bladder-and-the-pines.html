FURTHER EXPLANATION:

The prostate is an exocrine gland in the reproductive system of most male mammals. Its main function is to secrete and store the fluid that makes up two-thirds of the semen. It is  located between the bladder and the penis. The prostate varies from species to species in terms of anatomy, chemistry and physiology. Enlarged prostate is a common symptom suffered by men over the age of 50. Enlargement occurs in the central part of the prostate gland that surrounds the urinary tract (urethra). Continued peripheral enlargement of the prostate gland can lead to more serious stages of prostate cancer.

Prostate disease is a variety of conditions that attack prostate function. The prostate organ is a gland in the male reproductive system that plays a role in sperm production. Not only that, infection or abnormal tissue also triggers prostate disorders. Explanation:

The male reproductive system is a grouping of organs that make up a man’s reproductive and urinary systems. These organs do the following jobs within your body:

They produce, maintain and transport sperm (the male reproductive cells) and semen (the protective fluid around the sperm).

They discharge sperm into the female reproductive tract.

They produce and secrete male sex hormones.

The male reproductive system is made up of internal (inside your body) and external (outside your body) parts. Together, these organs help you urinate (rid your body of liquid waste materials), have sexual intercourse and make children.
