4. Functions of Communication with examples


Communication

Communication is an activity in which a person interacts with others through verbal and non-verbal symbols, such as verbal or sign language. Communication carried out by a person has a specific purpose, both for his personal survival and the survival of society.

Here are the Communication Functions along with examples:

Social Communication Function

The function of communication as social communication at least implies that communication is important for building our self-concept, self-actualization, for survival, for obtaining happiness, avoiding pressure and calm, among others through entertaining communication, and fostering relationships with other people.

Examples of Social Communication Functions:

Communication with colleagues, from superiors to subordinates will certainly know who we are through our daily work environment.

Expressive Communication Function

Expressive communication does not automatically aim to influence others, but it can be done as long as the communication becomes an instrument to convey our feelings (emotions). Those feelings are mainly through nonverbal messages. Feelings of affection, care, longing, sympathy, joy, sadness, fear, concern, anger and hatred can be conveyed through words, but mainly through nonverbal behavior.

Examples of Expressive Communication Functions:

When a mother caresses her child as an expression of affection, demonstrations by students, a person who cries is not because he is sad but happy, for example when he graduates, wins a competition, and so on.

Ritual Communication Function

The function of this ritual communication is more likely to be mass, although it is possible that it is individual. Rituals are related to repetitive, ceremonial, habitual actions.

Examples of this ritual communication function are:

Celebrations of independence ceremonies, major religious holidays, birthday celebrations, celebrations of regional traditions, and the like. Religious activities such as worshiping God are also included in this type of communication function.

Instrumental Communication Function

Instrumental communication has several general purposes: informing, teaching, encouraging, changing attitudes and beliefs, and changing behavior or driving action, as well as entertaining.

Examples of Instrumental Communication Functions:

A lecturer who teaches, a politician who campaigns, or also someone who makes a business presentation to a group of people.

19. Functions of Communication with examples


The word communication means exchanging information or news. It refers to sending and receiving of information through different sources. There are two major types of communications. These are the following:  

Verbal - it refers to the use of languages to transfer and to receive information.  Non-Verbal - it refers to the use of other means to transfer and to receive information such as body language, gestures and facial expressions.  

Functions of Communication Regulation - it controls the behavior of humans and to regulates the surroundings and the activities that the humans were engaged in.  Social Interactions - it can be used to produce social interactions.  Motivation - it refers to a person that uses a language to express their needs, wants, likes and dislikes, and desires. Information - it usually comes in the form of the statement of facts  Emotional Expression - it can be express verbally and non-verbally. It is the central part of who we are.  Special Case of Expressing Opinions - it has broad functions that cover every category of communication functions. It also belongs depending on the purpose and setting in which it is done.  

Importance of Communications

In everyday life, we use communication in every situation. Whether it is just a small gesture, as long it expresses a message or a piece of information, it is still a form of communication.
