My father made a promise that he would buy me a laptop?6.) We have an obligation to take care of the environment?7.) The construction workers made a request to allow them to go back to work?8.) Our classroom has the capacity to accommodate 30 students?9.) I met the basic requirement for me to enroll in online class?10.) Many families were given the chance to bond during the lockdown?​


1)    Possibility

There is a possibility that I will be chosen to join the contest?

→ There is a possibility that something is true or that something could happen.

2)    Likelihood

The weather forecast stated that the likelihood of rain tomorrow is high.

→ When we want to make a guess about something, we can use these modal verbs (also known as modals of deduction, speculation, or certainty).

3)    Necessity

Eating healthy foods is a necessity.

→ Speakers typically use "don't have to", "don't need to", or "needn't" to express a lack of necessity (also known as absence of obligation).

4)    Willingness

Many people expressed their willingness to help those in need.

→ To express willingness, we can use the verb 'will' as a modal verb or as a main verb.

5)    Promise

My father made a promise that he would buy me a laptop.

→ We can make promises with the modal verb will: I'll always remember you. We'll send you the contract the next day.

6)    Obligation

Do we have an obligation to take care of the environment?

→ Laws and regulations, for example, use must express obligation or duty.

7)    Request

The construction workers made a request to allow them to go back to work.

→ We use the modal verbs can, could, and would to offer or invite people to do things for us.

8)    Capacity

Does our classroom have the capacity to accommodate 30 students?

→ We can discuss ability using modal verbs (either the general ability or specific ability in a particular situation).

9)    Requirements

I met the basic requirement for me to enroll in an online class.

→ When the modal verbs 'should' or 'ought to' are used, it indicates that an action is required but has not yet been completed.

10)    Chance

Many families were given the chance to bond during the lockdown.

→ There is a possibility that something is true or that something could happen.

Further explanation:Modal Verbs

Modal verbs are a subset of auxiliary verbs, which are verbs that cannot be used on their own. They must be used in conjunction with another (main) verb. Modal verbs include can, could, may, might, must, shall, should, will, and would.

They provide more information about the verb that comes after them. Modal verbs are used to express ability, obligation, permission, possibility, request and offer advice, and make requests and offers. They can have multiple meanings depending on the context in which they are used.

He can go now (permission).I can make money (ability).

Modal Verb Structure→ Infinitive + subject + modal verb (without to)

Modals differ from regular verbs in the following ways:

There are no's' in the third person singular.They invert questions ('she can go' becomes 'can she go?').They are immediately followed by the infinitive of another verb (without 'to').
