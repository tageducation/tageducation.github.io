What are the parts of the microscope that magnify the image of the specimen? 2. Which part of the microscope is used for sharpening the image of the specimen after it is focused? 3. If you wanted to change the objective lenses, which part of the microscope would you manipulate? 4. Suppose the eyepiece magnification is 10x and the low-power objective lens magnification is 40x. What is the total magnification of the image? Explain your answer. 5. If your science teacher instructed you to move the microscope, describe how you would carry it.​


1.Objective Lenses - The objective lens gathers light from the specimen, magnifies the image of the specimen, and projects the magnified image into the body tube. ... Ocular Lens - The ocular lens, or eyepiece, magnifies the image.

2.Focus (coarse), The coarse focus knob is used to bring the specimen into approximate or near focus. Focus (fine), Use the fine focus knob to sharpen the focus quality of the image after it has been brought into focus with the coarse focus knob.

3.Revolving Nosepiece or Turret: This is the part of the microscope that holds two or more objective lenses and can be rotated to easily change power.

4.For example, a biological microscope with 10x eyepieces and a 40x objective has 400x magnification.

5.Always carry the microscope with 2 hands—place one hand on the microscope arm and the other hand under the microscope base. Do not touch the objective lenses. Keep the objectives in the scan position and keep the stage low when adding or removing slides.

What are the parts of the microscope that magnify the image of the specimen? 2. Which part of the microscope is used for sharpening the image of the specimen after it is focused? 3. If you wanted to change the objective lenses, which part of the microscope would you manipulate? 4. Suppose the eyepiece magnification is 10x and the low-power objective lens magnification is 40x. What is the total magnification of the image? Explain your answer. 5. If your science teacher instructed you to move the microscope, describe how you would carry it.​


Answer:

1. Ocular Leans

2. Coarse Focus Knob

3. Revolving Nosepiece or Turret

4. 400x magnification

5. When moving your microscope, always carry it with both hands (Figure 1, at left). Grasp the arm with one hand and place the other hand under the base for support. Turn the revolving nosepiece so that the lowest power objective lens is "clicked" into position (This is also the shortest objective lens).

Explanation:

1. Enlargement or magnification of a specimen is the function of a two-lens system; the ocular lens is found in the eyepiece, and the objective lens is situated in a revolving nose-piece.

2. Focus (coarse), The coarse focus knob is used to bring the specimen into approximate or near focus. Focus (fine), Use the fine focus knob to sharpen the focus quality of the image after it has been brought into focus with the coarse focus knob.

3. Revolving Nosepiece or Turret: This is the part of the microscope that holds two or more objective lenses and can be rotated to easily change power.

4. A microscope's total magnification is a combination of the eyepieces and the objective lens. For example, a biological microscope with 10x eyepieces and a 40x objective has 400x magnification.

Eyepiece: The lens the viewer looks through to see the specimen. The eyepiece usually contains a 10X or 15X power lens.

Diopter Adjustment: Useful as a means to change focus on one eyepiece so as to correct for any difference in vision between your two eyes.

Body tube (Head): The body tube connects the eyepiece to the objective lenses.

Arm: The arm connects the body tube to the base of the microscope.

Coarse adjustment: Brings the specimen into general focus.

Fine adjustment: Fine tunes the focus and increases the detail of the specimen.

Nosepiece: A rotating turret that houses the objective lenses. The viewer spins the nosepiece to select different objective lenses.

Show what you have learned about using a microscope.

1. What are the parts of the microscope that magnify the image of

the specimen? - EYEPIECE or OCULAR LENS

The ocular lens, or the eyepiece is the part of the microscope that will magnify the image on the glass slide produced by the microscope’s objective so that it your eye can be see the sample.

2. Which part of the microscope is used for sharpening the image

of the specimen after it is focused?  - FINE ADJUSTMENT KNOB

This part of the microscope allows you to adjust the image to be viewed on the glass slide to be clearer and sharper.

3. If you wanted to change the objective lenses, which  part of the microscope would you manipulate? - REVOLVING NOSEPIECE

The revolving nosepiece holds the objective lenses. They are used so that you can change which objective lens you needed. This nosepiece usually holds three to four objective lenses.

4. Suppose the eyepiece magnification is 10x and the  low-power objective lens magnification is 40x. What is  the total magnification of the image?

- TOTAL MAGNIFICATION IS EQUAL TO 40 x 10 = 400X

To solve the total magnification of the microscope, just multiply the eyepiece magnification to the low-power objective lend magnification.

If your science teacher instructed you to move the microscope, describe how  you would carry it.​ - If the science teacher instructed you to move the microscope, you should hold the microscope on its ARM with your one hand and support its BASE with your other hand.
