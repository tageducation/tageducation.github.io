12. a grade 10 learner commit to practice self discipline through


The grade 10 learner commit to practice self discipline through these:

1. Tell Them to Prioritize

2. Avoid the Habit of Procrastination

3. Be assertive

4. Avoid Perfectionism

5. Create a backup plan

EXPLANATION:

Discipline can be defined as compliance with the regulations or subject to supervision and control. Discipline aims to practice self-development in order to behave in an orderly manner. Time discipline means being able to use and manage time well. Basically, discipline arises from the habit of living and learning and teaching regularly and loving and appreciating his work. A success can be achieved by using the time you have well.

Because time is very valuable, we must make the most of it. To be able to use time well is also not easy, it takes practice especially for people who are undisciplined at first.

Here are some lessons to practice time discipline for grade 10 students:

1. Tell Them to Prioritize

Start managing time to be more efficient by setting priorities. The tasks that you think are important should always be at the top of your schedule.

2. Avoid the Habit of Procrastination

Don't get used to procrastinating tasks / work, because it will make your tasks pile up. The more tasks that are postponed, the more ineffective your time will be.

3. Be assertive

The point is to say “no” and reject tasks we cannot do. If indeed we are not able to solve it, never force yourself.

4. Avoid Perfectionism

Learning to avoid perfection will help you manage your time. Seeking perfection especially for trivial and unimportant things will be a waste of your time.

5. Create a Backup Plan

Starting to make backup plans will really help you when your main plans are not achieved, so it will make your time more efficient.

My Commitment to Self Discipline

Self-discipline is a coined term for self-control wherein we control our emotions, thoughts, feelings, face and overcome our weaknesses,  and have a goal and a clear intention towards our dreams. When we master the art of self-discipline, it means we are focused and unstoppable to reach the goals that we are aiming for. We don't let procrastination ruin us and overthrow what those things that we had to accomplish by the end of the day are. This means we are aware and conscious of our negative actions, and we immediately find a solution to change them.

Now, to stay committed to my goals and master the art of self-discipline, I have to make sure first what I want to accomplish and those goals that I want to achieve. And what are those possible hindrances that I have to face and overcome? My commitment to achieving self-discipline is high; I've been in a situation wherein I want to stop or quit, by I know It's not a good idea because I need to finish what I have to do. And the result is I feel like I'm just doing tasks not because I want to, but I have to, and this idea and fixed mindset is what I want to change.
