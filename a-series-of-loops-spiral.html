6. these are a series of animated images or soundless video in the smallest file size, that loop continuously without anyone pressing play?​


A series of animated images or soundless video in the smallest file size, that loop continuously without anyone pressing play

GIF (Graphical Interchange Format) is an image format invented in 1987 by Steve Wilhite, a US software writer looking for a way to animate images with the smallest file size possible. In a nutshell, GIFs are a series of images or soundless video that will loop indefinitely without requiring anyone to press play. GIFs become instantly familiar as a result of this repetition, much like the beat of a song.

Is it "GIF" or "Jif"? This is our generation's "Tomato, tomahto" – regardless of how you say it, we know GIFs are here to stay. Fortunately, people nowadays are less concerned with grammar and more concerned with the image itself. Along with memes and viral videos, millennials' favorite file format has recently become the default tool of web humor. They've become so popular that the term "GIF" was named the Oxford Dictionary Word of the Year in 2012.
