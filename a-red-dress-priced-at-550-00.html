1. A red dress priced at php 550 is marked 15% off. how much will be the discount


Answer:

The discount will be php 82.50

Finding the Discount

The discount refers to the amount that is being deducted from the original price. It is determined by the rate. The rate is the number written with the percent symbol (%). To find the discount, you have to convert the rate to decimal, then multiply it by the original price.

Going back to the problem above, you have to find the discount on the red dress which is marked 15% off.

Step-by-step explanation:

550 x 15%=82.50

550-82.50=467.50

Now, we can get the equation as:

[tex]\quad\quad\quad\begin{aligned} \tt{(100 \% - 20 \%)x} & = \tt{550} \\ \tt{(80 \%)x} & = \tt{550} \\ \tt{(0.8)x} & = \tt{550} \\ \tt{x} & = {\tt{\frac{550}{0.8}}} \\ & \boxed{\tt{x = 687.5}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the original price of the dress that Elvie bought is 687.5
