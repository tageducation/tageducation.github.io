7. A simple pendulum is 50cm long and a frequency of 3Hz. Find its wavelength


SOLUTION:

A simple pendulum oscillates back and forth with a frequency given by:

[tex] f = \frac{1}{(2\pi)} \times \sqrt{(\frac{g}{L})} [/tex]

where L is the length of the pendulum and g is the acceleration due to gravity.

In this case, we are given that the pendulum has a length of [tex]L = 50[/tex] cm and a frequency of [tex]f = 3[/tex] Hz. We can solve for the acceleration due to gravity:

[tex] g = \frac{(4\pi ^2 L)}{T^2} [/tex]

where [tex] T [/tex] is the period of the pendulum, which is the inverse of the frequency:

[tex] T = \frac{1}{f} [/tex]

Substituting the values given, we get:

[tex] T = \frac{1}{3} = 0.3333 \: \mathrm{s} [/tex]

[tex] g = \frac{(4\pi ^2 (50))}{(0.3333)^2} \approx 981 \: \mathrm{cm/s^2} [/tex]

Now, the wavelength of the pendulum can be found using the formula:

[tex] \lambda = \frac{v}{f} [/tex]

where [tex] v [/tex] is the velocity of the wave. For a pendulum, the velocity is given by:

[tex] v = \sqrt{(gL)} [/tex]

so we have:

[tex] \lambda = \sqrt{\frac{(gL)}{f}} = \sqrt{\frac{981 \times 50}{3}} \approx 31.8 \: \mathrm{cm} [/tex]

Hence, the wavelength of the pendulum is approximately 31.8 cm

18. What is the period and frequency of a 4 m long simple pendulum on Earth?​


Answer:

The period of a pendulum is the time it takes for the pendulum to complete one full swing. The frequency of a pendulum is the number of swings it makes in a given time period, usually measured in swings per second or hertz.

The period of a simple pendulum is given by the formula:

T = 2 * pi * sqrt(L / g)

Where:

T is the period of the pendulum in seconds

pi is the constant approximately equal to 3.14

L is the length of the pendulum in meters

g is the acceleration due to gravity, which is approximately 9.8 m/s^2 on Earth

So, for a simple pendulum with a length of 4 meters, the period would be:

T = 2 * 3.14 * sqrt(4 / 9.8) = 2.78 seconds

The frequency of a pendulum is the reciprocal of the period, or:

f = 1 / T

So, the frequency of a 4 meter long pendulum would be:

f = 1 / 2.78 = 0.36 Hz
