Steps in Reducing RadicalsMake sure that the radicands are not prime numbers.Express the radicands as a product of two or more numbers, such that,  two or more numbers are a perfect square. Rewrite it as a product of different radicals.Simplify the perfect square.

For example, [tex]\sqrt{27}[/tex]. We know that [tex]27[/tex] is not a prime number and we can find two factors which are 3 and 9. The number 9, in this case, is a perfect square.

                                                       

We can now rewrite it as a  product of two different radicals and simplify the perfect square.

Thus, the reduced form of [tex]\sqrt{27}[/tex] is [tex]3\sqrt{3}[/tex].
