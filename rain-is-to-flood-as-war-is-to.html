7. the banks of the viver (floods,flood) during the heavy rains


The banks of the river FLOOD during heavy rains.

The subject of the sentence is “the banks” and is plural.

According to the Subject-Verb aggreement, if the subject is plural the verb is also plural, and if it is singular and so as the verb.

The verb there is the word “flood”. The plural form of verbs doesn’t have ‘s’ on them unlike nouns. So, flood is the answer and not floods.

18. rain flood war analogy


Answer:

Rain is to flood as practice is to improvement. The analogy is that rain and practice are the cause while flood and improvement are the effect.

Explanation:

Flood is an outcome of excessive rain. When it rains heavily for a long time the result is the rise of the water level of rivers and other water bodies. This ultimately gives rise to floods. Thus the cause of floods is heavy rains. The effect of rain is flood.

Similarly practice incessantly leads to improvement. Repeated practice aims at perfection. Hence practice is the cause that leads to the effect which is improvement.
