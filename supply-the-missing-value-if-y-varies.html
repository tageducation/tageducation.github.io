Joint and Inverse Variations

The two quantities are said to be inversely proportional and each term varies inversely with the other. Inversely proportional relationships are also called inverse variations.Joint variation occurs when a variable varies directly or inversely with multiple variables. For instance, if x varies directly with both y and z, we have x = kyz. If x varies directly with y and inversely with z, we have x = kyz.

23. Learning Task 2A. Supply the missing value if y varies jointly as x and z?​


If y varies jointly as x and z and inversely as w, and y=25 when x=4, z=75 and w=6. Find y when x=6, z=50 and w=15.

The formula would be y=kxz/w where k is a constant and found by rearranging the equation to yield

k=y*w/(x*z) = 25*6/(4*75) = 25*3*2/(2*2*25*3) = 1/2

y=.5xz/w = .5*6*50/15 = 10
