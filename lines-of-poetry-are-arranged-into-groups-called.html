1. Lines of poetry are arranged into groups called​


Answer:

In poetry, stanzas are visual groupings of lines. A group of two lines is called a couplet. A three line stanza is called a tercet. A four line stanza is a quatrain, and a five line stanza is a quintet.


2. lines of poetry are arranged into groups called:​


Answer:

stanza, a division of a poem consisting of two or more lines arranged together as a unit. More specifically, a stanza usually is a group of lines arranged together in a recurring pattern of metrical lengths and a sequence of rhymes.

12. 1. In poetry, a group of lines placed together is called a what?A. LineB. Stanza C. RowD. Paragraph​


Answer:

B.Stanza

Explanation:

A stanza is a group of lines that form the basic metrical unit in a poem. So, in a 12-line poem, the first four lines might be a stanza. You can identify a stanza by the number of lines it has and its rhyme scheme or pattern, such as A-B-A-B. There are many different types of stanzas.

17. A type of poetry that does not adhere to certain conventions like rhyme and line length is called?​


Answer:

Free verse

Explanation:

**Free verse** is a literary device that can be defined as poetry that is free from limitations of regular meter or rhythm, and does not rhyme with fixed forms. Such poems are without rhythm and rhyme schemes, do not follow regular rhyme scheme rules, yet still provide artistic expression. In this way, the poet can give his own shape to a poem however he or she desires.
