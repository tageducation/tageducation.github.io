5. 1. Among the statements below, what best describes intersubjectivity?a. Intersubjectivity is about how humans treat plants.b. It is a state of sharing of subjective states by two or moreindividuals.c. It is about how we judge other persons.d. We treat others as objects in intersubjective relationships.​


From the statement given, the best statement to describe intersubjectivity is B. It is a state of sharing subjective states by two or more individuals.

Explanation details:

Simply put, intersubjectivity can be defined as the subjective influence that one person gives or receives on another because they are in a close relationship that can influence each other.

The meaning of subjective itself is a view that is based on personal feelings, not facts or actual things.

Examples of intersubjectivity are:

Since childhood, Anna was always told by her mother that holding a stray cat was dirty and that there might be germs in its fur that could cause illness. From then until adulthood, Anna did not like and did not want to be near stray cats.

From here we can see that Anna's dislike of the stray cat because she is afraid that the stray cat is dirty, is influenced by the subjective view she received from her mother and she believed it. In fact, not all stray cats are dirty and have germs that can cause illness. Anna's view of this cat is a form of intersubjectivity.

So the best statement to describe intersubjectivity is that it is a state of sharing subjective states by two or more individuals.

The multiple explicit and implicit relationships between individuals (subjectivities) that make up social and cultural meaning systems are referred to as intersubjectivity.

As there is a school of thought that highlights how humans make meaning through both their bodies and language, the idea of intersubjectivity incorporates the significance of interpersonal connections in the process of meaning-making from the start.

11. Key questions1. What is intersubjectivity ?2. Differentiate l-It from - Thou relationship.​


1.Intersubjectivity, a term originally coined by the philosopher Edmund Husserl (1859–1938), is most simply stated as the interchange of thoughts and feelings, both conscious and unconscious, between two persons or “subjects,” as facilitated by empathy.

2.Fundamentally, "It" refers to the world as we experience it. By contrast, the word pair I–Thou describes the world of relations. This is the "I" that does not objectify any "It" but rather acknowledges a living relationship.

Author: Martin Buber

Subject: Philosophy

Language: German
