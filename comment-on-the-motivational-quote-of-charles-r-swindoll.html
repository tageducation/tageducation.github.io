Comment on the motivational quote of Charles R. Swindoll, "We cannotchange our past.

We cannot change the fact that people will act in a certain way. We  cannot change the inevitable. The only thing we can do is play on the one string we have and that is our attitude. I am convinced that life is 10% what happens to me and 90% how I react to it."

People do know those questions because they've already faced difficulties and some other obstacles and their everyday lives. Somehow, the people used to resolve the obstacles they faced. The most positive thing we can do is to be in touch with each other and encourage people who just need the opinion of the other people, who need to be understood and felt because of their experiences. That helps a lot to understand the things you just don't understand in your life. The more I live, the more I understand the effect of an attitude on my life. Attitude, to me, is more important than past schooling, than money, than conditions, than failure, than successes, than people think or say or do. It is more critical than the presence of talent or ability. It's going to make or break a business... a church... a school... a house. The remarkable thing is that we have a decision every day over the mindset that we're going to adopt for the day. We're not going to change our past... We don't alter the fact that people are going to behave in a certain manner. We don't want to change the enivitable. The only thing we can do is play on the one string we've got, and that's our mindset. I'm persuaded that life is 10% of what happens to me and 90% of how I respond to it. And so it's for you, we're in charge of our ATTITUDES.

The achievement of pleasure is a matter of preference. It's a positive attitude that we want to share. It's not a present shipped to our door every morning, nor does it arrive through the window. And it's true that our conditions are not the ones that make us happy. If we wait for them to get right, we're never going to joke again.

We must stop striving and trust that God will provide what He thinks is best and whatever time He wishes to make it open. Although this kind of trusting does not come easily. It is a moral crisis of the will in which we must continue to practice our faith.
