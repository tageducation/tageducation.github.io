1. Beowulf

- Behavioral Pattern:

Performance Risk-taking

- Evidence:

In the epic poem Beowulf, the hero of the story, Beowulf, is known for his bravery and willingness to take risks in battle. In one notable scene, Beowulf goes up against the monster Grendel and fights him without weapons, using only his bare hands. This bravery and willingness to take on a dangerous foe without fear demonstrate Beowulf's willingness to take risks to accomplish his goals.

2. King Hrothgar

- Behavioral Pattern:

Information gathering

- Evidence:

In the epic poem Beowulf, King Hrothgar is described as a wise and noble ruler. In one notable scene, he provides counsel to Beowulf and offers him advice on how to defeat the monster Grendel. Hrothgar speaks of the importance of bravery and honor in battle and encourages Beowulf to use his strength and skill to overcome any obstacles that may come his way. Despite his advanced age, Hrothgar is still revered as a wise and respected leader among his people.

3. Grendel

- Behavioral Pattern:

Performance Risk-taking

- Evidence:

The monster Grendel is portrayed as a ruthless and cunning foe. Despite the fact that he is feared and reviled by many, Grendel is willing to take risks in order to achieve his goals. In one notable scene, Grendel attacks the hall of the Danish king Hrothgar and kills many of his warriors. This act of violence and aggression demonstrates Grendel's willingness to take risks in order to satisfy his bloodthirsty nature.

4. Grendel's mother

Behavioral Pattern:

Reflecting

Evidence:

Grendel's mother is portrayed as a vengeful and violent creature who seeks to avenge her son's death at the hands of Beowulf. In one notable scene, she attacks the hall of the Danish king Hrothgar and tries to kill Beowulf in retaliation for slaying her son. This act of revenge demonstrates Grendel's mother's intense desire to protect her offspring and her willingness to take risks in order to do so.

5. Wiglaf

- Behavioral Pattern:

Collaborating

- Evidence:

Wiglaf is a warrior who fights alongside Beowulf in his battle against the dragon. In one notable scene, Wiglaf helps Beowulf defeat the dragon by providing him with support and encouragement in the heat of battle. This act of collaboration and teamwork demonstrates the strong bond between Beowulf and Wiglaf and their willingness to work together in order to overcome a common enemy.

6. Dragon

Behavioral Pattern:

Performance Risk-taking

Evidence:

The dragon is portrayed as a fierce and powerful foe. Despite its immense strength and deadly fire-breathing ability, the dragon is willing to take risks in order to protect its hoard of treasure. In one notable scene, the dragon attacks the hall of the Danish king and sets it on fire, showing its willingness to use violence and destruction in order to defend its hoard. This act of aggression demonstrates the dragon's willingness to take risks in order to achieve its goals.
