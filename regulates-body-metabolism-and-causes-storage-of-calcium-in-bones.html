1. regulates body metabolism and causes storage of calcium in bones


Answer:

The thyroid hormone is the one regulating the body's metabolism while the parathyroid is the one regulating the Calcium level of our body.

Explanation:

Thyroid and parathyroid are two different glands, though are very near with each other, don't have a relationship in terms of functions.

The parathyroid is the one responsible in regulating the Calcium levels of our body. Calcium is critical. It regulated muscle contraction and helps in signalling impulses in the nerves. If parathyroid is not functioning well, secretion of Calcium is at stake and there are diseases that may arise. Diseases related to parathyroid malfunction are hypo and hyperthyroidism as well as osteoporosis.

The thyroid meanwhile is the one responsible in regulating the body's metabolism. Where metabolism is the process of converting food into energy that is used by our body. Damaged thyroid may cause hyper and hypothyroidism, goiter, and even cancer.
