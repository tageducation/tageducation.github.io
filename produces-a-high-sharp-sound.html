Question:

Are the sounds of the words sharp and hard, long and soft, or changing?

Answer:

Yes, Because

In English, there are two different sounds for the consonants "c" and "g." A hard "g" sounds almost like a "k," as in the words great, good, and pig. A soft "g" sounds more like a "j," as in the words large, general, and giant By contrast, a hard "c" sounds like a "k," as in the words cup, class, and fact. A soft "c" sounds like an "s" as in city, receive, and cell. Simple rules can help to determine whether the pronunciation of these consonants should be hard or soft.
