"Organisms did not change despite of the changes in the environment. "is incorrect statement about living organisms.

Based on the theory of evolution according to J.B. de Lamarck, organisms can change in response to the environment. The changes obtained during his life will also always be passed on by an organism to its offspring.

EVOLUTION THEORY

Jean Baptiste Lamarck, a naturalist from France through his book entitled Philosophie Zoologique (1800) argued that the current form of life is based on the use and not using organs (use and disuse). This theory was put forward by Lamarck fifty years before the emergence of Darwin's theory, although later, Darwin was more popular, but Lamarck had a big role in the development of science.

An example of the theory put forward by Lamarck is about the giraffe's neck extending by stretching it to reach a higher tree. This physical trait will later be passed on to the next generation of giraffes. The word use refers to the organ used by this animal which will continue to grow and develop. Meanwhile, disuse refers to organs that are not used by the animal and will automatically be reduced.
