Oral communication is more efficient than writing an email or sending a text. 1. Formal conversations - These occur frequently at work since you have to communicate with supervisors, coworkers, and stakeholders like clients and customers all the time. Formal exchanges are brief, concise, and direct. Everyone has a limited amount of time, so you must make your argument in a concise manner.

2. Informal conversations - These are the types of discussions you have with your coworkers, as well as your friends and family. The majority of them lack agendas. You can share your day's events, organize your lunch, or talk about your weekend plans. These are cordial discussions laced with light humor.

3. Speeches - Speeches are crucial in industries like event planning and community service. In a business setting, leaders and senior management are the only ones allowed to speak.

I have also learned about the different strategies for effective communication, such as structuring a message, using appropriate language, and being aware of nonverbal cues.

Furthermore, I have learned about the importance of being an active listener. Active listening requires concentration, asking questions, listening to understand rather than to reply, and reflecting on what is being said. Active listening helps to ensure that both parties are able to effectively communicate their ideas and feelings.

Finally, I have learned the importance of using feedback. Feedback provides an opportunity for both the speaker and the listener to understand each other better. It allows for further clarification and helps to ensure that both parties understand what is being communicated.

26. Essay about winning over the challenges of life through oral Communication​


Answer:

Life is full of challenges and obstacles that we must overcome in order to achieve our goals and be successful. One of the most important tools we have at our disposal for conquering these challenges is oral communication. Through effective oral communication, we are able to express our thoughts, feelings, and needs in a clear and convincing manner, which can be the key to winning over obstacles and reaching our desired outcomes.

One of the most basic ways in which oral communication can help us to overcome challenges is by allowing us to clearly state our position or point of view. By speaking in a concise and persuasive manner, we can make a convincing argument for why we believe or deserve something, which can be critical in a negotiation or other situation where we need to advocate for ourselves.

Another way in which oral communication can help us to win over challenges is by building and maintaining relationships. By speaking clearly and effectively, we are able to build trust and credibility with others, which can be invaluable in situations where we need the support or cooperation of others in order to achieve our goals. Additionally, by maintaining open and honest communication with those around us, we are able to address any issues or conflicts that may arise and work to resolve them in a constructive and effective manner.

Oral communication also helps to build confidence in yourself, as it gives you the ability to express yourself effectively, makes you more comfortable with public speaking and enhances your ability to persuade others to see your point of view. This can be especially important when it comes to challenging situations, as confidence is often the key to facing and overcoming them.

Furthermore, by listening actively and attentively when others speak, we are able to gain new insights and perspectives, which can be invaluable in solving problems and finding creative solutions to challenges. By truly hearing and understanding what others have to say, we can often come up with a solution that benefits all parties involved.

In conclusion, oral communication is an essential tool for winning over the challenges of life. Through clear and effective communication, we can express ourselves persuasively, build and maintain relationships, enhance our confidence and gain valuable insights from others. It's important to practice and develop your oral communication skills, as they will be the key to conquering obstacles and achieving your goals.
