Research design refers to the overall strategy utilized to carry out research that defines a succinct and logical plan to tackle established research question through the collection, interpretation, analysis, and discussion of data.

3. state the meaning of quantitative research designs.​


Answer:

Quantitative research is the process of collecting and analyzing numerical data. It can be used to find patterns and averages, make predictions, test causal relationships, and generalize results to wider populations.

Answer:

Quantitative research design relates to the design of a research project which uses quantitative research methods. The design varies depending on the method used, which could be telephone interviews, face-to-face interviews, online surveys, or surveys by post  for instance. Other methodologies include SMS / Test Message surveys, or physical counts.

Quantitative research design is aimed at discovering how many people think, act or feel in a specific way. Quantitative projects involve large sample sizes, concentrating on the quantity of responses, as opposed to gaining the more focused or emotional insight that is the aim of qualitative research. The standard format in quantitative research design is for each respondent to be asked the same questions, which ensures that the entire data sample can be analysed fairly. The data is supplied in a numerical format, and can be analysed in a quantifiable way using statistical methods. Surveys can, however, be tailored to branch off if the respondent answers in a certain way - for instance people who are satisfied or dissatisfied with a service may be asked different questions subsequently.

Quantitative research design tends to favour closed-ended questions. Providing respondents with a set list of answers, they will not normally be able to give lengthy open-ended responses. This design ensures that the process of quantitative research is far more efficient than it would be if qualitative-style open ended questions were employed. It is more efficient because it is then not necessary to carry out the time-consuming process of coding vast quantities of open-ended responses.  However, quantitative research design does often allow the inclusion of an ‘Other’ category in the list of possible responses to questions, where appropriate. This allows those respondents who do not fit directly into the main categories to still get their precise responses recorded and used in the analysis of the research project results.

Chapter 3: Introduction

The Introduction chapter of a research study provides an overview of the research project, including the research question, the purpose of the study, and the significance of the study. It also provides background information on the topic being studied and sets the context for the research.

Research design:

The research design is the plan or framework for conducting a study. It outlines the methods and techniques that will be used to gather and analyze data. In a correlational study, the research design involves collecting data on two or more variables and examining the relationship between them.

Sampling procedure:

The sampling procedure refers to the method used to select a sample of participants for a study. It is important to choose a representative sample in order to ensure that the results of the study are generalizable to the larger population.

Scope and delimitation:

The scope of a study refers to the boundaries or limits of the research, including what will be studied and what will not be studied. The delimitation of a study refers to the limitations or constraints of the research, including any factors that may impact the results or interpretation of the study.

5W1H:

The 5W1H method is a technique used to gather and organize information about a particular topic. It stands for "what," "where," "when," "who," "why," and "how." These questions can be used to gather information about the context, purpose, and details of a research project or other topic.

BPL (Business Permit and Licensing):

A Business Permit and Licensing (BPL) is a document that allows a business to legally operate within a particular jurisdiction. It is typically issued by a local government agency and includes information about the business, such as its name, location, and type of operation.

In the context of research, the BPL may be relevant if the study involves collecting data from businesses or if the research is being conducted as part of a business or organizational context.
