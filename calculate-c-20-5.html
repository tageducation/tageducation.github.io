25. A 5-kg rock detaches from the side of a cliff 20 m above the ground. Calculate its (a) potential energy before it falls, (b) total mechanical energy, and (c) speed before it hits the ground.


Answer:

a 980 J

b 980 J

c 19.7 m/s

Explanation:

Calculating its potential energy:

Remember that Potential Energy = mass × gravity × height

[tex]\implies \rm Potential \ Energy = 5 \times 9.8 \times 20[/tex]

[tex]\implies \rm Potential \ Energy = 100 \times 9.8[/tex]

[tex]\implies \boxed{\rm Potential \ Energy = 980 \ J}[/tex]

Calculating its total mechanical energy:

Mechanical Energy = Kinetic Energy + Potential Energy

The total mechanical energy is equal to the initial total mechanical energy and final total mechanical energy because if the potential energy decreases its kinetic energy increases.

[tex]\implies \rm Mechanical \ Energy = Mechanical \ Energy_i = 0 + Potential \ Energy = Mechanical \ Energy_f = Kinetic \ Energy + 0[/tex]

[tex]\implies \rm Mechanical \ Energy = 980 \ J[/tex]

Calculating its speed before it this the ground:

Remember that [tex]\rm Speed = \sqrt{2gh}[/tex]

[tex]\implies \rm Speed = \sqrt{2(9.8)(20)}[/tex]

[tex]\implies \rm Speed = \sqrt{388}[/tex]

[tex]\implies \boxed{\rm Speed \approx 19.7}[/tex]
