5. which expands the greatest when heated at the same temperatures a. gas b. solid c. liquid d. all expand equally


The answer is C. Solid. This is because solids, when heated, can experience an increase in area and length.

When heated, the substance undergoes expansion, which causes expansion. An expansion is an event that expands a substance due to an increase in temperature that occurs. Objects can change shape to increase in length, width, area, or change in volume.

Solid particles are always moving (vibrating). When a solid receives heat energy, the particles move faster and thus require a larger space between them. The distance between the particles is also getting bigger, which in turn makes the solid expand, increase in length, expand, and finally increase in volume.

There are three forms of solid expansion, namely: long expansion, wide expansion, and volume expansion. Long-term expansion occurs when an object receives heat. In length expansion, the width and thickness values are very small compared to the length values of the object, so the width and thickness are considered non-existent.

Liquids have the main property of adapting to the shape of their container. Therefore, the expansion of liquids does not involve long or broad expansion but is only known as space expansion or volume expansion. The higher the temperature applied to a liquid, the greater its volume expansion. Examples of the expansion of liquids are in mercury or alcohol on a thermometer, which expands and contracts due to changes in temperature.
