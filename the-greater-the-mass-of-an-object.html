28. The greater the mass of an object,the greater is the force needed to___the object​


Answer:

The greater the mass of an object, the greater the force needed to move or accelerate the object. This is due to Newton's Second Law of Motion, which states that the acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on the object and inversely proportional to its mass. In other words, the more mass an object has, the more force is required to produce a given acceleration. For example, a small car requires less force to accelerate than a large truck because the truck has a greater mass. Similarly, it takes more force to lift a heavy object than a lighter object because the heavy object has more mass.
