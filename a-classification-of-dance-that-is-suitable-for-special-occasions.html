A classification of dance that is suitable for special occasions

The answer is Festival dance.

Festivals are intended to celebrate and encourage arts and activities exclusive to a specific community or society. Festivals may be a festival or a commemoration, but they both reflect the cultural heritage of the concerned group.

 

Festival dances are folk dances performed by a group of people who share the same faith to the heavy beats of percussion instruments, typically in honor of a Patron Saint or in thanksgiving for a bountiful harvest. Festival dances may be religious or secular. But the great thing about festivals is that they contribute to the joy and celebrations of the place where they are held, which is why they are called festival dances in the first place. Festival dances represent the people's way of life by gestures, costumes, and instruments native to their place. Festivals have become a constant crowd-drawing practice that, in turn, boosts the economy of a location through tourism and entertainment value.  Whatever festival we celebrate, whether it is in honor of a religious figure, history, custom, values, or industry, festival dances express the solidarity of Filipinos who remain resilient in the face of economic, physical, environmental, spiritual, and political challenges.

NATURE OF FESTIVAL DANCES

A.Cultural with religious influence

There are dances that are distinguished from movements that express respect for a religious figure claimed to have interceded in their personal life. Fiestas are celebrated pompously and joyfully in the Philippines, where the bulk of the population are Catholics. This festival shows appreciation for a bountiful harvest as well as a blessing or prayer given to them by divine intercession.

MORIONES FESTIVAL

An annual festival celebrated during Holy Week on the Philippine island of Marinduque. The "Moriones" are men and women dressed in robes and masks resembling biblical Roman soldiers as viewed by locals. The Moriones or Moryonan tradition has influenced the development of other festivals in the Philippines that incorporate cultural traditions or folk history into street festivals.
