2. what is the importance of nutrition assessment?​


The goal of nutritional assessment is to determine a patient's nutritional status, identify clinically related malnutrition, and monitor improvements in nutritional status. This method of evaluation would include a health and dietary background, a physical examination based on detecting signs and effects of nutritional changes, an anthropometric and body shape appraisal, laboratory testing, and appropriate complementary evaluations in each case. Since no particular nutritional measure is deemed a gold standard for making nutritional diagnoses, all nutritional appraisal metrics should be viewed collectively. a thorough examination of the patient's eating abnormalities history, including the causes and severity of dementia, coexisting conditions, the occurrence of behavioral issues, personal lifestyle, and sociocultural aspects.

14. give and explain the importance of maternal nutrition in pregnancy brainly


give and explain the importance of maternal nutrition in pregnancy

The ability of a mother to provide nutrients and oxygen to her child is critical to the health and survival of the fetus and the mother. Fetal malnutrition can result from a lack of adequate nutrients to meet fetal demand. Undernutrition causes the fetus to respond and adapt, permanently altering the body's structure and function. Overnutrition in the mother has long-term and negative consequences for the offspring's health. There is mounting evidence that maternal nutrition of the mother can alter the fetal genome's epigenetic makeup. Evidence from epidemiological and animal studies suggesting that fetal responses to the intrauterine environment of the mother may underlie the prevalence of many adult chronic diseases, including Type 2 (non-insulin-dependent) diabetes, has only recently emerged.
