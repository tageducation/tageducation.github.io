12. Learning Task 3: Answer the following separate sheet. 1. List all the four quantum numbers. a.b.c.d.​


Answer:1. List all the four quantum numbers.

a. energy (n)

The primary quantum number, n, denotes the primary electron shell. Because n specifies the most likely distance of electrons from the nucleus, the bigger the number n, the further the electron is from the nucleus, the larger the size of the orbital, and the larger the atom.

b. angular momentum (ℓ)

The geometry of an orbital, and hence its angular distribution, is determined by the orbital angular momentum quantum number l. The number of angular nodes equals the angular momentum quantum number l.

c. magnetic moment (mℓ)

The number of orbitals and their orientation inside a subshell are determined by the magnetic quantum number ml. As a result, its value is determined by the orbital angular momentum quantum number l. Given a given l, ml is an interval spanning from –l to +l, which means it can be zero, a negative integer, or a positive integer.

d.spin (ms)

Unlike n, l, and ml, the electron spin quantum number ms is independent of other quantum numbers. It denotes the direction of the electron spin and can have a spin of +1/2, denoted by, or –1/2, denoted by. This means that when ms is positive, the electron has an upward spin, which is referred to as "spin up," and when it is negative, the electron has a downward spin, which is referred to as "spin down." The significance of the electron spin quantum number is that it determines whether or not an atom can generate a magnetic field.

21. What are the four quantum numbers? explain each​


What are the four quantum numbers?

ANSWER

The four quantum numbers are the following:

Principal, Angular Momentum, Magnetic, and the Spin Quantum Number.

1. Principal or Energy (n) is the first quantum number which is described as the electron shell of an atom, it is also referred to as the energy level. This quantum number has a symbol of "n" with possible values of 1,2,3 . . . so on. Also, the principal quantum number will show which principal electron shells the electrons have occupied.

2. Angular Momentum (l) is also known as the Azimuthal or the Orbital quantum number and has a symbol of "l". This second quantum number describes the subshell of an atom and it gives the magnitude of the orbital-angular momentum through the relation. In quantum chemistry, azimuthal quantum number is very important because it specifies and describes the shape of an atomic orbital and it greatly influences bond angles and chemical bonds. Orbital quantum numbers have values from zero to n-1.

3. Magnetic Moment (ml) is the third set of the quantum numbers, with a given symbol of "ml" and values from -l to l, with integer steps in-between, for example; if the value of the angular momentum is 2, therefore ml values are -2,-1,0,1,2. Thus, these values are of this quantum number is associated with the orbital orientation. Magnetic moment describes the energy levels available within a subshell of an atom and will yield a projection of the orbital-angular momentum along the specific axis.

4. Spin (ms) is the fourth and last set of the quantum numbers. As it is called, this quantum number describes the spin of an electron around the orbital of an atom, it is as the intrinsic angular momentum. This quantum number has a value of either positive or negative 1/2, depending if the spin is clockwise or counter-clockwise. According to Pauli's exclusion principle, each electron in any individual orbital must have different spins, so, an orbital must never contain more than two electrons. Spin quantum number, in other words, describes the angular momentum of an electron.
