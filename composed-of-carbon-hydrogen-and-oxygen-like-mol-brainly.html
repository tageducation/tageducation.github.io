6. 4. A compound is composed of 52.14% carbon, 13.13% hydrogen, and 34.73% oxygen by mass. A) What is the empirical formula? B) if the molar mass of a compound is138.204 g/mol, what is the molecular formula?


A) SOLUTION:

Step 1: Assume that the mass of a compound is 100 g.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & mass_{\text{C}} = \text{52.14 g} \\ & mass_{\text{H}} = \text{13.13 g} \\ & mass_{\text{O}} = \text{34.73 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of each element.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & n_{\text{C}} = \text{52.14 g} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{12.011 g}} = \text{4.341 mol} \\ & n_{\text{H}} = \text{13.13 g} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{1.008 g}} = \text{13.026 mol} \\ & n_{\text{O}} = \text{34.73 g} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{15.999 g}} = \text{2.171 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Represent an empirical formula.

[tex]\text{empirical formula} = \text{C}_x\text{H}_y\text{O}_z[/tex]

Step 4: Divide the moles of each element by the least number of moles.

In this case, the least number of moles is 2.171 mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & x = \frac{\text{4.341 mol}}{\text{2.171 mol}} \approx 2 \\ & y = \frac{\text{13.026 mol}}{\text{2.171 mol}} = 6 \\ & z = \frac{\text{2.171 mol}}{\text{2.171 mol}} = 1 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 5: Write the empirical formula.

[tex]\boxed{\text{empirical formula} = \text{C}_2\text{H}_6\text{O}}[/tex]

Hence, the empirical formula of a compund is C₂H₆O.

---------------------------------------------------------

B) SOLUTION:

Step 1: Represent a molecular formula.

[tex]\text{molecular formula} = (\text{C}_2\text{H}_6\text{O})_n[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the empirical mass.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{empirical mass} & = \text{2(12.011 g/mol) + 6(1.008 g/mol) + 15.999 g/mol} \\ & = \text{46.069 g/mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Divide the molar mass by the empirical mass to find the value of n.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n & = \frac{\text{molar mass}}{\text{empirical mass}} \\ & = \frac{\text{138.204 g/mol}}{\text{46.069 g/mol}} \\ & \approx 3 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Multiply the subscripts by the value of n to obtain the molecular formula.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molecular formula} & = (\text{C}_2\text{H}_6\text{O})_3 \\ & = \boxed{\text{C}_6\text{H}_{18}\text{O}_3} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molecular formula of a compound is C₆H₁₈O₃.

8. The explosive, Trinitrotoluene (TNT) is composed of 37.0% Carbon, 2.20% hydrogen, 18.5% nitrogen, and 42.3% oxygen.a. The molar mass for TNT is 227 g/mol. b. Determine the empirical formula of TNT​


Finding the Empirical Formula of a compound.

Given

%C = 37.0 %%H = 2.20 %%N = 18.5 %%O = 42.3 %

Let the mass of the compound be 100g, so we derive the percentage per element in the given mass.

Mass of Carbon = 37 g

Mass of Hydrogen = 2.20 g

Mass of Nitrogen = 18.5 g

Mass of Oxygen = 42.3 g

Calculating the number of moles per element.

Molar Mass of Carbon = 12.0107 g/mol

37 g C x ( 1 mol C / 12.0107 g/mol C )

= 3.080586 mol

Molar Mass of Hydrogen = 1.0078 g/mol

2.20 g H x ( 1 mol H / 1.0078 g/mol H )

= 2.182973 mol

Molar Mass of Nitrogen = 14.0067 g/mol

18.5 g N = ( 1 mol N / 14.0067 g/mol )

= 1.320796 mol

Molar Mass of Oxygen = 15.9994 g/mol

42.3 g x ( 1 mol O / 15.9994 g/mol )

= 2.643849 mol

Dividing the number of moles per element by the least number of moles.

Represent the Empirical Formula

CⱼHₖNₗOₘ

j = 3.080586 / 1.320796

j = 2

k = 2.182973 / 1.320796

k = 2

l = 1.320796 / 1.320796

l = 1

m = 2.643849 / 1.320796

m = 2

Empirical Formula = C₂H₂NO₂
