Inequality has grown to ridiculous levels in the West as a whole. In economics, this is one of the most important things to think about.

In the past, this problem was solved by progressive taxation and controls on rents and wages. The less wealthy had strong and healthy bargaining power as a group because of the growing labor movement and a sense of teamwork that reached all parts of society.

Since neoliberalism came into power, the labor movement has been destroyed and society is more divided than ever. A British philosopher named Mary Midgely said that her generation was lucky because they had an ideal they could believe in. Today, people only believe in real politics, and market mechanisms have spread to every part of society, which is dangerously bad for society as a whole. The proletariat has been renamed the precariat, and many of us now live in a world that is very uncertain and confusing. There are many signs of a better world, but we don't have many tools to make real change, not the empty, meaningless change that passes for real change today.

The main idea behind redistributive economic justice, which is your third choice, is that there is enough for everyone. The question is how to get there in a world that is becoming more and more automated. Basic income is an idea that has been talked about a lot lately.
