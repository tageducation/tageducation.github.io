2. which of the following containers is used to store sauces?


Which of the following containers is used to store sauces Most sauces can be made in advance and refrigerated or frozen, making them perfect as last-minute additions. Place in a small container or bowl filled with plastic in a refrigerator or in a heavy-duty zip bag. A smaller container means less air pollution and less spoilage. Gently reheat sauces to  drink warm at low pressure. Caramel and chocolate sauce are better served over simmering water in a  double boiler.

Sauces should be packed in airtight containers and kept in a cool dry position away from moisture, water, light and pests. Nutrition made from starches containing eggs, milk, cream and other dairy products is  susceptible to bacterial contamination and foodborne illness. Sauces made with these ingredients should be kept out of the hazardous temperature zone.  

Thick sauce should also be prepared, eaten and stored with care. Such items should be kept in the  refrigerator and should never be left to remain at room temperature for too long.

Food safety: the sauce lasts a few days in the refrigerator, it lasts weeks and months in the freezer or in the jar Time to prepare: putting your sauce in the freezer means being prepared in advance to  use your sauce, and defrosting the night before, in the refrigerator or in the jar you can only open and use it  Time to store: in the case of jarring / canning, the storage process is tedious for one-off. Jarring will have the best shelf life in all likelihood (assuming you don't contaminate your jars, or end up with exploding glass), as freezing your sauce may cause a separation, and in the fridge it will pose a health risk quickly.

Storing sauces in tightly closed plastic freezer bags, in the fridge, is my favorite approach in the situation  you have mentioned, because it helps you to portion out smaller amounts, has a reasonable  midrange period of food protection, and is unlikely to cause any taste issues within three to six months,  let alone within one month. As for defrosting, it sounds like you're already preparing your dishes, so  placing a bag of sauce on a plate in the refrigerator in the morning wouldn't be a concern.
