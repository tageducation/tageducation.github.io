An autopsy report's contents list all the methods and evidence used in the investigation of a person's death. The correlation between the pathological and medical findings is emphasized in the report. The report includes a summary of all data pertaining to the person's passing. Depending on the sort of toxicology reports required, the report may require several weeks to complete.

Autopsy Report Format

The autopsy report follows a common format approved by the National Association of Medical Examiners. It looks different depending on the state, but includes the following information:

Diagnosis

Medical records are an important part of an autopsy. In addition to medical history,  records may include mental health issues, prescription and medication history, and family and social history. What appears to be a simple fall and head injury can be viewed in light of the history of falls and their consequences.  

Toxicology report

Some of these results are available immediately, while others take weeks to run and record.

Opinion

This section contains a summary of initial findings and conclusions. In many cases, the conclusions of this section will dictate what additional research is required.

Circumstances of death

Autopsy findings include the cause of death (this includes factors that contribute to death, such as “blunt trauma”). Cause of death (natural death, accidental death, suicide, homicide, undetermined) and mechanism of death (this notation refers to a stab wound, gunshot wound, or drug overdose).

Documents verifying the identity of the testator

Contains important statistics.  General description of clothing and personal items. This section is self-explanatory.

Proof of medical intervention

This part of the report refers to evidence from previous surgical procedures and prescription drugs.

Physical examination

An external examination covers the body from head to toe and includes the location, measurements, and recording of wounds, scars, markings, tattoos, and body and structural conditions.

Internal examination of the body

This is usually thought of when describing an autopsy. This looks into a surgical procedure to remove an organ for examination. It may also include an evaluation of bullet trajectories and other perceptible factors. I can attach a picture of the result for later reference. Hand Calculations in Structural Analysis

Simple hand calculations are an extremely fast and easy way to evaluate the effects of simple forces on simple structures

Finite Element Analysis (FEA) is a complex numerical method used to solve complicated problems which contain a number of variable inputs such as boundary conditions, applied loads and support types. It is a far more complicated, yet accurate method to run structural analysis compared to hand calculations
