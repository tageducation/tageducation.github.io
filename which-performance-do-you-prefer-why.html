The weighted raw score of the students' summative assessments will serve as the basis for all grades. An individual learning area's passing grade is 60, which is converted to 75 on the report card. For final grades and quarterly grades, the lowest score that can be recorded is 60.

The new K–12 grading system, unlike the KPUP, has a minimum grade of 60 but will convert to a rating of 75 on the report card. For all the interim and final grades, a student can only receive a 60.

A grading system's objectives are to provide kids with feedback so they may take control of their learning and to notify all those who assist these students, including instructors, special educators, parents, and others.

Ballroom dance is a type of partner dance originating at the end of the sixteenth century in France. Commonly used as shorthand for any partner dance, ballroom has today evolved into two main subgenres – standard/smooth and Latin/rhythm.

Dances within these categories include the waltz, tango and foxtrot, and pasodoble, bolero and samba.

Ballroom is a popular form of competitive dance, or dancesport, with competitions being held all over the world.
