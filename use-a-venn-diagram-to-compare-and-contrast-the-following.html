10. Using the Venn diagram ,Compare and contrast the following terms " Articulation' Pronunciation"​


Articulation can loosely be defined as making sounds through the movement of speech organs. This means an individual can change the speech sounds that he makes by moving his teeth, lips and tongue. In phonology, much emphasis is laid on articulation. It talks about the manner in which sound is produced with the assistance of speech organs and the air flow. It also pays attention to how consonants and vowels are sounded in a very systematic manner. However, overall articulation is very much related to producing sound through speech organs. Now, let us look at pronunciation.

Articulation refers to the usage of speech organs such as tongue, jaws, lips, etc. in order to produce sounds while pronunciation refers to the way words need to be sounded when speaking. In this sense it can be stated that the main difference between articulation and pronunciation lies in the fact that articulation is more individualistic where it concentrates on the individual making sounds whereas pronunciation is more about how syllables of a word need to be said focusing on rhythm, stress and intonation.

A. Difference Between website and printed materials:

There are several differences between websites and printed materials:

Format: Websites are digital and can be accessed online, while printed materials are physical and must be physically handled.

Content: Websites can contain a wide range of content, including text, images, audio, and video, while printed materials are typically limited to text and images.

Update frequency: Websites can be updated frequently, while printed materials are static and cannot be easily updated once they are published.

Accessibility: Websites can be accessed from any device with an internet connection, while printed materials can only be accessed in physical form.

Cost: Websites can be created and maintained at a lower cost than printed materials, as there are no printing or distribution costs.

Similarities: Both websites and printed materials can be used to convey information, and both can use visual elements such as images and layout to enhance the reader's experience.

B. Difference Between images and podcasts:

Format: Images are static visual representations, while podcasts are audio recordings that can be streamed or downloaded.

Content: Images can depict a wide range of subjects, from people and places to abstract concepts and ideas. Podcasts are typically audio recordings of spoken content, such as interviews, lectures, or discussions.

Accessibility: Images can be accessed on any device with a screen, while podcasts require a device that can play audio, such as a smartphone or computer.

Update frequency: Images are static and do not change, while podcasts are typically updated regularly with new episodes.

Similarities: Both images and podcasts can be used to convey information and ideas, and both can be shared and accessed online. Both can also be used to tell a story or convey a message in a creative and engaging way.
