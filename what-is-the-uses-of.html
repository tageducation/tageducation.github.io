9. What is uses of taiko instrument​?What is uses of Sheng instrument?What is uses of piri instrument?


Answer:

TAIKO

A taiko is a drum made from an open-ended wooden barrel with animal skin stretched over both ends. It is played with two bachi (wooden sticks). There are two distinct methods of fitting the animal skin to the drums. Those that have skins tacked directly onto both ends are collectively called byodome-daiko.

SHENG

sheng, Chinese free reed wind instrument consisting of usually 17 bamboo pipes set in a small wind-chest into which a musician blows through a mouthpiece. Each pipe has a free reed, made of metal (or formerly of bamboo or reed), that vibrates to produce sound when a finger hole on the pipe is covered.

PIRI

The piri is a cylindrical double-reed bamboo oboe with eight finger holes, one in the back for the thumb and seven in the front. Its large reed and cylindrical bore gives it a mellower sound than that of many other types of oboe.

Answer:

answer: 4n-12

Step-by-step explanation:

Four times a number decreased by twelve. "x" is my favorite variable so I will use it. I would interpret this to be 4(x-12).

4(x-12) is literally 4 times (a number "x" decreased by "-" 12).

If x were 22 then it would be 4(x-12) = 4(22-12) = 4(10) = 40

BUT some may read it as 4x-12 which would be 4(22)-12 = 88-12 = 76

So the reading of these two equations[4(x-12) and 4x-12] are very similar if not the same although they produce very different values for each number "x".

This highlights the importance of the order of operations (PEDMAS), interpretation, and clarity if ever asked to put an equation into words. It is important to look for key words in order to understand what operations are going on.

Lets look at the example:

Let n= a number (remember when it is says "a number" it is talking about a variable and a variable can be represented by any letter)

Four times a number decreased by twelve

4 x n - 12

times means multiplication

decreased means subtraction
