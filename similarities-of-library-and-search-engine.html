21. Similarities and Differences in Using The Library and Search Engine​


The Library and Search Engine

Similarities

Both of the library and search engine provide definitions while also educating you and helping you learn.

Differences

Books and articles in the library authored by journalists or subject-matter specialists Subject matter experts and publishers review every piece of information in the database to ensure its validity and veracity. routinely reviewed and updated.

Internet Anyone may share their beliefs and ideas online due to search engines' lack of regulation. Not assessed (for the most part). Websites must be more thoroughly assessed for bias, accuracy, and completeness. Many websites are not regularly updated and can eventually become obsolete.

Many of the library's databases feature a citation generator that will create an APA or MLA style reference for the article you choose. These tools serve as a solid beginning point for crediting your articles in a certain manner, while you may still need to "tweak" this citation.

The majority of websites that may be accessed on a search engine do not offer a citation generator or a ready-made APA or MLA reference for the webpages on their website. Using APA or MLA style guides, handouts, or other resources provided by your instructor or the library, you must start your citation from fresh.
