First, before responding to your question, I'd like to do a quick review on "cohesive devices" to ensure understanding.

Cohesive devices (sometimes called "cohesive ties", and also known as anaphors). A cohesive device is essentially a word or a phrase that refers to an earlier word or phrase (e.g., "my cousin said she was coming", "she" is used as an anaphor for "my cousin").

Cohesive devices are words or phrases that are used to connect ideas between different parts of text. There are three (3) main types of cohesive devices:

(1) Pronouns that refer back to a previously mentioned noun. Example: Sissy went out to the playground across the street from school. She played on the swings for hours.(2) Substitution of a synonym for a previously mentioned noun. Example: There was a lot of food, but she only ate garlic bread.(3) Transition words and inter-clausal connectors such as so or because. Example #1: Ellena was late, so she took the bus. Ellena was late, because she took the bus. Example #2: Jim played on the swings. Later, Jim played ball.

You'll notice that cohesive devices affect comprehension because they help readers integrate information between sentences in a text. The pronoun, synonym, or transition word must be tied to a previous word, phrase or clause (the antecedent) in the sentence or the text.
