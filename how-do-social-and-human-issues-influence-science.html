15. How do social and human issues influence science?​


Answer & Explanation:

Social and human issues can have a significant impact on the development and direction of scientific research. Some ways in which these issues can influence science include:

1. Determining research priorities: Social and human issues can help determine which scientific questions and areas of research are considered important and deserving of funding and resources. For example, a major health crisis may lead to increased funding for medical research, while concerns about climate change may lead to greater investment in environmental science.

2. Shaping public perception: Social and human issues can shape the public's perception of science and the role it plays in society. For example, debates about the morality of certain scientific advancements, such as genetic engineering or cloning, can influence public opinion and impact the trajectory of scientific research in these areas.

3. Driving technological innovations: Social and human issues can also drive technological innovations in science. For example, advances in communication technologies have been driven in part by a desire to connect people and address social and human issues, such as the need for greater access to information and the ability to communicate over long distances.

4. Ethical considerations: Social and human issues can also raise ethical considerations that must be addressed in scientific research. For example, issues related to privacy, autonomy, and informed consent are increasingly important as scientific advances have the potential to impact individuals and society in profound ways.

Influence of society on science

Social and human issues influence science by in that studies that try to address them may be inspired by these issues. Social Issues in science is to investigate the arguments over how science and technology affect human life.

Let’s we describe in detail

Social and Human issues are problems that men and women (human beings) or a community must address and resolve. Science's knowledge and worldview have an impact on society. The social issues that serve as the stimulus for scientific study are also frequently taken into consideration when selecting the goals of scientific research. The application of scientific knowledge helps to satisfy a number of fundamental human needs and goals and raise living standards, which has an impact on science in the same way that social and human issues do. The motivation for scientific inquiry is provided by human conditions.

Society also plays a role in deciding how resources are allocated to support scientific research, supporting some types of research while discouraging others. The interests and needs of society have a direct impact on scientists, who frequently focus their study on issues that will benefit society. At the most fundamental level, society influences scientists' aims, expectations, attitudes, and assumptions, all of which influence the research questions they choose to address and the methods they use to do so.
