The Cedar Waxwing often migrates from one place to another. On the other hand, the Cardinal stays in one place year after year. Cardinal Cedar Waxwing​


Answer:

COMPARE AND CONTRAST

Explanation:

is a way of finding similarities and differences

GRAMMATICAL SIGNALS

these are different words used in the sentence to point out the

similarities and differences of the two items.

Here is the answer to the above questions:

GRAMMATICAL SIGNALS WORDS USED ARE;

-' but'

-' some differences'

-'both'

-' on the other hand'

COMPARE/COMPARISON

-The Cardinal and Cedar Waxwing are both common birds

-They have crests on their heads.

CONTRAST:

- The crest that can be found in Male Cardinal is bright

red in color

-The crest in Waxwing bird is brown in color.

-The Cedar waxwing birds usually do a migration or transfer from one place to another.

-The Cardinal bird stays for at least 1 year in one place

Answer:

GRAMMATICAL SIGNAL WORDS

Explanation:

GRAMMATICAL SIGNAL WORDS serves as a writing instrument that aids to maintain text coherence.

It is also considered as the phrases and special words that create a connection between ideas.

There are common grammatical/signal words emphasis, comparison or contrast, cause and effect, and  illustration.

Here is the answer to the above paragraph.

*GRAMMATICAL SIGNAL WORDS

- 'both'

-' two common'

-' some differences'

- 'while'

- 'On the other hand'

*COMPARISON AND CONTRAST of Two Different Birds 'Cardinal and Cedar Waxwing'

A. COMPARISON

-Both have crests on their head

-they are known as Common Birds

B. CONTRAST

- Male Cardinal has bright red crests on its head

-Cardinal stays in one place for at least a year-long before migrating to another place.

-Cedar Waxwing has brown color crests

-Cedar Waxwing usually migrates from one place to another.
