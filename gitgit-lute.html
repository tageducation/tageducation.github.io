A lute is string instrument that is deeply rounded with a ovoid body, but early on, lute included just about any instrument that had a neck, soundboard, and a body. It was very popular in the 16-18th century, during the Renaissance, in Europe (including Spain) and other countries such as France, Italy, and Germany. During this time the size of the instruments were most frequently used for solo performances. It was used by traveling musicians and also for publishing, purchasing, and performing music.
