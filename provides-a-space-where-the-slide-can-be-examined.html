2. it provides a space where the slide can be examined​


Answer:

Stage

Explanation:

A microscope is a laboratory instrument used to examine objects by producing enlarged images of objects that are too small to be seen by the naked eye. In the late 16th century, a Dutch spectacle maker invented the light microscope using lenses and light to magnify images. Today’s high-power microscopes or compound microscopes use two sets of lenses to provide a higher level of magnification and clarity. The first set of lenses is called oculars or the eyepiece and the second set is called the objectives. The eyepiece is what the viewer looks into while the objectives are the lenses closest to the specimen.

All microscopes are include a stage. The stage is where the specimen is placed for observation. Stages often have a mechanical device that holds the specimen slide in place called stage clips. The mechanical stage can be moved back and forth as well as from side to side in order to observe a specimen better.

7. what part of the microscope provides a space where the slide can be examined​


Answer:

Stage

Stages are often equipped with a mechanical device that holds the specimen slide in place and can smoothly translate the slide back and forth as well as from side to side.
